Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1853

Name: Hidden-Driver Convergence Theory of Oncogene-Negative LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: A large fraction of apparently oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinomas are not driverless; they are produced by convergent activation of a limited set of signaling states through lesion classes that standard hotspot panels under-detect. These hidden classes include rare noncanonical coding variants, structural fusions, exon-skipping events, focal or complex amplifications, noncoding/promoter lesions, and regulatory or proteomic activation states. The practical implication is that driver-negative LUAD should be reclassified by mechanism of pathway activation rather than by absence of canonical mutations.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Driver negativity is often an assay-defined artifact rather than a biological absence of oncogenic control

Statement: In lung adenocarcinoma, especially in never-smokers and tumors initially negative on limited RTK/RAS/RAF testing, a substantial subset of 'driver-negative' cases are reclassified as driver-positive when broader assays interrogate structural variation, splicing, noncanonical exons/domains, copy-number architecture, and RNA/protein consequences; therefore, the observed driver-negative state is frequently produced by incomplete observability of driver classes rather than true absence of dominant oncogenic lesions.

Domain/Scope: Applies to primary lung adenocarcinoma cohorts initially classified as negative by hotspot genotyping, limited clinical panels, standard WES, or mutation-centric workflows, particularly never-smoker cohorts and studies that subsequently added RNA-seq, WGS, FISH, fusion assays, deep sequencing, or proteogenomics. It does not claim that all driver-negative tumors are assay artifacts; some remain unexplained after broad profiling.

Special Cases

  1. A residual subset remains unresolved even after WGS/RNA/proteomics, so the law is probabilistic rather than universal.
  2. Low tumor purity/cellularity can itself be a major cause of false-negative calls even when the correct assay class is used.
  3. Some studies define driver negativity differently, so rescue rates are assay-definition dependent.

Statement 1: Hidden drivers organize into recurrent mechanistic subtypes rather than an undifferentiated residual class

Statement: After broad molecular profiling, oncogene-negative LUAD segregates into recurring mechanistic subtypes defined by the mode of pathway activation: kinase-fusion driven, ligand-fusion/ERBB-context driven, RasGAP/PI3K suppressor-collapse driven, enhancer or super-enhancer rewiring driven, complex amplification/ecDNA or rearrangement driven, and proteomic or phospho-regulatory state-driven tumors. These subtypes are partially predictable from histology, smoking status, lineage state, and phosphoproteomic context, and each implies a distinct therapeutic logic.

Domain/Scope: Applies to lung adenocarcinoma after exclusion or separate labeling of standard canonical drivers and after use of broad assays that can detect structural, regulatory, and protein-state abnormalities. Most strongly supported in never-smoker LUAD, invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma, and WGS/proteogenomic cohorts. It is a subtype theory, not a claim that every tumor maps cleanly to one pure class.

Special Cases

  1. Some tumors harbor mixed mechanisms or unresolved biology despite broad profiling.
  2. Histology is informative but not deterministic; for example mucinous morphology enriches NRG1 and some kinase fusions but is not exclusive to them.
  3. Certain suppressor-loss states may be more accurately defined by pathway activation than by any single genomic event.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If all clinically driver-negative LUADs undergo joint DNA/RNA fusion calling, splice-aware analysis, copy-number architecture reconstruction, and phosphoproteomics, the residual truly unexplained fraction will fall substantially below panel-defined driver-negative rates.
  2. Invasive mucinous adenocarcinomas that are KRAS-negative will be enriched for ligand-fusion or noncanonical kinase-fusion mechanisms relative to non-mucinous driver-negative LUAD.
  3. Tumors with strong pathway phospho-activation but no coding driver will disproportionately harbor noncoding/promoter lesions, focal suppressor TSS deletions, or enhancer/super-enhancer rewiring on WGS plus chromatin profiling.
  4. ALK/ROS1/RET/NTRK fusion-positive hidden-driver tumors will show cleaner mutual exclusivity with other major drivers than regulatory-rewiring or suppressor-collapse tumors, because the former more often act as single dominant kinase engines.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. A clinically useful hidden-driver classifier built from histology plus a low-plex phosphoprotein panel plus shallow WGS breakpoint features will predict the mechanistic subtype of driver-negative LUAD without full proteogenomics.
  2. Some currently unresolved driver-negative LUADs will prove to be microbiome- or ligand-conditioned tumors in which no genomic alteration is necessary for pathway addiction, but the state will still map onto one of the same phospho-signaling archetypes.
  3. A subset of driver-negative LUADs with no DNA/RNA lesion but stable phospho-ERBB3 or phospho-SHP2 activation will be maintained by extracellular ligand traps or juxtacrine signaling from stromal compartments rather than tumor-intrinsic genomic events.
  4. Long-read plus Hi-C profiling of residual unexplained cases will reveal a recurrent class of TAD-boundary or ecDNA-mediated enhancer rewiring around noncanonical LUAD genes not yet recognized as drivers.

Negative Experiments

  1. If comprehensive multi-omic testing fails to reduce the residual driver-negative fraction compared with hotspot/WES-only workflows, the observability-artifact component of the theory is weakened.
  2. If hidden drivers discovered by broad profiling do not cluster into reproducible mechanistic subtypes with distinct pathway outputs, the subtype-taxonomy component is weakened.
  3. If histology, smoking status, and phosphoproteomic context show no enrichment for specific hidden-driver classes, the phenotype-predictability claim is weakened.
  4. If most rescued lesions prove to be biologically incidental and not therapeutically or functionally dominant, the convergence component is weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some cohorts explicitly report no specific rescued hidden driver and instead identify transcriptomic or prognostic states without mechanistic resolution, indicating that current data still leave a residual unexplained compartment. [e2042.0] [e2072.0] [e2129.0] [e2085.0] [e2079.0]
  2. Some spatially heterogeneous tumors may appear driver-negative because of sampling rather than assay class alone; this is related but not fully resolved by the present theory. [e2137.0] [e2100.0]
