Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1848

Name: Evolutionary Field-State Theory of Never-Smoker and Driver-Poor LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: A major subset of driver-poor LUAD, especially in never-smokers and early lesions, is driven by cell-state evolution within a pre-neoplastic epithelial field rather than by abrupt acquisition of a single dominant oncogene. Intermediate epithelial states, lineage plasticity, angiogenic tipping points, APOBEC mutagenesis, and copy-number-quiet stem-like evolutionary routes create tumors that can appear genomically underdriven at diagnosis. This theory explains why some never-smoker LUADs have few canonical drivers, low SCNA burden, or precursor-dominant biology yet still progress aggressively.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Some driver-poor LUADs arise through prolonged pre-malignant field states rather than early dominant-driver fixation

Statement: In never-smoker and low-copy-number LUAD, a subset of tumors emerges from long-lived epithelial precursor fields characterized by quiescent or intermediate alveolar states, low SCNA burden, high intratumor heterogeneity, and delayed clonal dominance; therefore, apparent driver-negativity can reflect evolutionary timing and field-state persistence rather than absence of oncogenic potential.

Domain/Scope: Applies primarily to never-smoker lung adenocarcinoma, precursor-rich early-stage disease, and copy-number-quiet/piano-like tumors; most relevant to East Asian or never-smoker cohorts enriched for precancerous lesions and low-SCNA disease. Not meant for genomically unstable smoker-associated LUAD dominated by truncal TP53/RTK-RAS lesions.

Special Cases

  1. Some never-smoker tumors still have canonical EGFR, ALK, RET, or other strong drivers and are outside the core of this law.
  2. The law is about one evolutionary route, not all driver-negative tumors.
  3. Carcinoids share some piano-like features but are biologically distinct from LUAD.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. The copy-number-quiet piano subtype of never-smoker lung cancer showed low TMB, low WGD, long telomeres, high subclonal mutation burden, fewer known drivers, and evidence consistent with tumors arising from quiescent adult stem-like cells before a dominant clone emerges. [e2008.0] [e1966.0]
  2. The same never-smoker evolutionary study reported that some piano tumors had no detected known cancer driver gene mutations or fusions, directly supporting a field-state explanation for apparently underdriven tumors. [e2008.0] [e1966.0]
  3. Early progression multi-omics in never-smokers showed non-linear progression across AAH, AIS, and MIA, with subtype transitions not reducible to a simple linear driver accumulation model and instead heavily shaped by changing endothelial, stromal, and immune composition. [e1984.0] [e1984.1] [e1984.2] [e1984.5] [e2015.0]
  4. KRT8-positive alveolar intermediate cells (KACs) were enriched in tumor-adjacent normal tissue, increased from matched normal to AAH to invasive LUAD, and were positioned as intermediates between AT2 cells and tumor cells, indicating a pre-malignant field state. [e2005.0] [e1976.0] [e1923.2]
  5. KRAS transcriptional programs were detectable in KACs and even in some KRAS-wild-type LUAD, with poor-survival associations, showing that field-state progression can precede or exceed genotype-level classification. [e1976.1] [e2005.0] [e1976.2]

Statement 1: Late transition to invasion in driver-poor LUAD is often microenvironment-triggered

Statement: In early and never-smoker driver-poor LUAD, the decisive transition from indolent precursor-like growth to invasion is commonly mediated by a switch to angiogenic and stromal remodeling states—especially tip endothelial cell and matrix-CAF co-enrichment—rather than by a newly dominant canonical oncogene mutation.

Domain/Scope: Applies to early-stage never-smoker LUAD progression from AAH/AIS to MIA and to angiogenesis-high EGFR/ALK-wild-type adenocarcinoma; strongest in lesions with radiologic pGGO to non-pGGO transition and microenvironment-dominant biology.

Special Cases

  1. Canonical oncogene-positive lesions can also invade and are not excluded by this law.
  2. The law concerns transition timing, not necessarily initiation of the lesion.
  3. In advanced unstable tumors, later invasion may be driven by additional genomic events as well.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. AIS3 was identified as the key transition state before invasion, with increased endothelial and stromal scores, enrichment of tip endothelial cells and matrix CAFs, and association with non-pGGO radiology. [e1984.1]
  2. Spatial transcriptomics and paired MIA invasive versus noninvasive analyses showed higher endothelial-cell abundance in invasive regions and genes upregulated in invasive components enriched in endothelial programs. [e1984.2]
  3. The angiogenesis-high subtype in EGFR/ALK-wild-type never-smoker LUAD was marked by TGF-β/cell-migration programs, CAF-associated immune exclusion, proangiogenic proteins such as LGALS3, FGF2, CXCL12, and PDGFB, and poor prognosis. [e1949.3]
  4. AAH and AIS/MIA progression studies explicitly argued for endothelial and stromal remodeling, rather than a single new mutation, as hidden drivers of early progression and invasion. [e1984.0] [e2015.0]
  5. AQP4 expression correlated positively with angiogenesis and EMT in LUAD, providing independent transcriptomic support for a microenvironment-remodeling axis outside canonical driver mutation logic. [e1942.1]

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Serially sampled pGGO lesions that later become invasive will show an endothelial/CAF state transition before they show any recurrent new canonical driver mutation.
  2. Piano-like never-smoker LUADs will contain broader histologic or molecular field abnormalities in adjacent non-tumor epithelium than forte-like tumors.
  3. KAC-high, KRAS-signature-high but KRAS-wild-type lesions will be enriched in cases with multifocal or metachronous adenocarcinoma development.
  4. Within early never-smoker lesions, non-pGGO conversion will correlate better with tip-EC/mCAF signatures than with total mutation burden.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Interception of the AIS3 endothelial-CAF transition with vessel-normalizing or CAF-modulating therapy may prevent invasion even when epithelial genotype remains unchanged.
  2. Some piano tumors may never acquire a single dominant oncogene and instead progress by distributed subclonal cooperation across a field, which would challenge clone-centric treatment logic.
  3. Spatially resolved lineage tracing in human-like organoid or xenograft models may reveal that MIF-high glycolytic cells, KAC-like cells, and AIS3 endothelial-rich niches are sequential states of one broader never-smoker field evolution program.
  4. Longitudinal plasma methylome or cfRNA assays may detect driver-poor field-state progression earlier than ctDNA mutation assays in piano-like or precursor-dominant disease.

Negative Experiments

  1. If non-pGGO conversion and invasion consistently follow newly acquired canonical driver mutations rather than angiogenic/stromal transition, the microenvironment-triggered transition law is weakened.
  2. If piano-like tumors do not show greater field-state persistence, subclonality, or adjacent epithelial abnormalities than other never-smoker tumors, the field-state component is weakened.
  3. If KAC-like states are absent from precursor lesions and tumor-adjacent lung in independent cohorts, the intermediate-field mechanism is undermined.
  4. If blocking endothelial/CAF remodeling has no effect on invasion in representative preclinical models of driver-poor progression, the transition law is challenged.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Many never-smoker LUADs are clearly driven by EGFR mutation, ALK fusion, RET fusion, or other canonical drivers and are not explained by a field-state route. [e2014.0] [e1949.0] [e1977.0] [e1951.0]
  2. The exact causal lesion in many piano tumors remains unspecified, and the stem-like interpretation is currently inferential rather than experimentally proven. [e2008.0] [e1966.0]
