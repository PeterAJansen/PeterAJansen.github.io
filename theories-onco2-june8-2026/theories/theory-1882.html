Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1882

Name: Pathway-reinstatement without canonical oncogenes

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: A large fraction of ostensibly driver-negative lung adenocarcinomas are not truly pathway-negative. Instead, they achieve the same proliferative outputs usually produced by RTK/RAS/RAF oncogenes through less obvious mechanisms: combinatorial loss of negative regulators of RAS and PI3K signaling, rare coding mutations outside standard hotspot panels, exon-skipping events, cryptic fusions, focal amplifications, and occasionally protein-level activation uncoupled from obvious DNA lesions. The central implication is that 'driver-negative' LUAD is often a failure of driver representation, not absence of oncogenic circuitry.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Hidden-pathway-reactivation law

Statement: In lung adenocarcinomas classified as lacking canonical RTK/RAS/RAF oncogenes, recurrent hidden lesions disproportionately converge on reactivation of MAPK and/or PI3K-AKT signaling, so true biological driver-negativity is rarer than panel-negative status.

Domain/Scope: Applies to primary and metastatic LUADs initially classified as oncogene-negative, pan-negative, non-CAGA, or RTK/RAS/RAF-negative by targeted genotyping, WES, or incomplete clinical assays; strongest where deeper WGS, RNA-seq, fusion calling, exon-skipping analysis, or phospho/protein assays were subsequently performed.

Special Cases

  1. A residual subset remains unexplained even after WGS/RNA-seq and may use noncanonical lineage, immune, metabolic, or epigenetic mechanisms rather than direct RTK/RAS/RAF reactivation.
  2. Some tumors called driver-negative by narrow clinical testing become canonical-driver-positive after broader profiling; this law concerns the whole class of such reclassifications and pathway-convergent rescues.
  3. Protein-level activation can occur without a matching coding lesion, so genomic negativity does not imply signaling inactivity.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. In comprehensive LUAD profiling, 38% of tumors were initially classified as oncogene-negative for known activating RTK/RAS/RAF events, but this same analysis identified MET amplification, ERBB2 amplification, NF1 mutation, RIT1 mutation, and MET exon 14 skipping as recurrent alternative lesions in this subset, explicitly expanding targetable pathway alterations. [e2290.0] [e2275.2] [e2255.0] [e2255.1]
  2. In WGS refinement of RTK/RAS/RAF-negative LUAD, 28 of 85 cases initially negative by WES/RNA-seq were rescued as pathway-altered by WGS, including hidden KRAS mutations, EGFR/ARAF/MAPK1/SOS1 amplifications, NF1 and RASA1 deletions, and NRG1 fusions/mutations, demonstrating that a large fraction of apparent driver-negative cases are assay-limited false negatives. [e2279.0] [e2284.0] [e2284.1] [e2284.2]
  3. Pan-negative clinical cohorts repeatedly yielded occult canonical or near-canonical drivers upon broader interrogation: EGFR extracellular-domain M277E in 1 of 143 pan-negative LUADs, ROS1 fusions in 2 of 202 East Asian never-smoker pan-negative cases, CD74-NRG1 in pan-negative never-smoker IMAs, and KIF5B-RET in triple-negative or double-negative lung adenocarcinomas. [e2277.0] [e2277.1] [e2277.2] [e2285.1] [e2299.0] [e2299.1] [e2299.2] [e2325.0] [e2325.1] [e2332.0] [e2332.1] [e2332.2]
  4. RNA-seq of canonical-driver-negative LUAD uncovered multiple nonstandard kinase alterations including novel tyrosine kinase fusions and MET exon 14 skipping, and even rescued EGFR mutations missed by conventional screening. [e2270.0] [e2270.1] [e2270.2]
  5. Integrated transcriptomic meta-analysis of driver-unknown lung cancers showed that recurrent NRG1 fusions/outlier expression, Hippo pathway fusions, NF1 truncating events, and MET exon 14 skipping together explained about 16% of driver-unknown tumors, indicating that alternative recurrent lesions are common within the apparently negative pool. [e2291.0] [e2291.1] [e2291.3] [e2291.4]
  6. Rare coding alterations in canonical signaling nodes can define true hidden-driver subsets within oncogene-negative LUAD, including RIT1 switch II mutations in 5 of 87 oncogene-negative TCGA tumors and recurrent RAF-family hotspot mutations in tumors lacking known major oncogenes. [e2281.0] [e2281.1] [e2281.2] [e2309.0] [e2309.1] [e2309.2]
  7. Proteogenomic data show that pathway output can be present even when canonical DNA drivers are absent or incomplete: EGFR-mutant tumors had strong SHP2 Y62 phosphorylation without RNA or protein abundance change, and EGFR-wild-type tumors with high MAPK signaling existed in phosphoproteomic analysis, mostly explained by KRAS but showing that signaling-state assays can reveal biology missed by mutation labels. [e2304.2] [e2311.1] [e2342.0] [e2357.0]
  8. The review literature explicitly argues that many LUADs lacking actionable mutations are rescued by deeper profiling and that broader genomic and proteogenomic assays are needed because standard testing misses actionable alterations. [e2275.1] [e2256.0] [e2284.2] [e2357.1]

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This theory is built from existing observations but integrates them into a broader explanatory framework that unifies WGS rescue, RNA-based rescue, and phosphoproteomic rescue under one pathway-convergence principle.

What Already Exists: It is already established that some panel-negative LUADs harbor occult fusions, amplifications, exon-skipping events, or rare coding mutations. The general idea of deeper sequencing rescuing hidden drivers is therefore known.

What is Novel: The novel synthesis is that these disparate findings are not isolated anecdotes but expressions of a higher-order law: most clinically important 'driver-negative' LUADs are better understood as pathway-reinstated tumors whose lesions are distributed across under-profiled molecular layers rather than absent altogether.

Statement 1: Suppressor-convergence law

Statement: A major biologic subclass of true oncogene-negative LUAD is driven by combinatorial loss of negative regulators of RAS and PI3K signaling rather than by a single activated proto-oncogene; in this subclass, dual-pathway output is the driver-state and the genotype is distributed across multiple tumor-suppressor lesions.

Domain/Scope: Applies to oncogene-negative LUADs lacking known proto-oncogene alterations but showing pERK/pAKT activation, especially tumors with NF1, RASA1, PTEN, STK11, KEAP1, or related suppressor loss; strongest in mouse models and human tumors validated by pathway IHC or pathway signatures.

Special Cases

  1. Single suppressor loss is often insufficient; the law concerns combinatorial or functionally convergent loss.
  2. Some suppressor-defined tumors remain only moderately dependent on downstream inhibitors unless the full combinatorial state is present.
  3. This law explains a substantial subtype, not all residual driver-negative tumors.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Autochthonous oncogene-negative LUAD modeling identified combined inactivation of Nf1, Rasa1, and Pten as the strongest tumor-initiating combination; triple loss was nearly as potent as KRASG12D, all mutational routes to the triple state were accessible, and the triple-mutant genotype had the highest relative fitness. [e2268.0] [e2268.1] [e2268.2] [e2259.0] [e2259.1] [e2259.2] [e2262.0] [e2262.1]
  2. Human oncogene-negative LUADs validated this pathway-state model: over 60% had alterations in either RAS or PI3K pathways, 22% had alterations in both, about 45% of oncogene-negative tumors showed moderate-to-strong pERK and pAKT, and many PTEN-protein-low tumors lacked PTEN genomic mutation, implying convergent non-genomic suppressor loss as well. [e2268.0] [e2268.1] [e2262.0] [e2262.1]
  3. RASA1 and NF1 co-inactivation defines a distinct smoking-associated NSCLC subset that is mutually exclusive with major canonical EGFR/KRAS/ALK/RET/ROS1/BRAF/METex14/ERBB2 drivers and strongly sensitive to MEK inhibition, illustrating a human suppressor-convergence route to MAPK addiction. [e2328.0] [e2328.1] [e2328.2]
  4. RPA(-)G and noncanonical LUAD analyses repeatedly find enrichment of suppressor loss rather than activated RTKs: TP53, KEAP1, STK11, SMARCA4, NF1, and RASA1 deletions or mutations are common in the WGS-confirmed negative subset. [e2279.0] [e2279.1] [e2279.2] [e2284.1] [e2284.2] [e2267.0]
  5. STK11/LKB1 loss can create pathway-defined states even when sequence-level assignment is incomplete: STK11-wild-type KL tumors often still showed low LKB1 protein/mRNA and STK11 copy loss, implying functionally suppressor-negative states beyond simple mutation calls. [e2272.1]
  6. KEAP1-pathway loss in EGFR-driven LUAD demonstrates that suppressor alterations can dominate therapeutic phenotype even in oncogene-positive tumors, supporting the broader principle that suppressor-defined signaling states are biologically primary and can outcompete canonical oncogene labels. [e2323.0] [e2323.1] [e2323.2] [e2304.4]

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If all panel-negative LUADs are profiled by tumor-normal WGS plus stranded RNA-seq plus phosphoproteomics, the fraction remaining unexplained by either canonical pathway lesions or suppressor-convergent pathway activation will fall substantially below the current clinical 'driver-negative' label.
  2. If pERK/pAKT IHC and PTEN/NF1/RASA1 protein assessment are added prospectively to oncogene-negative LUAD workups, a clinically useful RAS/PI3K-active subgroup will emerge that is enriched for response to SHP2 plus AKT or MEK combinations.
  3. If narrow clinical panels are expanded to include EGFR extracellular domain, RAF-family CR2 hotspots, NTRK fusions, MET exon 14 splice mutations, and noncanonical fusions, a measurable portion of currently untreated driver-negative patients will become targetable.
  4. If copy-number, promoter, and protein analyses are integrated, more tumors with apparent 'wild-type' PTEN, KEAP1, STK11, or EGFR will be reclassified as pathway-activated through noncoding, dosage, or protein-level mechanisms.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. A clinically important subgroup of WGS-negative but phospho-MAPK/phospho-AKT-high LUAD may prove to be driven by recurrent phosphatase-proteolysis circuits or RNA-editing states that are invisible to current DNA-centric profiling.
  2. Some oncogene-negative LUADs may depend on transient ligand-driven signaling loops that create episodic RTK addiction without stable genomic lesions, making time-resolved phosphoproteomics superior to static sequencing for driver detection.
  3. A fraction of residual unexplained tumors may be driven by microbiome- or injury-induced paracrine signaling that phenocopies RTK activation and could be therapeutically disrupted without directly targeting tumor genome lesions.
  4. Integrated single-cell phosphoproteogenomics may reveal that several seemingly distinct 'driver-negative' classes collapse into a few conserved signaling attractor states, irrespective of the exact upstream lesion.

Negative Experiments

  1. Profile a large, rigorously panel-negative LUAD cohort with WGS, RNA-seq, copy-number, and phosphoproteomics; if most cases still show neither hidden pathway lesions nor RAS/PI3K output, the pathway-reinstatement theory is weakened.
  2. Test NF1/RASA1/PTEN- or RASA1/NF1-deficient patient-derived models with SHP2/MEK/AKT combinations; if these suppressor-convergent tumors do not preferentially depend on these pathways, the suppressor-convergence law is weakened.
  3. Prospectively compare standard clinical driver testing with expanded multi-omic testing in newly diagnosed 'driver-negative' LUAD; if expanded profiling rarely rescues targetable or pathway-convergent biology, the theory is weakened.
  4. Assess whether pERK/pAKT-high oncogene-negative LUADs are actually enriched for suppressor loss compared with pERK/pAKT-low oncogene-negative tumors; lack of enrichment would weaken the convergence claim.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. This theory does not fully explain driver-negative subsets centered on estrogen signaling, developmental quiescence, or immune-state programs that are not obviously MAPK/PI3K driven. [e2366.0] [e2347.0] [e2300.0] [e2264.1] [e2294.0]
  2. It also does not by itself explain pattern progression driven mainly by non-genetic state change rather than acquisition of pathway lesions. [e2310.0] [e2310.1] [e2310.2] [e2266.2]
