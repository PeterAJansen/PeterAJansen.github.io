Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1845

Name: State-Space Driver Theory of Oncogene-Negative LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: A substantial fraction of oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinomas are not truly driverless and are not explained by one hidden rare kinase alone. Instead, they fall into a small number of recurrent pathway-state classes: concealed RTK/RAS/RAF activation, tumor-suppressor-combination states that phenocopy RTK signaling, lineage/epigenetic reprogramming states, and microenvironment-dominant angiogenic or immune states. The core claim is that driver-negative LUAD should be classified by functional signaling state and ecosystem architecture, not only by absence of canonical genomic drivers.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Driver negativity is often assay-relative rather than biologically driverless

Statement: In LUAD, 'driver-negative' status is frequently a consequence of limited assay scope, low tumor purity, or failure to detect noncanonical coding, fusion, splicing, structural, or regulatory alterations; therefore, the probability that a tumor is truly driverless decreases as profiling expands from hotspot panels to RNA sequencing, WGS, proteomics, and phosphoproteomics.

Domain/Scope: Applies to lung adenocarcinoma cohorts initially classified as lacking canonical actionable alterations by routine clinical testing or restricted sequencing panels, especially never-smoker and standard-testing-negative cohorts. Most applicable when comparing panel testing versus broader DNA/RNA/proteogenomic assays.

Special Cases

  1. Some tumors remain negative even after WGS/RNA-seq and may instead be driven primarily by non-genetic pathway states or tumor-suppressor combinations.
  2. Low-purity samples can mimic biology that is actually technical false negativity.
  3. This law does not imply every negative case will resolve to a single targetable kinase lesion.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Comprehensive genomic profiling of triple-negative LUAD detected genomic alterations in 94% of cases and guideline-defined actionable alterations in 31%, with ROS1-rearranged cases showing clinical responses to ceritinib, demonstrating that routine EGFR/KRAS/ALK-negative status often misses hidden drivers. [e1936.0] [e1936.1]
  2. In never-smoker LUAD initially called oncogene-negative by WES, many cases were later reclassified after deeper sequencing or orthogonal testing as harboring ALK fusions, ROS1 fusions, EGFR mutations, ERBB2 mutations, KRAS mutations, MET exon 14 skipping, or AKT1 mutations. [e1989.0] [e2031.0]
  3. The same never-smoker LUAD study found that reclassified initially negative tumors had lower predicted tumor cellularity than readily driver-positive tumors, directly supporting technical false negativity as one source of apparent driver negativity. [e2031.1]
  4. Whole-genome characterization of RTK/RAS/RAF-pathway-negative LUAD showed that a substantial fraction of WES-negative tumors were reclassified as pathway-positive by WGS because WGS rescued cryptic KRAS, ARAF, EGFR, SOS1, NRG1, RIT1, NF1, amplification, or structural events. [e1940.0]
  5. Comprehensive molecular profiling showed that focal MET amplification and focal ERBB2 amplification can emerge specifically within oncogene-negative LUAD, indicating that copy-number drivers are missed if negativity is defined only by point mutations and common fusions. [e1961.3] [e1961.4]
  6. Multiple studies identify hidden fusions in ostensibly negative LUAD, including KIF5B-RET, NTRK1 fusions, CD74-NRG1, and other kinase fusions, showing that transcriptome- or genome-level fusion discovery resolves a portion of negative cases. [e1951.0] [e1951.1] [e1977.0] [e2004.0] [e1952.0] [e1995.0] [e1932.0] [e1932.1]
  7. Several studies define driver-negative cohorts only by absence of EGFR/KRAS/ALK or related limited panels, implying that many 'negative' labels are conditional on assay design rather than on complete biological absence of drivers. [e1964.0] [e1953.0] [e1945.0] [e1926.0] [e1941.0]
  8. This law was induced by integrating evidence that broader genomic, transcriptomic, and proteogenomic profiling repeatedly converts a meaningful fraction of panel-negative tumors into driver-positive or pathway-positive tumors.

Statement 1: Driver-negative LUAD partitions into functional state classes that transcend single-gene labels

Statement: After exclusion of canonical actionable oncogenes, residual LUAD repeatedly resolves into four recurrent functional classes: (1) occult RTK/RAF activation by rare coding/fusion/splicing/regulatory events, (2) RAS/PI3K pathway activation by combinatorial loss of negative regulators, (3) lineage/epigenetic reprogramming states driven by chromatin, splicing, or differentiation-factor disruption, and (4) microenvironment-dominant ecosystem states such as angiogenic/CAF or immune-ferroptotic states. These classes are more stable explanatory units than any single hidden mutation frequency estimate.

Domain/Scope: Applies to oncogene-negative or partially resolved lung adenocarcinoma across never-smoker-enriched and all-comer cohorts, especially where broad profiling uncovers heterogeneous noncanonical lesions and pathway states. Intended as a classification law for unresolved LUAD biology, not for canonical EGFR/ALK-addicted disease.

Special Cases

  1. Some tumors occupy hybrids of two classes, such as STK11-loss metabolic-immune states with occult RTK activity or mucinous lineage states with NRG1 fusions.
  2. Smoking-associated high-TMB tumors may obscure state assignment because of heavy passenger burden.
  3. This law is less useful when a dominant canonical driver already explains most phenotype and treatment response.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a large multi-omic driver-negative LUAD cohort is clustered jointly by RNA, phosphoproteomics, copy number, and spatial microenvironment features, most cases will map onto a small number of recurring state classes approximating occult RTK/RAF, RAS/PI3K suppressor-loss, lineage/epigenetic, and microenvironment-dominant states rather than forming a continuous undifferentiated cloud.
  2. Among panel-negative LUADs, RNA-seq plus splice-aware DNA analysis will enrich particularly for hidden events in mucinous tumors, never-smokers, and younger patients, whereas phosphoproteomic state analysis will add most value in tumors that remain negative after WGS.
  3. Driver-negative tumors classified into the suppressor-loss RAS/PI3K state will show concordant pERK and pAKT positivity more often than tumors classified into lineage/epigenetic or immune-dominant states.
  4. Tumors assigned to lineage/epigenetic states will show weaker canonical phospho-RTK signatures but stronger chromatin- or differentiation-factor abnormalities, such as NKX2-1-loss gastric programs or spliceosome/chromatin regulator lesions.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Single-cell multiome profiling may reveal that some apparently distinct classes are evolutionarily ordered, with lineage/epigenetic reprogramming creating a permissive state that later acquires either occult RTK activation or angiogenic ecosystem dependence.
  2. A clinically useful 'state-first' treatment algorithm may outperform mutation-first triage in unresolved LUAD, particularly by assigning WGS-negative tumors to SHP2/AKT, vascular remodeling, ferroptosis, or immune-modulating strategies.
  3. A subset of truly WGS/RNA-negative tumors may prove to be driven by recurrent noncoding enhancer-hijacking or 3D-genome lesions analogous to ERBB2 super-enhancer structural activation, but involving different lineage-restricted targets.
  4. Some tumors currently classified as immune- or angiogenesis-dominant may contain a microbiome- or airway-injury-triggered initiating program that is necessary for state establishment but leaves little genomic trace.

Negative Experiments

  1. If integrated profiling of broad driver-negative LUAD cohorts fails to recover any reproducible state classes beyond random patient-specific heterogeneity, this theory is weakened.
  2. If tumors assigned to the proposed suppressor-loss RAS/PI3K class do not show biochemical RAS/PI3K pathway activation or differential drug sensitivity relative to other negative tumors, the classification loses mechanistic meaning.
  3. If most WGS/RNA-negative tumors are later found to harbor ordinary concealed canonical drivers rather than distinct pathway-state classes, the state-space aspect of the theory is overstated.
  4. If lineage/epigenetic-state tumors consistently respond exactly like occult RTK-driven tumors despite lacking pathway or chromatin differences, the proposed class distinction would be undermined.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some studies define driver negativity narrowly by limited gene panels or only by EGFR wild-type status, making cross-study subtype alignment imperfect. [e1964.0] [e1953.0] [e1965.0] [e2011.0]
  2. The theory does not yet explain how multiple genomic-instability processes such as APOBEC or CIN should be assigned relative to the four state classes; they may be modifiers rather than primary classes. [e1978.1] [e1978.2] [e2014.1] [e1956.1]
