Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1850

Name: Ligand-and-Lineage Substitution Theory of Driver-Negative LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: A biologically distinct subset of oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma is driven neither by classic kinase-domain activation nor by generic tumor-suppressor loss, but by substitution of the normal alveolar epithelial control system with alternative signaling/logistic programs. Two recurrent substitutions appear in the evidence: ligand substitution, where membrane-tethered or fusion-derived ligands such as NRG1 activate ERBB2/ERBB3 without a receptor mutation, and lineage substitution, where loss or reduction of NKX2-1/TTF1 and related lineage control permits gastric/GI-like or stem-like cell states that can serve as the organizing biology of the tumor. This theory proposes that a meaningful fraction of unresolved driver-negative LUAD is maintained by 'who supplies the signal' and 'what lineage state interprets it', not just by which kinase is mutated.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Ligand-presenting fusions define a non-kinase driver class

Statement: In a subset of oncogene-negative LUAD, the primary transforming lesion is not mutation of a receptor kinase but creation of an aberrant ligand-presentation module, most clearly via NRG1 fusions that preserve the EGF-like domain and drive ERBB2/ERBB3 signaling. Such tumors are therefore better modeled as ligand-driven receptor activation states than as receptor-mutant cancers.

Domain/Scope: Applies to lung adenocarcinoma subsets initially negative for canonical driver panels, especially invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma and never-smoker tumors, in which NRG1 fusion discovery was performed by RNA-seq, RT-PCR, FISH, or expanded targeted fusion testing. Most directly supported for CD74-NRG1 and SLC3A2-NRG1; conceptually extends to other ligand-like or receptor-context-dependent fusions in which the fusion product activates intact receptor machinery.

Special Cases

  1. The theory is best established for NRG1 fusions and should not be generalized to all non-kinase fusions without mechanistic testing.
  2. The signaling phenotype still depends on recipient-cell expression of ERBB2 and ERBB3; the ligand fusion is not fully autonomous from receptor context.
  3. Some NRG1-aberrant cases show outlier expression without a detected fusion, so ligand substitution may occur through additional mechanisms not yet resolved.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. CD74-NRG1 was discovered in never-smoker pan-negative LUAD and shown to present the NRG1 EGF-like domain extracellularly, activate ERBB2/ERBB3 and PI3K-AKT signaling, and increase anchorage-independent growth. [e2080.0] [e2131.0] [e2131.1]
  2. In KRAS-negative invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma, CD74-NRG1 was recurrent and activated HER2/HER3, ERK, and AKT; the signaling and soft-agar phenotype were suppressed by lapatinib and afatinib, supporting a ligand-driven rather than receptor-mutant model. [e2110.0] [e2152.0]
  3. SLC3A2-NRG1 was found in the same KRAS-negative/pan-negative IMA setting and was interpreted as an analogous NRG1 ligand fusion preserving the EGF-like domain and activating HER-family signaling. [e2110.1] [e2152.1]
  4. Transcriptome meta-analysis found multiple NRG1 rearrangements and NRG1 outlier-expression cases concentrated in driver-unknown lung cancers, indicating that ligand-based oncogenesis generalizes beyond one fusion partner and can exist as either fusion or regulatory overexpression. [e2069.0] [e2069.4]
  5. All available CD74-NRG1-positive tumors were invasive mucinous adenocarcinomas in never-smoker women, implying a lineage-selective ecological niche for ligand-substitution drivers. [e2080.0] [e2131.0] [e2152.0]
  6. Logical induction from the evidence above: because the transforming entity is a membrane-tethered or fusion-derived ligand acting through intact ERBB receptors, these cancers constitute a driver class orthogonal to kinase-domain mutation and should be classified by ligand logic rather than by receptor genotype alone.

Statement 1: Loss of pulmonary lineage control creates alternative interpretable driver states

Statement: When pulmonary lineage regulators such as NKX2-1/TTF1 are reduced or lost, LUAD can enter alternative lineage states—mucinous gastric-like, GI-like, or stem-like—that reorganize which signaling inputs become oncogenic and may themselves function as driver-equivalent states. In these tumors, lineage-state transition is not merely a downstream phenotype but part of the causal architecture of tumor maintenance.

Domain/Scope: Applies to mucinous/gastric-like LUAD, NKX2-1-low or NKX2-1-negative tumors, KRAS-associated mucinous adenocarcinoma models, low-TTF1 KRAS subgroups, and genomically quiet never-smoker piano-like tumors with stemness features. Includes both mouse mechanistic data and human correlative pathology/transcriptomic data.

Special Cases

  1. NKX2-1 effects are context dependent: it suppresses KRAS-driven mucinous tumorigenesis but can support other lung epithelial states and is amplified in some LUADs.
  2. Lineage-state change alone is not always sufficient for transformation; cooperating oncogenic or suppressor-loss events may still be required.
  3. Not every NKX2-1-low tumor is driver-negative; the theory concerns lineage-state substitution within the driver-negative or noncanonical subset.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. KrasG12D plus Nkx2-1 haploinsufficiency induced invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma of the lung, whereas KrasG12D alone did not generate the same phenotype, showing that reducing pulmonary lineage control changes what oncogenic program the tissue can sustain. [e2108.0]
  2. Loss of Nkx2-1 revealed a latent gastric differentiation program in mouse lung adenocarcinoma and adult pulmonary epithelium, including Hnf4a, Pdx1, Ctse, Gkn1, and mucinous markers; human NKX2-1-negative mucinous adenocarcinomas showed corresponding gastric markers. [e2153.1] [e2153.3]
  3. Nkx2-1 loss globally redistributed Foxa1/2 binding from pulmonary loci to de novo gastric/GI loci, providing a direct chromatin mechanism by which lineage substitution rewires the tumor's regulatory interpretation of signaling. [e2153.2]
  4. Combined loss of Nkx2-1 and Hnf4a de-repressed Hmga2, linking lineage-state escape to an embryonal and more primitive tumor program. [e2153.0]
  5. KRAS-mutant KC tumors had low NKX2-1/TTF1, frequent CDKN2A/CDKN2B loss, mucinous/GI-like differentiation markers, and a distinct proteomic state, indicating that low pulmonary-lineage fidelity defines a coherent biologic subtype rather than a passive consequence. [e2062.0]
  6. Never-smoker piano tumors were genomically quiet yet enriched for stemness/development features and lacked clear recurrent canonical drivers, supporting the idea that alternative cell-state control can substitute for canonical oncogene logic in some tumors. [e2075.0] [e2149.0]
  7. Logical induction from the evidence above: if reduction of pulmonary lineage control repeatedly changes transcriptional targets, histology, and the set of usable oncogenic inputs, then lineage-state transition must be treated as part of the driver architecture rather than as mere downstream differentiation.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Among unresolved driver-negative mucinous LUADs, the highest-yield additional assay after DNA/RNA sequencing will be phospho-ERBB2/ERBB3 IHC or phosphoproteomics, which will enrich for ligand-substitution tumors even when the fusion partner is initially unknown.
  2. NRG1-fusion-positive and other ligand-substitution tumors will show stronger dependence on receptor-context proteins such as ERBB2, ERBB3, and downstream PI3K-AKT than on MAPK-only blockade.
  3. Driver-negative LUADs with low NKX2-1/TTF1 and gastric-marker expression will be enriched for mucinous histology, altered enhancer occupancy, and noncanonical structural or regulatory events rather than classic kinase-domain mutations.
  4. In tumors with low NKX2-1/TTF1 but no identified canonical driver, chromatin assays will reveal redistributed FOXA-family occupancy and lineage-specific enhancer activation resembling the gastric-like mouse models.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Additional non-NRG1 ligand-presenting fusions in oncogene-negative LUAD will be found that act through intact receptor complexes, creating a broader family of ligand-substitution drivers.
  2. Forced restoration of pulmonary lineage control in a subset of NKX2-1-low driver-negative LUAD models will collapse tumor fitness even without directly inhibiting the nominal signaling pathway.
  3. Lineage-substituted tumors will prove selectively vulnerable to therapies that interrupt receptor-context interpretation, such as HER-family dimerization blockade combined with chromatin-state modulation.
  4. Some piano-like driver-negative tumors will not require a stable clonal genomic driver at all, but instead depend on self-reinforcing lineage-state circuits that can be reset by differentiation therapy.

Negative Experiments

  1. If broad testing of unresolved mucinous/never-smoker driver-negative LUAD fails to reveal enrichment of NRG1-like ligand-presenting mechanisms or ERBB2/ERBB3 activation, the ligand-substitution part of the theory is weakened.
  2. If NKX2-1-low or gastric-like driver-negative tumors do not show reproducible lineage-specific chromatin rewiring or signaling-context changes compared with NKX2-1-high tumors, the lineage-substitution component is weakened.
  3. If receptor-context blockade is not more effective than generic pathway inhibition in ligand-substitution tumors, the claim that these are mechanistically distinct from receptor-mutant cancers is weakened.
  4. If restoring pulmonary lineage programs in appropriate model systems fails to alter fitness or signaling interpretation, the theory overstates the causal role of lineage state.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The theory does not directly explain suppressor-loss-driven noncanonical states such as NF1/RASA1/PTEN-driven oncogene-negative LUAD, which may belong to a different biological subclass. [e2044.0] [e2060.0] [e2145.0]
  2. It does not directly address broad occult canonical rescue cases where the main issue is assay failure rather than alternative ligand or lineage logic. [e2111.0] [e2092.0] [e2076.0]
