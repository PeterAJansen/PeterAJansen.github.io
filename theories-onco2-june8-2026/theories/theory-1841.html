Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1841

Name: Convergent State-Driver Theory of Oncogene-Negative LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: A substantial fraction of oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinomas are not best explained as simply 'missing one rare kinase lesion.' Instead, many converge onto a limited number of recurrent biologic states that can be generated by different upstream mechanisms: cryptic RTK/RAS/RAF activation, derepression of RAS/PI3K by loss of negative regulators, angiogenic-stromal invasion programs, proliferation/checkpoint programs, lineage-state reprogramming, immune-structured states, and metabolism-centered states. In this theory, genomic negativity at the canonical panel level often masks biologic positivity at the pathway-state level; therefore, clinically useful classification should begin with state measurement and then resolve the causal mechanism within each state.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Driver-negativity often reflects hidden pathway convergence rather than absence of oncogenic circuitry

Statement: In lung adenocarcinomas that are negative for canonical actionable RTK/RAS/RAF drivers by routine assays, oncogenic behavior is frequently maintained either by occult lesions missed by limited testing or by noncanonical mechanisms that converge onto active pathway states; therefore, clinical driver-negativity does not imply biologic pathway-negativity.

Domain/Scope: Applies to lung adenocarcinoma designated driver-negative, oncogene-negative, pan-negative, triple-negative, EGFR/KRAS/ALK-negative, EGFR/ALK-wild-type, or RTK/RAS/RAF-pathway-negative by targeted clinical assays, panel testing, WES, or even WGS-based filtering. Most applicable to cohorts enriched for never-smokers, early-stage resected tumors, or clinically routine-testing-negative cases. Does not claim every driver-negative tumor has a single dominant addiction, only that many do, and that pathway activity often persists despite canonical genomic negativity.

Special Cases

  1. Some initially driver-negative tumors are technical false negatives caused by low tumor purity, low allelic fraction, incomplete primer coverage, limited panel scope, intratumoral heterogeneity, or insufficient fusion/splice detection.
  2. A subset of truly unresolved tumors may be early evolutionary states, highly subclonal, or ecosystem-dominated, and may not yet show one dominant tumor-cell-intrinsic addiction.
  3. Some tumors are genuinely driven by rare discrete lesions rather than broad state convergence; in such cases lesion-first and state-first classification will often agree.

Explanation: This is broader than existing hidden-driver papers, which usually emphasize one lesion class or one subtype. It synthesizes discrete cryptic lesions, protein-state activation, and multi-omic subtype findings into one driver-negative interpretation framework.

What Already Exists: It is established that some driver-negative LUADs harbor missed fusions, exon-skipping events, rare mutations, or noncanonical amplifications, and that proteogenomics can reveal pathway activity not obvious from DNA alone.

What is Novel: The novel aspect is the explicit unification of these findings into a single principle: clinically negative LUAD is often biologically positive at the pathway-state level, so pathway-state assignment should be treated as the primary explanatory unit rather than an optional add-on to lesion finding.

Statement 1: A limited state-space explains much of driver-negative LUAD heterogeneity

Statement: A large share of biologically recurrent driver-negative LUADs can be categorized into a small number of partially overlapping state classes—RAS/PI3K-derepressed, angiogenic-stromal, proliferation/checkpoint, lineage-reprogrammed, immune-structured, and metabolism-rewired—and within each class, different genomic or non-genomic lesions are partially interchangeable if they generate the same systems-level phenotype.

Domain/Scope: Applies to cohort-level stratification of driver-negative, oncogene-negative, or EGFR/ALK-wild-type lung adenocarcinoma using integrated transcriptomic, proteomic, phosphoproteomic, and microenvironmental data. Especially applicable to never-smoker LUAD and to canonical-driver-negative cohorts, but also informative for subsets embedded within broader LUAD cohorts.

Special Cases

  1. A single tumor may occupy more than one state simultaneously, for example an angiogenic-stromal state with coexisting immune exclusion or a lineage-reprogrammed state with ligand-fusion signaling.
  2. Preinvasive lesions and early-stage tumors may sit in transitional states that map only partially onto invasive-state classes.
  3. Highly unstable tumors may express mixed or drifting states rather than one stable category.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a large driver-negative LUAD cohort is profiled with matched DNA, RNA, proteomics, phosphoproteomics, and spatial microenvironment readouts, most tumors will reproducibly map onto a small number of pathway-state clusters even when the upstream lesions differ.
  2. If treatment response is modeled using state-first labels such as RAS/PI3K-active, angiogenic-stromal, immune-high, proliferation/checkpoint, lineage-reprogrammed, and metabolism-rewired, prediction accuracy will exceed that obtained from the binary label 'driver-negative' alone.
  3. If low-purity or panel-negative driver-negative tumors are re-analyzed by RNA fusion calling, WGS, and phosphoproteomics, the fraction of completely unexplained tumors will shrink, but the same recurrent state classes will remain.
  4. If pERK/pAKT, angiogenesis/CAF score, NKX2-1/HNF4A lineage score, and immune-hot/cold state are measured prospectively in routine-testing-negative LUAD, these state markers will explain survival heterogeneity better than routine molecular negativity alone.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If state-first classification is applied prospectively in canonical-driver-negative LUAD, a compact panel of approximately 15-25 proteins/phosphoproteins plus one spatial stromal score may outperform large DNA panels for short-term therapy selection in the subset lacking actionable lesions.
  2. If longitudinal sampling is performed under therapy, some driver-negative LUADs will transition along a finite state-transition graph—for example from proliferation/checkpoint to angiogenic-stromal or from lineage-reprogrammed to immune-excluded—rather than evolving in an unconstrained manner.
  3. If single-cell multiome profiling is applied to the residual truly unresolved driver-negative tumors after deep genomic rescue, a new class may emerge in which no dominant tumor-cell-intrinsic state exists and progression is governed mainly by ecosystem architecture.

Negative Experiments

  1. Profile a large independent driver-negative LUAD cohort with integrated DNA/RNA/protein/phosphoprotein/spatial data; if tumors do not form reproducible recurrent state classes, the theory is weakened.
  2. Test whether state-defined grouping predicts therapy response better than lesion-defined grouping in retrospective or prospective cohorts; if it does not, the state-first claim is weakened.
  3. Examine whether different upstream lesions assigned to the same proposed state are functionally interchangeable with respect to downstream signaling and drug response; if they are not, the convergence model is weakened.
  4. Assess whether residual driver-negative cases after deep genomic rescue remain enriched for recurring protein/TME states; if not, the theory overstates convergence.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some studies imply that a substantial fraction of driver-negative disease is simply assay failure or low cellularity rather than distinct biology, so the balance between true convergence and technical miss is unresolved. [e1989.0] [e2031.0] [e2031.1] [e1936.0] [e1940.0]
  2. Copy-number-quiet piano never-smoker tumors may represent a stem-like low-driver state that does not map cleanly onto the main pathway-state classes proposed here. [e2008.0] [e1966.0]
  3. Some progression studies emphasize non-genetic plasticity and histologic evolution without defining corresponding actionable pathway states, leaving some state boundaries underspecified. [e1972.0] [e2021.0] [e2015.0] [e1923.0] [e1923.1]
