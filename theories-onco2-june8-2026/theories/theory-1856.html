Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1856

Name: Driver-Substitution Theory Across LUAD and Other Epithelia

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: Oncogene-negative LUAD is not primarily explained by one undiscovered recurrent gene, but by substitution of canonical kinase-driver logic with alternative activating architectures that are recurrent across cancers: ligand fusions, rare RAF/RAS-family regulatory-site mutations, exon-skipping or domain-skipping RTKs, enhancer/SV rewiring, and co-loss of redundant tumor suppressors. The cross-cancer parallel is that when one usual route to MAPK/RTK addiction is absent, tumors repeatedly substitute another architecture that preserves pathway output while evading standard assays.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Pathway output is preserved by architecture substitution

Statement: When common LUAD drivers in EGFR/KRAS/ALK-class loci are absent, tumors frequently substitute alternative activating architectures that preserve RTK/MAPK/PI3K output: noncanonical extracellular or regulatory-domain mutations, kinase or ligand fusions, exon-skipping events, or rare paralogous hotspot mutations in pathway regulators.

Domain/Scope: Applies to lung adenocarcinoma and closely related epithelial tumors where pathway addiction is commonly mediated through RTK/RAS/RAF signaling, especially in cohorts preselected as canonical-driver negative. It is a comparative theory and not limited to one histology, though the main target is LUAD.

Special Cases

  1. Some substitutions activate lineage or chromatin programs rather than direct RTK/MAPK signaling.
  2. Not every alternative architecture is equally transforming; some events remain candidates without full functional proof.
  3. A fraction of tumors may instead be driven by tumor suppressor co-loss rather than a substituted positive oncogene.

Statement 1: Cross-cancer parallels reveal recurrent hidden-driver motifs that LUAD should inherit

Statement: Rare driver architectures discovered in other cancers predict hidden-driver classes in LUAD, because the same mechanistic motifs recur across tissue types: extracellular-domain receptor activation, tandem-duplication or atypical ALK architecture, enhancer hijacking, noncoding promoter mutations, and kinase truncation/fusion activation.

Domain/Scope: Applies to hypothesis generation for unresolved lung adenocarcinoma by analogy to mechanistically related epithelial cancers and other solid tumors. It is intended for discovery prioritization, not immediate clinical assumption without validation in LUAD.

Special Cases

  1. Cross-cancer transfer works best for motif-level mechanisms, not for exact gene frequencies.
  2. Tissue-specific enhancer context and lineage factors can prevent a motif from being active in LUAD even if it is oncogenic elsewhere.
  3. Some examples cited from glioma or colorectal cancer are mechanistic analogies, not direct LUAD observations.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Systematic reanalysis of unresolved LUAD using motif-based filters will discover additional events in already implicated genes but unusual architectures, such as extracellular-domain receptor mutations, exon-skipping lesions, or promoter/enhancer rearrangements, more often than discovering a single new highly recurrent hotspot gene.
  2. Residual driver-negative LUADs enriched for MAPK signaling but lacking canonical drivers will show overrepresentation of noncanonical RAF/RAS-family lesions or structural BRAF-class events when long-read or RNA-based assays are added.
  3. Rare ligand fusions and noncanonical receptor-domain mutations will be disproportionately enriched in never-smoker and mucinous/lineage-skewed LUAD relative to smoking-high, high-TMB tumors.
  4. Using cross-cancer motif priors will improve validation yield of candidate LUAD hidden drivers compared with agnostic gene-frequency ranking alone.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Some unresolved LUADs will harbor atypical ALK/ROS1/RET/NTRK rearrangements analogous to C2orf44-ALK that are negative by standard fusion assays but positive by long-read or transcript-assembly methods.
  2. A subset of piano or regulatory-state LUADs will be driven by enhancer hijacking of lineage survival genes other than ERBB2, potentially including NKX2-1-adjacent or surfactant-lineage loci.
  3. Noncanonical extracellular-domain mutations in ERBB3, ERBB4, FGFR, or MET will emerge as a recurrent but tiny hidden-driver class in pan-negative LUAD once full ectodomain sequencing becomes routine.
  4. Cross-cancer promoter-fusion architectures that overexpress intact coding oncogenes, analogous to TBL1XR1-PIK3CA or WASF2-FGR, will be found in a small number of currently unexplained LUADs.

Negative Experiments

  1. If motif-driven reanalysis of unresolved LUAD fails to enrich for unusual architecture classes relative to unselected tumors, the substitution framework would be weakened.
  2. If cross-cancer motifs such as enhancer hijacking, ectodomain activation, and promoter fusion do not produce any additional validated LUAD discoveries beyond already known examples, the predictive value of the cross-cancer analogy would be weakened.
  3. If newly found rare architecture events in unresolved LUAD systematically fail functional validation or target dependency testing, the theory would overestimate the causal importance of architectural substitution.
  4. If exact gene recurrence, rather than architecture class, proves to be the dominant successful discovery prior in unresolved LUAD, the motif-first component would be weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. This theory does not by itself explain purely lineage-state or stemness-driven quiet tumors without an obvious substituted activating architecture. [e2075.0] [e2149.0]
  2. It does not fully account for protein-state-only dependencies such as ARHGEF5 phospho-activation that may occur without a clear genomic substitute architecture. [e2089.3]
