Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1867

Name: Lineage-State Diversification Theory of Residual Driver-Negative LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: After assay-rescuable canonical drivers are removed, residual oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma is not best modeled as a single genomically obscure class. Instead, it recurrently resolves into lineage-constrained disease states in which cell identity, histologic program, and protein/pathway state determine what kind of hidden driver is likely to be present. The major attractors supported by the evidence are: a mucinous gastric-like lineage-switch state enriched for ligand-fusion and differentiation-remodeling mechanisms; a terminal-respiratory-unit-like fusion/kinase-architecture state enriched for structurally activated kinase drivers; and a suppressor-loss/proteomic-state class in which pathway activation is produced by loss of negative regulators, phospho-network rewiring, or epigenetic/protein-level inactivation rather than one dominant canonical oncogene lesion.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Mucinous oncogene-negative LUAD is preferentially a lineage-switched, ligand- or differentiation-program disease

Statement: In invasive mucinous, mucinous-cribriform, and NKX2-1/TTF1-low gastric-like lung adenocarcinoma, hidden drivers are disproportionately ligand-fusion, lineage-switch, and differentiation-program mechanisms rather than canonical hotspot kinase mutations. Reduction or loss of NKX2-1 opens a gastric/GI secretory state that increases the probability that oncogenic output will be carried by NRG1-class ligand presentation, ERBB2/ERBB3 activation, or lineage-remodeling co-events such as CDKN2A/B loss.

Domain/Scope: Applies to human invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma, KRAS-negative mucinous adenocarcinoma subjected to expanded fusion analysis, mucinous-cribriform adenocarcinoma enriched for kinase fusions, and genetically engineered Kras/Nkx2-1 mouse models that recapitulate mucinous lineage switching. It is intended for adenocarcinoma biology, especially TTF1-low or GI-like tumors, and not for all nonmucinous LUAD or squamous cancers.

Special Cases

  1. Not all mucinous tumors are NRG1- or fusion-driven; KRAS-mutant mucinous tumors are a major parallel class.
  2. Some mucinous tumors retain TTF1 positivity or mixed lineage features, including KIAA1468-RET-positive IMA and TRIM24-BRAF-positive IMA.
  3. NKX2-1 is context-dependent: amplification can be oncogenic in nonmucinous/alveolar-lineage LUAD, whereas loss promotes mucinous gastric-like reprogramming.
  4. Mucinous morphology is an enrichment variable, not a perfect classifier of gastric-like lineage state.

  2. In a pan-negative Chinese LUAD cohort, both detected CD74-NRG1 fusion cases occurred in female never-smokers with stage I invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma, reinforcing the mucinous enrichment of NRG1-type hidden drivers. [e2175.3]

Across transcriptome meta-analysis of driver-unknown lung cancers, NRG1 fusions and outlier expression accounted for 3.9% of driver-unknown cases and were linked to preserved EGF-domain signaling and mucinous/HNF4A-associated biology, supporting ligand-presentation as a hidden-driver mechanism.   5. KRAS-negative IMA is enriched for hidden fusions beyond NRG1, including EZR-ERBB4, TRIM24-BRAF, and KIAA1468-RET, indicating that mucinous disease is a fusion-rich discovery space rather than a generic driver-negative residue. [e2230.3] [e2230.4] [e2230.5] [e2230.0]

  8. KrasG12D plus Nkx2-1 haploinsufficiency in mice induces mucinous adenocarcinoma with mucin expression and secretory differentiation, demonstrating that lineage-factor reduction can itself redirect tumor identity toward the mucinous state. [e2228.0]

Nonmucinous NKX2-1 amplification can be oncogenic and lineage-survival-like in alveolar/TRU tumors, establishing that the same lineage regulator can be an oncogene in one branch and a suppressive lineage constraint in another, which is consistent with a lineage-state diversification model rather than a universal NKX2-1 rule. Statement 1: Residual nonmucinous oncogene-negative LUAD partitions into a TRU-like fusion branch and a suppressor-loss/proteomic branch

Statement: Among residual nonmucinous oncogene-negative LUAD, hidden-driver mechanisms recurrently segregate into two explanatory branches. One is a TRU-like, fusion- and kinase-architecture branch in which structural kinase or ligand fusions act as primary drivers with relatively sparse canonical co-drivers. The other is a suppressor-loss/proteomic branch in which pathway output is generated by combinatorial loss of negative regulators, epigenetic or protein-level silencing, and phosphoproteomic rewiring despite absence of a dominant proto-oncogene lesion. Branch assignment is therefore best inferred from lineage markers, phosphoproteomics, and negative-regulator status rather than from hotspot DNA testing alone.

Domain/Scope: Applies to nonmucinous or mixed-pattern lung adenocarcinoma remaining negative after standard hotspot driver testing and, especially, to cohorts with RNA fusion discovery, WGS structural analysis, or proteogenomic/phosphoproteomic profiling. It is most relevant to never-smoker and mixed LUAD cohorts in which canonical driver-negative cases persist after broader testing.

Special Cases

  1. Some tumors occupy intermediate positions, such as RIT1-, ARAF-, EGFR ectodomain-, or HER2 non-kinase-domain mutant tumors, which are single-lesion drivers but not classic fusion/TRU cases.
  2. Some apparently suppressor-loss tumors may in fact harbor still-missed cryptic canonical drivers due to purity, structural complexity, or noncoding events.
  3. ALK- or other fusion-driven tumors can still show secondary phospho-network outputs such as ARHGEF5/SRGAP1 signaling, so branch identity refers to primary explanatory logic rather than exclusivity of downstream signals.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If residual driver-negative LUADs are clustered jointly by histology, NKX2-1/TTF1, HNF4A, mucin markers, and phosphoproteomics, they will separate more cleanly into mucinous gastric-like, TRU-like fusion, and suppressor-loss/proteomic subclasses than they do by DNA mutation burden alone.
  2. If pan-negative mucinous tumors are prospectively screened with broad RNA fusion calling plus ERBB2/ERBB3 phospho-IHC or phosphoproteomics, p-ERBB3-positive cases will be enriched for NRG1-class or other ligand-presentation mechanisms.
  3. If nonmucinous driver-negative tumors with dual pERK and pAKT but no canonical oncogene are profiled for negative regulators and protein loss, they will be enriched for NF1/RASA1/PTEN/STK11/KEAP1-class lesions or non-genomic PTEN loss rather than for hidden kinase fusions.
  4. If NKX2-1-low/HNF4A-high tumors are examined for CDKN2A/B status and gastric-secretory markers, they will show stronger lineage-linked vulnerabilities than NKX2-1-high TRU-like fusion tumors.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If unresolved mucinous tumors are partly microbiome-instructed rather than purely genomically driven, spatial microbial composition will correlate with secretory/gastric-like ERBB-ligand states independently of canonical fusion status.
  2. If lineage state is truly causal rather than correlative, forced restoration of NKX2-1 in selected human NKX2-1-low unresolved tumors will collapse not only mucin markers but also ligand dependence and ERBB phospho-signaling.
  3. If unresolved suppressor-loss tumors are primarily protein-state diseases, serial phosphoproteomics under therapy will show branch switching between fusion-like and suppressor-loss-like pathway states without corresponding acquisition of new canonical DNA drivers.
  4. If TRU-like and gastric-like branches arise from distinct premalignant cell-state trajectories, adjacent normal tissue single-cell analyses will reveal distinct pre-neoplastic intermediates linked to each branch.

Negative Experiments

  1. Profile a large residual driver-negative LUAD cohort and test whether lineage markers and histology fail to align with mechanism class. If mucinous/TTF1-low/GI-like cases are not enriched for ligand-fusion or differentiation-state mechanisms, the first branch law is weakened.
  2. Experimentally restore or suppress NKX2-1 and HNF4A in representative models. If lineage manipulation does not change the predicted mucinous signaling dependencies or secretory program, the lineage-causality claim is weakened.
  3. Test whether TRU-like fusion-positive tumors truly carry fewer cooperating suppressor alterations and more phospho-kinase-dominant states than suppressor-loss branch tumors. If the distributions are indistinguishable, the branch framework is oversimplified.
  4. Measure phosphoproteomic states in residual nonmucinous tumors. If phospho-state branch assignment does not correlate with negative-regulator lesions, lineage markers, or therapeutic vulnerability, the proteomic branch law is weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Piano/quiescent never-smoker tumors with very low SCNA burden and rare drivers such as UBA1 or NKX2-1 mutation do not fit neatly into the mucinous/TRU/suppressor-loss framework. [e2193.0] [e2193.2]
  2. Some studies argue that LUAD histologic progression can be largely non-genetic and driven by epigenetic/transcriptional progression rather than stable branch-specific hidden drivers, adding another axis beyond the proposed branches. [e2210.0] [e2163.1]
  3. Residual RPA(-)G tumors remain heterogeneous after WGS, with noncoding, amplicon, and suppressor lesions but no single unifying logic proven for all cases. [e2202.1] [e2218.1]
