Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1855

Name: Hidden-Driver Compression Theory of Oncogene-Negative LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: A large fraction of supposedly oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinomas are not biologically driverless; they are assay-compressed into a negative bin because their drivers lie outside standard hotspot-panel assumptions. These tumors are enriched for four hidden driver classes: structural kinase/ligand fusions, noncanonical coding variants outside usual hotspots/domains, regulatory or noncoding structural alterations, and pathway activation visible only at protein/phosphosite level. The practical implication is that driver-negative LUAD should be reclassified by driver class and evidence layer rather than by absence of panel-detected mutations.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Most driver-negative LUADs are hidden-driver positive under broader assays

Statement: When lung adenocarcinomas initially called driver-negative are reassessed with fusion-aware, splice-aware, copy-number-aware, and whole-genome or proteogenomic methods, a substantial subset is reclassified as containing oncogenic events; therefore, 'driver-negative' is often an assay-state rather than a biological state.

Domain/Scope: Applies to lung adenocarcinoma cohorts initially classified as lacking canonical RTK/RAS/RAF pathway drivers by limited hotspot assays, standard WES, or mutation-centric workflows; especially relevant in never-smoker LUAD and in cohorts where DNA-only testing predominated. It does not imply all residual negative tumors are explained by current methods.

Special Cases

  1. A residual subset remains unexplained even after broad profiling, implying genuinely unresolved biology rather than mere assay failure.
  2. The rescue fraction depends strongly on assay breadth, tumor purity, RNA quality, and whether structural and noncoding events are assayed.
  3. Panel-defined 'no driver' cohorts that incompletely tested all genes mix true negatives with untested cases.

Statement 1: Residual unresolved LUADs separate into pathway-state subtypes rather than a single null class

Statement: Among LUADs that remain negative after broad genomic testing, the residual tumors are better explained as biologically distinct pathway-state subtypes: (i) dual RAS/PI3K negative-regulator loss states, (ii) lineage-state or stemness-dominant low-SCNA 'quiet' states, (iii) regulatory/chromatin-rewired transcriptional outlier states, and (iv) protein-state driven signaling outliers not inferable from RNA alone.

Domain/Scope: Applies to the subset of LUADs that remain unexplained or only partially explained after canonical driver analysis and, ideally, after expanded genomic testing. Particularly relevant to never-smoker, non-CAGA, and whole-genome-profiled cohorts. It is not intended for canonical EGFR/KRAS/ALK-driven tumors unless discussing co-driver biology.

Special Cases

  1. Some tumors will span more than one subtype, such as a quiet genomic state plus stemness, or regulatory activation plus protein-state dependency.
  2. These subtype boundaries are probabilistic and cohort-dependent because different studies used different assay layers.
  3. Mucinous ligand-fusion tumors form a distinct specific subtype and are handled separately in a more specific theory.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a prospective LUAD cohort classified as driver-negative by panel sequencing is reprofiled with paired WGS plus RNA-seq plus phosphoproteomics, at least three separable residual groups should emerge: structural-rescued, suppressor-loss RAS/PI3K-active, and regulatory/protein-state outlier tumors.
  2. In tumors with no canonical genomic driver but strong pERK and pAKT staining, PTEN protein loss or NF1/RASA1 functional deficiency will be more common than in genomically similar but signaling-low tumors.
  3. In non-CAGA or unexplained LUADs, transcriptional outlier genes linked to H3K27Ac peaks and SV breakpoints will show stronger therapeutic relevance than rare coding mutations of uncertain significance.
  4. Across multiple institutions, the fraction of 'truly unresolved' LUAD will inversely correlate with assay breadth, especially the inclusion of RNA fusion calling and WGS-level structural analysis.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. A substantial subset of piano-like, genomically quiet tumors will prove to be driven by noncoding chromatin topology lesions or stem-cell-state epigenetic circuits rather than by undetected coding drivers.
  2. Proteome-only outlier states such as ARHGEF5 phospho-activation or KEAP1-NRF2 pathway activation will define therapeutically targetable subgroups even when neither DNA nor RNA profiling identifies a convincing lesion.
  3. Some tumors now classified as regulatory outliers will be driven by ecDNA or enhancer-hijacked mini-amplicons that are only recoverable by long-read or optical mapping approaches.
  4. Mutational-process subtyping, especially APOBEC-high versus carcinogen-like versus quiet/stemness states, may outperform single-driver assignment for prognosis in unresolved never-smoker LUAD.

Negative Experiments

  1. If broad multi-omic reanalysis of a carefully selected driver-negative LUAD cohort yields very few new drivers and no coherent biological subgrouping, the assay-compression framework would be weakened.
  2. If PTEN protein loss, NF1/RASA1 dysfunction, or pathway activation are not enriched among unresolved oncogene-negative LUADs, the suppressor-loss subtype claim would be weakened.
  3. If regulatory outlier genes linked to SE/SV or methylation states fail to show reproducible expression/protein activation or therapeutic dependence, the regulatory-state arm of the theory would be weakened.
  4. If proteomic/phosphoproteomic states closely mirror RNA abundance in unresolved LUAD, the claim that protein-state biology forms a distinct hidden-driver layer would be weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some studies explicitly failed to find recurrent pattern-specific genomic lesions and instead favored non-genetic progression mechanisms, which this theory accommodates only broadly rather than mechanistically. [e2126.0]
  2. Several cohorts reported no specific hidden genomic driver discovery despite extensive profiling, indicating that residual biology may sometimes be dominated by evolution, sampling, or non-driver programs rather than discreet hidden lesions. [e2079.0] [e2100.0] [e2081.0] [e2127.0]
