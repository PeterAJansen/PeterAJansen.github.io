Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1879

Name: State-Space Taxonomy Theory of Driver-Negative LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: Driver-negative LUAD is not one disease but a mixture of at least four biologically distinct state classes: pathway-driven proliferative tumors, stromal-angiogenic tumors, immune-structured tumors, and lineage/metabolic-plastic tumors. These classes recur across transcriptomic, proteomic, and single-cell studies under different naming schemes, implying that negative LUAD can be organized by latent state-space rather than by absence of a mutation. This taxonomy predicts subtype-specific validation strategies and therapies.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Four-state decomposition law

Statement: When canonical driver lesions are absent or clinically unavailable, LUAD can be decomposed into recurrent latent states centered on proliferation, angiogenic/stromal organization, immune organization, and lineage/metabolic plasticity, and these states recur across independent multi-omic classification systems.

Domain/Scope: Applies to oncogene-negative, EGFR/ALK-wild-type, or no-known-actionable-driver LUAD cohorts analyzed by transcriptomics, proteomics, phosphoproteomics, or single-cell/spatial methods. Most relevant in never-smoker LUAD and in clinically driver-negative subsets, but extends to broader LUAD where actionable drivers are absent or not explanatory.

Special Cases

  1. Not all studies recover exactly four groups; some collapse or split one state into adjacent subtypes.
  2. A single tumor may show hybrid features or transition between states over time.
  3. Histology-specific subsets such as invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma may overlay rather than replace this state-space.

Statement 1: Therapeutic orthogonality law

Statement: The major latent states of driver-negative LUAD imply orthogonal therapeutic vulnerabilities: proliferative states favor cell-cycle/replication-stress targeting, angiogenic-stromal states favor stromal-vascular disruption, immune-structured states favor checkpoint or immune-context therapies, and lineage/metabolic states favor metabolism, ferroptosis, or cell-state-directed interventions.

Domain/Scope: Applies to driver-negative-like LUAD state classes defined by multi-omics or single-cell profiling, especially when no canonical targeted therapy is available. Best supported by preclinical or inferential evidence rather than prospective trials.

Special Cases

  1. Vulnerabilities are often inferential or preclinical; clinical validation remains limited.
  2. Hybrid tumors may require combinations rather than single-state-matched therapy.
  3. Some therapies may act on the microenvironment rather than tumor cells directly.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. The proliferation-high never-smoker subtype showed candidate vulnerabilities involving CDK2, CDK5, PLKs, ATR, E2F1, and CDK9 dependency, linking the proliferative state to cell-cycle-directed therapy logic. [e2408.1]
  2. The angiogenesis-high subtype showed poor prognosis with CAFs, immune exclusion, and candidate targets including LGALS3, CXCL12/SDF-1, and FGF2, matching a stromal-vascular therapeutic axis. [e2408.3]
  3. The immune-high subtype showed checkpoint-rich immune activation with candidate therapeutic directions involving CCL5, CXCL13, CD27, TRAF2, and LPAR-linked immunomodulation, matching an immune-context treatment axis. [e2408.2]
  4. The metabolism-high subtype showed oxidative phosphorylation/lipid-metabolism enrichment and candidate metabolic liabilities including ACACA/mtFAS and CERS4-related programs, matching a metabolic-targeting axis. [e2408.4]
  5. FeSig-high EGFR-wild-type LUAD linked fibroblast-rich ferroptosis-resistant state to 6-mercaptopurine sensitivity and knockdown-validated ferroptosis genes, supporting the metabolic/microenvironment branch of the theory. [e2420.0]
  6. Driver-negative glycolysis-high LUAD identified a MIF-HIF-1alpha axis whose inhibition reversed glucose uptake, lactate production, LDH activity, and proliferation in AT2 cells, supporting a lineage-metabolic vulnerability class. [e2379.0] [e2379.1] [e2379.2]

  11. This law is also supported by induction: once negative LUAD is treated as a state-space rather than a mutation bucket, each state repeatedly nominates a different therapeutic axis across independent studies.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a clinically driver-negative LUAD cohort is jointly profiled by RNA-seq, proteomics, and spatial imaging, most tumors will map reproducibly onto one of four latent states rather than remaining biologically unstructured.
  2. Therapeutic response ex vivo will correlate more strongly with state class than with negative-call gene list: proliferative tumors with CDK/ATR sensitivity, angiogenic tumors with stromal/vascular sensitivity, immune-high tumors with checkpoint-context signatures, and metabolic/lineage tumors with ferroptosis or glycolysis sensitivity.
  3. Independent subtype systems from different populations will align by latent axes even if they use different labels.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. A small but clinically important fifth state may emerge from microbiome- or secretome-dominated driver-negative LUAD, orthogonal to the four-state taxonomy.
  2. Longitudinal sampling may show deterministic transitions from lineage/metabolic states to stromal-angiogenic or proliferative states during progression or therapy resistance.
  3. State-matched combination therapies may outperform mutation-guided standard-of-care in a prospective basket trial of driver-negative LUAD.

Negative Experiments

  1. Show that multi-omic subtypes in driver-negative LUAD are not reproducible across cohorts and collapse after batch correction; this would undermine the state-space theory.
  2. Demonstrate that proposed state-matched vulnerabilities do not segregate by subtype in organoid or ex vivo drug testing.
  3. Find that most clinically negative LUADs are ultimately just hidden rare canonical drivers with no residual recurrent state structure.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. This theory does not specifically explain single-gene rescued subsets such as ARAF, EGFR extracellular-domain mutants, NTRK1 fusions, or KIF5B-RET, which behave more like rare canonical driver classes than state classes. [e2432.0] [e2369.0] [e2412.0] [e2394.0] [e2441.0]
