Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1872

Name: Resolution-Stratified Driver Architecture of Oncogene-Negative LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: Apparent oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma is not one disease state. It is a superposition of at least three mechanistic strata organized by assay resolution: (1) assay-limited negatives later rescued as rare canonical or canonically targetable lesions; (2) broad-profile negatives driven by pathway-state activation, tumor-suppressor co-loss, or noncoding/structural regulatory events; and (3) residual lineage- and ecosystem-dominant tumors whose fitness is maintained by cell-state plasticity and microenvironmental circuitry. The central claim is that 'driver-negative' must be interpreted as a conditional label produced jointly by biology and ascertainment depth, and that therapeutic logic should shift from lesion-targeting to state-targeting as negative-call confidence rises.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Assay-depth rescue law

Statement: When lung adenocarcinomas classified as oncogene-negative by narrow or incomplete testing are re-profiled with broader DNA and RNA assays, a substantial subset is reclassified as harboring rare but canonical or canonically targetable oncogenic lesions; the rescue fraction declines as the initial negative definition becomes broader, more orthogonal, and more purity-aware.

Domain/Scope: Applies to lung adenocarcinomas initially labeled driver-negative using limited or intermediate diagnostic frameworks, especially EGFR/KRAS/ALK-only, EGFR/ALK-only, or hotspot/fusion-limited panels, and to cohorts subsequently re-profiled by broad hybrid-capture NGS, WES/WGS, RNA-seq, fusion calling, or splice-junction analysis. Strongest in resected and advanced LUAD cohorts where matched tumor DNA and RNA are both available and where purity/cellularity are measurable.

Special Cases

  1. Does not imply that all WGS plus RNA-seq-negative tumors are false negatives; a residual high-confidence biologic negative class may remain.
  2. Rescue yield is strongly modulated by tumor purity, assay breadth, and whether RNA-based fusion and exon-skipping analyses were included.
  3. Histology- and exposure-enriched subsets, particularly invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma and never-smoker LUAD, can have disproportionately high rescue by ligand fusion, kinase fusion, or atypical ERBB/MET events.
  4. Some 'rescues' are not classic point mutations but copy-number, non-kinase-domain, fusion, or splice-altering lesions.

Statement 1: State-dominance law in high-confidence negatives

Statement: In LUADs that remain negative after broad DNA plus RNA interrogation, malignant control is more likely to be maintained by pathway-state activation, tumor-suppressor co-loss, noncoding/cis-regulatory activation, structural-genome mechanisms, lineage plasticity, or ecosystem circuitry than by a single undiscovered high-frequency kinase oncogene.

Domain/Scope: Applies to high-confidence oncogene-negative LUAD after broad genomic characterization, especially cohorts classified by WGS or WES plus RNA-seq and then further profiled by methylation, proteomics, phosphoproteomics, enhancer mapping, or tumor-microenvironment analysis. Strongest for residual cases unresolved after canonical rescue analysis.

Special Cases

  1. Rare intermediate-frequency coding drivers may still remain undiscovered and can contribute to a minority of residual negatives.
  2. Some cohorts labeled negative in proteomic or transcriptomic studies use loose definitions and may still contain missed canonical oncogene-positive tumors.
  3. The relative dominance of state-based mechanisms may differ by disease stage; early subsolid or preinvasive disease may overrepresent progression-state mechanisms compared with advanced metastatic disease.

Statement 2: Subtype partition law

Statement: Biologic driver-negative LUAD is better explained by recurrent mechanistic families than by the umbrella negative label itself; the most reproducible families are hidden canonical lesion, dual-pathway suppressor-loss, noncoding/cis-regulatory activation, lineage-plasticity state, metabolic-dependency state, and microenvironment-dominant ecosystem state.

Domain/Scope: Applies to the full spectrum of oncogene-negative or no-known-actionable-driver LUAD after harmonizing negative-call definitions and separating assay-limited negatives from broad multi-omic negatives. Intended as a unifying taxonomy across genomic, proteomic, transcriptomic, and microenvironment-focused studies rather than as a strict replacement for existing histologic categories.

Special Cases

  1. A single tumor can belong to more than one family, for example STK11-loss plus immune-cold plus metabolic rewiring, or regulatory activation plus canonical ERBB dependence.
  2. Some families are enriched in specific contexts, such as ligand/kinase fusions in invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma, estrogen signaling in EGFR/ALK-negative never-smokers, or cholesterol/ER-stress programs in subsolid nodules.
  3. Technical false-negative cases should be separated before assigning a biologic family.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a historical 'driver-negative' LUAD cohort is reannotated with a standardized negative-call confidence score, narrow-panel-negative tumors will be enriched for rescued canonical lesions, whereas WGS plus RNA-seq-negative tumors will be enriched for suppressor-loss, regulatory, lineage, and ecosystem families.
  2. If RNA fusion calling and splice-junction analysis are added systematically to historical pan-negative mucinous and never-smoker LUAD cohorts, the largest incremental diagnostic gains will occur for NRG1-family, ROS1/RET/NTRK-family, and MET exon 14 events.
  3. If high-confidence broad-negative LUADs are jointly clustered by phosphoproteomics, methylation, enhancer activity, and TME composition, they will separate more reproducibly by mechanism family than by the umbrella 'driver-negative' label.
  4. If therapy is assigned by mechanism family rather than by umbrella negative status, within-group pharmacologic response variance will be lower than in the undifferentiated driver-negative population.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If truly lesion-minimal driver-negative LUAD exists as a stable class, deep single-cell phylogenomics plus long-read genome/transcriptome sequencing will reveal convergent lineage-state attractors without recurrent trunk oncogene lesions; failure to find such tumors would imply that almost all negatives are lesion-explainable or assay-limited.
  2. If residual WGS plus RNA-seq-negative LUAD is heavily driven by noncoding architecture, combined long-read sequencing, Hi-C, and H3K27ac profiling will identify a new class of low-copy enhancer-juxtaposition events that account for a large fraction of currently unexplained tumors.
  3. If some ecosystem-dominant negatives are maintained by tumor-extrinsic loops rather than tumor-intrinsic lesions, organotypic co-culture perturbation of CAF/endothelial/immune interactions will collapse tumor fitness in models that show weak dependency in tumor-cell monoculture.
  4. If coding-driver discovery is nearing saturation specifically in high-confidence negatives, future pan-cancer significance analyses restricted to coding mutations will plateau in explanatory power while enhancer, structural, and phosphoproteomic analyses continue to reduce the unexplained fraction.

Negative Experiments

  1. Assemble only high-confidence WGS plus RNA-seq-negative LUADs and test whether hidden canonical lesions still dominate after orthogonal validation. If they do, the theory overstates the shift toward state/regulatory biology.
  2. Apply the six-family taxonomy prospectively to an external broad-negative cohort. If tumors fail to partition reproducibly or show no family-specific molecular coherence, the subtype partition law is weakened.
  3. Quantify rescue probability as a function of assay breadth across harmonized cohorts. If rescue yield does not decrease with broader initial testing, the assay-depth rescue law is false.
  4. Test whether family-based therapy matching improves biomarker coherence or response consistency relative to umbrella 'driver-negative' assignment. If not, the taxonomy may be biologically interesting but clinically non-predictive.
