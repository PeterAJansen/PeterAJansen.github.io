Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1847

Name: Pathway-State Convergence Theory of Oncogene-Negative LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: A large fraction of oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinomas are not truly driverless and are not best explained by undiscovered single RTK/RAS/RAF mutations alone. Instead, they converge onto a small number of pathway states that can be reached through heterogeneous mechanisms: occult canonical drivers missed by limited assays, combinatorial loss of negative regulators of RAS and PI3K signaling, immune- and stromal-dominant microenvironmental programs, and proteomic/phosphoproteomic activation states not predictable from DNA alone. This theory predicts that driver-negative LUAD should be subclassified primarily by active pathway state, with genotype used as one contributor rather than the sole organizing principle.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Many apparent driver-negative LUADs are assay-negative rather than biology-negative

Statement: When LUAD is classified as driver-negative using restricted hotspot panels or shallow genomic assays, a substantial subset will be reclassified by broader DNA/RNA/proteogenomic profiling as harboring occult canonical or near-canonical oncogenic events; therefore, the observed driver-negative pool is a mixture of true alternative biology and false-negative canonical-driver cases.

Domain/Scope: Applies to clinical and research cohorts of LUAD called driver-negative or oncogene-negative after targeted testing for canonical RTK/RAS/RAF pathway alterations, especially panel-based testing or WES without comprehensive RNA/fusion/splicing/structural variant analysis. Most relevant to adenocarcinoma cohorts enriched for never-smokers or standard-testing-negative cases.

Special Cases

  1. Does not imply all driver-negative tumors are false negatives; a residual subset remains negative even after WGS/RNA-seq/proteomics.
  2. Reclassification rates depend on assay breadth, tumor purity, and whether noncoding, splicing, fusion, and amplification events are captured.
  3. Some occult events are still canonical pathway lesions, whereas others are rare pathway-adjacent lesions such as RIT1 or ARAF.

Statement 1: True oncogene-negative LUADs converge onto recurrent pathway states rather than one hidden gene class

Statement: After comprehensive genomic rescue of occult canonical drivers, the remaining oncogene-negative LUADs are best explained by a finite set of active pathway states—especially dual RAS/PI3K activation via loss of negative regulators, immune-high microenvironmental states, angiogenesis/stromal states, proliferation/cell-cycle states, metabolism/oxidative-stress states, and lineage-plasticity states—each of which can be generated by multiple different genomic and non-genomic mechanisms.

Domain/Scope: Applies to residual oncogene-negative or RTK/RAS/RAF-negative LUAD after comprehensive DNA/RNA profiling, and to EGFR/ALK-wild-type or major-driver-negative subtype analyses in LUAD. Especially relevant to never-smoker adenocarcinoma, standard-testing-negative cohorts, and proteogenomic studies where pathway activity can be measured directly or inferred from integrated data.

Special Cases

  1. Some residual tumors may still have undiscovered single-gene drivers, especially noncoding or enhancer-hijacking lesions.
  2. Different pathway states can coexist in one tumor, especially advanced or heterogeneous tumors.
  3. State labels are not strictly mutually exclusive and may shift over progression or treatment.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a residual driver-negative LUAD cohort is first cleaned by WGS plus RNA-seq plus phosphoproteomics, the fraction explained by occult canonical drivers will fall, but the remainder will cluster into a limited number of pathway states rather than a uniform residual group.
  2. Across independent cohorts, pERK/pAKT-high oncogene-negative tumors will be enriched for combinations of NF1, RASA1, PTEN, SOS1, or related pathway-regulator defects more than for novel recurrent RTKs.
  3. Immune-high driver-negative tumors will have fewer occult canonical oncogenic fusions than proliferation-high or metabolism-high driver-negative tumors, because their biology is more microenvironment-dominant.
  4. FeSig-high EGFR-wild-type tumors and angiogenesis-high EGFR/ALK-wild-type tumors will overlap more by fibroblast/CAF abundance than by shared recurrent mutations.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Single-cell phosphoproteomics may show that some residual driver-negative tumors cycle between two or more pathway states within the same lesion, making 'driver-negative' a dynamic ecosystem rather than a fixed molecular class.
  2. A pan-cancer classifier trained on state variables rather than mutations may identify driver-negative LUAD analogues in colorectal, pancreas, and breast cancers that share angiogenic, ferroptosis-resistant, or epigenetic/splicing-deregulated programs despite different tissue origins.
  3. Some apparently true-negative LUADs may be driven by topological noncoding events such as enhancer hijacking or ecDNA-mediated activation of lineage-compatible weak oncogenes, which would collapse the residual pool even further.
  4. The clinically most refractory driver-negative tumors may be those with no dominant state but high state entropy, and this entropy may predict failure of both targeted and immune therapies better than TMB.

Negative Experiments

  1. If comprehensive profiling does not materially reduce the apparent driver-negative fraction relative to targeted panels, the assay-negative component of the theory is weakened.
  2. If residual true-negative tumors do not reproducibly partition into pathway states across independent cohorts, the convergence claim fails.
  3. If pERK/pAKT, immune, angiogenic, proliferative, metabolic, and lineage-state classes are no more reproducible than random gene-expression clusters, the state-based law is undermined.
  4. If therapeutic sensitivities are unrelated to inferred pathway state after controlling for canonical genotypes, the central functional premise of the theory is weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. A residual subset of WGS-negative RTK/RAS/RAF-negative LUAD remained highly heterogeneous without a single recurrent compensatory lesion, and this theory does not yet specify the causal mechanism for each such case. [e1940.0]
  2. Some cohorts define driver-negative only by absence of a narrow panel, whereas others use broader or different criteria, so cross-study subtype alignment remains imperfect. [e1964.0] [e1953.0] [e1945.0] [e1926.0] [e1941.0]
