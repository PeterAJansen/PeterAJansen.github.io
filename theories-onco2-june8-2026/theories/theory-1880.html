Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1880

Name: Residual oncogene-negative LUAD is a pathway-output disease, not a pathway-absence disease

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: The main hidden structure within 'driver-negative' lung adenocarcinoma is not simply whether a DNA driver was found, but whether the tumor has already reinstated canonical oncogenic signaling output through noncanonical routes. Many apparent driver-negative tumors are actually covert RTK/RAS/RAF-MAPK and/or PI3K-AKT tumors whose activation is achieved by rare coding variants, exon-skipping, fusions, negative-regulator loss, amplification, enhancer hijacking, or protein-state activation without a canonical hotspot lesion. After stricter exclusion of false negatives, the remaining residual class is predicted to split into at least two biologically distinct branches: an Onc-negative RAS/PI3K branch with pathway output already high, and a noncanonical-state branch whose main liabilities are lineage, immune, metabolic, or epigenetic.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Ascertainment-compression law

Statement: The apparent frequency of oncogene-negative LUAD decreases monotonically as assay breadth expands from limited hotspot/fusion panels to RNA-based splice/fusion discovery to WES/WGS with copy-number, structural, noncoding, and protein-state profiling, because a substantial fraction of 'negative' tumors are assay-limited false negatives rather than a single coherent biological class.

Domain/Scope: Applies to human lung adenocarcinoma cohorts classified as driver-negative, pan-negative, non-CAGA, RPA-negative, or EGFR/KRAS/ALK-negative using non-uniform clinical or research assays. Most applicable when comparing panel-negative, RNA-seq-assisted, WES-negative, and WGS/proteogenomic-negative definitions across resected or advanced LUAD cohorts, especially never-smoker-enriched cohorts where uncommon fusions and splice alterations are frequent.

Special Cases

  1. Less informative in cohorts already profiled by high-quality WGS plus RNA-seq plus broad CNV/SV/noncoding analyses, because the residual class there is closer to true biological negativity.
  2. The apparent decrease can be attenuated by low tumor purity, limited tissue, FFPE degradation, low-VAF subclonality, or incomplete capture of phosphoprotein-only activation states.
  3. Some residual tumors remain unexplained even after WGS, so the law concerns shrinking frequency, not elimination, of the negative class.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Targeted clinical negativity often collapses after broader sequencing: a 33-year-old never-smoker LUAD judged EGFR/KRAS/EML4-ALK-negative by clinical testing was found by WGS/RNA-seq to harbor KIF5B-RET. [e2332.0] [e2286.0] [e2325.0] [e2325.1]
  2. Small replication cohorts of triple-negative or double-negative primary lung adenocarcinomas also yielded KIF5B-RET after deeper transcript analysis, showing that clinically negative status can be a prelude to rescue of a canonical kinase driver. [e2332.1] [e2332.2]
  3. In East Asian never-smoker LUAD, tumors initially negative for EGFR/HER2/KRAS/BRAF/ALK could be rescued by later ROS1 fusion testing; ROS1 fusions were found only after extending the assay set. [e2285.0] [e2285.1]
  4. Pan-negative LUADs defined after standard driver testing contained hidden EGFR extracellular-domain mutants; among 143 panel-negative tumors, additional EGFR ECD sequencing identified M277E and other ECD mutations. [e2277.0] [e2277.1] [e2277.2]
  5. RNA-seq rescue occurred even within conventionally screened tumors: six tumors negative by conventional EGFR screening were found by RNA-seq to harbor EGFR point mutations, while additional tumors carried novel kinase fusions or MET exon 14 skipping. [e2270.0] [e2270.1] [e2270.2] [e2270.3]
  6. Broad hybrid-capture NGS in triple-negative LUAD revealed actionable alterations in 31% of cases and recovered EGFR and KRAS mutations missed by conventional assays, plus ROS1/RET fusions, MET amplification, ERBB2 mutation, and BRAF mutation. [e2256.0] [e2275.1]
  7. Within RTK/RAS/RAF-negative LUAD, WGS rescued occult canonical pathway lesions in about one-third of WES-negative tumors, including hidden KRAS mutations, ARAF/EGFR/MAPK1/SOS1 amplifications, RASA1/NF1 deletions, and NRG1 fusions/mutations. [e2279.0] [e2284.0] [e2284.1]
  8. The WGS-negative RPA(-)G study explicitly attributes a fraction of WES-negative status to low purity and technical detection failure, showing that assay depth and completeness materially alter the apparent size of the negative class. [e2279.0] [e2284.0]
  9. Driver-negative/driver-unknown lung cancers in RNA meta-analysis were partially rescued by recurrent NRG1 fusions/outlier expression, Hippo fusions, NF1 truncating fusions, and MET exon 14 skipping, jointly explaining about 16% of the driver-unknown population. [e2291.0] [e2291.1] [e2291.2] [e2291.3] [e2291.4] [e2291.5]
  10. NTRK1 rearrangements were identified in LUAD tumors considered negative by standard clinical assays, showing that even highly actionable kinase fusions can hide beneath standard negative designations. [e2308.0] [e2308.1] [e2308.2]
  11. The non-CAGA LUAD work notes that integrated profiling rescued an occult ERBB2 activation mechanism through structural rearrangement and super-enhancer formation despite absence of canonical actionable ERBB2 alterations in the initial screening logic. [e2292.0] [e2292.1] [e2292.2] [e2345.3]
  12. The review literature explicitly generalizes this rescue phenomenon: broad hybrid-capture sequencing can identify actionable alterations in tumors otherwise negative by earlier genomic testing approaches. [e2275.1]
  13. Driver-negative labels based only on EGFR/ALK or EGFR/KRAS/ALK exclusion are especially unstable because subsequent profiling identifies ERBB2 exon 20 insertion, KRAS mutations, RET/ROS1/NRG1/NTRK1 rearrangements, and noncanonical EGFR lesions in such cohorts. [e2274.0] [e2274.1] [e2299.0] [e2302.0] [e2308.0] [e2277.0] [e2256.0]
  14. This law is also supported negatively: cohorts with only targeted-panel negativity still retain sizable unexplained groups, but those studies themselves note that broader exome/genome sequencing is needed to identify hidden drivers. [e2285.0] [e2293.0] [e2254.0] [e2265.0] [e2285.2]

Statement 1: Canonical-output convergence law

Statement: When a driver-negative LUAD shows high MAPK and/or PI3K-AKT pathway output, the most likely hidden causal architecture is not a wholly novel downstream biology but a noncanonical route back into canonical oncogenic signaling, such as RasGAP loss, PTEN loss, rare RAF-family mutation, splice-driven MET activation, fusion-driven RTK/ligand signaling, enhancer hijacking, amplification, or phosphoprotein activation without a canonical hotspot lesion.

Domain/Scope: Applies to strict or moderate-confidence driver-negative human LUADs and oncogene-negative mouse LUAD models with direct or inferred evidence of elevated pERK, pAKT, phospho-S6, phosphoproteomic MAPK/PI3K signatures, or functional sensitivity to SHP2/AKT/MEK/RTK blockade. Most applicable to resected LUAD, never-smoker LUAD, and non-CAGA/RPA-negative cohorts after at least moderate exclusion of canonical EGFR/KRAS/ALK/ROS1/RET/BRAF/MET/ERBB2 lesions.

Special Cases

  1. Immune-high or lineage-plastic tumors may show attenuated bulk phospho-signals despite focal signaling in malignant subclones.
  2. Protein-state activation can be transient, ligand-driven, or microenvironmentally sustained rather than fixed by a stable genomic lesion.
  3. Some pathway-high tumors may still harbor truly novel effectors, but current evidence suggests convergence on canonical output is common.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. The strongest direct evidence is the oncogene-negative mouse/human RAS-PI3K model: combined loss of NF1, RASA1, and PTEN drove oncogene-negative LUAD in vivo, produced high pERK and pAKT, and defined a human Onc-negative RAS/PI3K subtype with pathway-targetable vulnerabilities. [e2268.0] [e2268.1] [e2268.2] [e2262.0] [e2262.1] [e2259.0] [e2259.1] [e2259.2]
  2. A related human NSCLC subset with concurrent RASA1 and NF1 loss lacked canonical EGFR/KRAS/ALK/RET/ROS1/ERBB2/BRAF/MET exon 14 drivers and instead converged on MAPK/PI3K output with strong trametinib sensitivity, supporting RasGAP-loss convergence onto canonical signaling. [e2328.0] [e2328.1] [e2328.2]
  3. Rare coding driver substitutes also converge on canonical signaling: RIT1 mutations in oncogene-negative LUAD activated MEK/ERK and PI3K/AKT, transformed cells and xenografts, and responded to PI3K/MEK blockade. [e2281.0] [e2281.1] [e2281.2]
  4. Rare RAF-family hotspot mutations in otherwise driver-poor LUAD likewise converged on MAPK activation and sorafenib/trametinib sensitivity, including ARAF S214C and paralogous ARAF/RAF1 CR2 hotspot lesions. [e2309.0] [e2309.1] [e2309.2]
  5. Splice-driven MET activation is another direct convergence route: MET exon 14 skipping occurred in canonical-driver-negative tumors, was recurrently found in driver-unknown cohorts, and in functional studies sustained ligand-dependent MET/MAPK signaling by preventing CBL-mediated downregulation. [e2270.2] [e2288.0] [e2291.4]
  6. Fusion-driven RTK activation repeatedly rescued negative tumors into canonical pathway biology, including KIF5B-RET, ROS1 fusions, NTRK1 fusions, NTRK2 fusions, novel RTK fusions, and ALK/RET/ROS1-type fusions in broader cohorts. [e2332.0] [e2325.0] [e2285.1] [e2308.0] [e2308.1] [e2308.2] [e2329.2] [e2270.1] [e2341.3] [e2359.1]
  7. Ligand-driven fusion biology also converges on canonical RTK output: CD74-NRG1 and related NRG1 fusions in pan-negative mucinous LUAD activated ERBB2/ERBB3 and PI3K-AKT signaling, with phospho-ERBB3 enrichment and functional dependence on the NRG1-ERBB axis. [e2299.0] [e2299.1] [e2299.2] [e2302.0] [e2291.1]
  8. Structural/regulatory rewiring can restore canonical receptor signaling without canonical mutations: ERBB2 super-enhancer/rearrangement in non-CAGA LUAD caused HER2 overexpression and sensitivity to poziotinib and trastuzumab deruxtecan. [e2292.0] [e2292.1]
  9. EGFR pathway output can also be restored without canonical kinase-domain mutation: EGFR amplification occurred independently of EGFR mutation and correlated with higher EGFR expression; EGFR extracellular-domain mutation M277E transformed cells and xenografts and responded to erlotinib/cetuximab; EGFR exon 25/26 structural deletion was oncogenic and erlotinib-sensitive. [e2367.5] [e2277.0] [e2277.1] [e2297.1]
  10. Outlier kinase expression without canonical rearrangement suggests an additional convergence route at the protein or transcript abundance level: ROS1 and FGFR3 outlier-expression cases occurred almost exclusively in driver-unknown samples despite absent FISH-detectable rearrangement. [e2291.5] [e2286.2]
  11. Proteogenomics independently supports the same law: EGFR-mutant LUAD showed strong SHP2 Y62 phosphorylation without RNA/protein abundance change, demonstrating that signaling-state activation can reside at the phosphoprotein layer rather than the DNA hotspot layer alone. [e2304.2]
  12. In early-stage East Asian LUAD, a small EGFR-wild-type subset with high MAPK phosphoproteomic activity was mostly explained by KRAS mutations, reinforcing the principle that when pathway output is high, hidden or alternate canonical activators are often nearby. [e2311.1]
  13. The TCGA/combined LUAD oncogene-negative analysis reached the same conclusion at the DNA level: MET amplification, ERBB2 amplification, NF1 mutation, RIT1 mutation, and MET exon 14 skipping were specifically enriched in tumors lacking canonical RTK/RAS/RAF drivers, expanding targetable lesions within the same pathway logic rather than replacing it. [e2290.0] [e2275.2] [e2255.0] [e2255.1]

Statement 2: Residual noncanonical-state law

Statement: After exhaustive exclusion of covert canonical signaling drivers, the remaining true residual oncogene-negative LUADs are more likely to be maintained by state variables at chromatin, lineage, immune, or metabolic layers than by a single undiscovered recurrent kinase gene.

Domain/Scope: Applies to the strict residual class of human LUAD after broad genomic and transcriptomic exclusion of canonical RTK/RAS/RAF and major fusion/splice/amplification lesions, especially cohorts with WGS, RNA-seq, methylation, copy-number, structural, or proteogenomic profiling. This law is not intended for low-confidence panel-negative cohorts.

Special Cases

  1. Some residual tumors may still harbor undiscovered rare coding drivers below current cohort power, particularly intermediate-frequency genes or low-VAF subclones.
  2. Residual tumors can still contain downstream oncogene amplifications or tumor suppressor losses that partially overlap with canonical pathway biology.
  3. Bulk-omics residual states may mix tumor-intrinsic and microenvironment-derived signals.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. The clearest WGS-based residual class, RPA(-)G LUAD, lacked a single recurrent replacement oncogene and instead showed enrichment for TP53, KEAP1, SMARCA4, STK11 loss, ILF2 promoter mutation, complex structural variation, and downstream amplifications such as MYC, MCL1, MYCL, MDM2, CCNE1, KAT6A, and NKX2-1. [e2279.0] [e2279.1] [e2279.2] [e2279.3] [e2284.1]
  2. That WGS study explicitly concluded that no unique recurrent genetic lesion compensated for absence of RTK/RAS/RAF alterations, supporting heterogeneity of residual biology rather than substitution by one new kinase driver. [e2279.0]
  3. Non-CAGA LUAD likewise emphasized regulatory and epigenetic dysregulation: most MAML2-locus alterations were noncoding, associated with suppressed H3K27ac enhancer activity, reduced MAML2 expression, and methylation-linked repression, without direct proof of a replacement kinase driver. [e2345.0] [e2345.1] [e2345.2] [e2345.4]
  4. In the same non-CAGA setting, ERBB2 super-enhancer/rearrangement cases were explicitly a small rescued subset, implying that the broader residual cohort cannot be explained mainly by recurrent covert ERBB2-like lesions. [e2292.0] [e2292.1] [e2345.3]
  5. EGFR/ALK-negative never-smoker LUAD (NENA) was organized not by a dominant hidden oncogene but by an estrogen-signaling/SRC/GPER1 state associated with STK11 mutation, chr14/chr21 deletion, and LLGL2/ST14 hypomethylation. [e2366.0] [e2366.1] [e2366.3] [e2366.4] [e2347.0] [e2347.1] [e2347.4] [e2347.5] [e2347.6]
  6. A separate driver-gene-negative LUAD cohort was organized around glycolytic state rather than a hidden kinase lesion, with MIF-high AT2-like malignant cells and a proposed MIF-HIF-1α axis defining glycolysis-dependent biology. [e2258.0] [e2258.1]
  7. Proteogenomic subtype work in EGFR/ALK-wild-type never-smoker LUAD also resolved the negative cohort into proliferation, immune, angiogenesis, and metabolism states rather than into one replacement oncogene class. [e2274.0] [e2274.3]
  8. The APOLLO/CPTAC expression-proteomic frameworks similarly identify PP and PI states, where many tumors lack targetable oncogene mutations yet are defined by metabolic rewiring, immune activation/exclusion, or tumor suppressor context rather than a single novel kinase lesion. [e2365.2] [e2365.3] [e2267.2] [e2267.3] [e2335.0] [e2365.4] [e2335.2]
  9. Piano/driver-poor never-smoker LUADs are characterized by low SCNA, low TMB, long telomeres, high subclonality, stemness/development signatures, and scarcity of known recurrent drivers, supporting a stemness/quiescence or lineage-state explanation for at least part of the residual class.   10. Steady never-smoker LUAD is another driver-sparse state-like subtype characterized by high alveolar lineage fidelity, low proliferation, low immune suppression, and better prognosis rather than a specific replacement oncogene. [e2264.1]
  11. Early LUAD progression studies argue that histologic progression from lepidic to solid is often driven by non-genetic transcriptional and microenvironmental reprogramming rather than acquisition of new recurrent genetic drivers, reinforcing the plausibility of state-maintained residual disease. Single-cell and spatial studies identify KRT8-positive alveolar intermediate cells (KACs), angiogenic tip endothelial cells, and matrix CAF states as progression-relevant programs in LUAD, suggesting that some residual driver-negative tumors may be sustained by cell-state ecology rather than canonical oncogene dominance.   14. Residual classes are also captured by prognosis-only frameworks based on immune or transcriptomic states, such as IRGP signatures and second-level non-CAGA clustering, further implying that downstream state architecture remains informative even when recurrent genomic drivers are not. [e2294.0] [e2294.1] [e2345.2]

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a prospectively collected LUAD cohort is profiled in stages (panel, RNA fusion/splice, WES, WGS/SV/noncoding, phosphoproteomics), the apparent driver-negative fraction will shrink at each stage, with the steepest drop occurring when RNA splice/fusion discovery and WGS/SV/noncoding analysis are added.
  2. If strict residual driver-negative LUADs are stratified by pERK/pAKT or phosphoproteomic MAPK/PI3K output, the pathway-high group will be enriched for negative-regulator lesions (NF1, RASA1, PTEN, STK11/KEAP1-related pathway release), rare RAF/RIT1 lesions, RTK/ligand fusions, exon-skipping events, or regulatory overexpression mechanisms.
  3. If ERBB2/EGFR/ROS1/FGFR3 outlier-expression cases lacking canonical lesions undergo long-read WGS plus Hi-C/H3K27ac profiling, a nontrivial fraction will show enhancer hijacking, local SV rewiring, or focal regulatory gains rather than true expression-only phenomena.
  4. If a strict residual cohort is separated after removal of all covert-canonical cases, the remaining tumors will cluster more strongly by metabolic, immune, chromatin, or lineage state than by recurrent kinase-gene mutation.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If residual pathway-low strict oncogene-negative LUADs are sampled longitudinally from premalignant lesions, some will initiate as lineage-state or injury-state tumors and only later acquire MAPK/PI3K-high maintenance states, implying sequential transition from state-driven initiation to covert-canonical maintenance.
  2. If phosphoproteomic pathway-output classifiers are applied prospectively to strict driver-negative LUAD, they may outperform genomic labels in predicting treatment response, potentially redefining precision therapy around pathway state rather than lesion class.
  3. If airway microbiome or chronic epithelial injury contributes to the state-driven residual branch, spatial microbial signatures or epithelial injury programs will co-segregate with KAC-like, piano-like, or estrogen-signaling states independently of canonical kinase output.
  4. If some residual tumors are maintained by ligand ecology rather than stable intrinsic lesions, matched co-culture with fibroblasts/macrophages will restore phospho-RTK activity and drug sensitivity patterns not seen in tumor-only culture.

Negative Experiments

  1. Profile a rigorously WGS/RNA-negative LUAD cohort with phosphoproteomics and test whether most pathway-high tumors still map to noncanonical canonical-signaling mechanisms. If many pathway-high tumors remain unexplained after broad genomic, structural, regulatory, and protein-state analysis, the canonical-output convergence law is weakened.
  2. Prospectively compare panel-negative, RNA-augmented, WES-negative, and WGS-negative LUAD classification on identical specimens with controlled purity. If assay breadth does not reduce the apparent negative fraction, the ascertainment-compression law would fail.
  3. In a strict residual cohort, test whether lineage/immune/metabolic/chromatin states are actually enriched relative to rescued false-negative cases. If no such enrichment appears, the residual noncanonical-state law is undermined.
  4. Functionally test SHP2/AKT/MEK/RTK dependence in pathway-high strict residual tumors. If they are broadly insensitive despite high canonical phospho-output, the theory overstates the relationship between output convergence and hidden canonical addiction.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Very rare coding candidates such as POU4F2, ZKSCAN1, SOS1, ARHGAP35, ARAF/RAF1 hotspots, and SIK2 duplication may represent bona fide additional drivers that do not fit cleanly into either the covert-canonical branch or the state-driven branch on current evidence. [e2265.1] [e2265.2] [e2358.1] [e2358.2] [e2309.1] [e2297.2] [e2261.1]
  2. Some recurrence-risk biomarker studies based on IRGPs, circulating tumor cells, S-MMR scores, or recurrence-associated RNA-protein discordance may track downstream behavior rather than core driver biology and are therefore only partially addressed by this theory. [e2294.0] [e2294.1] [e2289.1] [e2257.2] [e2282.1]
