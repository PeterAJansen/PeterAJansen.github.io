Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1877

Name: Ecosystem-to-Pathway Driver Transfer Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: Many driver-negative LUADs are not explained by hidden mutations alone because the effective driver resides in a stable tumor ecosystem state that can substitute for a classical oncogene. In this framework, tumors are maintained by self-reinforcing loops between tumor cell state and microenvironmental programs: immune-active/TLS states, angiogenesis-CAF exclusion states, fibroblast-ferroptosis-resistance states, estrogen-signaling never-smoker states, and ER-stress or glycolysis programs. The clinically relevant subtype unit is therefore an ecosystem program with a downstream signaling dependency, not necessarily a discrete DNA lesion.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Microenvironmental substitute-driver law

Statement: In a substantial fraction of oncogene-negative LUAD, stable microenvironmental programs can function as substitute drivers by sustaining tumor fitness, immune escape, and progression despite absence of a dominant canonical oncogene; the main recurrent ecosystem archetypes are immune-high/TLS, angiogenesis-CAF exclusion, fibroblast-linked ferroptosis resistance, and lineage-coupled estrogen signaling states.

Domain/Scope: Applies to driver-poor or clinically non-actionable LUAD cohorts defined by EGFR/ALK-wild-type, EGFR-wild-type, broad clinical-panel negative, or proteogenomic state-based classifications, especially never-smoker and early-stage disease where transcriptomic/proteomic ecosystem signals are strong. Does not claim these states are mutually exclusive or purely extrinsic; many are mixed intrinsic-extrinsic programs.

Special Cases

  1. Immune-high states may improve prognosis rather than worsen it, but still act as dominant biology shaping therapy response.
  2. Angiogenesis-high and fibroblast-rich states can coexist with TP53/KRAS/STK11/KEAP1 lesions, so ecosystem state may be downstream of genomics rather than fully independent.
  3. Never-smoker estrogen-signaling states are only formally established in EGFR/ALK-negative cohorts and should not be generalized to all driver-negative LUAD.
  4. Some apparent ecosystem states may actually be secondary to undiscovered hidden drivers in a minority of cases.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. EGFR- and ALK-wild-type never-smoker LUAD segregated into immune-high, angiogenesis-high, metabolism-high, and proliferation-high subgroups, indicating that driver-negative biology is often organized by stable ecosystem or pathway states rather than a single lesion. [e2408.0] [e2408.1] [e2408.2] [e2408.3] [e2408.4]
  2. The immune-high subgroup showed high T- and B-cell infiltration, TLS features, checkpoint receptor/ligand expression, and the best prognosis, demonstrating that an ecosystem program can define a clinically coherent driver-negative subtype. [e2408.2]
  3. The angiogenesis-high subgroup showed CAFs, MDSCs, TGF-β/cell-migration signaling, LGALS3/FGF2/CXCL12/PDGFB expression, immune exclusion, and poor prognosis, supporting an angiogenic stromal substitute-driver state. [e2408.3]
  4. EGFR-wild-type LUAD with high ferroptosis-related signature showed fibroblast-enriched microenvironment, enhanced fibroblast-tumor communication, IGF-binding pathway activity, worse survival, and preclinical vulnerability linked to 6-mercaptopurine and ferroptosis biology. [e2420.0]
  5. Never-smoker LUAD without EGFR or ALK alterations showed elevated estrogen signaling at RNA and protein levels, associated with STK11 mutation, chromosome 14/21 loss, LLGL2/ST14 hypomethylation, and GPER1 CNA/trans effects, supporting a pathway ecosystem rather than a single classical hidden oncogene. [e2457.0] [e2457.1] [e2457.2] [e2475.0]
  6. Driver gene-negative LUAD cohorts defined by broad clinical panels separated mainly by immune-context differences, including checkpoint expression and immune infiltration, without discovery of a dominant genomic driver, again supporting an ecosystem-state framing. [e2407.0] [e2416.0] [e2416.2]
  7. Single-cell and spatial analyses in LUAD and related early disease identified epithelial-stromal states such as CLDN2+ AT2/COL6A3+ fibroblast interaction and THBS2+ CAF programs that predict progression or poor immunotherapy response, consistent with ecosystem-level tumor maintenance. [e2388.3] [e2388.4]
  8. This law was inferred by induction across multiple independently defined negative or non-actionable LUAD cohorts: when no single hidden lesion dominates, reproducible microenvironmental programs recur and stratify prognosis and therapy more strongly than lesion identity.

Explanation: This theory is somewhat related to existing work because its ingredients are known, but the claim that ecosystem programs should be elevated to the same conceptual status as hidden genomic drivers in driver-negative LUAD is a stronger, integrative reformulation.

What Already Exists: Individual ecosystem states such as immune-hot, angiogenic CAF-rich, ferroptosis-related, and estrogen-signaling never-smoker LUAD have already been reported in separate studies. Microenvironmental stratification of LUAD is therefore established at the component level.

What is Novel: The novelty is the synthesis that these states are not merely modifiers of oncogene-defined disease but form a substitute-driver class for many driver-negative LUADs, where the operative biological unit is a self-stabilizing ecosystem program.

Statement 1: Ecosystem-state therapeutic law

Statement: When oncogene-negative LUAD is organized by an ecosystem state, therapeutic sensitivity is better predicted by state-specific ligands, stromal cell types, checkpoint context, or metabolic crosstalk than by canonical genomic driver status alone; therefore, treatment should be assigned by ecosystem archetype rather than by residual negative-call category.

Domain/Scope: Applies to driver-negative or no-known-actionable-driver LUAD in which transcriptomic, proteomic, phosphoproteomic, or spatial profiling identifies a dominant ecosystem state. Best supported in preclinical or retrospective settings for immune-high, angiogenesis-high, estrogen-signaling-high, ferroptosis-high/fibroblast-rich, and ER-stress-high disease. Not yet established as a standard prospective treatment algorithm.

Special Cases

  1. Some ecosystem markers are prognostic but not yet predictive in prospective trials.
  2. A single tumor may contain more than one ecosystem state, requiring regional or single-cell profiling.
  3. State-targeted therapy may need to be combined with lesion-targeted therapy in tumors rescued by deeper sequencing.
  4. Different biomarkers may be needed for early-stage versus metastatic disease.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. The immune-high EGFR/ALK-wild-type never-smoker subgroup showed potential combination immunotherapy targets including CCL5, CXCL13, CD27, TRAF2, and LPAR6-associated vulnerabilities, even though no single actionable oncogene defined the subgroup. [e2408.2]
  2. The angiogenesis-high subgroup suggested candidate interventions against LGALS3/galectin-3, CXCL12/SDF-1, and FGF2, indicating that stromal-ligand context can nominate therapy in driver-negative disease. [e2408.3]
  3. The metabolism-high subgroup nominated ACACA/mtFAS and PRISM-matched compounds such as clorsulon, supporting metabolically assigned treatment rather than genomic-driver assignment. [e2408.4]
  4. The estrogen-signaling NENA state prioritized saracatinib and showed greater in vitro sensitivity in STK11-mutant and STK11/ERBB2-mutant models, implying that pathway-state context can guide therapy in EGFR/ALK-negative never-smoker LUAD. [e2457.0] [e2457.1] [e2475.0]
  5. The fibroblast/ferroptosis EGFR-wild-type state nominated 6-mercaptopurine and linked fibroblast-rich communication signatures to ferroptosis resistance, with knockdown and xenograft data supporting state-linked therapy. [e2420.0]
  6. ER-stress-high early LUAD/SSN states were vulnerable to PDI inhibition with CCF642 and to Pdia3 knockdown in PDX, PTC, allograft, and knockdown models, showing that a pathway-state can itself be the therapeutic entry point. [e2458.2] [e2482.1] [e2482.0]
  7. Transcriptomic driver-negative cohorts repeatedly linked high-risk states to immune or metabolic programs and suggested differential immunotherapy or chemotherapy benefit even without discovery of a hidden genomic driver. [e2407.0] [e2416.0] [e2416.1] [e2416.2]
  8. This law was logically inferred from the repeated observation that distinct ecosystem states in negative LUAD each nominate different targets, whereas the negative-call label alone does not predict therapy.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If driver-negative LUADs are profiled jointly by spatial transcriptomics and plasma cytokine/ligand assays, ecosystem archetypes will be classifiable using a small circulating panel such as CXCL13, CCL5, LGALS3, CXCL12, FGL1, and MIF.
  2. If therapy-naive broad-negative LUADs are assigned to immune-high, angiogenesis-high, estrogen-signaling-high, fibroblast-ferroptosis-high, or ER-stress-high states, response patterns will segregate by state-specific biomarkers better than by broad negative-call status alone.
  3. If serial biopsies are taken during progression of driver-negative LUAD, many tumors will switch between ecosystem archetypes without acquiring a new canonical oncogene, especially from immune-high or steady states toward angiogenesis/CAF or stress-adaptive states.
  4. If fibroblast-rich ferroptosis-high tumors are cocultured with matched CAFs, ferroptosis resistance will increase and become reversible by disrupting predicted ligand-receptor programs such as IGF-related or fibroblast signaling circuits.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. A pan-LUAD 'ecosystem driver score' combining TLS density, CAF state, angiogenic ligand abundance, ferroptosis state, estrogen-signaling score, and ER-stress markers may outperform mutation-based stratification even in some nominally driver-positive tumors.
  2. Some chaotic or piano tumors may prove to be metastable ecosystem states that can be therapeutically reprogrammed into immune-responsive states without targeting any hidden genomic lesion.
  3. Microbiome or airway colonization patterns, not yet measured in these datasets, may map selectively onto angiogenesis-high or immune-high driver-negative LUAD and act as upstream stabilizers of those ecosystem states.
  4. Ecosystem archetypes may be detectable by radiomics plus blood biomarkers alone, allowing noninvasive assignment of therapy in negative LUAD without repeat tissue biopsy.

Negative Experiments

  1. Prospectively show that state-specific biomarkers such as CAF abundance, estrogen signaling, TLS markers, ferroptosis/fibroblast signatures, or ER-stress markers do not improve therapy prediction over the undifferentiated negative-call label; this would undermine the theory.
  2. Demonstrate that most ecosystem-classified driver-negative tumors are reclassified into single canonical hidden drivers after exhaustive sequencing and lose their state-specific therapeutic behavior; this would argue the ecosystem is secondary rather than substitute-driving.
  3. Show in matched models that disrupting the dominant ecosystem loop leaves tumor fitness unchanged despite marker suppression; this would challenge the claim that the ecosystem state is functionally driver-like.
  4. Find that spatially distinct ecosystem states within a tumor do not correspond to distinct outcome or drug-response behavior; this would weaken the subtype utility of the theory.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1.
