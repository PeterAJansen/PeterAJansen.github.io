Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1849

Name: Distributed Driver Architecture in Oncogene-Negative LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: A substantial fraction of apparently oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinomas are not truly driverless. Instead, they are generated by a distributed architecture of causal lesions that evade classic hotspot-centric RTK/RAS/RAF testing. This architecture contains at least four recurrent classes: occult canonical drivers missed for technical reasons, rare noncanonical coding drivers in known signaling genes, structural/splice/ligand-fusion drivers that require RNA- or WGS-aware assays, and pathway-equivalent states produced by loss of negative regulators, cell-cycle checkpoints, or lineage/chromatin control. The theory therefore treats 'driver-negative LUAD' as an admixture of assay-missed canonical disease plus biologically distinct noncanonical states, not as a single coherent molecular entity.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Most apparent driver negativity is assay-relative, not biology-free

Statement: When lung adenocarcinomas are called driver-negative after limited hotspot/fusion testing, routine clinical assays, or standard-depth exome pipelines, many are reclassified as driver-positive when orthogonal structural assays, deeper sequencing, RNA-splicing/fusion analysis, and copy-number-aware WGS are added; therefore, driver-negativity is primarily a conditional property of assay scope and sample quality rather than a stable biological class.

Domain/Scope: Applies to human lung adenocarcinoma cohorts initially classified as lacking canonical oncogenic drivers in the RTK/RAS/RAF pathway or closely allied actionable kinase pathways by targeted panels, limited clinical assays, standard WES, or mutation-centric pipelines. Especially relevant in never-smoker LUAD, low-purity tumors, mucinous tumors, and cohorts where first-pass assays incompletely cover fusions, exon-skipping, extracellular/non-kinase-domain mutations, amplifications, or noncanonical RAF-family lesions.

Special Cases

  1. Does not imply every driver-negative tumor will be rescued by broader testing; a residual subset remains unresolved even after WGS/RNA/proteomic profiling.
  2. The rescue fraction depends strongly on tumor purity, exon capture quality, panel content, whether fusion/splicing assays are included, and whether copy-number or structural-variant interpretation is integrated with expression.
  3. Some broader-assay discoveries remain candidate rather than proven drivers, so expanded testing increases both true-positive rescue and uncertain findings.

Statement 1: Driver-negative LUAD contains pathway-equivalent noncanonical states

Statement: Among tumors that remain unexplained after broad canonical driver rescue, a substantial fraction are maintained by pathway-equivalent states rather than by a single classic oncogene: combined loss of RAS/PI3K negative regulators, cell-cycle checkpoint collapse, and lineage/chromatin-state deregulation can generate tumor-maintaining signaling and growth without a canonical RTK/RAS/RAF point driver.

Domain/Scope: Applies to human LUADs and closely related NSCLCs that remain unresolved or are only partially explained after broad canonical oncogene discovery, including tumors with NF1/RASA1/PTEN loss, NF1-RASA1 co-inactivation, PTEN protein loss without genomic PTEN mutation, CDKN2A/CDK4/MDM2 cell-cycle lesions, and genomically quiet piano-like tumors with stemness or lineage-state signatures. Also includes mechanistic mouse or cell models used to establish causality for these states.

Special Cases

  1. Some pathway-equivalent states coexist with canonical oncogenes and act as cooperating lesions rather than primary initiating drivers.
  2. Not all recurrent tumor suppressor losses are sufficient alone; several require multi-hit combinations or pathway co-activation to produce efficient tumorigenesis.
  3. Evidence is strongest for pathway activation and therapeutic dependence, and weaker for assigning full clonal primacy in some human tumors.

Statement 2: Residual unresolved tumors are enriched for noncoding and protein-state drivers

Statement: After broad DNA/RNA rescue of occult canonical and noncanonical coding drivers, the remaining unresolved oncogene-negative LUADs are enriched for mechanisms best seen in chromatin-state and protein-state space: cis-regulatory structural rewiring, promoter/TSS lesions, methylation-driven expression changes, and phospho-proteomic signaling outliers. Therefore, the unresolved tail of driver-negative LUAD should shrink more by adding chromatin and protein-state assays than by simply enlarging hotspot DNA panels.

Domain/Scope: Applies to residual unresolved LUADs after targeted panel, WES/WGS, fusion, and splice-aware analyses; especially relevant to non-CAGA LUAD, WGS-characterized RPA(-)G cases, and integrated proteogenomic cohorts where RNA abundance alone failed to explain tumor-driving signaling states.

Special Cases

  1. Some proteomic outliers are downstream consequences of already-known genomic drivers rather than independent primary drivers.
  2. Many noncoding or epigenetic candidates remain hypothesis-generating unless orthogonally validated by chromatin conformation, CRISPR perturbation, or matched functional assays.
  3. Certain protein-state outliers may mark vulnerability rather than initiation.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a contemporary 'driver-negative' LUAD cohort is re-profiled with a fixed multimodal workflow of deep DNA panel plus RNA fusion/splice sequencing plus WGS-level copy-number/SV calling plus phosphoproteomics, the largest incremental diagnostic yield over standard practice will come first from structural/splice rescue and then from regulatory/protein-state rescue in the residual cases.
  2. Residual unresolved tumors after DNA/RNA rescue will be enriched for low-purity samples, mucinous or lineage-shifted histology, and discordance between RNA and protein/phosphosite activity, rather than for additional undiscovered hotspot SNVs.
  3. Never-smoker driver-negative LUADs will contain a higher fraction of structural, ligand-fusion, and lineage-state driver classes, whereas smoker-associated driver-negative tumors will more often resolve into suppressor-loss or complex-SV/amplification states.
  4. Tumors with no canonical driver but strong pERK and pAKT by IHC/proteomics will disproportionately harbor combined negative-regulator loss, especially NF1/RASA1/PTEN alterations or PTEN protein loss without genomic PTEN mutation.
  5. A substantial minority of tumors labeled negative by panel sequencing but showing outlier receptor abundance will prove to carry either noncanonical domain mutations, splicing events, or cis-regulatory structural lesions rather than broad unexplained signaling noise.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. A nontrivial subset of currently unresolved driver-negative LUAD will be explained by enhancer-topology lesions affecting lineage-restricted receptor-adaptor genes such as FRS2 or CAV2, but only in specific histologic or lineage contexts.
  2. Some apparently driver-negative tumors with high APOBEC activity will prove to be maintained by distributed subclonal pathway lesions rather than a single dominant clonal oncogene, creating a true 'poly-driver' state.
  3. A residual genomically quiet piano-like subgroup will depend on stemness regulators or chromatin-state circuits and will be more vulnerable to differentiation or chromatin-remodeling therapy than to kinase inhibition.
  4. Phosphoproteomic outlier states in unresolved tumors will define therapeutically reproducible 'drivers without recurrent mutations', enabling treatment selection by signaling-state class rather than lesion identity.
  5. A subset of driver-negative mucinous tumors will be found to use ligand-presenting or juxtacrine fusion logic beyond NRG1, involving other membrane-tethered growth-factor families.

Negative Experiments

  1. Profile a large independent driver-negative LUAD cohort with comprehensive DNA, RNA, copy-number, methylation, chromatin, and proteomics; if the majority of cases still do not resolve into assay-missed canonical, noncanonical coding, structural/splice/ligand, or pathway-equivalent/protein-state classes, the theory is weakened.
  2. Test whether broader assay breadth meaningfully increases driver yield beyond standard panel testing; if rescue rates are negligible in well-powered cohorts with adequate purity, the first statement is false.
  3. Compare pathway activation in unresolved tumors to combined suppressor-loss and protein-loss states; if pERK/pAKT activation does not track with negative-regulator loss or protein-level loss, the pathway-equivalent component is weakened.
  4. Add chromatin and phosphoproteomic assays to residual unresolved tumors after deep DNA/RNA profiling; if these assays do not enrich for explanatory lesions or vulnerabilities compared with further hotspot-panel expansion, the third statement is weakened.
  5. In low-purity tumors reclassified as negative by routine assays, repeat profiling with high-sensitivity orthogonal methods; if low purity does not materially affect rescue probability, the assay-relative-negativity claim is weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The theory does not fully explain why some recurrent amplification loci such as 14q13.3/NKX2-1-region events fail functional sufficiency tests in certain mouse models, beyond placing them in the category of context-dependent or cooperative lesions. [e2108.1]
  2. The theory does not specifically explain prognostic-only transcriptomic or radiomic studies of driver-negative LUAD that did not perform molecular rescue analyses and therefore remain biology-rich but mechanism-poor. [e2072.0] [e2129.0] [e2048.0]
  3. Some studies define driver-negative disease only by EGFR or EGFR/ALK wild-type status, making them too heterogeneous to map cleanly onto the four proposed classes. [e2043.0] [e2085.0]
