Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1881

Name: Driver-negative LUAD is a state-space mosaic of four recurrent ecological modes

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: True driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma is better understood as a mosaic of recurring system states than as a single leftover category. Across the evidence, most unexplained tumors fall predominantly into one or more of four ecological modes: covert canonical signaling, lineage/quiescence-plasticity, immune ecology, and metabolic-redox stress. Different studies recover different fractions of these modes because negativity definitions, assay depth, and tissue context differ. Rare gene events matter mainly insofar as they seed or stabilize one of these modes.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Four-mode partition law

Statement: Driver-negative or driver-poor LUADs recurrently organize into four dominant systems-level modes: (1) covert canonical signaling, where hidden lesions or suppressor losses reconstitute RTK/RAS/RAF or PI3K output; (2) lineage/quiescence-plasticity, where lineage identity, stem-like quiescence, or epithelial-state transitions dominate; (3) immune ecology, where prognosis and treatment are governed primarily by immune infiltration, exclusion, or checkpoint state; and (4) metabolic-redox stress, where glycolysis, ferroptosis resistance, NRF2/stress adaptation, or hormone-SRC metabolic wiring dominate. Different cohorts enrich different modes because assay layer and cohort ascertainment determine which state variables are visible.

Domain/Scope: Applies to lung adenocarcinoma cohorts called driver-negative, oncogene-negative, non-CAGA, RTK/RAS/RAF-negative, EGFR/ALK-wild-type never-smoker, or driver-poor by partial or comprehensive profiling. Most accurate in studies with multi-omic or orthogonal validation; also applies as an interpretive framework to adjacent driver-poor LUAD-like states in never-smoker cohorts and histology-linked subgroups such as mucinous adenocarcinoma.

Special Cases

  1. A single tumor can occupy multiple modes simultaneously, especially advanced tumors, mixed-histology tumors, or spatially heterogeneous tumors.
  2. Mode assignment can be distorted when negativity is defined by narrow panels; in those settings, some apparent 'modes' may actually be false-negative covert canonical cases.
  3. Some proliferative copy-number amplicon states (for example TPX2, WHSC1L1, 20q11, 8p12) may cut across the four-mode structure as an orthogonal mitotic/chromosomal branch.
  4. The framework is intended for LUAD and LUAD-like tumors; lung squamous and neuroendocrine comparators are informative analogies but not part of the core target domain.

Statement 1: Mode-specific therapeutic law

Statement: In driver-negative LUAD, treatment sensitivity follows dominant ecological mode more reliably than residual-gene naming alone: covert canonical signaling predicts benefit from pathway-restoring kinase-axis therapy (for example RTK, SHP2, MEK, AKT, HER2, TRK, RET, MET); immune-ecology predicts checkpoint or immune-modulating strategies; metabolic-redox mode predicts sensitivity to ferroptosis, redox, glutamine, SRC, HSP90, or cell-cycle-metabolic combinations; and lineage/quiescence-plasticity predicts benefit from interception or state-targeting approaches such as anti-angiogenic, stromal, developmental, or lineage-biomarker-guided therapy.

Domain/Scope: Applies to driver-negative or driver-poor LUADs after mode assignment by integrated DNA/RNA/protein/phosphoprotein/spatial data, and to translational models such as organoids, PDXs, and genetically engineered mice that recapitulate the same mode. Most reliable when pathway activity is measured functionally rather than inferred only from DNA.

Special Cases

  1. Tumors with overlapping modes may require combination therapy across modes rather than a single mode-matched drug.
  2. Gene-specific sensitivity can supersede mode assignment when a strong validated hidden canonical driver is present, such as RET, ROS1, NTRK1, MET exon 14, or EGFR M277E.
  3. Immune predictions can be confounded by purity and stromal composition in bulk assays.

Statement 2: Lineage-gates-driver law

Statement: In driver-negative LUAD, lineage state and epithelial cell identity constrain which hidden mechanisms are oncogenically accessible: mucinous or NKX2-1-low states preferentially admit ligand-fusion or gastric-program mechanisms; stem-like or quiescent states preferentially admit low-burden driver-poor evolution; and terminal-respiratory-unit or EGFR-compatible states preferentially admit covert receptor-kinase signaling lesions.

Domain/Scope: Applies to LUAD subsets with strong lineage-state resolution, including mucinous/invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma, NKX2-1-high TRU-like tumors, piano/steady-like stemness states, and KAC/lepidic precursor-like states. Best supported in never-smoker, low-smoking-signature, and histology-resolved cohorts.

Special Cases

  1. Lineage state can evolve over time, so the accessible hidden-driver class is dynamic rather than fixed.
  2. Some canonical lesions such as KRAS or TP53 can later override lineage constraints and produce mixed or dedifferentiated states.
  3. Lineage-compatible covert canonical lesions can coexist with apparently driver-poor states, so lineage bias is probabilistic rather than absolute.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a prospective strict driver-negative LUAD cohort is profiled with WGS, RNA-seq, phosphoproteomics, methylation, and spatial transcriptomics, most tumors will cluster more reproducibly by the four ecological modes than by any residual-gene list.
  2. If tumors are assigned to the covert canonical mode using functional pathway readouts, those tumors will show higher response rates to SHP2/MEK/AKT/RTK-matched therapies than tumors assigned only by partial DNA negativity labels.
  3. If mucinous or NKX2-1-low driver-negative LUADs are systematically tested with broad fusion and enhancer-hijack assays, they will be enriched for ligand-fusion, ERBB-family, or lineage-rewired mechanisms relative to non-mucinous driver-negative LUAD.
  4. If piano/steady-like driver-poor tumors are profiled for telomere length, subclonality, and stemness markers, they will segregate from covert-canonical tumors despite overlapping panel negativity.
  5. If driver-negative LUAD organoids are stratified by immune-ecology versus metabolic-redox mode, checkpoint-modulating interventions will preferentially affect the former while ferroptosis/redox/SRC/glutamine interventions will preferentially affect the latter.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If the four-mode taxonomy is complete, single-cell multi-omics may reveal that apparently distinct residual entities such as NENA, piano, and PP are not separate diseases but stable attractor states connected by rare transition routes.
  2. If lineage truly gates hidden-driver access, forced reprogramming of LUAD organoids from TRU-like to mucinous/NKX2-1-low states will alter which occult fusions or enhancer-rewired events are transforming.
  3. If metabolic-redox and immune modes are mechanistically coupled through ferroptosis and antigen presentation, pharmacologic ferroptosis induction in STK11/KEAP1-rich driver-negative tumors may convert part of the immune-cold class into checkpoint-responsive disease.
  4. If quiescence-derived piano tumors arise years before radiographic disease, matched normal-appearing airway/alveolar tissue from such patients will contain expanded long-telomere, stem-like epithelial fields with partial driver-poor transcriptional programs.

Negative Experiments

  1. Profile a large, prospectively accrued strict driver-negative LUAD cohort across institutions. If the four proposed modes do not emerge reproducibly or do not meaningfully outperform a simpler continuum model, the theory is weakened.
  2. Test whether mode assignment predicts drug response better than mutation-panel negativity, smoking status, TMB, or histology alone. If it does not, the mode framework may be overfit or nonessential.
  3. Evaluate whether mucinous/NKX2-1-low, TRU-like, and piano/steady-like tumors are actually enriched for their predicted hidden-driver classes. Failure of enrichment would falsify the lineage-gates-driver law.
  4. Perturb representative lesions from each mode in matched organoid/PDX systems. If mode-matched vulnerabilities are not preferentially observed, the mode-specific therapeutic law would be undermined.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. A subset of amplification-centered mitotic candidates such as TPX2, WHSC1L1, 20q11.2, and unresolved 22q11 amplicons do not map cleanly into one of the four proposed modes and may represent an orthogonal proliferative-chromosomal branch. [e2364.3] [e2340.1] [e2340.2] [e2367.2]
  2. Some partial-testing cohorts may be dominated by technical false negatives rather than true residual biology, making mode assignment uncertain without deeper profiling. [e2285.0] [e2294.0] [e2293.0] [e2254.0] [e2287.0]
