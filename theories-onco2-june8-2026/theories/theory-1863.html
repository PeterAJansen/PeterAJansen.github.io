Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1863

Name: Hidden-Driver Compression Theory of Oncogene-Negative LUAD

Type: general

Theory Query: Build theories about what drives oncogene negative lung adenocinomas. About 30 percent of lung adenos do not demonstrate readily identifiable drivers in RTK/RAS/RAF pathway genes. Is it rare genes we are missing? Are we missing something at the proteomic level? Is it a ligand? Different pathway? Microbiome? Different biology? What are some novel hypotheses and how can we validate these? Are there parallels in other cancers? Can we categorize driver negative cancers into different subtypes? Will there be therapeutic implications for this, based on the following results.

Description: A large fraction of apparently oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma is not biologically driverless but diagnostically compressed into one bucket by incomplete or mutation-centric assays. The missing drivers fall into recurrent classes: cryptic kinase fusions/rearrangements, noncanonical coding variants outside hotspot domains, exon-skipping/splice lesions, focal or broad copy-number activation of wild-type pathway genes, enhancer/super-enhancer rewiring, and combinatorial loss of negative regulators that converges on MAPK or PI3K signaling. The practical implication is that 'driver-negative' LUAD should be redefined as a hierarchy of residual classes after sequential DNA, RNA, copy-number, structural, and phosphoproteomic interrogation rather than as a single biological entity.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Sequential-resolution law

Statement: As molecular profiling moves from limited hotspot/panel assays to combined WES/WGS, RNA-seq/fusion calling, copy-number analysis, and structural/noncoding interrogation, the apparent frequency of driver-negative LUAD falls substantially because many tumors harbor cryptic but recurrent oncogenic events that are invisible to narrower assays.

Domain/Scope: Applies to lung adenocarcinoma cohorts initially classified as lacking canonical actionable drivers by limited clinical panels, hotspot testing, WES alone, or RTK/RAS/RAF-focused assays; strongest in resected or metastatic human LUAD cohorts subjected to orthogonal DNA/RNA/structural profiling.

Special Cases

  1. A residual subset remains unresolved even after WGS, implying either true noncanonical biology or limits of current assays.
  2. Low tumor purity, stromal contamination, and insufficient RNA quality can create false-negative driver assignments.
  3. Different studies use different 'driver-negative' definitions; frequencies are therefore not directly interchangeable.

Statement 1: Convergent-pathway replacement law

Statement: When canonical RTK/RAS/RAF oncogenes are absent, LUAD can achieve equivalent proliferative signaling by three recurrent replacement modes: structural activation of wild-type lineage or receptor genes, noncanonical coding/splicing lesions in pathway nodes, or combinatorial loss of negative regulators that jointly activate MAPK and/or PI3K signaling.

Domain/Scope: Applies to LUADs classified as oncogene-negative or pan-negative after standard canonical-driver testing; strongest for cohorts with integrated copy-number, fusion, structural, and functional follow-up.

Special Cases

  1. Not all replacement modes are equally recurrent; some are rare but highly actionable.
  2. Some lesions may be maintenance mechanisms rather than initiating lesions.
  3. Some broad gains or low-level amplifications may be passengers unless linked to expression or dependency.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If panel-defined driver-negative LUADs undergo DNA+RNA+copy-number+SV profiling in a prospective cohort, the resolved fraction should exceed that obtained by DNA-only testing, with the largest incremental gains coming from fusion/exon-skipping detection and wild-type-gene activation events.
  2. Among residual WGS-negative tumors, phosphoproteomic pERK/pAKT activation will identify a subgroup enriched for low-PTEN protein, RasGAP loss, or noncoding/structural lesions rather than truly signaling-silent cancers.
  3. Driver-negative tumors with broad 4q12 or focal RTK amplifications will show stronger clinical relevance when copy-number gain is paired with RNA/protein/phosphosite outlier status than when copy number alone is used.
  4. A refined classifier separating cryptic-fusion, splice-driven, enhancer-rewired, lineage-amplified, and RasGAP/PI3K-derepressed classes will predict therapy response better than a binary driver-positive/driver-negative label.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Some of the currently unresolved RPA(-)G tumors will prove to be driven by recurrent noncoding lesions in regulatory regions of Ras/MAPK phosphatases or ligand genes that are only detectable with long-read or single-cell multiome methods.
  2. A subset of apparently driver-negative LUAD may be maintained by stable post-transcriptional or protein-state activation without recurrent DNA events, producing reproducible phosphoproteomic classes that remain invisible even to WGS.
  3. The residual unresolved class may split by cell of origin, with AT2-lineage, basal-like, and mucinous-lineage tumors each favoring different hidden-driver architectures rather than sharing one universal mechanism.

Negative Experiments

  1. If comprehensive DNA/RNA/SV/noncoding profiling in modern prospective driver-negative LUAD cohorts does not materially reduce the unresolved fraction relative to limited panels, the sequential-resolution law would be weakened.
  2. If residual oncogene-negative tumors do not show recurrent pathway convergence at phosphoproteomic or transcriptomic levels, the convergent-pathway replacement law would be weakened.
  3. If wild-type-gene activation events such as NKX2-1 amplification or ERBB2 SE-SV rewiring are not associated with expression/protein dependency in additional cohorts, the replacement architecture framework would be weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The theory does not fully explain tumors whose dominant biology appears immune, metabolic, or stromal rather than RTK/MAPK/PI3K convergent, such as MIF-HIF glycolytic driver-negative LUAD or fibroblast-linked ferroptosis states. [e1848.0] [e1904.0]
  2. The theory does not directly explain non-genetic histologic progression states where no recurrent genetic lesion distinguishes lepidic-to-solid transition. [e1895.0] [e1844.0] [e1844.3]
