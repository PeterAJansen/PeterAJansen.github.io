assay_strategy_and_detection_limits Discovery used paired whole-genome sequencing (WGS) of blood and metastatic liver tumor plus transcriptome sequencing (RNA-seq) from liver metastasis, followed by PCR and Sanger validation of genomic inversion and fusion cDNA. The paper notes that ab initio detection of rearrangements such as translocations/inversions is challenging in WGS alone without specific targets, while transcriptome sequencing helps detect fusion transcripts more easily. It also states that the primary lung cancer DNA came from FFPE tissue with insufficient quality for discovery, so primary comparisons were done using liver metastasis vs blood; the bone metastasis DNA amount was too low for library generation. The authors also caution that many initial RNA-seq fusion calls were likely false positives, so they applied homology, fusion-spanning read, and fusion-point filters. For RNA editing they used conservative criteria and only one tumor-normal pair, limiting sensitivity for missed editing events.
missed_driver_reclassified_after_broader_testing Yes. A tumor initially classified as driver-negative by standard EGFR/KRAS/ALK testing was reclassified after broader WGS + RNA-seq as KIF5B-RET fusion-positive. The rescued alteration was a kinase-preserving KIF5B-RET fusion caused by a 10.6-Mb chromosome 10 inversion; it was missed initially because routine clinical tests only assayed EGFR/KRAS point mutations and EML4-ALK rearrangement, not genome-wide structural variants or RET fusions.

candidate_driver_gene_or_locus KIF5B-RET fusion at chromosome 10, involving KIF5B (10p11.22) and RET proto-oncogene (10q11.21); breakpoints mapped to introns with a validated inversion breakpoint at chr10: 32,351,306-42,931,601 (hg18).
structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism A pericentric inversion brings KIF5B and RET together to create a chimeric transcript. The KIF5B portion contributes a coiled-coil dimerization domain and the active KIF5B promoter context, while the RET portion contributes the tyrosine kinase domain; this enables constitutive dimerization/autophosphorylation and drives overexpression of the RET kinase domain from fusion-derived exons.
functional_consequence Overexpression of the RET kinase domain and spontaneous cellular transformation. RET exons downstream of the breakpoint (exons 12-20) were expressed exclusively from the fusion, and the fusion protein was reported to be capable of transforming cells via activated receptor tyrosine kinase signaling.
co_alteration_or_combinatorial_requirement No cooperating alteration was required or reported for the main driver interpretation. The tumor was notable for lacking EGFR, KRAS, and EML4-ALK alterations; the authors also reported only 10 somatic nonsynonymous SNVs/indels and 10 RNA-editing candidates in the metastatic tumor, which they considered unlikely to be drivers.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment Found in 3 total AK55 tissues/contexts by validation (primary lung, bone metastasis, liver metastasis) but, as a molecular event in the patient, it was present in the major subpopulation of the liver metastasis. In replication, 2 additional KIF5B-RET-positive cases were found among 20 primary lung adenocarcinomas (LC_S2 and LC_S6), i.e. 2/20 = 10% in that small replication cohort. The authors estimate the fusion frequency at about 6% in lung adenocarcinoma overall, with an estimated range of 4.3%-8% from their enrichment calculations, and note TCGA RET overexpression in 3/32 samples (9.4%), while emphasizing other mechanisms may also cause RET overexpression.
functional_or_clinical_validation Validation included PCR and Sanger sequencing of cDNA and genomic DNA, inversion-specific PCR showing the rearrangement in tumor tissues but not blood, and transcriptome evidence of junction-spanning reads and discordant pairs. The fusion was functionally supported by the biology of RET as an oncogene and by a reported spontaneously transforming chimeric RET receptor tyrosine kinase; the paper infers transforming potential from preserved dimerization and kinase domains, but does not present a direct in vitro transformation assay in the text provided. Independent cohort replication found KIF5B-RET in two additional primary lung adenocarcinomas.
