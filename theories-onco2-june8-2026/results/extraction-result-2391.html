Paper Title: Distinct patterns of somatic genome alterations in lung adenocarcinomas and squamous cell carcinomas

Paper Venue: Nature Genetics

Paper TL;DR: Although targeted therapies for lung ADC and SqCC are largely distinct, immunotherapies may aid in treatment for both subtypes.

Paper Abstract: To compare lung adenocarcinoma (ADC) and lung squamous cell carcinoma (SqCC) and to identify new drivers of lung carcinogenesis, we examined the exome sequences and copy number profiles of 660 lung ADC and 484 lung SqCC tumor–normal pairs. Recurrent alterations in lung SqCCs were more similar to those of other squamous carcinomas than to alterations in lung ADCs. New significantly mutated genes included PPP3CA, DOT1L, and FTSJD1 in lung ADC, RASA1 in lung SqCC, and KLF5, EP300, and CREBBP in both tumor types. New amplification peaks encompassed MIR21 in lung ADC, MIR205 in lung SqCC, and MAPK1 in both. Lung ADCs lacking receptor tyrosine kinase–Ras–Raf pathway alterations had mutations in SOS1, VAV1, RASA1, and ARHGAP35. Regarding neoantigens, 47% of the lung ADC and 53% of the lung SqCC tumors had at least five predicted neoepitopes. Although targeted therapies for lung ADC and SqCC are largely distinct, immunotherapies may aid in treatment for both subtypes.

Negativity was established using exome sequencing for SSNVs/indels, SNP6.0 array CNV profiling, and RNA-seq-based fusion analysis, with manual review of additional canonical EGFR/KRAS/ERBB2 calls and complex indels.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver hypothesis is that oncogene-negative LUAD are driven by rare, previously underdetected alterations in the RTK/Ras/Raf axis and related Rho-kinase signaling, rather than by a single universal novel driver. The paper emphasizes low-frequency mutations in pathway members such as SOS1, RASA1, VAV1, and ARHGAP35, plus pathway-level activation that may include an additional unrecognized KRAS-related alteration in tumors remaining negative after deeper testing.

implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway RTK/Ras/Raf signaling with extensions into the Rho kinase pathway; specific implicated genes include SOS1, RASA1, VAV1, ARHGAP35, FGFR1/WHSC1L1, PDGFRA/KIT/KDR, MAPK1, MET, ERBB2, EGFR, KRAS, and NF1.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is genetic and correlative rather than direct phosphoproteomic readout. The authors show enrichment of mutations in known pathway members among oncogene-negative samples, recurrent activating-like mutations in autoinhibitory domains (SOS1 p.N233Y; VAV1 p.S67Y), and pathway-relevant focal amplifications (FGFR1/WHSC1L1, PDGFRA/KIT/KDR, MAPK1) found only in oncogene-negative tumors. No pERK/pAKT/IHC or functional phosphorylation assay is reported for these cases.
driver_negative_subtype An RTK/Ras/Raf-pathway-negative genomic subtype of LUAD, with an implicit further subset of tumors enriched for rare pathway-member mutations and pathway-region amplifications. The paper does not give a formal transcriptomic or histologic subtype label for the negative group.
clinical_or_exposure_context The broader LUAD cohort included both smokers and never smokers; the paper notes that 15% to 25% of lung ADCs still lack a known detectable RTK/Ras/Raf alteration. Smoking history is relevant to mutational signatures, but a specific clinical phenotype uniquely defining oncogene-negative cases is not established. Some pathway events show sex enrichment (e.g., EGFR mutations in females, SMARCA4 in males), but this is not specific to the negative subgroup.
cross_cancer_parallel The authors compare lung SqCC with head and neck squamous cell carcinoma and bladder cancer, and note that Pan-Cancer analysis can reveal pathway alterations across tumor types even when individual drivers differ. For oncogene-negative LUAD, the parallel logic is to other cancers where rare pathway mutations or pathway-state alterations are uncovered by larger cohort analyses.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The therapeutic implication is that oncogene-negative LUAD may still harbor actionable RTK/Ras/Raf-pathway alterations discoverable by deeper genomic interrogation, potentially expanding use of pathway-targeted therapies. The paper specifically links RTK/Ras/Raf alterations to RTK inhibitor responsiveness in known driver-positive LUAD, and suggests that finding additional rare drivers could have treatment relevance; however, this is inferential and not directly validated for the negative tumors.

For the oncogene-negative hypothesis specifically, validation is limited to statistical enrichment and co-mutation/copy-number patterning; no cell-line, organoid, xenograft, or clinical-response validation is presented for the negative subgroup.
