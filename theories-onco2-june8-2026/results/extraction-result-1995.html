brief_description A novel somatic fusion identified in lung adenocarcinoma, joining the first six exons of CD74 to exons encoding the EGF-like domain of NRG1 III-β3. The fusion is recurrent in invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma of never-smokers and acts as an activating lesion rather than a passenger.

driver_negative_definition The study defines driver-negative / pan-negative lung adenocarcinoma as tumors lacking canonical oncogenic alterations after targeted screening and transcriptome-based discovery. Specifically, the validation cohort consisted of 102 adenocarcinomas of never-smokers that were wild-type for EGFR, KRAS, BRAF, ERBB2, ALK, ROS, and RET; the discovery cohort also began with 25 never-smoker lung adenocarcinomas lacking KRAS or EGFR mutations and underwent chromosomal copy-number analysis plus RNA-seq to search for additional oncogenic driver alterations.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver hypothesis is ligand-driven ERBB receptor signaling in a distinct mucinous LUAD subgroup. The paper argues that CD74-NRG1 creates a membrane-tethered NRG1 ligand that activates ERBB2-ERBB3 receptor complexes, leading to PI3K-AKT signaling and oncogenic growth.
alteration_or_activation_mode Somatic gene fusion that produces abnormal expression of a ligand domain rather than kinase activation; specifically, extracellular presentation of the EGF-like domain of NRG1 III-β3 from a CD74 fusion partner. The mechanism is ligand-receptor stimulation of ERBB2/ERBB3 heterodimers.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway CD74-NRG1 / NRG1 III-β3 / ERBB2-ERBB3 axis, with downstream PI3K-AKT and S6K signaling.
pathway_activation_evidence In the index case, ERBB2 and ERBB3 were expressed and phosphorylated, and phospho-ERBB3 was detected specifically in fusion-bearing tumors by immunohistochemistry (5/5 CD74-NRG1-positive invasive mucinous tumors versus 6/241 unselected adenocarcinomas; P < 0.0001). In cell models, ectopic CD74-NRG1 increased p-ERBB2, p-ERBB3, p-AKT, and p-S6K, and the effect depended on the EGF-like domain; AKT activation was also seen in coculture with Ba/F3 cells expressing ERBB2 and ERBB3.
driver_negative_subtype An invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma subtype of lung adenocarcinoma, especially in never-smokers and women, emerges as the explicit subtype enriched for the fusion. The subtype is histology-defined and further supported by recurrent CD74-NRG1 positivity and frequent association with KRAS mutations in the same histologic class.
clinical_or_exposure_context All 5 CD74-NRG1-positive cases were invasive mucinous adenocarcinomas from never-smokers, all women in the validation set shown in the paper. The authors note this subtype frequently presents with multifocal unresectable disease and is otherwise often associated with KRAS mutations.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares this biology to other lung cancers with actionable kinase alterations such as EGFR-, ALK-, ROS1-, and RET-driven tumors, emphasizing a similar oncogene-dependence logic. It also references the broader ERBB signaling network and heregulin-dependent HER2/HER3 signaling as a cancer paradigm.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The fusion is proposed as a therapeutic opportunity for invasive mucinous LUAD. The implied vulnerabilities are ERBB2/ERBB3-targeting drugs and inhibitors of downstream pathways, particularly PI3K-AKT signaling; the paper does not test a drug response directly, so this is preclinical/biological rationale rather than clinical proof.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated through transcriptome sequencing, RT-PCR screening of an independent 102-case pan-negative cohort, and break-apart/fusion FISH plus hybrid-capture genomic sequencing. Functional validation used NIH-3T3 cells, H2052 cells, H322 and H1568 lung cancer cell lines, Ba/F3 cells engineered to express ERBB2 and ERBB3, flow cytometry, immunohistochemistry, Western blotting, and soft-agar colony assays; a truncated fusion lacking the EGF-like domain served as a mechanistic control.
