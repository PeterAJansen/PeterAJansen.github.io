Paper TL;DR: Tumor samples from 3 of 91 patients with lung cancer without known oncogenic alterations assayed by next-generation sequencing or fluorescence in situ hybridization demonstrated evidence of NTRK1 gene fusions.

Paper Abstract: We identified new gene fusions in patients with lung cancer harboring the kinase domain of the NTRK1 gene that encodes the high-affinity nerve growth factor receptor (TRKA protein). Both the MPRIP-NTRK1 and CD74-NTRK1 fusions lead to constitutive TRKA kinase activity and are oncogenic. Treatment of cells expressing NTRK1 fusions with inhibitors of TRKA kinase activity inhibited autophosphorylation of TRKA and cell growth. Tumor samples from 3 of 91 patients with lung cancer (3.3%) without known oncogenic alterations assayed by next-generation sequencing or fluorescence in situ hybridization demonstrated evidence of NTRK1 gene fusions.

driver_negative_definition The paper defines driver-negative / oncogene-negative LUAD as tumors that 'did not contain known genetic alterations using standard clinical assays' and were 'without known oncogenic alterations' by targeted NGS or FISH. Specifically, 36 lung adenocarcinomas lacking known alterations by clinical testing were first subjected to targeted NGS, and 56 additional similar samples were screened by break-apart FISH; across the broader cohort, 3 of 91 lung cancer patients were identified with NTRK1 fusions despite being negative for common/known drivers. The excluded alteration class is broad 'known oncogenic alterations' rather than a single gene list, but the context clearly includes canonical lung cancer drivers such as EGFR and ALK (and related fusion oncogenes), which were absent by standard assays.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver hypothesis is a rare oncogenic gene fusion mechanism: previously unrecognized NTRK1 rearrangements act as the functional driver in tumors that otherwise appear driver-negative. The paper argues these are activating receptor tyrosine kinase fusions that substitute for the missing canonical driver and provide a therapeutically targetable TRKA-dependent state.
alteration_or_activation_mode In-frame gene fusion involving the kinase domain of NTRK1, specifically MPRIP-NTRK1 and CD74-NTRK1. The fusion partners likely promote constitutive dimerization/localization and thereby ligand-independent TRKA kinase activation; the kinase-domain-containing fusion protein is autophosphorylated.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway NTRK1/TRKA signaling, with downstream MAPK/ERK and PI3K/AKT pathway activation. The paper also notes TRK-related phosphorylation sites and, in validation, suppression of pERK and AKT signaling upon inhibitor treatment.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence includes TRKA autophosphorylation in patient-derived CUTO-3 cells and in 293T/NIH3T3/Ba/F3 cells expressing the fusions; phosphorylation at critical TRKA tyrosine residues; increased ERK1/2 and AKT phosphorylation in fusion-expressing cells; high NTRK1 kinase-domain mRNA expression in rearranged tumors; and inhibition of pTRKA, pERK1/2, and MAPK/AKT pathway activation by TRKA inhibitors. The activation is not explained by common canonical lung oncogenic alterations, implying an otherwise hidden driver.
driver_negative_subtype A small molecular subtype of driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma defined by NTRK1 rearrangement. The paper does not assign a formal name beyond NTRK1-rearranged NSCLC/LUAD, but it is an integrated genomics-defined subtype characterized by absent standard clinical drivers, recurrent NTRK1 fusion, and TRKA pathway dependence.
clinical_or_exposure_context The identified cases were lung adenocarcinomas lacking known genetic alterations by routine clinical assays; one index patient with MPRIP-NTRK1 had no standard therapies available and received crizotinib off trial. The paper does not provide a detailed smoking/sex/age phenotype summary for the subtype in the main text, beyond being lung adenocarcinoma cases selected for absence of known oncogenic alterations.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares these fusions to other targetable fusion-driven cancers and to prior examples in lung cancer, including ALK, EGFR, ROS1, RET, and FGFR fusions. It also notes NTRK1 fusions previously reported in colorectal and thyroid cancers, supporting a cross-cancer paradigm of hidden kinase fusions in driver-negative tumors.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The subtype is predicted to be vulnerable to TRKA/TRK kinase inhibition. Preclinical evidence supports sensitivity to the pan-TRK inhibitor ARRY-470 and the multi-kinase inhibitor CEP-701, with weaker activity for crizotinib; clinical off-label crizotinib produced only a minor, transient response in the index patient. The authors conclude that clinical studies of selective TRKA inhibitors in NTRK1-rearranged NSCLC are warranted.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is strong and multi-layered: targeted NGS discovery, RT-PCR and cDNA cloning confirmation, FISH breakpoint validation, quantitative PCR showing elevated NTRK1 kinase-domain expression, expression of fusion constructs in 293T, NIH3T3, and Ba/F3 cells, demonstration of kinase-dead dependence, Ba/F3 IL-3-independent proliferation, NIH3T3 anchorage-independent growth and xenograft tumorigenesis in nude mice, knockdown of TPM3-NTRK1 in KM12 cells reducing proliferation, inhibitor sensitivity assays, and an index-patient crizotinib treatment with minor radiographic/biochemical response.
