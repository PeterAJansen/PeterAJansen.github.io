Paper Abstract: Lung cancer in East Asia is characterized by a high percentage of never-smokers, early onset and predominant EGFR mutations. To illuminate the molecular phenotype of this demographically distinct disease, we performed a deep comprehensive proteogenomic study on a prospectively collected cohort in Taiwan, representing early stage, predominantly female, non-smoking lung adenocarcinoma. Integrated genomic, proteomic, and phosphoproteomic analysis delineated the demographically distinct molecular attributes and hallmarks of tumor progression. Mutational signature analysis revealed age- and gender-related mutagenesis mechanisms, characterized by high prevalence of APOBEC mutational signature in younger females and over-representation of environmental carcinogen-like mutational signatures in older females. A proteomics-informed classification distinguished the clinical characteristics of early stage patients with EGFR mutations. Furthermore, integrated protein network analysis revealed the cellular remodeling underpinning clinical trajectories and nominated candidate biomarkers for patient stratification and therapeutic intervention. This multi-omic molecular architecture may help develop strategies for management of early stage never-smoker lung adenocarcinoma.

driver_negative_definition The paper does not define a formal oncogene-negative panel; rather, it refers to tumors/patients without EGFR activating mutations (EGFR-WT) and, in one proteomic subtype, early-stage patients without EGFR-L858R mutations. Genomic profiling was based on WES, with additional RNA-seq, proteomics, and phosphoproteomics; negative status therefore means absence of the activating EGFR mutations emphasized in the cohort (L858R and exon 19 deletion/Del19), while some analyses also contrast EGFR-WT with EGFR-mutant cases and note a ‘mixed group’ of never-smokers without APOBEC signature, most of whom still had EGFR activating mutation.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden driver logic in driver-negative cases is pathway-state based rather than a single recurrent canonical oncogene: the paper argues that MAPK signaling can be activated in EGFR-WT tumors, and in some EGFR-WT tumors this higher MAPK activity is associated with KRAS mutations. The study also highlights APOBEC-related mutagenesis and carcinogen-associated mutational processes as background drivers of tumor initiation/progression in never-smokers, especially in younger females and in EGFR-WT women.
alteration_or_activation_mode Phosphoproteomic pathway activation with no obvious EGFR activating mutation; in the small EGFR-WT subset with higher MAPK activity, KRAS mutation was present in three of four cases. The broader mechanism is a proteome/phosphoproteome-defined activated signaling state, plus mutational processes (APOBEC and environmental carcinogen-like signatures) rather than a single dominant canonical driver.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway MAPK cascade / EGFR-MEK-ERK axis, with KRAS as a recurrent genetic correlate in some EGFR-WT high-MAPK tumors; the study also connects APOBEC3 family activity, DNA repair, and ERBB/MAPK signaling in carcinogen-signature groups.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence comes from phosphoproteomics and phosphocorrelation analysis: EGFR-pY1197 correlated with MAP2K2-pT394, which correlated with MAPK3-pT198/pT202 and downstream phosphoproteins, and a ranked co-phosphorylation MAPK signature showed that some EGFR-WT cases had high MAPK signaling. The activation is not uniformly genetically explained, because the paper states that MAPK signaling is commonly activated among both EGFR-WT and mutated patients, with different degrees of activation.
driver_negative_subtype A proteomic subtype containing early-stage patients without EGFR-L858R mutation (Subtype 2) is the clearest driver-negative-like subgroup described. More broadly, the paper identifies proteomic subtype 1 as including a mix of late-stage tumors and some early-stage ‘late-like’ tumors, while subtype 3 is early stage and relatively TP53-low; these are integrated proteomic/multi-omic states rather than purely genomic classes.
clinical_or_exposure_context The cohort is mostly never-smokers, predominantly female, East Asian, and early stage (mostly IA/IB). The paper emphasizes age- and sex-related effects, including a younger female EGFR-WT/APOBEC-high group, and notes that EGFR-WT female patients with high APOBEC signature may be better immunotherapy candidates.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares APOBEC mutational signatures to early-onset squamous cell carcinomas, and discusses immunotherapy response patterns in EGFR/ALK-driven non-small cell lung cancer as a contrast. It also notes that TP53-mutant tumors may exhibit synthetic-lethal vulnerability akin to other cancers where TP53-PARP-MCM chromatin-axis biology has been studied.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication For driver-negative or EGFR-WT-like cases, the paper suggests APOBEC-high, young EGFR-WT females may be candidates for immunotherapy. It also implies that pathway-state assessment could guide treatment beyond canonical driver testing. For EGFR-WT high-MAPK cases, the pathway implies MAPK-targeted logic, but the paper does not test a MAPK inhibitor. More generally, the study proposes biomarker-driven patient stratification and notes possible therapeutic intervention for protein-network-defined targets.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is primarily multi-cohort and correlative: the TW cohort is analyzed by WES/RNA-seq/proteomics/phosphoproteomics, and the EGFR-L858R versus Del19 outcome difference is validated in an independent retrospective cohort of 209 resected patients; APOBEC-high association with prolonged survival is also compared to TCGA and an immunotherapy cohort. There are no direct knockdown, overexpression, organoid, GEMM, or inhibitor perturbation experiments for driver-negative mechanisms in this paper.
