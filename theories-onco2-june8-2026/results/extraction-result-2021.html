Paper Abstract: Cancer evolution determines molecular and morphological intra-tumor heterogeneity and challenges the design of effective treatments. In lung adenocarcinoma, disease progression and prognosis are associated with the appearance of morphologically diverse tumor regions, termed histologic patterns. However, the link between molecular and histological features remains elusive. Here, we generated multi-omics and spatially resolved molecular profiles of histologic patterns from primary lung adenocarcinoma, which we integrated with molecular data from >2,000 patients. The transition from indolent to aggressive patterns was not driven by genetic alterations but by epigenetic and transcriptional reprogramming reshaping cancer cell identity. A signature quantifying this transition was an independent predictor of patient prognosis in multiple human cohorts. Within individual tumors, highly multiplexed protein spatial profiling revealed co-existence of immune desert, inflamed, and excluded regions, which matched histologic pattern composition. Our results provide a detailed molecular map of lung adenocarcinoma intra-tumor spatial heterogeneity, tracing non-genetic routes of cancer evolution.

Across TCGA, East Asian, and multi-region CHUV samples, no recurrent mutation, copy-number alteration, or gene fusion was enriched in a specific pattern, aside from limited PIK3CA mutations in lepidic samples and a trend toward more TP53 mutations in solid samples. The authors also tested candidate weak drivers and pathway-converging alterations without finding robust cross-dataset hits.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main proposed mechanism is non-genetic evolution via epigenetic and transcriptional reprogramming that reshapes cancer cell identity and tumor microenvironment. The paper emphasizes lineage-state plasticity rather than a single hidden oncogenic driver: lepidic resembles a differentiated, normal lung-like state, while solid reflects a proliferative, dedifferentiated state with immune exclusion/suppression.
alteration_or_activation_mode Epigenetic silencing of lepidic-associated transcriptional regulators and markers, coupled with broad transcriptional reprogramming toward proliferation and invasion programs. The mode is not a rare mutation or amplification-driven activation, but a progressive change in expression and DNA methylation state; lepidic master regulators and markers showed higher promoter methylation in solid samples.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Cell differentiation and development programs versus cell-cycle/proliferation programs; master transcriptional regulators included E2F transcription factors, MCM complex components, and FOXM1 on the solid side, and CRY2, ZBTB4, CASZ1, and WWC1 on the lepidic side. Functional categories also included extracellular matrix remodeling, MMP genes, collagen genes, and immune infiltration markers.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence came from concordant RNA-seq and DNA methylation differences across TCGA, EAS, and CHUV cohorts, plus single-cell RNA-seq-derived state scores and CRISPR knockout screening support for selected regulators. Solid-pattern regions showed higher expression of proliferation genes, MMPs, and immune infiltration/exclusion features, while lepidic regions showed differentiation/morphogenesis signatures and normal lung markers; these differences were not attributed to recurrent genetic alterations.
driver_negative_subtype An implicit pattern-defined subtype structure was proposed: lepidic, papillary, acinar, and solid are not independent molecular classes but rather a continuum from lepidic to solid, with papillary and acinar as intermediate states. This subtype structure is histology-based but supported by transcriptomic and epigenetic data, plus spatial immune phenotypes (immune desert, inflamed, excluded/suppressive).
clinical_or_exposure_context Pattern progression correlated with prognosis, with solid-prevalent tumors associated with poor prognosis and lepidic with less aggressive disease. The work also notes higher TMB in solid-prevalent tumors and early-stage intra-tumor pattern heterogeneity as clinically important for deciding who may need adjuvant therapy; the cohort includes primary early-stage LUAD and external validation in multiple human cohorts, including East Asian ancestry and >2,000 total LUAD samples.
cross_cancer_parallel The discussion explicitly compares morphological progression in lung adenocarcinoma with similar histologic evolution reported in breast cancer and hepatocellular carcinoma, suggesting that histopathology-guided multi-region sampling and spatial profiling could generalize to other cancers.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The main therapeutic implication is biomarker-guided patient stratification using the L2S signature and histologic pattern composition, including potential use to identify patients likely to progress or relapse and to select adjuvant treatment. The paper specifically highlights immunotherapy relevance: solid regions are immune-excluded/suppressive, acinar regions are inflamed and TLS-rich, and the authors propose testing associations between pattern composition/L2S and response to immune checkpoint inhibitors in adjuvant and neoadjuvant settings.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated using multi-omics and spatially resolved profiling of histology-guided multi-region samples from 10 primary LUADs (CHUV cohort), plus independent validation in TCGA, an East Asian cohort, another LUAD cohort, and >2,000 scored samples across 10 cohorts. Additional support came from single-cell RNA-seq, CRISPR knockout screening, multi-color immunofluorescence, and digital spatial profiling (NanoString GeoMX).
