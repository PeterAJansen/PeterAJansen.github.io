driver_negative_definition The paper defines the relevant driver-negative group as lung adenocarcinomas that are negative for kinase fusions detected by integrated histopathology-based screening and molecular assays, specifically ALK, ROS1, and RET fusions. In the full cohort, all 71 kinase-fusion-positive tumors were excluded from this category; these tumors were also negative for EGFR and KRAS mutations. Detection/confirmation used ALK immunohistochemistry, multiplex RT-PCR, ROS1 and RET split/fusion FISH, 5' RACE or 3' RACE for fusion partner identification, and fusion-specific RT-PCR.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The paper does not propose a specific alternative hidden driver for the fusion-negative tumors. Instead, it frames the main finding as the discovery that a substantial fraction of adenocarcinomas previously considered driver-negative actually harbor targetable kinase fusions (ALK, ROS1, RET), implying that remaining truly fusion-negative tumors may still have unidentified oncogenic alterations.
alteration_or_activation_mode Rare oncogenic fusion/rearrangement activating receptor or nonreceptor kinases; in the negative group, no ALK/ROS1/RET fusion and no EGFR or KRAS mutation were detected.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway ALK, ROS1, and RET kinase signaling axis; the paper also references EGFR and KRAS as canonical excluded drivers. No specific downstream pathway is experimentally mapped in the fusion-negative subset.
pathway_activation_evidence For the negative subset, the paper provides absence-of-evidence rather than pathway activation evidence: tumors were negative for ALK, ROS1, and RET fusion calls by IHC/FISH/RT-PCR and negative for EGFR/KRAS mutations. The paper does not report pERK, pAKT, pS6, expression signatures, or phosphoproteomic evidence in the driver-negative tumors.
driver_negative_subtype No molecular subtype of driver-negative LUAD is explicitly defined. The paper implicitly separates adenocarcinomas into kinase-fusion-positive versus kinase-fusion-negative groups, with the latter associated with worse prognosis but not otherwise subclassified by genomics, transcriptomics, or histology.
clinical_or_exposure_context Negative kinase-fusion status was one of four independent poor-prognosis factors in adenocarcinoma, alongside age ≥50 years, male sex, and high pathological stage. The kinase-fusion-positive tumors were enriched in younger patients, low smoking index, small tumor size, and mucinous cribriform histology, implying the remaining negative tumors are relatively less associated with these features. The paper also notes that more than 50% of younger lung adenocarcinoma patients may benefit from molecularly matched kinase inhibitors when both EGFR-mutant and kinase-fusion-positive cases are considered.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper compares lung adenocarcinoma kinase fusions to papillary thyroid carcinoma, where RET fusions are established and enriched in radiation-associated disease, and to secretory breast carcinoma, which harbors an ETV6-NTRK3 fusion and shows a cribriform secretory pattern. These comparisons support the idea of hidden kinase drivers and morphology-linked fusion biology across cancers.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The therapeutic implication is that many tumors previously thought to be driver-negative are actually targetable if fusion testing is performed; corresponding kinase inhibitors may benefit these patients. For RET fusions specifically, the paper tests vandetanib preclinically and shows inhibition of KIF5B-RET-transformed Ba/F3 cells, whereas crizotinib was ineffective against KIF5B-RET in this assay. No targeted therapy is proposed for the truly fusion-negative subset beyond the implication that additional molecular diagnostics are needed.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is strong for the fusion-positive/negative classification and indirect for the concept of hidden drivers: tissue microarray screening of 1,528 lung cancers, confirmatory FISH and RT-PCR/RACE, comparison with 324 positive/negative controls, and clinicopathologic and multivariate survival analysis in 1,116 adenocarcinomas. Functional validation of the discovered drivers uses mouse 3T3 fibroblast transformation assays, nude mouse tumorigenicity assays, and Ba/F3 growth-dependence/drug-sensitivity assays. No dedicated functional validation is performed on the fusion-negative tumors themselves.
