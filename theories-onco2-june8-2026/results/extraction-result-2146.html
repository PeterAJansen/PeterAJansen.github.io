oncogene_negative_definition The index tumor (AK55) and replication tumors were defined as oncogene-negative for the canonical lung adenocarcinoma drivers tested in the clinic and/or study: EGFR mutation-negative (exons 18-21 assessed by PCR/DNA sequencing), KRAS mutation-negative (exon 2 assessed by PCR/DNA sequencing), and EML4-ALK fusion-negative (assessed by FISH in pathologic studies). assay_strategy_and_detection_limits Discovery used paired whole-genome sequencing (WGS) of blood and liver metastasis plus transcriptome sequencing (RNA-seq) of liver metastasis, with cross-validation by PCR and Sanger sequencing on genomic DNA and cDNA; primary lung and bone metastasis were also tested by PCR/Sanger. The authors note that ab initio detection of chromosomal rearrangements such as translocations/inversions is still challenging in WGS alone without specific targets, and that transcriptome sequencing helps detect fusion genes more easily. They also state that many candidate intrachromosomal adjacent-gene fusions and one interchromosomal fusion were likely nonfunctional; only KIF5B-RET had supporting genomic rearrangement in WGS. The primary lung cancer DNA was FFPE-derived and had too high redundancy for discovery analysis, so liver metastasis versus blood was the main discovery comparison.
missed_driver_reclassified_after_broader_testing Yes. A tumor initially classified as negative for EGFR, KRAS, and EML4-ALK was reclassified as driven by KIF5B-RET after broader/deeper testing with combined WGS and RNA-seq, followed by PCR and Sanger validation. It was likely missed initially because the canonical diagnostic assays did not interrogate this fusion/rearrangement and because the key alteration was a structural inversion rather than a point mutation or the known EML4-ALK fusion.
driver_event_class Complex structural rearrangement / kinase fusion (inversion-generated fusion transcript)
candidate_driver_gene_or_locus KIF5B-RET fusion involving KIF5B at 10p11.22 and RET at 10q11.21; breakpoint region chr10:32,351,306-42,931,601 (hg18) with a 10.6-Mb inversion.
structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism A pericentric inversion creates an in-frame fusion that places the KIF5B coiled-coil dimerization domain upstream of the RET kinase domain, with the active KIF5B promoter driving expression of the chimeric transcript. The coiled-coil domain promotes homo-dimerization and RET autophosphorylation, activating the kinase.
functional_consequence Overexpression of chimeric RET receptor tyrosine kinase and spontaneous cellular transformation potential; RNA-seq showed exclusive expression of RET exons downstream of the breakpoint, indicating that most RET signal in the tumor derives from the fusion rather than native RET.
co_alteration_or_combinatorial_requirement No cooperating driver alteration was required or reported for the main mechanism. The tumor lacked EGFR, KRAS, and EML4-ALK alterations, and the fusion alone was presented as the likely driver in this oncogene-negative context. The paper notes a single-base-pair deletion near the breakpoint, suggesting error-prone DNA repair (e.g., NHEJ/FoSTeS/MMBIR) may have contributed mechanistically, but not as a co-driver.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment Found in 1 index case (AK55) and 2 additional primary lung adenocarcinomas in replication, for 3 total KIF5B-RET-positive cases across 21 primary lung adenocarcinomas analyzed in the replication study context (reported also as 2/20 in the replication study and overall ~6% estimate, with an estimated range of 4.3%-8% based on triple-negative and double-negative subsets). The paper also notes RET overexpression in 3/32 TCGA samples (9.4%), though this was not all proven to be KIF5B-RET.
functional_or_clinical_validation Validated by genomic inversion-specific PCR and Sanger sequencing in cancer tissues (primary lung, liver metastasis, bone metastasis) but not blood; validated fusion transcript by cDNA PCR and Sanger sequencing; transcriptome evidence included 34 discordant paired-end reads and 60 spanning reads; independent replication found the fusion in LC_S2 and LC_S6 among additional primary lung adenocarcinomas; the authors interpret the fusion as transforming based on RET oncogene biology and preserved dimerization/kinase domains, though no in vivo or drug-response assay was reported in this paper.
