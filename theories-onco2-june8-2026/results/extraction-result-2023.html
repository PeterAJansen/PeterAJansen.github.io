Paper Title: Proteogenomic characterization reveals therapeutic vulnerabilities in lung adenocarcinoma

Paper Venue: Cell

Paper TL;DR: Comprehensive proteogenomic characterization of 110 tumors and 101 matched normal adjacent tissues (NATs) incorporating genomics, epigenomics, deep-scale proteomics, phosphoproteomics and acetylproteomics identified therapeutic vulnerabilities associated with driver events involving KRAS, EGFR and ALK.

Paper Abstract: Summary To explore the biology of lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD) and identify new therapeutic opportunities, we performed comprehensive proteogenomic characterization of 110 tumors and 101 matched normal adjacent tissues (NATs) incorporating genomics, epigenomics, deep-scale proteomics, phosphoproteomics and acetylproteomics. Multi-omics clustering revealed four subgroups defined by key driver mutations, country and gender. Proteomic and phosphoproteomic data illuminated biology downstream of copy number aberrations, somatic mutations and fusions, and identified therapeutic vulnerabilities associated with driver events involving KRAS, EGFR and ALK. Immune subtyping revealed a complex landscape, reinforced the association of STK11 with immune-cold behavior and underscored a potential immunosuppressive role of neutrophil degranulation. Smoking-associated LUADs showed correlation with other environmental exposure signatures and a field effect in NATs. Matched NATs allowed identification of differentially expressed proteins with potential diagnostic and therapeutic utility. This proteogenomics dataset represents a unique public resource for researchers and clinicians seeking to better understand and treat lung adenocarcinomas.

driver_negative_definition The paper frames driver-negative LUAD as tumors that 'lack known or currently targetable mutations' and, more specifically in its multi-omics clustering, as samples not enriched for canonical drivers such as EGFR mutations, ALK fusions, KRAS mutations, or STK11 mutations. The data used to define this were WES/WGS for somatic mutation and fusion detection, RNA-seq/miRNA-seq, DNA methylation, and deep proteomics/phosphoproteomics/acetylproteomics; 'negative' means no known key driver alteration detected in the tested canonical drivers/fusion classes, not absence of all oncogenic alterations.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver theme is that pathway activation state, rather than an obvious recurrent mutation, can define biologically important subtypes. The paper especially emphasizes proteomic/phosphoproteomic outlier activity, immune-context programs, smoking-related exposure programs, and microenvironmental or lineage-state differences as mechanisms that may underlie tumors lacking canonical drivers.
alteration_or_activation_mode Predominantly outlier pathway activation or suppression detected at the protein/phosphosite level, sometimes without corresponding RNA change or obvious genomic alteration. The paper also considers rare kinase fusions, copy-number-driven trans effects, methylation-driven cis effects, and tumor microenvironment-associated pathway states as possible mechanisms.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Most prominently: RAS/RAF/RTK signaling broadly, SHP2/PTPN11, SOS1/KRAS, ALK fusion signaling, KEAP1/NFE2L2 (NRF2), STK11-associated immune-cold/neutrophil degranulation programs, and in smoker/non-smoker context Rho GTPase signaling via ARHGEF5/SRGAP1. The paper also points to pathway-level programs such as cell cycle, MAPK1/MAPK3 signaling, glycolysis, complement/clotting, IFNG axis, and antioxidant stress response.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence comes primarily from proteomics and phosphoproteomics: extreme phosphosite outliers, pathway enrichment, and PTM-SEA/ssGSEA signatures. Examples include ALK Y1507 phosphosite outlier staining by IHC, EGFR-mutant tumors with highly significant PTPN11/SHP2 Y62 phosphorylation without RNA/protein change, KRAS-mutant tumors with SOS1 S1161 phosphorylation, KEAP1 mutants with increased NFE2L2 S215/S433 phosphorylation, and STK11-mutant tumors with proteomic neutrophil degranulation despite no RNA-level signal. Some of these are genetically explained, while others are described as phosphorylation-state changes not captured by RNA or by the known canonical alteration.
driver_negative_subtype Implicitly, the most relevant subtype is the terminal respiratory unit / C4-like and proximal-proliferative/proximal-inflammatory multi-omics structure, which subdivides the proteogenomic landscape beyond canonical driver status. The paper also proposes an 'immune-cold' STK11-mutant state and a smoking/exposure-linked phenotype; however, it does not name a single dedicated 'driver-negative' subtype.
clinical_or_exposure_context The cohort includes many never-smokers and patients of diverse geography and sex, which is important because LUADs lacking canonical drivers are often enriched in never-smokers in general. The paper particularly links STK11 mutation to immune-cold behavior, and in non-smokers highlights ARHGEF5/Rho GTPase signaling as a potential oncogenic mechanism. Smoking score, country of origin, sex, and ethnicity are repeatedly noted as correlates of subtype structure.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper does not give a dedicated cross-cancer model for driver-negative LUAD, but it repeatedly references proteogenomic principles established in other cancers and notes that SHP2, SOS1, and NRF2-axis logic are broadly relevant across cancer biology. It also compares immune hot/cold logic and pathway-state biomarkers to established immuno-oncology paradigms.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications include SHP2/PTPN11 inhibition for EGFR-mutant and ALK-fusion tumors, SOS1 inhibition for KRAS-mutant tumors, anti-CTLA4 therapy and IDO1 inhibition for immune-hot tumors, and potential targeting of ARHGEF5 in never-smokers. The paper also suggests mutation-stratified management of venous thromboembolism and notes several outlier kinases with FDA-approved or investigational inhibitors.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is mostly proteogenomic and computational: association analyses across 110 tumors/101 NATs, independent component analysis, pathway enrichment, outlier analyses, and IHC confirmation for ALK Y1507. There is no direct functional perturbation in LUAD model systems in this paper for the hidden-driver hypotheses; the authors explicitly present many as hypothesis-generating, though they cite preclinical and clinical evidence from the literature for SHP2 and SOS1 targeting.
