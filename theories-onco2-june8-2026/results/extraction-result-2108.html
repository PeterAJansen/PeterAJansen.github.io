structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism Oncogenic Kras activation combined with reduced dosage of the lung lineage transcription factor Nkx2-1, resulting in loss of NKX2-1-mediated repression of mucin and tumor-promoting transcriptional programs. functional_consequence Increased lung tumor number and volume, invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma histology, goblet-cell differentiation, mucin production (including MUC5AC/MUC5B), and loss of NKX2-1 staining in tumor cells.

The converse context was also observed: NKX2-1 suppressed Kras-driven tumorigenesis but enhanced EGFRL858R-driven progression.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment Human mucinous adenocarcinoma of the lung is reported in the paper to be associated with KRAS mutations in approximately 76% of cases and rarely with EGFR mutations; however, the specific Nkx2-1 haploinsufficient combination was modeled in mice and not given a human frequency in this paper.
functional_or_clinical_validation Validated by transgenic mouse tumorigenesis, microCT tumor burden, histology, Alcian blue and mucin immunostaining, regression after Dox withdrawal, and in vitro colony-formation/AP-1 assays. The study also showed that NKX2-1 expression suppresses urethane- and KrasG12D-induced tumor formation and that NKX2-1 binds and represses mucinous tumor genes.
