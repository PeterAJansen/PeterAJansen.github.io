Paper Abstract: SUMMARY RTK/RAS/RAF pathway alterations (RPAs) are a hallmark of lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD). In this study, we use whole-genome sequencing (WGS) of 85 cases found to be RPA(–) by previous studies from The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) to characterize the minority of LUADs lacking apparent alterations in this pathway. We show that WGS analysis uncovers RPA(+) in 28 (33%) of the 85 samples. Among the remaining 57 cases, we observe focal deletions targeting the promoter or transcription start site of STK11 (n = 7) or KEAP1 (n = 3), and promoter mutations associated with the increased expression of ILF2 (n = 6). We also identify complex structural variations associated with high-level copy number amplifications. Moreover, an enrichment of focal deletions is found in TP53 mutant cases. Our results indicate that RPA(–) cases demonstrate tumor suppressor deletions and genome instability, but lack unique or recurrent genetic lesions compensating for the lack of RPAs. Larger WGS studies of RPA(–) cases are required to understand this important LUAD subset.

driver_negative_definition The paper defines negativity as absence of previously cataloged RTK/RAS/RAF pathway-activating alterations on initial WES and RNA-seq (RPA(–)E), using a broad predefined list of canonical oncogenic events. Specifically, cases were considered negative if they lacked activating mutations in KRAS, EGFR, BRAF, ERBB2, MET, RIT1, NRAS, RAF1, HRAS, ARAF, MAP2K1, and SOS1; loss-of-function mutations in NF1 and RASA1; fusions in ALK, ROS1, RET, MET, and NTRK2; and amplification in EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, MET, FGFR1, and MAPK1. WGS was then used to re-test negativity, and amplification required overexpression (z-score > 1.96) to count as oncogenic in the WGS reclassification step.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main proposed biology is not a single recurrent hidden oncogene, but a heterogeneous combination of tumor suppressor loss, genome instability, and structural variation; the authors also nominate ILF2 promoter mutation/overexpression as a candidate non-coding driver. They explicitly speculate that frequent loss of tumor suppressors such as STK11, and amplification of downstream effectors such as MYC, may cooperate to replace canonical RTK/RAS/RAF signaling, while also noting that a small subset may still be RTK/RAS/RAF-driven through undetected mechanisms.
alteration_or_activation_mode Mixed: focal promoter/transcription start-site deletions (STK11, KEAP1), coding mutations (TP53, STK11, KEAP1, SMARCA4), non-coding promoter mutations with increased expression (ILF2), and complex structural variants/high-level amplifications (double minutes, BFBC, tyfonas, pyrgo) affecting oncogenes such as NKX2-1, EGFR, MAPK1, UBL3, SOX21, and LIG4. The paper also discusses possible epigenetic dysregulation or less frequent pathway lesions as alternative explanations but does not prove them.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway RTK/RAS/RAF-MAPK signaling remains the reference axis, but the paper implicates tumor suppressor axes STK11/LKB1, KEAP1, TP53, NF1, and RASA1; oncogenic candidates include ILF2, MYC, NKX2-1, EGFR, MAPK1, SOS1, ARAF, and NRG1. The strongest recurrent non-coding candidate is ILF2, with promoter mutations linked to increased expression and higher SV burden.
pathway_activation_evidence Pathway activation is largely inferred genetically rather than measured by phosphoproteomics or IHC. Evidence includes rescue of 28/85 initially negative cases by WGS revealing canonical RTK/RAS/RAF alterations, overexpression accompanying amplifications of EGFR, MAPK1, ARAF, SOS1, and NRG1 fusions, and decreased expression after focal deletions of negative regulators NF1, RASA1, STK11, and KEAP1. No direct pERK/pAKT/pS6 readouts or functional kinase assays are reported in this paper.
driver_negative_subtype The paper proposes that the remaining RPA(–)G cases represent a heterogeneous RTK/RAS/RAF-independent LUAD subset characterized by high TP53 mutation frequency, elevated tumor mutation burden, recurrent focal deletions of tumor suppressors, recurrent ILF2 promoter mutations, and abundant complex structural variation. This is an integrated genomics-defined subtype rather than a transcriptomic or proteomic subtype.
clinical_or_exposure_context Recent smokers were enriched in the RPA(–)G group compared with RPA(+) cases, although tobacco mutational signature 4 was not different after purity adjustment. The paper also notes known clinical relevance of STK11 and KEAP1 alterations as prognostic and/or predictive biomarkers, and emphasizes that low tumor purity can contribute to false-negative driver detection.
cross_cancer_parallel The authors compare ILF2 amplification in LUAD to multiple myeloma, where ILF2 promotes tolerance of genomic instability and resistance to DNA-damaging therapy. They also compare RPA(–) biology conceptually to other cancers with rare RTK/RAS/RAF alterations, early TP53 loss, high TMB, chromosome instability, and different evolutionary paths.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications are mostly inferential and biomarker-oriented. The paper highlights that missed KRAS p.G12C calls matter because KRAS G12C inhibitors are clinically relevant, and it suggests that accurate detection of STK11 and KEAP1 alterations may become necessary for prognostic/predictive stratification. It also notes that ILF2 upregulation has been linked in myeloma to resistance to DNA-damaging therapies, but no therapy is tested here for LUAD.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is genomic and transcriptomic rather than functional: WGS rescued hidden drivers in 28/85 cases; WES re-review confirmed most KRAS calls when WGS prompted reinspection; RNA-seq/exon-junction evidence supported focal deletions in STK11, KEAP1, and TP53; SCISSOR confirmed transcript shape changes consistent with exon deletions; and expression analyses linked ILF2 promoter mutations to higher ILF2 expression. There are no cell-line, organoid, xenograft, GEMM, or drug-sensitivity experiments in this paper.
