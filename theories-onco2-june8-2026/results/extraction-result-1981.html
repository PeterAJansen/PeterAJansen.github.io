Paper Abstract: SUMMARY Lung adenocarcinoma, the most common subtype of non-small cell lung cancer, is responsible for over 500,000 deaths per year worldwide. Here, we report exome and genome sequences of 183 lung adenocarcinoma tumor/normal DNA pairs. These analyses revealed a mean exonic somatic mutation rate of 12.0 events/megabase and identified the majority of genes previously reported as significantly mutated in lung adenocarcinoma. In addition, we identified statistically recurrent somatic mutations in the splicing factor gene U2AF1 and truncating mutations affecting RBM10 and ARID1A. Analysis of nucleotide context-specific mutation signatures grouped the sample set into distinct clusters that correlated with smoking history and alterations of reported lung adenocarcinoma genes. Whole genome sequence analysis revealed frequent structural re-arrangements, including in-frame exonic alterations within EGFR and SIK2 kinases. The candidate genes identified in this study are attractive targets for biological characterization and therapeutic targeting of lung adenocarcinoma.

driver_negative_definition Implicitly, driver-negative or oncogene-negative LUAD refers to tumors for which the study could not assign a hallmark alteration via the 25-gene hallmark mapping or detect a mutation in a known driver gene. The paper states that 15% of tumors did not have a single hallmark alteration and that the study did not identify a mutated oncogene in every tumor sample; it also notes that several important but rarely mutated genes (AKT1, ERBB2, NRAS, HRAS) could not be statistically nominated in this cohort. Driver assignment used exome/genome sequencing plus SNP array copy-number analysis, with strict criteria: dominant genes were considered activated by recurrent COSMIC hotspot variants or high-level focal amplification, and recessive genes were considered inactivated by truncating mutation, compound missense, hemizygous missense, or homozygous loss.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver hypothesis is that some apparently driver-negative LUADs are explained by novel genes and by an epigenetic/RNA deregulation program rather than only by the canonical proliferative-signaling oncogenes. The discussion specifically proposes a putative '11th hallmark' of epigenetic and RNA deregulation, supported by recurrent alterations in U2AF1, ARID1A, RBM10, SETD2, and BRD3.
alteration_or_activation_mode For the hidden-driver axis, the paper emphasizes recurrent missense/splice-site/truncating mutations and rearrangements, plus copy-number alterations, rather than a single recurrent fusion or hotspot. The proposed mechanism in driver-negative tumors is therefore a mixture of rare coding mutations, loss-of-function events in RNA/chromatin regulators, and unrecognized alterations not captured by the tested hallmark set.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Epigenetic and RNA deregulation; spliceosome/RNA-binding factors (U2AF1, RBM10, SF3B1, U2AF2, PRPF40B) and chromatin remodeling/epigenetic regulators (ARID1A, SETD2, BRD3, SMARCA4). The paper also highlights the usual proliferative pathways as incompletely represented in these tumors (EGFR, ERBB2, MYC, KRAS, BRAF, PIK3CA, PTEN).
pathway_activation_evidence There is no direct phosphoproteomic or IHC evidence presented for a specific activated pathway in the driver-negative subset. Instead, the paper infers pathway gaps from genomics: many tumors lacked a mutation supporting sustained proliferative signaling, and a majority lacked a genetic explanation for invasion/metastasis or angiogenesis; the proposed epigenetic/RNA deregulation hallmark is based on recurrent somatic mutations in relevant genes.
driver_negative_subtype No formal molecular subtype label is assigned to driver-negative LUAD. The closest explicit subtype structure in the paper is the 5 mutation-spectrum clusters, especially Cluster 1 (low mutation rate; enriched in never/light smokers) and Cluster 4 (enriched in advanced stage), but these are mutational signatures rather than driver-negative subtypes. The paper also proposes a functional subtype-like class of tumors with epigenetic and RNA deregulation.
clinical_or_exposure_context Driver-negative/low-hallmark tumors are indirectly associated with never/light smoking status because Cluster 1 is enriched in never and light smokers and EGFR-mutant tumors correlate with that cluster. More broadly, the cohort included 27 never smokers, 17 light smokers, and 118 heavy smokers; low-mutation burden tumors were much more common in never smokers than smokers.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares its gene-hallmark framework to other cancer genome studies and notes that epigenetic and splicing regulator mutations are also seen in other cancers. It cites myelodysplastic syndrome for U2AF1 and broader cancer genome scans for epigenetic drivers, suggesting a cross-cancer parallel for RNA/splicing and chromatin-state dysregulation.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The paper's therapeutic implication is general rather than directly actionable for driver-negative LUAD: the authors argue that uncovering additional genes and hallmark mechanisms should guide diagnosis and treatment, and they specifically highlight that sustaining proliferative signaling is the most therapeutically targetable hallmark. For the newly identified EGFR deletion, they show erlotinib sensitivity, but they do not establish a specific therapy for the driver-negative/epigenetic-RNA-deregulated group.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is strongest for the genomics and for the novel EGFR deletion, not for a driver-negative subtype per se. The study validates mutations by WES/WGS cross-comparison and ultra-deep targeted resequencing, confirms genic fusions by PCR and Illumina sequencing, and functionally validates the EGFR exon 25/26 deletion in NIH-3T3 soft-agar assays, immunoblotting for EGFR/AKT phosphorylation, and Ba/F3 IL-3-independent growth plus erlotinib inhibition. The driver-negative hypothesis itself is inference from cohort-level genomic gaps and recurrence statistics.
