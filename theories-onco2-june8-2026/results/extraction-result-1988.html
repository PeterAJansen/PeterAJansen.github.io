name_short NKX2-1/TTF1
name_full NKX2-1 (thyroid transcription factor 1, TTF-1)
brief_description A lung lineage master regulator that is expressed in most lung adenocarcinomas, but is lost in a subset of poorly differentiated or mucinous tumors. In this paper, its loss is shown to trigger a shift from pulmonary identity to a latent gastric differentiation program in both tumors and normal adult lung epithelium.
citation_title Nkx2-1 Represses a Latent Gastric Differentiation Program in Lung Adenocarcinoma
mention_or_use use
driver_negative_definition The paper does not define 'oncogene-negative' or 'driver-negative' lung adenocarcinoma in the modern broad genomic sense; instead, it studies Nkx2-1-negative / NKX2-1-negative lung adenocarcinoma as a lineage-defined subset. Operationally, tumors are classified as negative by loss of NKX2-1 protein by IHC and/or engineered deletion of Nkx2-1 in Kras-driven mouse models; the human comparator set is NKX2-1-negative mucinous lung adenocarcinomas (11/37 human LUADs were NKX2-1-negative mucinous cases in the IHC panel). The study focuses on tumors lacking this lineage determinant rather than excluding specific oncogenic drivers such as KRAS, since many of the negative tumors still carry KRAS activation.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main proposed hidden driver is not a canonical oncogene but a lineage-state mechanism: loss of NKX2-1 releases a latent gastric differentiation program and allows gastric transcriptional circuitry to dominate. The paper further links tumor growth effects to downstream differentiation-state changes and to Hnf4α-dependent repression of the embryonal proto-oncogene Hmga2, suggesting that a co-opted differentiation program and loss of lineage restraint are central to the biology.
alteration_or_activation_mode Combinatorial loss of lineage control: Nkx2-1 deletion or downregulation (with intact KRAS signaling in many models) plus, in the more primitive state, additional loss of Hnf4α-mediated gastric differentiation control. This is framed as a transcriptional/epigenetic reprogramming event rather than a rare mutation or amplification.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Nkx2-1–Foxa1/Foxa2–Hnf4α axis controlling pulmonary versus gastric lineage state; downstream gastric markers include Gkn1, Gkn3, Vsig1, Ctse, Muc5AC, and Pdx1. For tumor progression, the paper also implicates de-repression of Hmga2 when Hnf4α is lost in the Nkx2-1-negative context.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is based on transcriptomic changes, IHC, ChIP-Seq, and functional perturbation rather than phosphoproteomics. After Nkx2-1 loss, GI/gastric genes are rapidly induced, lung markers (Sftpa1/Sftpb/Sftpc, proSPC, CCSP) decline, mucin markers accumulate, and human NKX2-1-negative mucinous tumors show diffuse GKN1 and CTSE expression. ChIP-Seq shows Nkx2-1 binds active promoters/enhancers of pulmonary genes and is required for proper Foxa1/2 localization; loss of Nkx2-1 causes de novo Foxa1/2 binding at gastric loci. The activation state is therefore genetically/epigenetically driven, not inferred from kinase markers.
driver_negative_subtype A mucinous, gastric-like lung adenocarcinoma subtype. It is defined by NKX2-1 negativity, mucin production, glandular architecture, and expression of stomach-associated genes/proteins (especially CTSE and GKN1; HNF4α frequently positive). The paper argues this subtype is distinct from the papillary Nkx2-1-positive tumors and resembles murine Nkx2-1-deleted tumors.
clinical_or_exposure_context Human NKX2-1-negative mucinous LUADs comprise about 5-10% of lung adenocarcinomas and often harbor KRAS mutations (reported as 60-80% of cases in the cited background). NKX2-1-negative tumors have worse prognosis than NKX2-1-positive tumors. The paper does not provide smoking/sex/ancestry-specific enrichment data, but emphasizes the mucinous histology and poor differentiation context.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares latent differentiation programs in lung cancer to foregut gene de-repression in pancreatic intraepithelial neoplasia and to intestinal-type shifts in precursor lesions of esophageal and gastric carcinomas. It also notes that HNF4α is relevant in liver and GI cancers, and that master differentiation regulators can shape tumor evolution in other tissues.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication No drug is directly proposed for NKX2-1-negative LUAD. The therapeutic implication is conceptual: lineage state and latent differentiation programs may be biomarker-relevant, and targeting the differentiation circuitry (for example, the Nkx2-1/Hnf4α/Foxa1/2 axis or downstream Hmga2-linked primitive state) could alter tumor burden or progression. The paper also suggests that retaining or restoring lineage control restrains tumor initiation/progression, but does not test a specific targeted therapy.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated in multiple systems: conditional GEMMs (KrasLSL-G12D; Nkx2-1F/F, KrasFSF-G12D; RosaCreERT2; Nkx2-1F/F, KrasLA2; Nkx2-1F/F, and combined Nkx2-1/Hnf4a loss), adult lung Nkx2-1 deletion, human LUAD IHC cohorts, ChIP-Seq for Nkx2-1 and Foxa1/2, qRT-PCR/microarrays, and rescue experiments in an Nkx2-1-negative lung adenocarcinoma cell line re-expressing Nkx2-1. Foxa1/2 RNAi in Nkx2-1-deleted cells reduced some GI transcripts, and re-expression of Nkx2-1 restored Foxa1/2 binding and pulmonary gene expression at selected loci.
