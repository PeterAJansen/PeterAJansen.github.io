Paper Title: An atlas of epithelial cell states and plasticity in lung adenocarcinoma

Paper Venue: bioRxiv

Paper Abstract: Understanding cellular processes underlying early lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD) development is needed to devise intervention strategies1. Here we studied 246,102 single epithelial cells from 16 early-stage LUADs and 47 matched normal lung samples. Epithelial cells comprised diverse normal and cancer cell states, and diversity among cancer cells was strongly linked to LUAD-specific oncogenic drivers. KRAS mutant cancer cells showed distinct transcriptional features, reduced differentiation and low levels of aneuploidy. Non-malignant areas surrounding human LUAD samples were enriched with alveolar intermediate cells that displayed elevated KRT8 expression (termed KRT8+ alveolar intermediate cells (KACs) here), reduced differentiation, increased plasticity and driver KRAS mutations. Expression profiles of KACs were enriched in lung precancer cells and in LUAD cells and signified poor survival. In mice exposed to tobacco carcinogen, KACs emerged before lung tumours and persisted for months after cessation of carcinogen exposure. Moreover, they acquired Kras mutations and conveyed sensitivity to targeted KRAS inhibition in KAC-enriched organoids derived from alveolar type 2 (AT2) cells. Last, lineage-labelling of AT2 cells or KRT8+ cells following carcinogen exposure showed that KACs are possible intermediates in AT2-to-tumour cell transformation.

driver_negative_definition The paper does not define a classic 'driver-negative' LUAD cohort by exhaustive absence of drivers; instead, it contrasts KRAS-mutant LUAD with EGFR-mutant, MET-mutant, and wild-type (WT) LUADs. Driver status was assigned by whole-exome sequencing (WES) and corroborated by scRNA-seq detection of KRAS G12D reads, with 'wild-type' meaning no detected KRAS G12D mutation in the tumor sample and 'KW-LUAD' meaning KRAS WT LUAD. The analysis also notes tumors without KRAS/EGFR/MET alterations as 'other' WT for these genes.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The paper does not provide a definitive hidden driver for driver-negative LUAD; instead it emphasizes a lineage/state-based mechanism, proposing that KACs and early KRAS signaling programs constitute an early permissive state that can precede malignant transformation. It also raises the possibility that injury-induced alveolar intermediate states or subtle/undetected oncogenic or suppressor alterations may govern progression.
alteration_or_activation_mode Predominantly a lineage state and pathway-activation mechanism rather than a newly identified mutation. The implicated operation is increased KRAS signaling along the AT2-to-AIC-to-KAC spectrum, with KACs sometimes carrying KRAS G12D mutations and showing elevated KAC/KRAS signatures even when KRAS WT by sequencing.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway KRAS signaling axis, especially KRAS G12D-associated transcriptional programs; overlapping pathway features include EMT, epithelial senescence, p53 signaling, NF-κB, and hypoxia. For the driver-negative angle, the paper most strongly points to early KRAS programs in a KRT8+ alveolar intermediate lineage rather than a distinct alternative oncogene.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence includes a KRAS signature derived from KRAS-mutant malignant cells that correlates strongly with the MP30 KRAS G12D program and is enriched in KM-LUADs, KACs, premalignant AAH, and invasive LUAD. KACs spatially overlap with KRT8/CLDN4-positive reactive regions and KRAS signatures, and KACs from KM-LUAD or NNK-exposed mouse lungs show higher KRAS signature scores than WT counterparts. In mouse and human KACs, pathway enrichment analysis showed activation of NF-κB, hypoxia and p53 signaling; however, the paper frames these as KAC-state features rather than as a genetically resolved hidden driver in driver-negative tumors.
driver_negative_subtype Implicit subtype: KRAS WT LUAD with a high KAC/early KRAS signature and alveolar intermediate lineage features. The paper also contrasts KM-LUAD versus KW-LUAD, and notes that the KAC signature is elevated in KM-LUADs and also present in KW-LUADs compared with normal lung, implying a continuum rather than a completely separate molecular subtype.
clinical_or_exposure_context KAC abundance rises with proximity to tumor, is enriched in tumor-adjacent normal regions, and increases progressively from matched normal lung to AAH to invasive LUAD. The state is linked to tobacco exposure/NNK in mice and to poor overall survival in human cohorts (TCGA and PROSPECT). The paper also notes that a KRAS signature in TCGA KW-LUADs was associated with significantly lower overall survival.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares KACs to Krt8+ transitional alveolar cells in acute lung injury and to Krt8+ transitional cells in pulmonary fibrosis, and it notes overlap with Krt8+ transitional cells reported in bleomycin-induced alveolar regeneration. It also references pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma KRAS G12D meta-programs as a source of the KRAS-associated transcriptional program.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication A major implication is susceptibility of KAC-rich lesions to targeted KRAS(G12D) inhibition. In mouse AT2-derived reporter organoids enriched for KACs, MRTX1133 reduced organoid size and KRT8/CLDN4 expression; in vitro it inhibited growth of KRAS-mutant mouse LUAD cell lines and suppressed phospho-ERK and phospho-S6. The paper suggests KAC signals, including early KRAS programs, could be targets for prevention or intervention, but this is preclinical evidence.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated across human scRNA-seq, spatial transcriptomics, multiplex IF/DSP, TCGA/PROSPECT survival analyses, and mouse carcinogenesis models. Key validation includes NNK-exposed Gprc5a−/− mice, AT2 lineage reporter mice, Krt8-creER tracing, organoid assays, and independent mouse/human comparisons. Pseudotime, lineage tracing, CNV, mutation mapping, and drug inhibition assays support the model that KACs are intermediates in AT2-to-tumor transformation.
