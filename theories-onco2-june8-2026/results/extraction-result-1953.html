driver_negative_definition Patients were screened among 626 pathologically confirmed LUAD cases using a 13-gene panel (EGFR, KRAS, BRAF, PIK3CA, NRAS, HER2, MET, AKT1, c-KIT, PDGFRA, ALK, RET, and ROS1). EGFR and KRAS were additionally validated by ARMS-PCR and ALK by FISH; the final 'driver gene negative' set comprised patients negative for ALK, EGFR, HER2, KRAS, MET, BRAF, ROS1, and RET mutations, and the paper states these cases could not utilize current mainstream targeted therapy.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The paper does not identify a canonical hidden oncogenic driver or activated signaling pathway. Instead, it argues that immune-related gene-pair states and the tumor immune microenvironment characterize this driver-negative subgroup and may underlie prognosis and treatment response.
alteration_or_activation_mode Not established as a genetic hidden driver. The main modeled signal is a combinatorial immune-related gene-pair expression state (IRGPs) derived from transcriptomic differences between tumor and precancerous tissue.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Immune-related gene pairs involving CD1A|CXCL13, CD1A|S100A7L2, IFNA7|CMTM2, IFNA7|CSF3, and CAMP|TFR2; immune checkpoint axis PD-1/PD-L1/CTLA-4; and tumor immune microenvironment composition.
pathway_activation_evidence No phosphoproteomic or direct signaling activation evidence is provided. Evidence is transcriptomic: 59 DEIRGs were identified from paired tumor/precancerous tissues, the 5-IRGP signature was prognostic, and immune microenvironment inference with CIBERSORT plus checkpoint gene expression differences suggested immune-state differences between risk groups. The paper does not demonstrate a genetically explained oncogenic pathway activation.
driver_negative_subtype An immune-stratified subtype of driver-gene-negative LUAD defined by a 5-IRGP prognostic signature and divided into high-risk and low-risk groups. The low-risk group showed higher B cell naïve, activated CD4 memory T cells, CD8 T cells, and M1 macrophages; the high-risk group showed higher memory B cells, dendritic cells, and resting Tregs, along with higher PD-1/PD-L1/CTLA-4 expression.
clinical_or_exposure_context The cohort had mean overall survival of 3.13 ± 1.62 years, 13 deaths during follow-up, and mixed sex distribution; ages ranged from 28 to 76 years, with stages I-IV included. Risk score correlated with tumor stage, N stage, and M stage, but not age or sex. The paper emphasizes that these patients cannot benefit from current mainstream targeted therapy.
cross_cancer_parallel No explicit cross-cancer hidden-driver analogy is developed. The paper briefly cites immune-gene prognostic signatures and checkpoint biology in other cancers and lung adenocarcinoma, but does not define a cross-cancer parallel subtype mechanism.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Low-risk patients may benefit more from commonly used chemotherapy regimens such as gemcitabine and paclitaxel according to pRRophetic predictions, while the high-risk group had lower predicted IC50 for doxorubicin, epothilone.B, gemcitabine, and paclitaxel and higher PD-1/PD-L1/CTLA-4 expression, suggesting possible greater sensitivity to immunotherapy. These are computational/inferential, not clinically validated treatment-response results.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is limited to the same retrospective single-center cohort of 46 paired LUAD and precancerous tissue samples. The study uses RNA expression microarray profiling, univariate and multivariate Cox regression, ROC/AUC analysis, Kaplan–Meier survival analysis, CIBERSORT immune deconvolution, checkpoint gene expression comparison, and pRRophetic-based drug sensitivity prediction. There is no independent external cohort, cell-line, animal, or mechanistic functional validation.
