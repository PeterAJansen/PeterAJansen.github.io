oncogene_negative_definition The relevant negative state is Nkx2-1-negative / TTF1-negative lung adenocarcinoma, modeled as KrasLSL-G12D;Nkx2-1F/F tumors after Ad-Cre, KrasFSF-G12D;RosaCreERT2;Nkx2-1F/F tumors after tamoxifen, and human NKX2-1-negative mucinous lung adenocarcinomas. assay_strategy_and_detection_limits Discovery pipeline was multiomic and histology-based rather than a canonical driver search panel: conditional mouse genetics, IHC, mucin stains, qRT-PCR, Affymetrix exon arrays, ChIP-Seq for Nkx2-1/Foxa1/2/histone marks, RNAi, and rescue by Nkx2-1 re-expression. The blind spot explicitly discussed is that loss of Nkx2-1 alone was insufficient to explain all poorly differentiated/metastatic states; additional genetic or epigenetic changes, including de-repression of Hmga2, were likely required. The paper also notes that some tumors with stochastic Nkx2-1 loss later became Hmga2-positive, while most engineered Nkx2-1-deleted tumors did not, implying assay or biology missed drivers beyond Nkx2-1 loss.
missed_driver_reclassified_after_broader_testing Yes, but only at the pathway/lineage level rather than as a newly discovered DNA lesion: tumors initially appearing simply Nkx2-1-negative were later found, after broader expression/epigenetic and functional testing, to have activation of a latent gastric differentiation program with Hnf4α, Gkn1, Ctse, Pdx1, and other GI markers. In a later combinatorial test, loss of Hnf4a with Nkx2-1 was sufficient to de-repress Hmga2, revealing a second differentiation-layer regulator not captured by Nkx2-1 loss alone. The paper also states that additional genetic and epigenetic changes are likely required for the highly metastatic Hmga2-positive state.

human_prevalence_or_enrichment NKX2-1-negative tumors are the subset most associated with mucinous human lung adenocarcinoma. The paper examined 37 human lung adenocarcinomas: 11 NKX2-1-negative mucinous tumors and 26 NKX2-1-positive tumors. GKN1 was expressed in 6/11 NKX2-1-negative mucinous tumors and in 0/26 NKX2-1-positive tumors; CTSE was strongly and diffusely expressed in 11/11 NKX2-1-negative mucinous tumors and was negative or only focal in most NKX2-1-positive tumors.
