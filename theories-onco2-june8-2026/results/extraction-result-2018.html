Paper TL;DR: A rationale and framework for the incorporation of high‐content RNA and protein measurements into integrative biomarkers are presented and the potential of this approach for predicting risk of recurrence in a group of lung adenocarcinomas is shown.

Paper Abstract: Introduction: Despite apparently complete surgical resection, approximately half of resected early‐stage lung cancer patients relapse and die of their disease. Adjuvant chemotherapy reduces this risk by only 5% to 8%. Thus, there is a need for better identifying who benefits from adjuvant therapy, the drivers of relapse, and novel targets in this setting. Methods: RNA sequencing and liquid chromatography/liquid chromatography–mass spectrometry proteomics data were generated from 51 surgically resected non–small cell lung tumors with known recurrence status. Results: We present a rationale and framework for the incorporation of high‐content RNA and protein measurements into integrative biomarkers and show the potential of this approach for predicting risk of recurrence in a group of lung adenocarcinomas. In addition, we characterize the relationship between mRNA and protein measurements in lung adenocarcinoma and show that it is outcome specific. Conclusions: Our results suggest that mRNA and protein data possess independent biological and clinical importance, which can be leveraged to create higher‐powered expression biomarkers.

pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is indirect and computational: genes were stratified by RNA-protein Spearman correlation, and pathway enrichment analysis was performed on low- versus high-correlated gene sets. The paper explicitly says no significant enrichment of RBP or microRNA motifs was found in the recurrent low-correlation gene set.

clinical_or_exposure_context The cohort consists of surgically resected early-stage lung adenocarcinoma patients, matched for recurrence and adjuvant chemotherapy status. Clinical context includes recurrence outcome, prior adjuvant therapy, stage distribution, sex imbalance between recurrent and nonrecurrent groups, and the distinction between patients who received chemotherapy versus those who did not.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper repeatedly compares its RNA-protein correlation patterns to prior proteogenomic studies in colorectal, breast, and ovarian cancers, noting that the mRNA splicing pathway behaved differently in lung adenocarcinoma than in those other cancers. It also cites a study in aging humans and rhesus macaques showing that RNA-protein correlation can vary by phenotype.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication No targeted therapy for a hidden driver is proposed. The therapeutic implication is biomarker-based: integrative RNA-protein signatures may help predict recurrence risk after surgery and potentially identify patients who could benefit from adjuvant therapy or intensified surveillance. The authors also speculate that their approach might find tumor-protein/RNA pairings relevant to clinical outcome, but they do not validate a drug sensitivity or specific inhibitor strategy.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is limited to internal computational validation within the patient cohort using leave-one-out cross-validation. The integrative biomarker achieved 36/51 (71%) recurrence prediction accuracy, outperforming RNA-only, protein-only, and simple combined RNA+protein approaches, but the authors explicitly note that independent validation is still needed. No cell lines, organoids, xenografts, CRISPR perturbations, rescue experiments, or drug sensitivity assays were performed.
