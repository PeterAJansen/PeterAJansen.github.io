Paper Abstract: Lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD) is one of the most common histological subtypes of lung cancer. The aim of this study was to construct consensus clusters based on multi-omics data and multiple algorithms. In order to identify specific molecular characteristics and facilitate the use of precision medicine on patients we used gene expression, DNA methylation, gene mutations, copy number variation data, and clinical data of LUAD patients for clustering. Consensus clusters were obtained using a consensus ensemble of five multi-omics integrative algorithms. Four molecular subtypes were identified. The CS1 and CS2 subtypes had better prognosis. Based on the immune and drug sensitivity predictions, we inferred that CS1 may be less responsive to immunotherapy and less sensitive to chemotherapeutic drugs. The high immune infiltration of CS2 cells may respond well to immunotherapy. Additionally, the CS2 subtype may also respond to EGFR molecular targeted therapy. The CS3 and CS4 subtypes were associated with poor prognosis. These two subtypes had more mutations, especially TP53 ones, as well as higher sensitivity to chemotherapeutics for lung cancer. However, CS3 was enriched in immune-related pathways and may respond to anti-PD1 immunotherapy. In addition, CS1 and CS4 were less sensitive to ferroptosis inhibitors. We performed a comprehensive analysis of the five types of omics data using five clustering algorithms to reveal the molecular characteristics of LUAD patients. These findings provide new insights into LUAD subtypes and potential clinical treatment strategies to guide personalized management and treatment.

driver_negative_definition The paper does not explicitly operationalize 'driver-negative' or 'oncogene-negative' LUAD. It instead analyzes 437 TCGA LUAD tumors after integrating transcriptomic, epigenomic, genomic, copy-number, and clinical data; there is no exclusion criterion stating that canonical drivers (e.g., EGFR, KRAS, TP53, KEAP1, etc.) must be absent. The closest relevant subset is the lower-mutation, lower-CNA CS2 subtype, but this is not defined as driver-negative.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main mechanistic theme is subtype-specific pathway state rather than a single hidden driver. The paper emphasizes immune-hot versus immune-cold states, metabolic reprogramming, cell-cycle/oncogenic pathway activation, and tumor microenvironment differences as determinants of prognosis and therapeutic response.
alteration_or_activation_mode Integrated multi-omics states: differential mutation burden, copy-number alteration patterns, DNA methylation, and gene-expression pathway activation. In particular, CS3 and CS4 show higher mutation burden and TP53 mutation frequency; CS4 shows strong activation of cell-cycle and oncogenic programs; CS2 shows more favorable immune infiltration and fewer CNAs.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway PI3K/AKT/mTOR signaling, cell cycle/G2M checkpoint, E2F targets, MYC targets, EMT, interferon response, IL-2-STAT5 signaling, IL-6/JAK/STAT3 signaling, metabolic pathways (Krebs cycle, sugar, serine, lipid metabolism), and mutation-linked genes including TP53, EGFR, KRAS, and KEAP1.
pathway_activation_evidence Pathway states were inferred from GSVA enrichment scores over hallmark/pathway gene sets and visualized by heatmaps. CS4 was enriched for mitotic spindle, PI3K/AKT/mTOR, G2M checkpoint, E2F targets, unfolded protein response, mTORC1 signaling, DNA repair, and MYC targets; CS2 and CS3 were enriched for immune/inflammatory pathways; CS1 and CS2 were enriched for lipid metabolism, while CS3/CS4 showed higher carbohydrate/nucleotide metabolism and EMT. No phosphoproteomic or IHC validation of pathway activation was reported.
driver_negative_subtype No explicit driver-negative subtype is named. The closest implicit candidates are CS1 and especially CS2, because CS2 had fewer copy-number alterations and a more favorable prognosis; however, both still harbored canonical alterations and are not described as driver-negative. The subtype structure is fully multi-omics-based and includes genomics, epigenomics, transcriptomics, and immune context.
clinical_or_exposure_context The LUAD cohort was TCGA-based; subtype associations were reported with age, sex, stage, and TP53 mutation status. CS2 was enriched in older patients and female patients, CS1/CS4 in males, and CS3/CS4 had markedly higher TP53 mutation rates. The paper also notes smoking history as part of the clinical annotations, but does not define a driver-negative clinical subgroup by smoking status or histology.
cross_cancer_parallel No explicit cross-cancer driver-negative parallel is made. The discussion briefly generalizes to non-small cell lung cancer and references broad concepts of targeted therapy, immunotherapy, and ferroptosis, but does not compare driver-negative LUAD to a specific other cancer with similar hidden-driver logic.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications are subtype-specific rather than centered on a hidden driver-negative oncogenic axis. CS2 is proposed as more likely to benefit from EGFR-targeted therapy and immunotherapy; CS3 may respond to anti-PD-1 immunotherapy and chemotherapy; CS3/CS4 are predicted to be more sensitive to cisplatin, paclitaxel, docetaxel, and vinorelbine; CS1 and CS4 are less sensitive to ferroptosis inhibitors (ML162, ML210, and possibly erastin). The evidence is computational/prediction-based, not clinical trial validation.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is by computational replication in three external GEO cohorts (GSE68465, GSE72094, GSE41271) using nearest template prediction (NTP) and survival analysis. Additional support comes from in silico drug-response prediction (pRRophetic/GDSC), TIDE immunotherapy prediction, SubMap comparison to melanoma immunotherapy responders, and mutation/CNA analyses in the TCGA discovery cohort. No experimental cell-line, organoid, xenograft, or prospective clinical validation was performed.
