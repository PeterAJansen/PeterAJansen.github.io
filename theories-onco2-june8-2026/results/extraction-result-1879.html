key_evidence From 59 differentially expressed immune-related genes, 1175 IRGPs were screened and 5 remained in multivariate Cox analysis. High-risk tumors had higher PD-1/PD-L1/CTLA-4 expression and predicted greater sensitivity to doxorubicin, epothilone B, gemcitabine, and paclitaxel, while low-risk tumors showed a more favorable immune-cell profile.

subtype_features Defined by absence of tested canonical drivers; subdivided into high-risk and low-risk groups by a 5-IRGP immune score. Low-risk tumors showed higher naïve B cells, activated CD4 memory T cells, CD8 T cells, and M1 macrophages; high-risk tumors showed higher memory B cells, dendritic cells, resting Tregs, and higher PD-1/PD-L1/CTLA-4 expression.
parallel_in_other_cancers The discussion cites prior immune-gene prognostic models in glioma, head and neck squamous cell carcinoma, bladder cancer, colorectal cancer, colon cancer, and early-stage lung adenocarcinoma, but does not describe a directly analogous 'driver-negative' subtype in another cancer.
validation_approach Orthogonal validation of key driver status with ARMS-PCR and FISH; internal prognostic validation using ROC curves, Kaplan–Meier survival, and Cox analyses; immune deconvolution with CIBERSORT; checkpoint-gene comparison; computational chemotherapy-response prediction with pRRophetic. The authors propose future clinical trials and further biological verification.
validation_result Orthogonal testing supported driver-negative classification for EGFR/KRAS and ALK in the cohort. The 5-IRGP signature and nomogram showed good internal prognostic performance, but no external validation or mechanistic perturbation experiments were reported.
therapeutic_implication The signature is proposed to guide treatment selection in driver-negative LUAD: high-risk patients may be better candidates for immunotherapy, whereas low-risk patients may benefit more from common chemotherapy regimens such as gemcitabine and paclitaxel.
therapeutic_evidence High-risk tumors had higher PD-1/PD-L1/CTLA-4 expression, suggesting immunotherapy sensitivity; computational drug-response analysis predicted lower IC50 for doxorubicin, epothilone B, gemcitabine, and paclitaxel in the high-risk group, though the discussion states low-risk patients may be more sensitive to chemotherapeutics and the text contains some internal inconsistency on this point.
clinical_or_demographic_association Risk score correlated with clinical stage, N stage, and M stage; age and sex were not correlated. The cohort included 22 males and 24 females, ages 28-76 (median 57.5 years), and tumors across stages I-IV.
study_limitations Small sample size, single-center retrospective design, partial exclusion due to RNA degradation/missing data, limited driver testing panel, absence of external validation, lack of functional experiments, and possible confounding from treatment guideline changes over the 2003-2015 accrual period.
