driver_negative_definition Not formally defined in the paper. The study operates on an unselected lung carcinoma cohort (NSCLC and SCLC; including adenocarcinoma, BAC, squamous, large-cell, and unspecified NSCLC) and identifies copy-number states relative to normal reference DNA using SNP arrays plus HMM/binary segmentation; 'negative' is not operationalized as absence of EGFR/KRAS/ALK or other canonical drivers.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver logic is that recurrent focal amplifications or homozygous deletions may mark oncogenes or tumor suppressor genes and thus reveal active pathways in lung tumors. In adenocarcinoma-containing NSCLC, the paper highlights RTK/tyrosine-kinase and cell-cycle pathway lesions, especially EGFR, FGFR1, ERBB2, MET, CDK4, CCNE1, and deletions involving CDKN2A, PTEN, FHIT, and LRP1B.
alteration_or_activation_mode Copy-number gain/amplification for candidate oncogenes and homozygous deletion for candidate tumor suppressors; some loci are recurrent focal amplicons or deletions, while EGFR amplification is shown to be partly independent of kinase-domain mutation status.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway RTK signaling (EGFR, FGFR1, ERBB2, MET), the RB/p16/CDK4/CDK6 cell-cycle pathway (CDKN2A, CDK4, CDK6, CCND1, CCNE1, RB1), and tumor-suppressor loss loci (PTEN, FHIT, LRP1B, PTPRD/BC028038 region).
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is primarily genomic copy-number evidence, supplemented by qPCR validation and limited expression data. For EGFR, the paper explicitly reports that amplification is independent of kinase-domain mutational status and that EGFR-copy-number gain/mutation correlates with higher EGFR expression on expression arrays; for ASPH/MGC34646 and CRKL, RT-PCR/qPCR showed increased transcript abundance in amplified samples, but protein-level activation was not consistently demonstrated.
driver_negative_subtype No explicit driver-negative LUAD subtype is defined. An implicit subtype appears as adenocarcinoma/NSCLC tumors with RTK amplification and/or cell-cycle alterations but without a single dominant canonical mutation pattern; however, this is not formalized as a molecular subtype in the paper.
clinical_or_exposure_context The paper includes adenocarcinoma, BAC, large-cell, and squamous NSCLC samples but does not systematically stratify by smoking status, sex, stage, or prognosis for driver-negative disease. It notes that EGFR mutations in lung cancer are associated with never-smokers in cited literature, but this study itself is not organized around exposure-defined cohorts.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares lung cancer copy-number drivers to other cancers in which amplification or deletion preceded mutation discovery, including PDGFRA, EGFR, ERBB2, and PIK3CA. It also notes FGFR1 amplifications in breast and urinary/bladder carcinoma and trastuzumab development in HER2-positive breast cancer.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The authors propose that kinases such as CDK4, FGFR1, and MET identified here may be future therapeutic targets in lung cancers upon further validation. They also discuss EGFR and ERBB2 in the context of targeted therapy, but no treatment response data are generated in this paper.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is genomic and transcriptional rather than functional: recurrent events were confirmed by real-time PCR, some by interphase FISH (e.g., ASPH/8q12-13 in H2171), and EGFR copy-number/amplification was compared against mutation and expression data. The study uses primary tumors and cell lines, but no knockdown, rescue, xenograft, or inhibitor-sensitivity experiments are performed.
