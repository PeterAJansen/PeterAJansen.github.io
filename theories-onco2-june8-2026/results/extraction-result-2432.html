driver_negative_definition The paper operationalizes 'driver-negative' / lacking a plausible oncogenic driver as lung adenocarcinoma cases without recurrent activating alterations in canonical lung cancer oncogenes and without known oncogenic alterations on genome sequencing. Specifically, the index tumor lacked hotspot mutations in KRAS, EGFR, BRAF, ERBB2, or PIK3CA; lacked ALK, ROS1, or RET fusions; and had no other known oncogenic alterations on WGS/RNA-seq, despite broad somatic mutation burden and low-level 4q12 copy gains. The authors also note that over 50% of lung adenocarcinomas lack a plausible driver in large genome surveys.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism A rare, recurrent RAF-family oncogenic mutation hot spot in ARAF/RAF1 is proposed as the hidden driver mechanism for a subset of oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinomas. The mechanism is tumor-intrinsic MAPK pathway activation through disruption of RAF regulation, rather than a pathway state without a lesion.
alteration_or_activation_mode Rare coding mutation / hotspot-like substitution in a negative regulatory phosphorylation site, with functional gain-of-function oncogenicity and inhibitor sensitivity.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway ARAF/RAF1 within the RAF–MEK–ERK MAP kinase pathway (Ras to MEK/ERK signaling).
pathway_activation_evidence The tumor showed expressed ARAF S214C with high tumor RNA variant fraction, and the mutation was linked experimentally to increased phospho-MEK in AALE cells. The paper also cites increased MEK/ERK phosphorylation for RAF1 mutants and attenuation of 14-3-3 binding for ARAF S214C as a plausible biochemical mechanism. In the patient, the pathway activation is genetically explained by the ARAF lesion.
driver_negative_subtype A rare RAF-family mutant subtype of otherwise driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma, defined by ARAF codon 214 or RAF1 codon 257/259 mutations. It is a genomics-defined subtype within a broader oncogene-negative LUAD population.
clinical_or_exposure_context Index case: 66-year-old light former smoker with stage IV lung adenocarcinoma, heavily pretreated before sorafenib. The patient experienced a near-complete, durable 5-year response to oral sorafenib and later progressed. The paper also notes that the recurrent RAF-family mutants were found in treatment-naive TCGA lung cancer samples.
cross_cancer_parallel The same RAF-family hot spot was also found across multiple tumor types in TCGA, including colorectal adenocarcinoma, gastric adenocarcinoma, and cutaneous melanoma, and RAF1 S257/S259 mutations were reported in ovarian cancer and Noonan syndrome literature. The authors additionally contrast the region with BRAF kinase-domain hot spots in lung adenocarcinoma.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The paper proposes ARAF/RAF1 mutations as biomarkers of sorafenib response in lung adenocarcinoma and suggests potential vulnerability to MEK inhibition (trametinib), based on preclinical cell-model sensitivity. The evidence is suggestive rather than clinically validated.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated by whole-genome sequencing and RNA-seq of the index tumor/normal pair, recurrence analysis in TCGA and published lung adenocarcinoma cohorts, and functional testing in immortalized human airway epithelial AALE cells. Mutant ARAF alleles transformed cells in soft agar, increased phospho-MEK, and were inhibited by sorafenib and trametinib; RAF1 mutants showed similar functional behavior in AALE and NIH-3T3 cells. Clinical response in the index patient provides additional human evidence.
