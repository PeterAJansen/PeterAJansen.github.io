Paper Title: Proteogenomics of non-small cell lung cancer reveals molecular subtypes associated with specific therapeutic targets and immune evasion mechanisms

Paper Venue: Nature Cancer

Paper TL;DR: Proteogenomic analysis of non-small cell lung cancer is performed and molecular subtypes with distinct immune-evasion mechanisms and therapeutic targets are identified and classification method is developed and validated in separate clinical cohorts.

Paper Abstract: Despite major advancements in lung cancer treatment, long-term survival is still rare and a deeper understanding of molecular phenotypes would allow the identification of specific cancer dependencies and immune-evasion mechanisms. Here we performed in-depth mass-spectrometry-based proteogenomic analysis of 141 tumors representing all major histologies of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). We identified six distinct proteome subtypes with striking differences in immune cell composition and subtype-specific expression of immune checkpoints. Unexpectedly, high neoantigen burden was linked to global hypomethylation and complex neoantigens mapped to genomic regions, such as endogenous retroviral elements and introns, in immune-cold subtypes. Further, we linked immune evasion with LAG-3 via STK11 mutation-dependent HNF1A activation and FGL1 expression. Finally, we develop a data-independent acquisition mass-spectrometry-based NSCLC subtype classification method, validate it in an independent cohort of 208 NSCLC cases and demonstrate its clinical utility by analyzing an additional cohort of 84 late-stage NSCLC biopsy samples. Lehtiö and colleagues perform proteogenomic analysis of non-small cell lung cancer and identify molecular subtypes with distinct immune-evasion mechanisms and therapeutic targets and validate their classification method in separate clinical cohorts.

driver_negative_definition The paper does not define a strict 'oncogene-negative' LUAD cohort by exclusion of canonical drivers; instead, it highlights a driver-negative-like phenotype within lung adenocarcinoma represented by proteome Subtype 4, where tumors are enriched for STK11, KEAP1, and SMARCA4 alterations and show immune-cold biology. The classification was built from proteomics integrated with panel sequencing, mRNA, and DNA methylation data across 141 NSCLC tumors, and the relevant negative context is the absence of the typical strongly targetable oncogene-driven state seen in EGFR-enriched Subtype 1, together with alternative pathway activation not explained by classic oncogenic drivers.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main proposed hidden driver is STK11/LKB1 inactivation causing loss of AMPK control, with downstream activation of a liver-specific HNF1A-FGL1-CPS1 program and associated mTOR pathway addiction. The paper also frames this as a proteomic/pathway-state subtype in which immune evasion is mediated by secreted FGL1 acting on LAG-3.
alteration_or_activation_mode Combinatorial loss/inactivation of STK11, including mutation-associated and potentially post-transcriptional/transcriptional downregulation, coupled to outlier overexpression of FGL1, CPS1, and HNF1A. The pathway state is inferred from protein/mRNA co-expression, methylation associations, and functional perturbation, rather than from a single canonical activating mutation.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway STK11/LKB1-AMPK-mTOR axis; HNF1A-driven liver-lineage transcriptional program; FGL1-LAG3 immune checkpoint axis; CPS1 as a downstream marker of the pathway state.
pathway_activation_evidence Subtype 4 showed strong coincidence between STK11 mutation and high FGL1 protein/mRNA, strong negative STK11-FGL1 protein correlation, and high CPS1 as a correlated marker of STK11 loss. In vitro, AMPK activation with A-769662 reduced HNF1A and FGL1 in HepG2 and STK11-mutant lung cancer cell lines, and STK11 re-expression in NCI-H1944 reduced FGL1 and HNF1A. Drug-sensitivity data from GDSC showed enhanced sensitivity of FGL1/CPS1-high NSCLC cell lines to mTOR inhibitors, supporting mTOR-addicted pathway state; the activation is not presented as fully explained by STK11 mutation alone because STK11 mutation alone did not predict mTOR-inhibitor sensitivity.
driver_negative_subtype Subtype 4 is the explicit candidate subtype. It is defined proteomically, enriched for adenocarcinoma, immune-cold, with low immune-cell signals, low antigen presentation relative to TMB, high TMB but low APM, high FGL1, and frequent STK11/KEAP1/SMARCA4 alterations; it also shows liver-specific gene expression and reduced methylation at HNF1A.
clinical_or_exposure_context Subtype 4 is associated with lung adenocarcinoma and poorer survival trends in the TCGA LUAD reanalysis. The paper notes that some STK11-wild-type LUADs still show the FGL1/CPS1-high state with reduced STK11 mRNA, indicating a context of alternative STK11 inactivation. The cohort overall includes mostly early-stage resected tumors, but the subtype is also reproducible in validation and biopsy cohorts.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper compares FGL1 and CPS1 expression to hepatocellular carcinoma, where these genes are normally high, and emphasizes that the LUAD subtype co-opts a liver-specific transcriptional program. It also notes that HNF1A is a master regulator of liver transcription and that its mouse ortholog TCF1 is linked to mTORC1-STAT3 in another immune context.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Potential combination therapy with LAG-3/FGL1 checkpoint blockade plus mTOR inhibitors is proposed for Subtype 4. The paper also suggests that FGL1/CPS1-high status could indicate mTOR addiction and that LAG-3 targeting may be more relevant than PD-1/PD-L1 blockade in this subtype because FGL1-mediated systemic T-cell suppression may dominate.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated by independent NSCLC cohorts using MS-based classification, by TCGA LUAD transcriptomics, by GDSC cell-line drug-response analysis, and by in vitro perturbation experiments. Model systems include HepG2 liver cells, STK11-mutant lung cancer cell lines NCI-H1944 and NCI-H1395, and STK11 rescue in NCI-H1944 with western blot readouts of HNF1A/FGL1. The classifier also reproduced the subtype in an independent 208-case validation cohort and a late-stage biopsy cohort.
