assay_strategy_and_detection_limits Discovery used integrated whole-genome sequencing of paired tumor/normal DNA plus transcriptome (RNA-seq) of the liver metastasis, with fusion calling from discordant paired-end reads and exon-spanning reads, then confirmation by PCR and Sanger sequencing on genomic DNA and cDNA; validation across primary, liver, and bone tumor tissues was also performed by inversion-specific PCR. The paper explicitly notes that ab initio detection of chromosomal rearrangements such as translocations/inversions is challenging in WGS alone and that transcriptome sequencing improves fusion detection; many fusion calls were adjacent intrachromosomal events lacking clear genomic support and likely nonfunctional, showing a major false-positive burden that required filtering and orthogonal validation.

A tumor initially negative for canonical lung adenocarcinoma drivers (EGFR, KRAS, EML4-ALK) was reclassified as driven by KIF5B-RET after broader DNA+RNA sequencing revealed a cryptic 10.6-Mb inversion on chr10 and a fusion transcript; the alteration was missed initially because standard clinical assays targeted only the canonical markers and would not detect this noncanonical structural rearrangement.

candidate_driver_gene_or_locus KIF5B-RET at chromosome 10p11.22 / 10q11.21; fusion junctions involved KIF5B exon 16 to RET exon 12 in AK55, with alternative KIF5B breakpoints in replication cases (AK55, LC_S2, LC_S6).
structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism A 10.6-Mb pericentric inversion juxtaposed the 5' KIF5B region to RET, placing RET kinase coding exons downstream of the active KIF5B promoter and preserving KIF5B coiled-coil dimerization motifs. The coiled-coil domain is predicted to enforce homodimerization and autophosphorylation of the RET kinase domain, analogous to other activated fusion kinases.
functional_consequence Marked RET exon 12-20 expression from the fusion allele, increased RET kinase expression in the cancer relative to RET-rearrangement-negative lung tumors, and spontaneous cellular transformation capability of the chimeric RET receptor tyrosine kinase.
co_alteration_or_combinatorial_requirement The paper does not report a cooperating suppressor loss or additional required mutation; it argues that KIF5B-RET itself is sufficient as a driver in this subset. A small 1-bp deletion adjacent to the breakpoint suggests error-prone DNA repair (e.g., NHEJ/FoSTeS/MMBIR) may have helped generate the inversion, but this is a mechanistic contributor rather than a cooperative oncogenic alteration.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment Observed in 3 total cases in the study set: 1/5 additional triple-negative primary lung adenocarcinomas (LC_S2) and 1/15 additional double-negative tumors (LC_S6), plus the index case AK55; the authors estimate an approximate frequency of ~6% in lung adenocarcinoma, with a rough range of 4.3%-8% based on the two replication denominators. RET overexpression was also seen in 3/32 TCGA samples (9.4%), though the paper notes additional mechanisms could explain those cases.

The paper reports that the fusion protein would contain an activating dimerization module and kinase domain and states that the chimeric oncogene could spontaneously induce cellular transformation; independent recurrence in additional primary tumors supports driver status.
