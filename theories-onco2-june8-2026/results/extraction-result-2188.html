assay_strategy_and_detection_limits Discovery used integrated massively parallel whole-genome sequencing (WGS) of blood and tumor DNA, transcriptome sequencing (RNA-seq) of metastatic tumor RNA/cDNA, plus PCR and Sanger sequencing validation of genomic inversion and fusion transcripts. The paper explicitly notes that fusion-gene detection is improved by transcriptome sequencing because ab initio detection of chromosomal rearrangements such as inversions/translocations is still challenging in WGS alone; only one corresponding chromosomal rearrangement was found among 52 transcriptome-predicted fusions. The clinical pre-screening assays were hotspot-style EGFR/KRAS exon sequencing and EML4-ALK FISH, which would not detect RET fusions or complex structural rearrangements.

structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism A 10.6-Mb chromosomal inversion places the RET kinase domain under the active KIF5B promoter and preserves the KIF5B coiled-coil dimerization domain.
