brief_description A novel somatic gene fusion identified in lung adenocarcinoma, in which the first six exons of CD74 are fused to the exons encoding the EGF-like domain of NRG1 III-β3. The fusion creates a membrane-tethered NRG1 ligand presentation that activates ERBB2/ERBB3 signaling and PI3K-AKT, representing a hidden driver in tumors otherwise negative for known oncogenic alterations.

oncogene_negative_definition The discovery cohort comprised 25 lung adenocarcinomas from never smokers that lacked KRAS and EGFR mutations and were analyzed by chromosomal copy-number analysis plus transcriptome sequencing. The validation cohort comprised 102 pan-negative lung adenocarcinomas of never smokers that were wild-type for EGFR, KRAS, BRAF, ERBB2, ALK, ROS, and RET; additional confirmation used RT-PCR, break-apart FISH, and hybrid-capture-based genomic sequencing.
assay_strategy_and_detection_limits The paper used SNP 6.0 copy-number analysis, RNA-seq/transcriptome sequencing with a custom split-read, read-pair, and de novo assembly fusion-detection pipeline, RT-PCR validation, break-apart and fusion FISH, and targeted hybrid-capture genomic sequencing of 333 cancer-associated genes. The main blind spot was that the initial screen only included driver-negative tumors by canonical alterations and relied on assays optimized for expressed fusions and targeted regions; the fusion was detected because RNA-seq captured a chimeric transcript and DNA capture subsequently confirmed the genomic breakpoint.
missed_driver_reclassified_after_broader_testing Yes. A tumor initially classified as driver-negative in the negative-screen cohort was reclassified as CD74-NRG1-positive after transcriptome sequencing revealed a chimeric transcript, then RT-PCR and FISH confirmed the rearrangement, and targeted genomic sequencing identified breakpoint-spanning reads. It was missed by conventional driver testing because it was a noncanonical fusion not among the routine negative markers (EGFR/KRAS/BRAF/ERBB2/ALK/ROS/RET) and required fusion-aware RNA/DNA assays to detect.
driver_event_class Gene fusion / somatic rearrangement
candidate_driver_gene_or_locus CD74-NRG1 (CD74 fused to NRG1, specifically to the EGF-like domain-encoding exons of NRG1 III-β3)
structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism The CD74 portion replaces the native transmembrane context of NRG1 III-β3, preserving a membrane-tethered EGF-like domain that is presented extracellularly rather than secreted. This creates an autocrine/paracrine-like ligand display enabling activation of ERBB2-ERBB3 receptor complexes.
functional_consequence High expression of the NRG1 III-β3 fusion isoform, extracellular presentation of the NRG1 EGF-like domain, phosphorylation of ERBB3, activation of ERBB2/ERBB3 and downstream PI3K-AKT signaling, increased p-S6K, and enhanced anchorage-independent colony formation.
co_alteration_or_combinatorial_requirement The fusion acted in the context of ERBB2 and ERBB3 expression in recipient cells; its signaling effect depended on the EGF-like domain of NRG1, and H1568 cells already displayed baseline oncogenicity, indicating the fusion augmented but was not strictly the sole proliferative input in that assay system. No required cooperating genomic alteration in the tumors was reported beyond the fusion itself.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment Detected in 1/25 never-smoker lung adenocarcinomas in the discovery cohort and 4/102 additional pan-negative never-smoker lung adenocarcinomas in validation (5 total cases including the index case). All five fusion-positive tumors were invasive mucinous adenocarcinomas of women who had never smoked. In the small East Asian IMA cohort (n=15), 4/15 (27%) had CD74-NRG1 and 6/15 (40%) had KRAS mutations; in the broader never-smoker negative cohort the authors estimated an overall frequency of about 1.7% in lung adenocarcinoma.
functional_or_clinical_validation Validated by transcriptome sequencing, RT-PCR, break-apart FISH, and targeted DNA sequencing in tumors; mechanistically validated by FACS showing membrane localization, immunohistochemistry showing p-ERBB3 enrichment in fusion-positive cases, Western blots showing p-ERBB2/p-ERBB3/p-AKT/p-S6K induction, coculture assays activating AKT, and soft-agar assays showing enhanced colony formation; the authors also note therapeutic relevance for ERBB/PI3K-AKT targeting.
