Paper Title: Distinct patterns of somatic genome alterations in lung adenocarcinomas and squamous cell carcinomas

Paper Venue: Nature Genetics

Paper TL;DR: Although targeted therapies for lung ADC and SqCC are largely distinct, immunotherapies may aid in treatment for both subtypes.

Paper Abstract: To compare lung adenocarcinoma (ADC) and lung squamous cell carcinoma (SqCC) and to identify new drivers of lung carcinogenesis, we examined the exome sequences and copy number profiles of 660 lung ADC and 484 lung SqCC tumor–normal pairs. Recurrent alterations in lung SqCCs were more similar to those of other squamous carcinomas than to alterations in lung ADCs. New significantly mutated genes included PPP3CA, DOT1L, and FTSJD1 in lung ADC, RASA1 in lung SqCC, and KLF5, EP300, and CREBBP in both tumor types. New amplification peaks encompassed MIR21 in lung ADC, MIR205 in lung SqCC, and MAPK1 in both. Lung ADCs lacking receptor tyrosine kinase–Ras–Raf pathway alterations had mutations in SOS1, VAV1, RASA1, and ARHGAP35. Regarding neoantigens, 47% of the lung ADC and 53% of the lung SqCC tumors had at least five predicted neoepitopes. Although targeted therapies for lung ADC and SqCC are largely distinct, immunotherapies may aid in treatment for both subtypes.

driver_negative_definition Lung adenocarcinomas were classified as "oncogene negative" if they had no activating SSNV, indel, amplification, or gene fusion in a known RTK/Ras/Raf driver. The authors explicitly excluded NF1-altered tumors from the oncogene-positive category because NF1 mutations are not entirely mutually exclusive with other Ras/Raf/RTK alterations. The classification was made using exome sequencing plus RNA-seq/fusion analysis and copy-number analysis on the lung ADC cohort (n=660 total, with 418 oncogene-positive and 242 oncogene-negative).
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hypothesis is that driver-negative tumors still harbor rare alterations in the RTK/Ras/Raf pathway and related signaling modules, rather than being truly driverless. Specifically, the paper highlights enrichment of SOS1 and RASA1 mutations and VAV1 and ARHGAP35 mutations, plus focal amplifications near FGFR1/WHSC1L1, PDGFRA/KIT/KDR, and MAPK1, suggesting hidden pathway activation through low-frequency oncogenic events and loss of negative regulators.
alteration_or_activation_mode Rare recurrent missense or in-frame mutations in pathway regulators (SOS1 p.N233Y; VAV1 p.S67Y), loss-of-function mutations in GAPs (RASA1, ARHGAP35), and focal copy-number amplifications near pathway components (FGFR1/WHSC1L1, PDGFRA/KIT/KDR, MAPK1). The paper also notes that some driver-negative cases retain STK11 mutations, implying a potentially unrecognized complementary KRAS-related alteration.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway RTK/Ras/Raf signaling and related Ras/Rho GEF-GAP circuitry, including SOS1, RASA1, VAV1, ARHGAP35, FGFR1/WHSC1L1, PDGFRA/KIT/KDR, MAPK1, and STK11 as a co-occurring alteration in some oncogene-negative tumors.
pathway_activation_evidence The paper does not present phosphoproteomic or IHC evidence such as pERK/pAKT/pS6 for these oncogene-negative tumors. Instead, activation is inferred genetically from enrichment of activating or loss-of-function alterations in canonical pathway members and from focal amplifications in pathway loci in the oncogene-negative set. The activation state is therefore genetically suggested rather than directly measured at the protein level.
driver_negative_subtype An implicitly defined genomic subtype of LUAD: RTK/Ras/Raf oncogene-negative tumors. The paper further suggests a possible hidden subgroup within these tumors characterized by enrichment of rare Ras-pathway mutations and pathway amplifications, but it does not formalize this as a separate transcriptomic or proteomic subtype.
clinical_or_exposure_context These tumors occur within lung adenocarcinoma broadly; the paper does not define a unique clinical phenotype for the oncogene-negative group. More generally, the cohort includes tumors from both smokers and never-smokers, and the study notes that 15% to 25% of LUADs still lack a detectable RTK/Ras/Raf alteration. Smoking status is relevant to mutational signatures, but no specific smoking association is reported for the oncogene-negative subset itself.
cross_cancer_parallel The authors discuss that lung SqCC resembles head and neck squamous carcinoma and bladder cancer more than lung ADC, but for oncogene-negative LUAD specifically they do not provide a direct cross-cancer hidden-driver analogy. The closest parallel is the general cancer-genomics logic that low-frequency pathway events can be uncovered by larger sample sizes and that distinct cancers may share pathway-level alterations even if their individual drivers differ.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The implication is that some clinically relevant, targetable RTK/Ras/Raf-pathway alterations remain undiscovered in ostensibly driver-negative LUAD, so additional molecular profiling could reveal eligibility for targeted therapy. The paper does not test drug sensitivity in these tumors, but it suggests that uncovering rare events in RTK/Ras/Raf or related regulators could expand use of RTK inhibitors and other pathway-targeted treatments.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is primarily statistical/genomic: large-cohort exome sequencing, RNA-seq fusion analysis, copy-number analysis, and enrichment testing in oncogene-negative versus oncogene-positive tumors. The paper also reports manual review of additional canonical mutations and states that 193/227 reviewed LUADs with RNA-seq had a predicted activating RTK/Ras/Raf alteration, but it does not provide cell-line, organoid, xenograft, or inhibitor-validation experiments for the oncogene-negative candidates.
