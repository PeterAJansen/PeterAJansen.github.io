Paper Abstract: Adenocarcinoma of the lung is the leading cause of cancer death worldwide. Here we report molecular profiling of 230 resected lung adenocarcinomas using messenger RNA, microRNA and DNA sequencing integrated with copy number, methylation and proteomic analyses. High rates of somatic mutation were seen (mean 8.9 mutations per megabase). Eighteen genes were statistically significantly mutated, including RIT1 activating mutations and newly described loss-of-function MGA mutations which are mutually exclusive with focal MYC amplification. EGFR mutations were more frequent in female patients, whereas mutations in RBM10 were more common in males. Aberrations in NF1, MET, ERBB2 and RIT1 occurred in 13% of cases and were enriched in samples otherwise lacking an activated oncogene, suggesting a driver role for these events in certain tumours. DNA and mRNA sequence from the same tumour highlighted splicing alterations driven by somatic genomic changes, including exon 14 skipping in MET mRNA in 4% of cases. MAPK and PI(3)K pathway activity, when measured at the protein level, was explained by known mutations in only a fraction of cases, suggesting additional, unexplained mechanisms of pathway activation. These data establish a foundation for classification and further investigations of lung adenocarcinoma molecular pathogenesis.

clinical_or_exposure_context The oncogene-negative group had similar tumor cellularity to oncogene-positive tumors. Across the cohort, smoking status correlated with transversion-high tumors, whereas transversion-low tumors were enriched for EGFR, PIK3CA, RB1, ALK/ROS1/RET fusions, and certain insertions; female sex was associated with EGFR mutations and male sex with RBM10 loss-of-function. The paper does not assign a single exposure phenotype to the oncogene-negative subset, but it emphasizes clinical heterogeneity.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper briefly compares RB1 mutation patterns with small-cell lung carcinoma, noting that RB1 frameshift indels in transversion-low adenocarcinoma differ from the mutational mechanism in SCLC. More broadly, it uses the hidden-driver logic common to cancer genomics: pathway activation in the absence of obvious canonical oncogenic events.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The main implication is expansion of targetable alterations in the RTK/RAS/RAF pathway, with increased implementation of MET inhibitors and ERBB2/HER2 inhibitors suggested. Because some oncogene-negative tumors appear to have unexplained MAPK or mTOR activation, the paper also implies that pathway-directed therapy may be relevant beyond currently known genomic biomarkers, but this is presented as a hypothesis rather than validated clinical evidence.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is primarily integrative and computational/associative: independent comparison of 230 TCGA tumors plus 182 previously published LUADs, resequencing confirmation of coding SNVs and indels at 99-100%, GISTIC copy-number analysis, RNA-seq confirmation of MET exon 14 skipping, and RPPA proteomic validation of pathway activity. For RIT1 specifically, functional validation came from NIH3T3 transformation and signaling assays. The study does not report drug-sensitivity or patient-response validation in model systems for the oncogene-negative subset as a whole.
