driver_negative_definition The paper defines driver-negative LUAD as tumors in which the studied canonical driver alterations were not detected. The cohort was first screened with a 13-gene panel including EGFR, KRAS, BRAF, PIK3CA, NRAS, HER2, MET, AKT1, c-KIT, PDGFRA, ALK, RET, and ROS1; EGFR and KRAS were additionally validated by ARMS-PCR and ALK by FISH. Ultimately, patients were classified as driver-gene negative when ALK, EGFR, HER2, KRAS, MET, BRAF, ROS1, and RET mutation negative, with the text emphasizing that these patients could not utilize current mainstream targeted therapy.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The paper does not nominate a classic oncogenic hidden driver such as a kinase mutation or fusion. Instead, it proposes that prognosis and treatment response in driver-negative LUAD are better explained by immune-state heterogeneity captured by immune-related gene pairs, immune cell infiltration, and checkpoint expression differences, suggesting an immune microenvironment-driven subtype structure.
alteration_or_activation_mode Transcriptomic immune-state differences encoded as immune-related gene-pair ordering patterns rather than a specific activating genomic alteration. The implicated signal is a combinatorial expression-state signature (5 IRGPs) with protective/risk stratification value, not a direct mutation, fusion, amplification, or phosphoproteomic activation event.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Immune-related genes and immune checkpoint axis: CD1A|CXCL13, CD1A|S100A7L2, IFNA7|CMTM2, IFNA7|CSF3, CAMP|TFR2; additionally PD-1/PD-L1/CTLA-4 are highlighted as immune-suppression markers. The broader functional theme is tumor-immune microenvironment regulation rather than a canonical oncogenic signaling pathway.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is indirect and transcriptomic/immune-profiling based. The study found 59 DEIRGs between tumor and precancerous tissue, built a 5-IRGP prognostic model, and reported significant differences in immune infiltration by CIBERSORT: low-risk tumors had higher B cell naïve, activated CD4 memory T cells, CD8 T cells, and M1 macrophages, whereas high-risk tumors had higher memory B cells, dendritic cells, and resting Tregs. PD-1, PD-L1, and CTLA-4 were higher in the high-risk group. No pathway activation was shown by phosphoproteomics, IHC for signaling activation, or direct genetic causality.
driver_negative_subtype An immune-defined, transcriptomic risk-stratified subtype within driver-negative LUAD: high-risk versus low-risk groups based on a 5-IRGP score, with a prognostic nomogram combining risk score and tumor stage. The subtype structure is based on transcriptomic immune gene-pair ordering, immune cell composition, checkpoint expression, and clinical stage correlation.
clinical_or_exposure_context The cohort came from 46 resected driver-negative LUAD patients at a single center, with stages I-IV represented. Risk score correlated with tumor stage, N stage, and M stage, but not age or sex. The discussion notes that many driver-negative patients cannot benefit from targeted therapy and that immunotherapy is relevant, though not uniformly effective.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper places its findings in the broader context of immune-related prognostic signatures across other cancers and cites prior IRG/IRGP prognostic models in colorectal cancer, glioma, head and neck squamous cell carcinoma, bladder cancer, colon cancer, and early-stage LUAD. However, it does not present a direct mechanistic parallel to a specific hidden-driver biology in another cancer.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The main therapeutic implication is treatment stratification. Low-risk patients may benefit more from common chemotherapy regimens such as gemcitabine and paclitaxel, while high-risk patients show higher PD-1/PD-L1/CTLA-4 expression and are suggested to be more suitable for immunotherapy. In silico pRRophetic analysis also suggested higher-risk tumors are more sensitive to doxorubicin, epothilone B, gemcitabine, and paclitaxel, based on lower predicted IC50 values.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation was internal and retrospective within one patient cohort. The study used 46 paired LUAD and precancerous tissue samples for microarray/transcriptomic analysis, univariate and multivariate Cox regression, time-dependent ROC analysis, Kaplan–Meier survival analysis, correlation with clinical variables, CIBERSORT immune deconvolution, checkpoint expression comparison, and pRRophetic drug-sensitivity prediction. No cell line, organoid, xenograft, or independent external cohort validation was reported.
