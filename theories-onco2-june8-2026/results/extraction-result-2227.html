oncogene_negative_definition The index tumor was called driver-negative based on clinicopathologic testing for the canonical lung adenocarcinoma markers: EGFR exon 18-21 nucleotide variations by PCR and DNA sequencing, KRAS exon 2 mutations by PCR and DNA sequencing, and EML4-ALK fusion by fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH); all were negative in the primary lung adenocarcinoma tissue. assay_strategy_and_detection_limits Initial clinical negative workup: targeted PCR/DNA sequencing of EGFR and KRAS and FISH for EML4-ALK. Broader discovery workup: whole-genome deep sequencing of blood, primary lung tumor (validation only), liver metastasis, and bone metastasis plus transcriptome sequencing of liver metastasis. Fusion detection used discordant paired-end reads and spanning reads with additional homology, fusion-spanning-read, and fusion-point filters, followed by PCR and Sanger validation of genomic inversion and cDNA fusion junctions. Limitations/blind spots explicitly noted: many lung adenocarcinomas remain unexplained by known markers; ab initio detection of chromosomal rearrangements such as inversions/translocations is challenging from whole-genome sequencing alone; the primary lung FFPE DNA quality was insufficient for discovery-phase WGS; 94.2% of detected fusions were likely adjacent-gene intrachromosomal fusions and often nonfunctional; and the initial clinical assays would not capture RET fusions or cryptic inversions.
missed_driver_reclassified_after_broader_testing Yes. The initially driver-negative tumor was reclassified after integrated WGS and RNA-seq as KIF5B-RET fusion-positive. The rescued alteration was a 10.6-Mb pericentric inversion on chromosome 10 producing a KIF5B-RET fusion transcript; it was missed initially because the clinical workup tested only EGFR/KRAS point mutations and EML4-ALK fusion, not RET fusions or cryptic structural rearrangements.

candidate_driver_gene_or_locus KIF5B-RET fusion on chromosome 10, involving KIF5B (10p11.22) and RET (10q11.21); breakpoints mapped to introns with nucleotide-resolution breakpoint at chr10:32,351,306-42,931,601 (hg18 coordinates reported in paper context).
structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism A 10.6-Mb pericentric inversion joins the 5' portion of KIF5B to the 3' RET kinase region, placing RET under the active KIF5B promoter and preserving the KIF5B coiled-coil dimerization domain, which promotes constitutive dimerization and autophosphorylation of the RET kinase.
functional_consequence Overexpression of the chimeric RET receptor tyrosine kinase and spontaneous cellular transformation. RNA expression showed RET exons downstream of the breakpoint (exons 12-20) were expressed exclusively/strongly in fusion-positive tumor, indicating fusion-driven RET expression rather than natural RET expression.
co_alteration_or_combinatorial_requirement No cooperating mutation was required in the paper; the tumor was otherwise negative for EGFR, KRAS, and EML4-ALK. The authors did note that additional mechanisms might explain RET overexpression in other samples, including negative mutants in RET regulatory domains, genomic amplification, or RET fusions with other partners.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment Found in 3 tumors total in the report: 1 index case (AK55) plus 2 of 20 primary lung adenocarcinomas in replication (LC_S2 and LC_S6). The authors estimated a frequency of about 6% in lung adenocarcinoma, with back-of-envelope estimates of 8% among triple-negative tumors and 4.3% among double-negative tumors; TCGA expression data showed RET overexpression in 3 of 32 samples (9.4%), although that was not specific for KIF5B-RET.
