Paper Title: Exploring the Glycolytic Mechanisms in “Driver Gene-Negative” Lung Adenocarcinoma (LUAD): A Single-Cell RNA Sequencing Approach to Identify the MIF-HIF-1α Axis

Paper Venue: Journal of Cancer

Paper TL;DR: This study elucidated glucose metabolic reprogramming mechanisms and highlighted the MIF-HIF-1α axis as a promising therapeutic target in "driver gene-negative" LUAD, which may offer new avenues for improving outcomes, particularly those lacking conventional targeted therapy options.

Paper Abstract: Background: Patients with "driver gene-negative" LUAD lack effective targeted therapies. This study aimed to elucidate the role of the glycolysis pathway in driver gene-negative LUAD to identify key genes and potential therapeutic targets. Methods: Bulk RNA sequencing data from 49 patients with driver gene-negative LUAD were analyzed. The driver gene-negative status of patients was confirmed by immunoblotting. Gene set enrichment analysis (GSEA) was conducted on six hallmark pathways related to glycolysis. Additionally, key genes were identified and a risk score model was constructed. Finally, single-cell RNA sequencing data were processed using the Seurat package for data cleaning, dimensionality reduction clustering, and cell type identification. Results: GSEA analysis revealed significant enrichment of the glycolysis pathway in driver gene-negative LUAD. Differential expression analysis identified 144 genes associated with the glycolysis pathway. Six glycolysis-related genes (ANKZF1, GPR87, KIF2A, LCT, MIF, SDHC) were identified associated with poor prognosis. Single-cell sequencing analysis validated the key role of MIF in the glycolysis process and revealed a positive feedback regulatory axis between MIF and HIF-1α, which may promoting glycolysis and malignant transformation. Conclusion: This study elucidated glucose metabolic reprogramming mechanisms and highlighted the MIF-HIF-1α axis as a promising therapeutic target in "driver gene-negative" LUAD, which may offer new avenues for improving outcomes, particularly those lacking conventional targeted therapy options.

In this cohort, the absence of these canonical actionable alterations precluded current mainstream targeted therapies; no patients had received antitumor therapy before biopsy.

The paper argues that this pathway state promotes malignant transformation in driver-negative LUAD and may represent the biologic basis of this subtype's aggressiveness and therapy resistance.

pathway_activation_evidence Bulk RNA-seq GSEA showed glycolysis as the only glucose-metabolism hallmark pathway significantly enriched in tumor versus normal tissues. Single-cell GSEA and AUCell scoring showed significantly higher glycolysis in MIF+AT2 cells, and KEGG enrichment of MIF-associated genes highlighted glycolysis/gluconeogenesis, HIF-1 signaling, cell cycle, and carbon metabolism. In patients, MIF and HIF-1α expression were positively correlated, and high HIF-1α expression associated with poorer prognosis; however, the paper does not present phosphoproteomic or direct kinase activity readouts.

The paper further subdivides epithelial cells into a MIF+AT2 subset, which shows increased glycolytic pathway activity and higher inferred malignancy/aneuploidy.
clinical_or_exposure_context The cohort consists of LUAD patients without prior antitumor therapy before biopsy. The paper notes that driver-negative LUAD represents approximately 22%-55% of LUAD and is associated with limited treatment options and poorer prognosis for patients with high MIF or HIF-1α expression; advanced disease was associated with higher MIF expression.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares MIF biology and MIF-HIF-1α feedback to pancreatic cancer, where MIF has been reported to form a positive feedback loop with HIF-1α, and it also cites prior work in driver-negative melanoma, breast, and lung cancer involving HIP as a therapeutic target. It additionally references MIF-related glycolysis and tumor progression across multiple cancers.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The MIF-HIF-1α axis is proposed as a promising therapeutic target in driver-negative LUAD. Preclinical evidence suggests MIF inhibition with ISO-1 reduces MIF- or HIF-1α-driven increases in lactate production, glucose uptake, LDH activity, and cell proliferation in AT2 cells; DMOG was used to stabilize/activate HIF-1α. The study frames this as a potential strategy for patients lacking conventional targeted therapy options.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation was performed using bulk RNA-seq from 49 driver-negative LUAD patients, single-cell RNA-seq from tumors/adjacent tissues of four driver-negative patients, and cell-based experiments in normal human AT2 cells. MIF overexpression, MIF inhibition (ISO-1), and HIF-1α stabilization (DMOG) were used to test effects on glycolysis and proliferation, with H1703 and Calu-3 lung cancer cell lines serving as positive controls for higher glycolytic/proliferative states. The study also used ROC and survival analyses to validate prognostic modeling, but direct interaction between MIF and HIF-1α remained inferential rather than biochemically proven.
