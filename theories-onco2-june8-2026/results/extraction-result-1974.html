brief_description The paper defines three reproducible LUAD subtypes from drug-response-associated genes derived from PRISM and GDSC pharmacogenomics data: S-I, S-II, and S-III. They differ in prognosis, immune landscape, pathway activity, and predicted drug sensitivity.

mention_or_use use
driver_negative_definition This paper does not define oncogene-negative or driver-negative LUAD by excluding canonical driver genes in a molecular diagnostic sense. Instead, it frames the setting as LUAD patients 'without driver mutations' in the introduction, where platinum chemotherapy is standard, and then builds subtypes from 195 drug response-associated genes identified by comparing sensitive versus resistant LUAD cell lines across nine drugs in PRISM and GDSC. No explicit assay-based definition of driver-negative status is provided beyond that general clinical context.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The dominant hidden-driver logic is subtype-specific pathway-state biology rather than a single canonical oncogenic alteration. S-I is inflammation/proliferation/immune-evasion driven, S-II is proliferative and genetics-driven, and S-III is metabolic and methylation-driven.
alteration_or_activation_mode Integrated expression-signature state, with supporting evidence from mutation frequency, copy-number alteration burden, and DNA methylation-driven genes. S-II appears to be driven by high mutation burden and widespread copy-number alterations, while S-III is associated with DNA methylation-based regulation; S-I is characterized mainly by inflammatory and proliferative pathway activation.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Inflammatory pathways (complement, inflammatory response, interferon signaling, interleukins, neutrophil degranulation), proliferative programs (MYC targets, MTORC1 signaling, G2M checkpoint, E2F targets, telomere maintenance, DNA replication), metabolic pathways (bile acid, fatty acid, surfactant, heme, phospholipid metabolism), plus genetics-related TP53 loss and DNA methylation-driven genes such as ADHFE1 and SFTA3.
pathway_activation_evidence Pathway activity was inferred by QuSAGE, Reactome enrichment, and ssGSEA across TCGA and seven GEO cohorts. S-I showed high inflammation and proliferation scores; S-II showed high proliferation scores and highest TMB/CNA burden; S-III showed high metabolic signature scores and enrichment of methylation-driven genes. The paper does not report phosphoproteomic markers such as pERK or pAKT.
driver_negative_subtype Three integrated transcriptomic/pharmacogenomic subtypes were proposed, but they are not explicitly labeled as driver-negative subtypes. Within the clinically relevant 'no driver mutation' context, S-I is immune-hot/immune-evasive and inflammatory-proliferative, S-II is immune-cold and genetics-driven, and S-III is moderate immune infiltration and metabolic/methylation-driven.
clinical_or_exposure_context The paper links subtype distribution to stage, survival status, and accepted LUAD molecular subtypes (TCGA 2014 TRU/PI/PP). S-I is associated with worse prognosis and advanced stage, S-III with best prognosis and earlier stage, and S-II with intermediate prognosis. The paper does not specifically analyze smoking status, sex, or never-smoker status as drivers of these subtypes.
cross_cancer_parallel The main cross-cancer parallel is melanoma: SubMap compared LUAD subtypes to melanoma patients receiving anti-CTLA-4/PD-1 therapy with different immunotherapy responses, and S-I showed similarity to responders. The paper also cites general cancer-immunity-cycle and tumor-heterogeneity concepts rather than a specific other driver-negative cancer type.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Subtype-guided therapy is the key implication. S-I was predicted to be sensitive to immune checkpoint blockers, doxorubicin, tipifarnib, AZ628, and AZD6244; S-II to cisplatin, camptothecin, roscovitine, and A.443654; S-III to docetaxel, paclitaxel, vinorelbine, and BIBW2992. Evidence came from SubMap, ssGSEA of DRGs, CMap, and pRRophetic; it is primarily computational/predictive rather than prospectively clinical.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation was done by internal cluster stability metrics, survival analysis in TCGA, and replication across seven independent GEO cohorts using IGP and SubMap. The authors also validated subtype-specific pathway patterns, immune landscapes, mutation/CNA/methylation features, and drug-sensitivity predictions computationally; no in vitro, in vivo, or prospective patient-treatment validation was performed in this paper.
