driver_negative_definition Lung adenocarcinoma cases described as lacking a clinically actionable genetic alteration and, in the specific profiled case, lacking hotspot mutations in KRAS, EGFR, BRAF, ERBB2, or PIK3CA; lacking ALK, ROS1, or RET fusions; and lacking any other known oncogenic alterations by whole-genome sequencing and RNA sequencing. The paper also notes broad low-level copy-number gains of sorafenib targets, but no high-level focal amplifications and no rare deleterious germline coding mutations in sorafenib target genes.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism A rare oncogenic ARAF mutation, with related recurrent RAF1 hotspot mutations, is proposed as the hidden driver mechanism in otherwise driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma. The broader idea is that rare but recurrent somatic mutations in RAF family members can explain oncogenicity and sorafenib responsiveness in tumors lacking canonical drivers.
alteration_or_activation_mode Rare somatic missense mutation at a RAF CR2-domain phosphorylation site: ARAF p.S214C (and related ARAF p.S214 variants; RAF1 p.S257/p.S259 variants in other tumors). The mechanism is loss of negative regulation via reduced 14-3-3 binding, leading to enhanced MAPK signaling, transformation, and drug sensitivity.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway ARAF/RAF1 within the Ras-Raf-MEK-ERK MAP kinase pathway, specifically a CR2-domain phosphorylation/14-3-3 regulatory axis.
pathway_activation_evidence Mutant ARAF in AALE cells increased phospho-MEK and mutant RAF1 variants increased MEK/ERK phosphorylation; the paper also reports preliminary biochemical evidence that ARAF p.S214C markedly attenuates 14-3-3 binding. In the patient tumor, ARAF S214C was present in tumor DNA and highly expressed in tumor RNA, supporting functional expression of the alteration; the activation appears genetically explained by the mutation rather than by a separate unexplained pathway state.
driver_negative_subtype A rare RAF-family mutant subset of driver-negative LUAD, defined genomically by absence of canonical lung adenocarcinoma oncogenes but presence of ARAF codon 214 or RAF1 codon 257/259 mutations. It is not presented as a transcriptomic or immune subtype, but as a low-frequency integrated genomic/functional subtype within otherwise oncogene-negative LUAD.
clinical_or_exposure_context The index patient was a 66-year-old light former smoker with stage IV lung adenocarcinoma and an exceptional, near-complete 5-year response to oral sorafenib after multiple prior therapies. Across the TCGA and related lung adenocarcinoma data, 4 of 6 cases with ARAF/RAF1 hotspot mutations lacked mutations in known lung adenocarcinoma oncogenes; the authors also note all TCGA lung cancer samples analyzed were treatment-naive.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper compares the RAF hot spot to BRAF kinase-domain hot spots in lung adenocarcinoma and notes analogous mutations in colorectal adenocarcinoma, gastric adenocarcinoma, and cutaneous melanoma, as well as RAF1 mutations reported in ovarian cancer and Noonan syndrome. It also cites C-RAF mutations as conferring RAF inhibitor resistance in melanoma cells, although that context differs from the sorafenib-sensitive cases here.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Suggests ARAF/RAF1 mutations may be biomarkers of sorafenib response in lung adenocarcinoma and implies sensitivity to sorafenib and also to MEK inhibition with trametinib. The evidence is preclinical plus a compelling single-patient clinical outlier response; the authors caution that additional profiling and functional validation are needed before clinical use.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation includes whole-genome sequencing and RNA-seq of the index patient tumor/normal samples, recurrence analysis in independent lung adenocarcinoma and pan-cancer cohorts including TCGA, and functional assays in immortalized airway epithelial AALE cells plus NIH-3T3 cells. The mutant alleles increased anchorage-independent growth and MEK/ERK signaling, and these effects were inhibited by sorafenib and trametinib; the paper also notes that naturally occurring mutant cell lines were not found and that knockdown/inhibitor studies would be useful for definitive endogenous validation.
