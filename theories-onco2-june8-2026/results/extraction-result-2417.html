The study instead profiled early lesions (AAH, AIS, MIA) in a cohort that was mostly nonsmokers and identified somatic mutations by WES and transcriptomes by bulk RNA-seq, but it did not establish a separate 'negative' category by excluding canonical drivers. candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism A hidden-driver-like mechanism is not argued in the driver-negative sense. The main mechanistic model is subtype-specific tumor microenvironment remodeling during early progression, especially angiogenesis in late AIS (AIS3) involving tip endothelial cells and matrix CAFs, with coordinated immune changes and progression toward invasion. The paper also notes positive selection of EGFR and TP53 and AIS-enriched BRAF/KRAS, so it does not support a driver-negative paradigm.
alteration_or_activation_mode Subtype-associated cell-state shifts and nonimmune cell composition changes, particularly angiogenesis-related enrichment rather than a single occult genomic alteration. implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Tip endothelial cell program, angiogenesis, matrix CAF (mCAF) program, EMT-related pathways, and tumor microenvironment/ecosystem state. The paper explicitly links mCAFs to matrix/collagen proteins and upregulation of TGF-β, KRAS, and EMT pathways; it also links EGFR mutations to VEGF/angiogenesis and TP53 loss to EMT.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence comes from bulk RNA-seq ssGSEA of nonimmune cell markers, scRNA-seq reference mapping, SCISSOR enrichment of tip ECs and mCAFs in AIS3-associated samples, and spatial transcriptomics showing EC enrichment in invasive MIA regions and fibroblast/plasma-cell spatial localization. The paper does not report phosphoproteomic readouts or IHC for pathway activation, so activation is inferred from transcriptomic/cell-state signatures rather than direct phosphorylation assays.

The closest subtype structure is the radiology/pathology-linked molecular subdivision of AIS into AIS1, AIS2, and AIS3 and MIA_nonInv into two subtypes, with AIS3 characterized by enrichment of angiogenesis-associated tip ECs and mCAFs and by transition toward non-pGGO behavior and invasion.
clinical_or_exposure_context Mostly East Asian, mostly never-smoker cohort with early-stage lung adenocarcinoma. The subtype framework is linked to CT phenotype (pGGO versus non-pGGO), histology (AAH, AIS, MIA), lesion size, and progression risk; the paper also notes that AAH and AIS are often pGGO and that AIS3 correlates with non-pGGO and likely invasion.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper parallels broader cancer literature on angiogenesis, CAF-driven EMT, and vessel normalization rather than another specific driver-negative cancer. therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications are indirect and hypothesis-generating: the authors suggest that antiangiogenic agents and vessel-normalization strategies could be early-intervention approaches for lung adenocarcinoma, potentially preventing progression and facilitating immune infiltration.
