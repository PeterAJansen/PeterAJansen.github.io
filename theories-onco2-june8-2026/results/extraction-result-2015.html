driver_negative_definition No explicit definition of oncogene-negative or driver-negative LUAD is provided. The study instead analyzes early-stage lesions in never-smokers using WES and RNA-seq and reports that EGFR and TP53 mutations are positively selected during progression, while BRAF and KRAS are enriched in AIS. Thus, it does not frame the cohort as driver-negative; rather, it characterizes molecular changes across lesions with known and known-like driver alterations.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main mechanistic hypothesis is microenvironment- and cell-state-driven progression rather than a hidden canonical driver: late AIS (especially AIS3) is proposed to involve angiogenesis with tip endothelial cells and EMT-driving cancer-associated fibroblasts (mCAFs), which then coincides with non-pGGO phenotype and precedes invasion into MIA. The paper also highlights coordinated immune infiltration changes and suggests a two-phase progression model.
alteration_or_activation_mode Pathway activation is inferred primarily from transcriptomic cell-type enrichment and ssGSEA signatures rather than from a novel mutation class. The implicated mechanism is increased abundance/activity of tip ECs and mCAFs, with evidence of positive selection of EGFR and TP53 mutations and enrichment of BRAF/KRAS mutations in AIS1, but no claim of a hidden alteration in oncogene-negative tumors.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Angiogenesis/tip endothelial cell axis; CAF programs, especially matrix CAFs; EMT-related signaling; EGFR–VEGF–angiogenesis axis; TP53-loss-associated EMT; BRAF/KRAS-associated proliferation. These are discussed as progression-associated pathways rather than as a single driver-negative mechanism.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence comes from ssGSEA scores, nonimmune-cell-marker PCA, SCISSOR enrichment, and spatial transcriptomics. EC and stromal ssGSEA scores decrease from normal to AAH/AIS1/2 and then rise in AIS3 and MIA_Inv; AIS3-associated ECs are enriched for tip EC markers; mCAFs co-vary with tip ECs; spatial transcriptomics shows higher ECs in invasive MIA regions and plasma cells in tumor regions. The paper does not use pERK/pAKT/pS6 or phosphoproteomics.
driver_negative_subtype No explicit driver-negative subtype is defined. The closest subtype structure is the molecular stratification of lesions into AIS1, AIS2, AIS3, MIA_nonInv1, MIA_nonInv2, and corresponding MIA_Inv1/MIA_Inv2, based on nonimmune cell markers and radiological phenotype. AIS3 is the most biologically distinctive subtype, associated with angiogenesis, tip ECs, mCAFs, and non-pGGO components.
clinical_or_exposure_context The cohort is largely East Asian and never-smoker enriched (92% nonsmokers; 69% female; median age 57). The study focuses on early-stage (0 or I) lung adenocarcinoma and precancerous lesions (AAH, AIS, MIA), with radiological distinctions between pGGO and non-pGGO nodules. It emphasizes never-smoker East Asian disease biology rather than smoking-associated LUAD.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper does not present a cross-cancer hidden-driver analogy for driver-negative LUAD. It does compare its EcoTyper-derived cell ecosystems to pan-cancer ecotypes from the EcoTyper reference study, noting CE6 as normal-like and CE1/CE8/CE10 as carcinoma-associated, but this is not framed as a driver-negative parallel.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications are indirect and tied to angiogenesis and vessel normalization rather than to a specific hidden driver in driver-negative LUAD. The authors suggest antiangiogenic agents and vessel-normalizing therapies as potential early interventions that may prevent progression and facilitate immune-cell infiltration. This is a conceptual implication, not a tested treatment result in this paper.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is multi-layered but not functional in the sense of perturbation experiments. The paper validates subtype/cell-state findings with independent scRNA-seq datasets, deconvolution analyses, SCISSOR-based bulk-single-cell integration, and spatial transcriptomics on two MIA samples. It also compares mutation patterns with external cohorts (TCGA and Chen2020). No cell-line, organoid, xenograft, GEMM, knockdown, rescue, or drug-sensitivity validation is reported.
