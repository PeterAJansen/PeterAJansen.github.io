candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism A rare, previously uncharacterized oncogenic RAF-family mutation is proposed as the hidden driver mechanism in an otherwise driver-negative tumor, specifically ARAF S214C. The paper argues that rare ARAF/RAF1 hotspot mutations can underlie oncogenicity and sorafenib responsiveness in a subset of driver-negative LUAD rather than a broader pathway state alone.
alteration_or_activation_mode Rare somatic missense mutation in a conserved regulatory residue of ARAF (p.S214C), with related rare missense mutations in paralogous RAF1 residues (p.S257/p.S259) in independent cases. The mutant allele is expressed, transforms cells, increases MEK/ERK signaling, and is sensitive to RAF/MEK inhibition.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway RAF family / MAPK signaling axis, especially ARAF and RAF1 acting upstream of MEK and ERK; the paper also discusses 14-3-3-mediated negative regulation of RAF activation and suggests that mutation may abrogate ARAF-14-3-3 binding.
pathway_activation_evidence Functional activation is supported by increased phospho-MEK in AALE cells expressing mutant ARAF, increased MEK/ERK phosphorylation with mutant RAF1 alleles, and biochemical evidence that ARAF p.S214C markedly attenuates 14-3-3 binding. The pathway activation is genetically explained by the mutations rather than being presented as unexplained non-genetic activation.
driver_negative_subtype A rare 'Raf-family-mutant' subset of driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma is implicitly defined by ARAF codon 214 and RAF1 codon 257/259 mutations. The subtype is genomically defined, low-frequency (~1%), and identified through integrated genome and RNA sequencing plus pan-cancer comparison.
clinical_or_exposure_context The index case was a 66-year-old light former smoker with stage IV lung adenocarcinoma who had failed multiple regimens before experiencing a near-complete and prolonged response to sorafenib for about 5 years. The paper also notes that all TCGA lung cancer samples with these RAF-family mutations were treatment-naive.
cross_cancer_parallel The same putative RAF-family hotspot is reported across multiple tumor types, including colorectal adenocarcinoma, gastric adenocarcinoma, and cutaneous melanoma; RAF1 p.S257/p.S259 mutations are also noted in ovarian cancer and Noonan syndrome as a germline Ras-pathway condition. The paper also references BRAF hotspot biology in melanoma and lung adenocarcinoma as a related RAF-family example.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The paper suggests ARAF/RAF1 mutations may serve as biomarkers of sorafenib response and may confer inhibitor-sensitive oncogene dependency. It also reports trametinib sensitivity in mutant ARAF/RAF1-expressing cells, implying potential vulnerability to MEK inhibition; however, these implications are presented as preclinical and require further clinical validation.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation includes whole-genome sequencing and RNA-seq of the responder's tumor, replication in independent lung adenocarcinoma and TCGA cohorts, and functional testing in immortalized tracheobronchial epithelial AALE cells and NIH-3T3 cells. Evidence includes soft agar transformation, phospho-MEK/pERK immunoblotting, dose-response inhibition by sorafenib and trametinib, and partial biochemical support for disrupted 14-3-3 binding.
