brief_description A novel somatic fusion joining the first six exons of CD74 to the exons encoding the EGF-like domain of the NRG1 III-β3 isoform. The fusion is recurrently found in invasive mucinous lung adenocarcinomas that are negative for canonical driver alterations and appears to function as an oncogenic ligand-presenting alteration.

driver_negative_definition The paper defines driver-negative or pan-negative lung adenocarcinoma as tumors lacking known oncogenic alterations after testing for canonical alterations including KRAS and EGFR mutations, and in the validation cohort additionally being wild-type/negative for BRAF, ERBB2, ALK, ROS, and RET. The cohort was assessed by transcriptome sequencing, chromosomal copy-number analysis, RT-PCR for the fusion, FISH confirmation, and targeted capture genomic sequencing to exclude known drivers and validate rearrangements.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The proposed hidden driver is a rare oncogenic fusion that creates a membrane-bound NRG1 ligand presentation state, driving ERBB receptor signaling rather than a kinase-activating mutation. The authors argue this represents ligand-dependent activation of an ERBB2-ERBB3 receptor complex, leading to downstream PI3K-AKT pathway stimulation and oncogenic growth.
alteration_or_activation_mode Somatic gene fusion with membrane-tethered presentation of an intact EGF-like NRG1 domain; not secretion of soluble ligand. The CD74 portion replaces the native NRG1 transmembrane domain, producing a cell-surface ligand-like fusion protein that can activate receptor heterodimers in a juxtacrine/paracrine-like manner.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway NRG1–ERBB3/ERBB2 signaling axis, with downstream PI3K-AKT and S6K activation. The paper specifically implicates phosphorylated ERBB3, phosphorylated ERBB2, p-AKT, and p-S6K as functional readouts of this pathway.
pathway_activation_evidence In patient tumors, phospho-ERBB3 staining was strongly enriched in fusion-positive cases and was detected in all five CD74-NRG1-positive invasive mucinous adenocarcinomas versus 6/241 unselected adenocarcinomas (P < 0.0001). In cell lines, ectopic CD74-NRG1 expression increased p-ERBB2, p-ERBB3, p-AKT, and p-S6K; the effect depended on the EGF-like domain and was reduced by truncation lacking that domain. The activation was not explained by ERBB1 expression, which was low in the index case.
driver_negative_subtype Invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma (IMA) emerges as the explicit subtype enriched for CD74-NRG1 among driver-negative LUAD. The subtype is defined histologically and clinically, with the fusion occurring specifically in women who never smoked; in the authors' limited IMA cohort, CD74-NRG1 accounted for 4/15 cases (27%) and KRAS mutations for 6/15 (40%).
clinical_or_exposure_context All five CD74-NRG1-positive tumors were invasive mucinous adenocarcinomas in women who had never smoked. The paper notes that invasive mucinous LUAD is otherwise strongly associated with KRAS mutations and often presents with multifocal unresectable disease; the validation cohort was from an Asian population, and the frequency may differ in Caucasians.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly frames CD74 as a partner seen in recurrent ROS1 fusions in lung adenocarcinoma and compares the mechanism to wild-type NRG1 signaling through ERBB receptors. It also references broader ERBB network biology and prior work on heregulin-dependent HER2/HER3 signaling, situating the finding within a general ligand-driven ERBB activation paradigm rather than a lung-specific kinase-fusion paradigm.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The authors propose CD74-NRG1 as a therapeutic opportunity because it may create dependence on ERBB2-ERBB3 signaling and downstream PI3K-AKT signaling. They explicitly mention available drugs targeting ERBB2, ERBB3, and downstream pathways as potential interventions, but the paper does not test drug response directly; the implication is preclinical/biomarker-guided.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is multi-layered: discovery by transcriptome sequencing in 25 never-smoker LUADs; RT-PCR screening of 102 additional pan-negative LUADs; break-apart and fusion FISH confirmation; hybrid-capture genomic sequencing of the index case; immunohistochemistry on a tissue microarray of 241 adenocarcinomas for p-ERBB3; and functional modeling in NIH-3T3, H2052, H322, H1568, and Ba/F3 cells. Overexpression of CD74-NRG1 activated ERBB signaling and PI3K-AKT, and increased soft-agar colony formation in H1568 cells; truncation of the EGF-like domain abrogated signaling and growth effects.
