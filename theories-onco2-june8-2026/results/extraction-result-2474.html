Paper Title: Multi-omics and clustering analyses reveal the mechanisms underlying unmet needs for patients with lung adenocarcinoma and identify potential therapeutic targets

Paper Venue: Molecular Cancer

Paper TL;DR: The persistent challenges of identifying alterations or driver mutations in coding regions related to tumorigenesis are overcome through a novel approach combining multi-omics data with clinical information to reveal the molecular mechanisms underlying non-CAGAs LUAD, stratify patients to improve patient prognosis, and identify potential therapeutic targets.

Paper Abstract: The cancer genome contains several driver mutations. However, in some cases, no known drivers have been identified; these remaining areas of unmet needs, leading to limited progress in cancer therapy. Whole-genome sequencing (WGS) can identify non-coding alterations associated with the disease. Consequently, exploration of non-coding regions using WGS and other omics data such as ChIP-sequencing (ChIP-seq) to discern novel alterations and mechanisms related to tumorigenesis have been attractive these days. Integrated multi-omics analyses, including WGS, ChIP-seq, DNA methylation, and RNA-sequencing (RNA-seq), were conducted on samples from patients with non-clinically actionable genetic alterations (non-CAGAs) in lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD). Second-level cluster analysis was performed to reinforce the correlations associated with patient survival, as identified by RNA-seq. Subsequent differential gene expression analysis was performed to identify potential druggable targets. Differences in H3K27ac marks in non-CAGAs LUAD were found and confirmed by analyzing RNA-seq data, in which mastermind-like transcriptional coactivator 2 (MAML2) was suppressed. The down-regulated genes whose expression was correlated to MAML2 expression were associated with patient prognosis. WGS analysis revealed somatic mutations associated with the H3K27ac marks in the MAML2 region and high levels of DNA methylation in MAML2 were observed in tumor samples. The second-level cluster analysis enabled patient stratification and subsequent analyses identified potential therapeutic target genes and treatment options. We overcome the persistent challenges of identifying alterations or driver mutations in coding regions related to tumorigenesis through a novel approach combining multi-omics data with clinical information to reveal the molecular mechanisms underlying non-CAGAs LUAD, stratify patients to improve patient prognosis, and identify potential therapeutic targets. This approach may be applicable to studies of other cancers with unmet needs.

implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway MAML2-centered regulatory axis involving Notch and Wnt/beta-catenin signaling; the downstream genes discussed include BCL2, CDKN1, ERBB2, CCND3, HES1, HEY1, NFKB1, CD44, CTNNB1, FN1, MMP7, SMYD3, VEGFA, CCND1, MYC, and prognosis-linked markers FAT4, HMCN1, CD302, UTRN, and FOXN3. The broader network analysis also points to PCNX1 and FTO as hub genes.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is mainly suppressive rather than activated signaling: H3K27ac enhancer activity at the MAML2 locus was ablated in a significant fraction of non-CAGA samples, MAML2 RNA expression was reduced in tumors versus adjacent normal tissue, and DNA methylation in the MAML2 region was high in tumors and inversely correlated with MAML2 expression. Several MAML2-correlated downstream genes were differentially expressed, and WGCNA linked MAML2 with co-expression modules, but the paper does not report direct pathway activity readouts such as phosphoproteomics, pERK, or pAKT.
driver_negative_subtype The paper proposes a non-CAGA LUAD subtype characterized by MAML2 down-regulation, loss of H3K27ac enhancer activity at the MAML2 locus, MAML2-region hypermethylation, and survival-associated expression programs. A second-level cluster-defined poor-survival subtype is also described, distinguished by higher mutation burden, more TP53 mutations, more smoking, more advanced stage, and distinct DEG/pathway enrichment.
clinical_or_exposure_context The high-risk second-level cluster had more smoking, advanced cancer stage, and higher tumor mutation burden; TP53 mutations were common in this group. Prognostic markers were tested in a Korean dataset and in a non-CAGA-like TCGA LUAD dataset, with CD302 and FOXN3 remaining prognostic across cohorts, suggesting cross-ethnic robustness.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares the mechanism to mucoepidermoid carcinoma, where CRTC1-MAML2 is an oncogenic driver and where combined Notch and EGFR targeting (DBZ plus Erlotinib) was effective in preclinical treatment. It also cites breast cancer literature for MAML2 epigenetic regulation and DNMT3B involvement, and notes PCNX1 associations in breast cancer and FTO involvement across cancers.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications include rescuing MAML2 expression, targeting DNA methylation to relieve MAML2 silencing, and targeting prognostic-marker-associated genes from the DEG/WGCNA analyses. The paper also suggests that immune checkpoint inhibitors might be an option for the poor-survival/high-TMB subgroup; these proposals are hypothesis-generating rather than validated in LUAD models or patients.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is multimodal and correlative: matched normal-versus-tumor comparisons, an internal LUAD cohort, an independent Korean dataset (GSE8894), and a non-CAGA-like TCGA LUAD dataset. The claims are supported by WGS, ChIP-seq, RNA-seq, DNA methylation profiling, Kaplan-Meier survival analyses, correlation analysis, and WGCNA, but no cell-line, organoid, xenograft, or rescue experiments are reported in this paper.
