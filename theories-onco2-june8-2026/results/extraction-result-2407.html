driver_negative_definition Tumors from LUAD patients were called "driver gene negative" when testing failed to detect ALK, EGFR, HER2, KRAS, MET, BRAF, ROS1, and RET mutations/alterations using a 13-gene panel screen on FFPE tissues, followed by confirmatory assays for EGFR and KRAS with ARMS-PCR and ALK with FISH. The paper operationally states these cases "could not utilize current mainstream targeted therapy."

The paper also discusses CXCL13, CSF3, CD1A, IFNA7, CAMP, CMTM2, S100A7L2, and TFR2 as biologically relevant genes.
pathway_activation_evidence RNA microarray-derived differential expression of immune-related genes, CIBERSORT-based immune infiltration differences, and higher PD-1/PD-L1/CTLA-4 expression in the high-risk group. No direct phosphoproteomic or kinase-activity evidence is provided, and pathway activation is not genetically explained.
driver_negative_subtype An immune-stratified transcriptomic subtype within driver-gene-negative LUAD defined by a 5-IRGP risk score, separating high-risk and low-risk groups. The subtype is based on integrated transcriptomic/clinical/immune-context features, not on a distinct genomic lesion.
clinical_or_exposure_context Patients had LUAD with clinical stages I-IV; sample set included 46 paired tumor/precancerous specimens after exclusions. Clinical variables associated with risk score included tumor stage, N stage, and M stage; age and gender were not correlated with risk score. The paper frames this as a subgroup that cannot benefit from standard targeted therapy, with potential relevance for immunotherapy and chemotherapy selection.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper makes broad comparisons to other cancers and prior immune-gene prognostic models, but does not identify a specific cross-cancer hidden-driver analogue for driver-negative LUAD. It cites prior work in colorectal cancer, glioma, head and neck squamous cell carcinoma, bladder cancer, and lung adenocarcinoma immune signatures.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The signature is proposed to help guide treatment choice in driver-negative LUAD. Low-risk patients may benefit more from chemotherapy agents such as gemcitabine and paclitaxel, whereas high-risk patients show higher PD-1/PD-L1/CTLA-4 expression and are suggested by the authors to potentially be more sensitive to immunotherapy; these are inferential, not clinically validated, implications.

Validation is statistical and computational: univariate/multivariate Cox regression, time-dependent ROC/AUC, Kaplan-Meier survival, CIBERSORT immune deconvolution, checkpoint expression comparison, and pRRophetic drug-sensitivity prediction. No cell-line, organoid, xenograft, or mechanistic perturbation validation is reported.
