Paper Title: A functional taxonomy of tumor suppression in oncogenic KRAS-driven lung cancer

Paper Venue: Cancer Discovery

Paper TL;DR: A multiplexed mouse model of oncogenic KRAS-driven lung cancer is used to quantify the impact of forty-eight known and putative tumor suppressor genes on diverse aspects of carcinogenesis at an unprecedented scale and resolution and uncover many previously understudied functional tumor suppressors that constrain cancer in vivo.

Paper Abstract: Cancer genotyping has identified a large number of putative tumor suppressor genes. Carcinogenesis is a multi-step process, however the importance and specific roles of many of these genes during tumor initiation, growth and progression remain unknown. Here we use a multiplexed mouse model of oncogenic KRAS-driven lung cancer to quantify the impact of forty-eight known and putative tumor suppressor genes on diverse aspects of carcinogenesis at an unprecedented scale and resolution. We uncover many previously understudied functional tumor suppressors that constrain cancer in vivo. Inactivation of some genes substantially increased growth, while the inactivation of others increases tumor initiation and/or the emergence of exceptionally large tumors. These functional in vivo analyses revealed an unexpectedly complex landscape of tumor suppression that has implications for understanding cancer evolution, interpreting clinical cancer genome sequencing data, and directing approaches to limit tumor initiation and progression.

driver_negative_definition The paper does not define oncogene-negative or driver-negative LUAD as its primary experimental setting; instead, it focuses on KRAS-driven tumors. It does note that candidate tumor suppressor genes were prioritized using human LUAD datasets and mutation-pattern criteria intended to distinguish tumor suppressors from oncogenes, including pan-cancer/LUAD mutational frequency, co-occurrence with KRAS, mutation dispersion versus hotspots, and truncating-versus-missense patterns from TCGA, GENIE, TRACERx, and COSMIC. 'Negative' in the sequencing-panel context refers to genes not included or not profiled in some clinical panels, but not to driver-negative tumors.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main mechanistic theme is loss of tumor suppressors rather than a hidden oncogenic driver. The paper argues that different tumor-suppressive pathways constrain distinct phases of carcinogenesis: tumor initiation, overall growth, and emergence of exceptionally large tumors, with examples including PI3K/mTOR, RAS signaling, COMPASS/chromatin regulation, DNA methylation, and cohesin-related mechanisms.
alteration_or_activation_mode Functional inactivation of tumor suppressor genes by CRISPR-mediated sgRNA knockout or Cre/lox deletion; the paper emphasizes loss-of-function rather than an alternative activating event. In the broader conceptual framework, driver discovery relies on protein-truncating mutation patterns and mutation hotspots, but the experimental alterations are mostly deletions/inactivations.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Multiple axes are implicated, including STAG2/cohesin, PTEN/PI3K-AKT, TSC1-mTOR, NF1/RASA1-RAS signaling, p53/Rb pathway, DNMT3A/DNA methylation, and COMPASS complex-mediated H3K4 methylation (KDM6A, NCOA6, KMT2C, KMT2D). RNF43/Wnt receptor regulation is also highlighted.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is indirect and phenotype-based in this paper, not a phosphoproteomic or transcriptomic pathway-activation map for driver-negative LUAD. For STAG2, the authors specifically report that immunohistochemistry and analysis of canonical target genes do not support chromosomal instability, DNA damage, MEK/ERK, or cGAS/STING as major drivers of the observed growth increase. For KDM6A, sex-comparative effects suggest loss of enzymatic function. Overall, no dedicated activation marker is used to define a driver-negative state.
driver_negative_subtype No explicit driver-negative LUAD subtype is defined. The study instead stratifies KRAS-driven lung adenocarcinoma by functional phenotype: genes that alter tumor initiation, broad growth, or heavy-tailed rare-giant-tumor behavior. Human STAG2-low/negative tumors are described as more poorly differentiated and more advanced, but not as a named molecular subtype.
clinical_or_exposure_context The paper’s only explicit human clinical context is lung adenocarcinoma cohorts from TCGA/GENIE/TRACERx and a 479-case STAG2 immunohistochemistry series. STAG2-low/negative tumors were often more poorly differentiated and more advanced. No smoking, never-smoker, ancestry, or prior-treatment stratification is reported here.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper repeatedly compares the identified functional suppressors to other cancers: RNF43 deficiency and porcupine-inhibitor sensitivity in colorectal/pancreatic cancer; STAG2 as a tumor suppressor in bladder cancer and other cancers; KDM6A/UTX in pancreatic cancer and sex-specific biology; and DNMT3A/CDKN2A effects previously observed in Kras-driven tumors. It also contrasts in vivo mouse findings with DepMap cell-line dependencies, showing context dependence across cancer models.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications are indirect but important: RNF43 loss is noted to sensitize cells to porcupine inhibitors in other cancers, suggesting Wnt-pathway vulnerability. The paper also suggests that identifying genes controlling initiation, growth, or exceptional growth may inform cancer prevention, early detection, and therapeutic targeting. For STAG2, the authors do not identify a specific drug vulnerability in this paper, but they note prior evidence linking cohesin defects to synthetic lethality and pathway alterations.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated in multiplexed autochthonous GEMMs using Tuba-seq with Cas9-positive and Cas9-negative mice, plus single-gene validation for STAG2 using both Cre/lox Stag2flox alleles and individual sgRNAs. Validation includes tumor burden, tumor number, size distributions, survival, histology, IHC for STAG2 and signaling markers, human LUAD IHC for STAG2 (479 tumors), and comparison with DepMap cell-line data. The study’s validation is strong for functional tumor-suppressor effects but does not validate a driver-negative subtype.
