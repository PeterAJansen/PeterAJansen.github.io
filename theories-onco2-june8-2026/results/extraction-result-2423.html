Paper Title: Druggable Oncogene Fusions in Invasive Mucinous Lung Adenocarcinoma

Paper Venue: Clinical Cancer Research

Paper TL;DR: Oncogenic fusions act as driver mutations in IMAs without KRAS mutations, and thus represent promising therapeutic targets for the treatment of such IMAs.

Paper Abstract: Purpose: To identify druggable oncogenic fusions in invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma (IMA) of the lung, a malignant type of lung adenocarcinoma in which KRAS mutations frequently occur. Experimental Design: From an IMA cohort of 90 cases, consisting of 56 cases (62%) with KRAS mutations and 34 cases without (38%), we conducted whole-transcriptome sequencing of 32 IMAs, including 27 cases without KRAS mutations. We used the sequencing data to identify gene fusions, and then performed functional analyses of the fusion gene products. Results: We identified oncogenic fusions that occurred mutually exclusively with KRAS mutations: CD74-NRG1, SLC3A2-NRG1, EZR-ERBB4, TRIM24-BRAF, and KIAA1468-RET. NRG1 fusions were present in 17.6% (6/34) of KRAS-negative IMAs. The CD74-NRG1 fusion activated HER2:HER3 signaling, whereas the EZR-ERBB4 and TRIM24-BRAF fusions constitutively activated the ERBB4 and BRAF kinases, respectively. Signaling pathway activation and fusion-induced anchorage-independent growth/tumorigenicity of NIH3T3 cells expressing these fusions were suppressed by tyrosine kinase inhibitors approved for clinical use. Conclusions: Oncogenic fusions act as driver mutations in IMAs without KRAS mutations, and thus represent promising therapeutic targets for the treatment of such IMAs. Clin Cancer Res; 20(12); 3087–93. ©2014 AACR.

driver_negative_definition In this study, driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma was operationalized as invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma (IMA) negative for representative driver aberrations in LADC. The cohort first underwent mutation testing and fusion testing; among 34 KRAS-negative IMAs, some had BRAF mutation, EGFR mutation, or EML4-ALK fusion, while the remaining 30 were 'pan negative' for representative driver aberrations. These pan-negative cases were then subjected to whole-transcriptome RNA sequencing, and fusion calls were validated by RT-PCR/Sanger sequencing; in the broader cohort, RT-PCR screening was used to test additional cases.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver hypothesis is that ostensibly driver-negative IMAs are driven by recurrent, druggable oncogenic fusions that activate RTK/RAS/MAPK signaling. The paper emphasizes NRG1 fusions activating HER2:HER3 signaling, plus ERBB4 and BRAF fusions that constitutively activate kinase signaling, with RET as an additional novel fusion class.
alteration_or_activation_mode Kinase fusion / ligand fusion-driven pathway activation. Specifically, CD74-NRG1 and SLC3A2-NRG1 create fusion ligands with membrane-tethered EGF-like domains; EZR-ERBB4 and TRIM24-BRAF create constitutively active kinase fusions; KIAA1468-RET is a novel RET fusion.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway ERBB/HER2:HER3 signaling and downstream ERK/AKT; ERBB4 kinase activation; BRAF/RAF-MEK-ERK signaling; RET as an additional kinase-fusion axis.
pathway_activation_evidence For CD74-NRG1, conditioned media from fusion-expressing cells induced phosphorylation of endogenous ERBB2/HER2 and ERBB3/HER3 in EFM-19 reporter cells, with increased ERK and AKT phosphorylation; these signals were suppressed by lapatinib and afatinib. For EZR-ERBB4, NIH3T3 cells showed serum-independent phosphorylation of ERBB4 tyrosine 1258 and elevated ERK phosphorylation, suppressed by lapatinib and afatinib. For TRIM24-BRAF, NIH3T3 cells showed serum-independent ERK phosphorylation, suppressed by sorafenib and U0126. The activation appears genetically explained by the identified fusions.
driver_negative_subtype An implicit molecular subtype of IMA/driver-negative LADC defined by 'pan-negative' status and the presence of specific oncogene fusions. The paper also suggests an IMA-feature-specific fusion subtype enriched for NRG1, ERBB4, BRAF, and RET rearrangements, with NRG1-fusion cases preferentially in female never-smokers.
clinical_or_exposure_context All findings are in invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma, a rare histologic subtype of lung adenocarcinoma. NRG1 fusion-positive cases were preferentially from female never-smokers; fusion-bearing IMAs occurred in both sexes, and Table 1 includes pathologic stages 1a, 1b, and 2b. The paper also notes that the novel fusions were not detected in 315 non-IMA LADCs from Japanese patients or 144 consecutive LADCs from U.S. patients.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper compares these fusions to established actionable lung cancer fusions such as EML4/KIF5B-ALK, KIF5B/CCDC6-RET, and ROS1 fusions in LADC, and notes that BRAF fusions resemble ESRP1-BRAF and AGTRAP-BRAF fusions reported in other cancers.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The identified fusions are proposed as targets for existing clinically used kinase inhibitors. Lapatinib and afatinib suppressed HER2/HER3, ERBB4, and downstream signaling; sorafenib and MEK inhibitor U0126 suppressed TRIM24-BRAF signaling; fusion-induced anchorage-independent growth was also inhibitor-sensitive. The authors explicitly suggest break-apart and fusion FISH diagnostics to enable clinical translation and targeted treatment of KRAS-negative IMAs.
