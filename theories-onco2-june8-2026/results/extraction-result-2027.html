Paper Abstract: Applying a next-generation sequencing assay targeting 145 cancer-relevant genes in 40 colorectal cancer and 24 non–small cell lung cancer formalin-fixed paraffin-embedded tissue specimens identified at least one clinically relevant genomic alteration in 59% of the samples and revealed two gene fusions, C2orf44-ALK in a colorectal cancer sample and KIF5B-RET in a lung adenocarcinoma. Further screening of 561 lung adenocarcinomas identified 11 additional tumors with KIF5B-RET gene fusions (2.0%; 95% CI 0.8–3.1%). Cells expressing oncogenic KIF5B-RET are sensitive to multi-kinase inhibitors that inhibit RET.

driver_negative_definition In this paper, the relevant 'driver-negative' lung adenocarcinomas are those that lacked previously known driver mutations or rearrangements detected by the assays used. Specifically, among the screened never/limited former smoker cohort, fusion-positive tumors had no mutations in EGFR, ERBB2, BRAF, or KRAS and no rearrangements in EML4-ALK or ROS1; the authors state that the lack of known oncogenic mutations in fusion-positive cases raises the possibility that KIF5B-RET is the driving event. Detection was by RT-PCR for KIF5B-RET in a cohort of 121 Europeans and 405 Asians, supplemented by IHC for RET and cDNA sequencing of RET-expressing tumors.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The paper proposes that a rare, previously unrecognized RET fusion drives oncogenesis in a subset of driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma. The mechanism is constitutive activation of RET signaling through dimerization provided by the KIF5B coiled-coil domain, coupled to kinase-domain retention and RET overexpression.
alteration_or_activation_mode Oncogenic gene fusion with in-frame rearrangement; no amplification was observed. The fusion creates an expressed chimeric transcript with increased RET expression beginning at exon 12, consistent with constitutive activation of the RET kinase.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway KIF5B/RET fusion signaling; RET receptor tyrosine kinase axis. The downstream pathway is not directly mapped in this paper, but the biology implies a kinase-driven oncogenic program susceptible to RET inhibition.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence includes cDNA sequencing across the fusion junction, 7.3-fold increased RET expression beginning at exon 12 relative to exons 1–11, focal moderate cytoplasmic RET immunoreactivity by IHC, and functional transformation of Ba/F3 cells expressing KIF5B-RET with IL-3-independent growth. RET phosphorylation was inhibited by sunitinib but not gefitinib, supporting activated RET kinase signaling. The activation is not genetically explained by common canonical drivers in the fusion-positive tumors, beyond the fusion itself.
driver_negative_subtype A small, genomically defined subtype of lung adenocarcinoma characterized by KIF5B-RET rearrangement, absence of known canonical drivers (EGFR, ERBB2, BRAF, KRAS, EML4-ALK, ROS1), and enrichment in never or limited former smokers. The paper does not assign a formal transcriptomic or proteomic subtype label beyond RET-rearranged lung adenocarcinoma.
clinical_or_exposure_context The index case was a 44-year-old male never-smoker of European ancestry with lung adenocarcinoma. In the expanded cohort, fusion-positive cases occurred in both European-ancestry and Asian subjects who were never or limited former smokers; the authors report 1/121 Europeans and 9/405 Asians positive by RT-PCR, with all additional fusions found in lung adenocarcinomas.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares KIF5B-RET in lung adenocarcinoma to PTC-RET translocations in papillary thyroid carcinoma and to KIF5B-ALK fusions in NSCLC, emphasizing shared coiled-coil-mediated kinase activation logic across cancers.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication RET fusions are proposed to be druggable. The paper reports preclinical sensitivity of KIF5B-RET-expressing Ba/F3 cells to sunitinib, sorafenib, and vandetanib, and states that RET kinase inhibitors should be tested in prospective clinical trials for patients with KIF5B-RET-rearranged NSCLC. It also suggests broader therapeutic relevance analogous to crizotinib for ALK-rearranged NSCLC.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation includes discovery in an index lung adenocarcinoma, cDNA sequencing of fusion junctions, screening of larger cohorts by IHC and RT-PCR, demonstration of RET protein expression, and functional testing in Ba/F3 cells showing IL-3-independent transformation and inhibitor sensitivity. This provides strong but preclinical validation, not patient-treatment response data.
