driver_negative_definition The paper does not provide a strict operational definition of oncogene-negative/driver-negative LUAD. Instead, it frames the comparison around lung adenocarcinomas that do not harbor the canonical alterations emphasized in the study and in prior work—especially activating EGFR and KRAS mutations and fusion kinases involving EML4-ALK, RET, and ROS1. The study uses whole-genome sequencing of 17 tumor-normal pairs, transcriptome sequencing (RNA-seq), validation by custom sequence capture/deep digital sequencing, and recurrence screening in an independent set of 94–96 primary lung adenocarcinomas to catalog alternative alterations.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main alternative driver logic is pathway-centric rather than a single common oncogene: recurrent activation/perturbation of the JAK-STAT pathway and cell-cycle program, together with lesions in DNA repair and chromatin modification genes. The authors also suggest that some cases may be driven by less common kinase mutations or fusions (e.g., JAK2, MET, HGF, EPHA3, ROS1, ALK), while others may involve tumor-suppressor loss or epigenetic dysregulation.
alteration_or_activation_mode Rare nonsynonymous point mutations, truncating/frameshift mutations, fusion genes, copy-number loss, and gene-expression-linked pathway alteration. The paper specifically notes recurrent JAK2 kinase-domain missense mutations, KRAS/EGFR founder-clone mutations in other tumors, chromatin-gene mutations, and expression-associated pathway perturbation.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway JAK/STAT signaling is the clearest pathway-level implication, with recurrent alterations in JAK2 plus JAK3 and STAT1. Additional implicated axes include cell-cycle regulation, DNA repair, chromatin modification, ErbB signaling, and kinase targets such as MET, HGF, EPHA3, ROS1, and ALK.
pathway_activation_evidence PathScan identified statistically significant enrichment of mutations in the JAK-STAT pathway (p = 0.04), and the authors state that recurrent mutations in the JAK2 protein kinase domain plus mutations in JAK3 and STAT1 indicate possible oncogenic activation in a subset of tumors. However, the activation claim is inferred from genomics and pathway aggregation rather than direct phosphoproteomic or IHC readouts; no pERK/pAKT/pS6-type data are provided for these tumors.
driver_negative_subtype An implicit 'never-smoker' or 'low-mutation-burden' LUAD subtype is suggested, characterized by lower overall mutation burden, a different mutational spectrum (more C:G→T:A transitions), a higher proportion of expressed variants, and enrichment of EGFR mutations and ROS1/ALK fusions in never-smokers. A separate pathway-defined subset is suggested by JAK-STAT-altered tumors, but the paper does not formally name a driver-negative subtype.
clinical_or_exposure_context The relevant context is lung adenocarcinoma in never-smokers versus smokers. Never-smokers had markedly lower mutation burden than smokers; the cohort also included one former light smoker and one large-cell carcinoma case. The paper emphasizes that EGFR mutations and ALK fusions are more common in lifelong never-smokers, while KRAS/TP53/BRAF/JAK2/JAK3 and mismatch-repair gene mutations are more common in smokers.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares DNA repair lesions to BRCA-deficient cancers in the context of possible PARP inhibitor sensitivity, and chromatin remodeling defects to other cancers with recurrent ARID1A/ARID2 mutations (ovarian clear cell carcinoma, endometriosis-associated ovarian cancer, hepatocellular carcinoma, glioma, prostate cancer). It also compares JAK2 activating mutations to myeloproliferative neoplasms.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications include targeting rare kinase alterations and fusions (EGFR, ALK, ROS1, RET, MET, JAK2, EPHA3, HGF), and considering pathway-directed therapy for JAK-STAT and cell-cycle abnormalities. The authors specifically mention that DNA repair pathway lesions may confer susceptibility to PARP inhibitors and that HDAC inhibitors/epigenetic therapy should be explored for chromatin modifier alterations. Evidence is hypothesis-generating and largely based on genomic annotation and existing drug knowledge, not on direct drug-response experiments in this paper.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is genomic and cohort-based: WGS discoveries were orthogonally validated by custom capture sequencing and deep digital sequencing, then screened for recurrence in an independent set of 94–96 primary lung adenocarcinomas. Fusion calls were cross-validated by combining RNA-seq algorithms with WGS structural-variation evidence. The paper does not include cell-line, organoid, xenograft, or functional perturbation experiments to test driver dependence.
