brief_description A novel somatic gene fusion found in lung adenocarcinoma that joins the first six exons of CD74 to NRG1 exon(s) encoding the EGF-like domain of the III-β3 isoform. The fusion is proposed to create a membrane-tethered presentation of the NRG1 ligand domain, enabling activation of ERBB2/ERBB3 signaling in otherwise driver-negative invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma.

driver_negative_definition The paper operationalized driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma as tumors lacking known oncogenic alterations after testing for canonical alterations in EGFR and KRAS in the discovery cohort, and later in the validation cohort as wild-type for EGFR, KRAS, BRAF, ERBB2, ALK, ROS, and RET. Negativity was assessed using targeted molecular testing and then deeper transcriptome sequencing and FISH to search for hidden fusion drivers.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver hypothesis is a ligand-presentation fusion that activates an ERBB receptor signaling state rather than a classical kinase alteration. The authors argue that CD74-NRG1 provides the extracellular EGF-like domain of NRG1 to stimulate ERBB2-ERBB3 heterodimers, leading to oncogenic PI3K-AKT pathway activation in invasive mucinous lung adenocarcinoma.
alteration_or_activation_mode Somatic fusion / ligand-receptor stimulation with membrane-tethered presentation of the NRG1 EGF-like domain; not a kinase domain fusion. The fusion likely replaces the transmembrane domain of wild-type NRG1 III-β3 and results in cell-surface exposure of the ligand domain, enabling autocrine or juxtacrine ERBB activation.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway CD74-NRG1 -> ERBB3/ERBB2 heterodimer signaling -> PI3K-AKT pathway, with downstream p-S6K activation. The paper also notes high ERBB2 and ERBB3 expression and specific phospho-ERBB3 positivity in fusion-positive tumors.
pathway_activation_evidence In the index tumor, ERBB2 and ERBB3 were expressed and phosphorylated, whereas EGFR/ERBB1 and ERBB4 were not expressed or not phosphorylated. In a tissue microarray of 241 lung adenocarcinomas, p-ERBB3 immunohistochemistry was almost exclusive to the five CD74-NRG1-positive cases (5/5 positive vs 6/241 total; P < 0.0001). In cell lines, ectopic CD74-NRG1 increased p-ERBB2, p-ERBB3, p-AKT, and p-S6K, and the p-AKT/p-ERBB3 signal depended on the EGF-like domain.
driver_negative_subtype Invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma (IMA) of the lung, specifically in women who had never smoked. The subtype is defined histologically and clinically, with the fusion recurrently occurring in this mucinous lineage-associated subset rather than across general lung adenocarcinoma.
clinical_or_exposure_context All five CD74-NRG1-positive cases were invasive mucinous adenocarcinomas from women who had never smoked. The authors also note multifocal, unresectable disease as a common clinical issue for this subtype and discuss limited treatment options.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares this biology to other kinase-fusion-driven lung cancers (EGFR, ALK, ROS1, RET) as an example of oncogene dependency, and to wild-type NRG1 signaling via ERBB heterodimers. It also situates ERBB network targeting as a broader cancer-therapy paradigm.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The fusion may create a therapeutic opportunity for invasive mucinous lung adenocarcinoma. The authors specifically point to available drugs targeting ERBB2, ERBB3, and downstream pathways, implying possible vulnerability to ERBB-directed or PI3K-AKT-pathway-directed therapies; however, this is preclinical/inferential rather than demonstrated clinical response.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation was multi-layered: discovery by RNA-seq in one case; RT-PCR screening of 102 pan-negative tumors identified four additional cases; break-apart and fusion FISH confirmed rearrangements; hybrid-capture genomic sequencing supported the genomic breakpoint; flow cytometry in NIH-3T3 and H2052 cells showed intracellular CD74 and extracellular NRG1 localization; Western blotting in H322/H1568 cells demonstrated pathway activation; a truncated fusion lacking the EGF-like domain abolished signaling; and soft-agar colony formation increased with CD74-NRG1 expression.
