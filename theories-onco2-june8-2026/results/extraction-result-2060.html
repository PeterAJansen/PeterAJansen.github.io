Paper Title: Combinatorial tumor suppressor inactivation efficiently initiates lung adenocarcinoma with therapeutic vulnerabilities

Paper Venue: bioRxiv

Paper TL;DR: It is demonstrated that oncogene-negative tumors and cell lines with activated RAS/MAPK and PI3K pathways are vulnerable to pharmacological inhibition of these signaling axes and transform the understanding of this prevalent yet understudied subtype of lung adenocarcinoma.

Paper Abstract: Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer death worldwide, with lung adenocarcinoma being the most common subtype. Many oncogenes and tumor suppressor genes are altered in this cancer type and the discovery of oncogene mutations has led to the development of targeted therapies and better clinical outcomes. However, a large fraction of lung adenocarcinomas lacks mutations in known oncogenes, and the genesis and treatment of these oncogene-negative tumors remain enigmatic. Here, we perform iterative in vivo functional screens with quantitative autochthonous mouse model systems to uncover the genetic and biochemical changes that enable efficient lung tumor initiation in the absence of oncogene alterations. Through the generation of hundreds of diverse combinations of tumor suppressor alterations, we demonstrate that inactivation of suppressors of the RAS and PI3K pathways drive the development of oncogene- negative lung adenocarcinoma. Pathway-level human genomic data analysis and histology identified RAS/MAPK and PI3K pathway activation as a common event in oncogene-negative human lung adenocarcinomas. We demonstrate that oncogene-negative tumors and cell lines with activated RAS/MAPK and PI3K pathways are vulnerable to pharmacological inhibition of these signaling axes. Collectively, these results transform our understanding of this prevalent yet understudied subtype of lung adenocarcinoma. STATEMENT OF SIGNIFICANCE Many lung adenocarcinomas do not have mutations in known proto-oncogenes, and as a result, targeted therapies are unavailable for treating these patients. Here, we uncover driver pathways in a subset of these oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinomas and demonstrate the therapeutic value of inhibiting these pathways.

oncogene_negative_definition Human LUAD tumors were classified as oncogene-negative if they had no alterations in known proto-oncogenes. The authors analyzed TCGA-LUAD and AACR GENIE LUAD datasets using somatic mutation data (SNPs and indels, including silent mutations), copy-number calls from GISTIC2 thresholded CNV (amplifications = 2, deletions = −2), fusion data, and MET exon-skipping data. In the experimental mouse work, tumors were checked for spontaneous hotspot mutations in Kras, Braf, Nras, and Egfr by sequencing 10 genomic regions; in 29 samples only one oncogene mutation (Kras G12V) was found.
assay_strategy_and_detection_limits The driver-discovery pipeline combined targeted in vivo functional CRISPR/Cas9 screening in autochthonous mouse models, Tuba-seq barcode quantification of clonal expansion, targeted sequencing of selected oncogenes (Kras/Braf/Nras/Egfr regions), and whole-exome sequencing on some mouse tumors. For human data, they used TCGA and GENIE somatic mutation/CNV/fusion/MET exon-skipping datasets, plus immunohistochemistry for pERK, pAKT, and PTEN and RNA-seq/ssGSVA in mouse tumors. The paper explicitly notes blind spots: gene panels were noncomprehensive, only a fraction of known pathway genes were analyzed in GENIE, and epigenetic/non-genomic mechanisms can activate RAS/PI3K or silence PTEN; thus genomic alterations are a floor, not a ceiling. The study also states that many non-genomic mechanisms and limited panel coverage can cause driver-negative labeling to miss the actual pathway lesion.
missed_driver_reclassified_after_broader_testing Yes. Human tumors initially lacking canonical oncogenic driver mutations were later reinterpreted as Onc-negativeRAS/PI3K tumors after broader testing showed frequent pathway activation. The rescued lesion was not one single recurrent hidden oncogene, but activation of RAS and/or PI3K pathways, often without a detectable genomic mutation in NF1/PTEN/other profiled genes; PTEN protein loss was seen by IHC in 13/20 tumors with no genomic PTEN mutation. The paper attributes the initial miss to the noncomprehensiveness of the gene panel and to epigenetic/non-genomic silencing mechanisms.

candidate_driver_gene_or_locus NF1, RASA1, PTEN, and the broader RAS and PI3K pathway negative regulators queried in Table S6; in the mouse model the strongest trio was Nf1/Rasa1/Pten.
structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism Loss of negative regulation of RAS and PI3K-AKT signaling through inactivation of tumor suppressors. The paper also points to epigenetic silencing of PTEN and other non-genomic mechanisms as regulatory activation mechanisms that would evade DNA-only assays.
functional_consequence Constitutive activation of RAS/MAPK and PI3K-AKT signaling, measurable by pERK and pAKT immunohistochemistry and pathway gene-expression signatures; this state supports oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma initiation and growth.
co_alteration_or_combinatorial_requirement Single or pairwise inactivation of these suppressors was usually insufficient; combinatorial inactivation of Nf1, Rasa1, and Pten was the most potent and was nearly as effective as oncogenic KrasG12D in driving tumor initiation. Additional pathway-negative-regulator alterations beyond this trio were also observed, but the paper emphasizes a combinatorial threshold for efficient tumorigenesis.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment In TCGA LUAD, over 60% of oncogene-negative tumors had alterations in either the RAS or PI3K pathways, and 22% had alterations in both pathways, consistent with Onc-negativeRAS/PI3K tumors. In the human cohort analyzed by IHC, ~45% of oncogene-negative tumors showed moderate-to-strong activation of both RAS and PI3K pathways. NF1 and RASA1 alterations were enriched in oncogene-negative tumors, while coincident genomic NF1/RASA1/PTEN alterations were rare.
functional_or_clinical_validation Functional validation came from in vivo mouse CRISPR/Cas9 tumor initiation experiments, Tuba-seq quantification, and biochemical readouts showing pERK/pAKT activation. Clinical/biological validation came from human LUAD IHC, TCGA/GENIE pathway analyses, and drug-sensitivity data: SHP2 inhibitor RMC-4550 and AKT inhibitor capivasertib synergized to suppress growth of mouse and human oncogene-negative RAS/PI3K cell lines and reduced tumor burden in autochthonous mouse tumors.
