Paper Title: Druggable Oncogene Fusions in Invasive Mucinous Lung Adenocarcinoma

Paper Venue: Clinical Cancer Research

Paper TL;DR: Oncogenic fusions act as driver mutations in IMAs without KRAS mutations, and thus represent promising therapeutic targets for the treatment of such IMAs.

Paper Abstract: Purpose: To identify druggable oncogenic fusions in invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma (IMA) of the lung, a malignant type of lung adenocarcinoma in which KRAS mutations frequently occur. Experimental Design: From an IMA cohort of 90 cases, consisting of 56 cases (62%) with KRAS mutations and 34 cases without (38%), we conducted whole-transcriptome sequencing of 32 IMAs, including 27 cases without KRAS mutations. We used the sequencing data to identify gene fusions, and then performed functional analyses of the fusion gene products. Results: We identified oncogenic fusions that occurred mutually exclusively with KRAS mutations: CD74-NRG1, SLC3A2-NRG1, EZR-ERBB4, TRIM24-BRAF, and KIAA1468-RET. NRG1 fusions were present in 17.6% (6/34) of KRAS-negative IMAs. The CD74-NRG1 fusion activated HER2:HER3 signaling, whereas the EZR-ERBB4 and TRIM24-BRAF fusions constitutively activated the ERBB4 and BRAF kinases, respectively. Signaling pathway activation and fusion-induced anchorage-independent growth/tumorigenicity of NIH3T3 cells expressing these fusions were suppressed by tyrosine kinase inhibitors approved for clinical use. Conclusions: Oncogenic fusions act as driver mutations in IMAs without KRAS mutations, and thus represent promising therapeutic targets for the treatment of such IMAs. Clin Cancer Res; 20(12); 3087–93. ©2014 AACR.

driver_negative_definition The paper defines driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma in this context as invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma (IMA) cases lacking representative known driver aberrations, especially KRAS mutations, and in some cases also negative for BRAF mutation, EGFR mutation, and EML4-ALK fusion. In the cohort, 30 of 34 KRAS-negative IMAs were 'pan-negative' for representative driver aberrations in LADCs; 27 of these pan-negative cases were sequenced. Negativity was established by targeted mutation/fusion testing and by RNA sequencing of tumors, with RT-PCR and Sanger sequencing validation of fusion transcripts.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver hypothesis is that oncogenic gene fusions, especially NRG1-, ERBB4-, BRAF-, and RET-related fusions, act as the driver lesions in KRAS-negative/pan-negative IMA. The paper further suggests that NRG1-mediated signaling may contribute not only to transformation but also to goblet-cell lineage/morphologic features.
alteration_or_activation_mode Rare oncogenic fusions causing pathway activation: ligand fusion producing secreted/membrane-tethered NRG1 activity, receptor kinase fusion activating ERBB4, and kinase fusion constitutively activating BRAF; plus a RET fusion as an additional driver fusion class.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway HER2:HER3/ERBB signaling for NRG1 fusions, ERBB4 kinase signaling for EZR-ERBB4, BRAF-MEK-ERK signaling for TRIM24-BRAF, and RET kinase signaling for KIAA1468-RET. The broader theme is kinase-pathway dependence in driver-negative IMA.
pathway_activation_evidence For CD74-NRG1, conditioned media from fusion-expressing cells induced phosphorylation of ERBB2/HER2, ERBB3/HER3, ERK, and AKT in EFM-19 reporter cells, consistent with autocrine HER2:HER3 signaling. For EZR-ERBB4, NIH3T3 cells showed serum-independent phosphorylation of ERBB4 (Y1258) and ERK. For TRIM24-BRAF, NIH3T3 cells showed serum-independent ERK phosphorylation. The pathway activation is genetically explained by the fusions and was suppressed by kinase inhibitors.
driver_negative_subtype An implicit molecular subtype of IMA: 'pan-negative' KRAS-negative invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma, enriched for mutually exclusive, recurrent fusion drivers and with a tendency for NRG1-fusion cases to occur in female never-smokers. The paper also implies an IMA-with-driver-fusions subtype defined by mucinous histology plus hidden kinase/ligand fusions rather than canonical point mutations.
clinical_or_exposure_context The relevant clinical context is invasive mucinous histology. NRG1 fusion-positive cases were preferentially female never-smokers, while the cohort overall included both smokers and never-smokers and tumors at mostly early pathologic stages (e.g., 1a, 1b, 2b in Table 1).
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares these findings to other cancers with actionable oncogenic fusions, including ALK, RET, ROS1, and KIF5B-ALK/KIF5B-RET/CCDC6-RET fusions in lung adenocarcinoma, and notes that RAF-pathway rearrangements had also been seen in prostate cancer, gastric cancer, and melanoma.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication These fusions are proposed as therapeutic targets for existing kinase inhibitors. CD74-NRG1/HER2:HER3 signaling was suppressed by lapatinib and afatinib; EZR-ERBB4 signaling was suppressed by lapatinib and afatinib; TRIM24-BRAF signaling was suppressed by sorafenib and U0126; fusion-driven anchorage-independent growth was also inhibited. The paper frames these as clinically relevant, druggable aberrations in KRAS-negative IMA, though validation in human cancer cell lines was not achieved.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation included whole-transcriptome RNA sequencing of 32 IMAs, RT-PCR and Sanger sequencing confirmation, RT-PCR screening in the remaining cohort, and FISH for CD74-NRG1. Functional validation used H1299-conditioned media with EFM-19 reporter cells, exogenous expression of fusion cDNAs in NIH3T3 fibroblasts, Western blot/phosphorylation assays, soft agar anchorage-independent growth assays, and nude mouse tumorigenicity assays for EZR-ERBB4 and TRIM24-BRAF. The authors also screened 200 human lung cancer cell lines and found none positive for these three fusions, noting that oncogenic properties remain unvalidated in human cancer cells.
