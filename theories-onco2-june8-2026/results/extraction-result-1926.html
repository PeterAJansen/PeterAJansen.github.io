Paper Abstract: Purpose To determine the frequency of driver mutations in Chinese non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) patients. Methods Comprehensive mutational analysis was performed in 1356 lung adenocarcinoma, 503 squamous cell carcinoma, 57 adenosquamous lung carcinoma, 19 large cell carcinoma and 8 sarcomatoid carcinoma. The effect of EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors (TKIs) on EGFR-mutated lung adenocarcinoma patients after disease recurrence was investigated. Results Mutations in EGFR kinase domain, HER2 kinase domain, KRAS, BRAF, ALK, ROS1 and RET were mutually exclusive. In lung adenocarcinoma cases “pan-negative” for the seven above-mentioned driver mutations, we also detected two oncogenic EGFR extracellular domain mutations (A289D and R324L), two HER2 extracellular and transmembrane domain mutations (S310Y and V659E), one ARAF S214C mutation and two CD74-NRG1 fusions. Six (1.2%) FGFR3 activating mutations were identified in lung squamous cell carcinoma (five S249C and one R248C). There were three (15.8%) EGFR mutations and four (21.1%) KRAS mutations in large cell carcinoma. Three (37.5%) KRAS mutations were detected in sarcomatoid carcinoma. In EGFR-mutated lung adenocarcinoma patients who experienced disease recurrence, treatment with EGFR TKIs was an independent predictor of better overall survival (HR = 0.299, 95% CI: 0.172–0.519, P < 0.001). Conclusion We determined the frequency of driver mutations in a large series of Chinese NSCLC patients. EGFR TKIs might improve the survival outcomes of EGFR-mutated lung adenocarcinoma patients who experienced disease recurrence.

alteration_or_activation_mode Rare activating mutations or fusions outside the standard hotspot/kinase-domain panel: EGFR ECD mutations (A289D, R324L), HER2 ECD/transmembrane mutations (S310Y, V659E), ARAF S214C mutation, and CD74-NRG1 fusions.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway ERBB-family signaling (EGFR/HER2/ERBB3-related signaling), ARAF/MAPK signaling, and NRG1 fusion-driven signaling. The paper specifically points to non-EGFR/HER2 kinase-domain ERBB alterations as uncommon but real oncogenic events.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is mutational and inferential rather than functional pathway readout: the authors identified oncogenic alterations in pan-negative LUAD by sequencing and cite prior literature showing these variants are oncogenic or driver mutations. The paper does not report pERK/pAKT/pS6, kinase activity assays, or phosphoproteomics in these tumors.
driver_negative_subtype An implicit molecular subtype of "pan-negative" LUAD characterized by low-frequency hidden oncogenic alterations in EGFR ECD, HER2 ECD/TM, ARAF, or NRG1. For NRG1 fusions, the subtype is further refined by histology: invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma.
clinical_or_exposure_context The pan-negative LUAD cases with CD74-NRG1 fusions were both female never-smokers with stage I invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma. The EGFR/HER2 ECD/TM and ARAF mutations were found in a small number of adenocarcinoma cases, but the paper does not define a single shared exposure profile for all pan-negative tumors.
cross_cancer_parallel The authors compare with prior reports in TCGA and Western cohorts, including oncogenic ERBB2/ERBB3 mutations in lung cancer and ARAF mutations that were reported as oncogenic and sorafenib-sensitive in lung adenocarcinoma. They also note NRG1 fusions as previously identified novel oncogenic drivers in lung adenocarcinoma and characterize them as associated with invasive mucinous histology.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications are exploratory and biomarker-linked: the paper suggests that identifying rare oncogenic alterations in pan-negative LUAD may open opportunities for targeted therapy. It specifically notes that ARAF mutations have been reported as sorafenib-sensitive in prior work and that hidden driver discovery supports genotype-directed therapy, but it does not test targeted drugs against the rare pan-negative alterations here.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is limited to clinical sequencing of a large surgical cohort plus somatic confirmation for newly identified variants by sequencing paired normal lung DNA. There is no experimental functional validation in cell lines, organoids, xenografts, or rescue assays in this paper; support for oncogenicity is drawn from prior literature and the recurrence of known hotspot-like alterations.
