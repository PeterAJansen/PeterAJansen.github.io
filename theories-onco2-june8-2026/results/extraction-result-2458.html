Paper Title: Proteogenomic characterization reveals tumorigenesis and progression of lung cancer manifested as subsolid nodules

Paper Venue: Nature Communications

Paper TL;DR: Proteogenomic analysis of 66 LUAD displayed as SSNs from 3 histological stages is performed and sustained endoplasmic reticulum stress is demonstrated to be a hallmark and a reliable biomarker of AIS progression to IAC and suggests preventive and therapeutic strategies for LUAD.

Paper Abstract: Lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD) radiologically displayed as subsolid nodules (SSNs) is prevalent. Nevertheless, the precise clinical management of SSNs necessitates a profound understanding of their tumorigenesis and progression. Here, we analyze 66 LUAD displayed as SSNs covering 3 histological stages including adenocarcinoma in situ (AIS), minimally invasive adenocarcinoma (MIA) and invasive adenocarcinoma (IAC) by incorporating genomics, proteomics, phosphoproteomics and glycoproteomics. Intriguingly, cholesterol metabolism is aberrantly regulated in the preneoplastic AIS stage. Importantly, target ablation of proprotein convertase subtilisin/kexin type 9 (PCSK9) promotes the initiation of LUAD. Furthermore, sustained endoplasmic reticulum stress is demonstrated to be a hallmark and a reliable biomarker of AIS progression to IAC. Consistently, target promotion of ER stress profoundly retards LUAD progression. Our study provides comprehensive proteogenomic landscape of SSNs, sheds lights on the tumorigenesis and progression of SSNs and suggests preventive and therapeutic strategies for LUAD. Understanding the development of subsolid nodules (SSNs) in lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD) is important for their clinical management. Here, the authors perform proteogenomic analysis of 66 LUAD displayed as SSNs from 3 histological stages and highlight the role of endoplasmic reticulum stress in progression.

The paper frames these activations as pathway-state phenomena rather than a fully explained genetic driver.

The paper instead defines three NMF-derived multi-omics clusters and two clinical progression categories (A1 rapid progression, A2 stable) among SSNs; cluster 1 corresponds to pre-invasive/AIS-MIA-like biology with ECM receptor interaction, leukocyte transendothelial migration, renin-angiotensin system, and cell adhesion molecules, while cluster 3 is stress-adaptive and enriched for apoptosis, autophagy, ferroptosis, and metabolic pathways.
clinical_or_exposure_context SSNs are more common in East Asian/Chinese patients in this cohort; lower CTR indicates less aggressive phenotype. Rapidly progressing A1 versus stable A2 lesions were separated clinically by CT growth behavior. The cohort also notes EGFR mutation frequencies consistent with Asian LUAD prevalence.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares its subtype structure to prior LUAD proteogenomic studies and notes that ER stress is broadly relevant across multiple cancers.
