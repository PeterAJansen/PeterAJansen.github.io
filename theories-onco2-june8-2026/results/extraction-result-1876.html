validation_result Fusion-positive tumors were confirmed by orthogonal assays. p-ERBB3 was detected in all 5 CD74-NRG1-positive IMA cases but in only 6/241 unselected adenocarcinomas (P < 0.0001). In cell lines, CD74-NRG1 increased p-ERBB2, p-ERBB3, p-AKT, and p-S6K, and the ΔEGF fusion lost signaling activity. CD74-NRG1 also enhanced soft-agar colony formation in H1568 cells.
therapeutic_implication CD74-NRG1-positive invasive mucinous LUAD may be targetable by ERBB2/ERBB3-directed therapies and inhibitors of downstream PI3K-AKT signaling; the fusion may serve as a biomarker for therapy selection.
therapeutic_evidence The authors explicitly state that available drugs targeting ERBB2, ERBB3, and downstream pathways could represent a therapeutic opportunity. Functional data showed pathway activation dependent on the EGF-like domain, and the disease context is described as having no effective treatment; however, no drug-response experiment is reported in this paper.
clinical_or_demographic_association Enriched in never-smoker women; associated with invasive mucinous histology. The paper also notes that invasive mucinous LUAD is otherwise commonly associated with KRAS mutations.
study_limitations Small cohorts (25 discovery, 102 validation, 15 IMA subset); enrichment observed in an Asian validation cohort; no direct therapeutic drug testing; incomplete enumeration of all possible alternative drivers beyond the screened genes.
