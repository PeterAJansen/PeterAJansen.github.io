brief_description A lung lineage transcription factor that is frequently decreased or absent in human mucinous lung adenocarcinoma. In this paper it functions context-dependently as a suppressor of KrasG12D-driven mucinous tumorigenesis, while enhancing EGFRL858R-driven tumorigenesis.
citation_title KrasG12D and Nkx2-1 haploinsufficiency induce mucinous adenocarcinoma of the lung
mention_or_use use
driver_negative_definition This paper does not define oncogene-negative/driver-negative LUAD as a genomically screened cohort lacking canonical drivers; rather, it models a mucinous lung adenocarcinoma state associated with absent or reduced NKX2-1 and with KRAS-mutant, EGFR-wild-type biology. 'Negative' in the relevant sense is the NKX2-1-negative tumor cell state (and, in human comparison, mucinous LUAD is rarely associated with EGFR mutations), with phenotype assessed by histology, Alcian blue, mucin markers, CK7/CK20, and gene-expression profiling.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism Hidden-driver logic is not the main framework here; instead the main mechanism is lineage-state control by loss of the lung differentiation factor NKX2-1, which removes a brake on mutant KRAS-driven mucinous adenocarcinoma and permits a mucin/goblet-cell program. The paper also implicates AP-1-dependent transcription and a tumor-goblet-cell program distinct from airway goblet metaplasia.
alteration_or_activation_mode Haploinsufficiency / reduced expression of a lineage transcription factor combined with oncogenic KrasG12D; the operative change is tumor-suppressive factor loss rather than a new recurrent activating mutation. NKX2-1 also directly binds and suppresses target genes, including mucin and oncogenic pathway genes.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway NKX2-1-centered lineage program; KRAS/MAPK-driven mucinous tumorigenesis; AP-1 transcriptional activity; suppression of mucin genes (MUC5AC, MUC5B), oncogenes (FGFR1, CDK6, IGF1R), and induction of apoptotic genes (FAS, TIMP3).
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is indirect/transcriptional rather than phospho-kinase based: gene-expression microarrays showed induction of mucus/goblet-cell genes and tumor-associated genes in KrasG12D;Nkx2-1+/- lungs, while NKX2-1-expression in A549 cells suppressed those genes and induced apoptosis genes. ChIP-seq showed direct NKX2-1 binding at MUC5AC, FGFR1, and other targets; AP-1 reporter assays showed NKX2-1 inhibited AP-1 activity, and PMA rescued NKX2-1 suppression of FGFR1 mRNA. No pERK/pAKT/pS6 readouts are reported for this subtype.
driver_negative_subtype A mucinous adenocarcinoma / goblet-cell subtype characterized by NKX2-1 loss or low expression, abundant intracytoplasmic mucin, Alcian blue positivity, CK7 and CK20 expression, and a gene-expression program overlapping with chronic airway mucous metaplasia but lacking SPDEF/FOXA3 expression. It is defined by histology and transcriptomic lineage state rather than by a new DNA driver.
clinical_or_exposure_context Human mucinous lung adenocarcinoma is noted to be highly invasive, associated with decreased or absent NKX2-1, and enriched for KRAS mutations (~76%) while being rarely EGFR-mutant. The paper compares this state with chronic lung disease mucous metaplasia (asthma/HDM, COPD, cystic fibrosis) and emphasizes its distinct pathology from nonmucinous LUAD.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper contrasts lung mucinous adenocarcinoma with airway mucous metaplasia in asthma/HDM exposure and notes lack of dependence on the SPDEF/FOXA3 program that drives non-neoplastic goblet-cell metaplasia. It also briefly references thyroid adenoma and squamous cell lung carcinoma in discussing NKX2-1 lineage-oncogene versus tumor-suppressor behavior across cancers.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutically, the paper suggests NKX2-1 status and the mucinous lineage program could matter for treatment selection: NKX2-1 sensitized A549 cells to cisplatin-induced apoptosis, implying possible chemotherapy vulnerability. The authors also propose that distinct approaches may be required for mucinous adenocarcinoma of the lung and identify NKX2-1-regulated genes/pathways as potential therapeutic targets.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated in conditional mouse models (Scgb1a1-rtTA;[tetO]-Kras4bG12D crossed with Nkx2-1+/-), urethane carcinogenesis, and reciprocal NKX2-1 overexpression models. Validation also used human lung carcinoma cell lines (A549, H441, HBEC3, KRAS- and EGFR-mutant lines), mRNA microarrays, ChIP-seq, AP-1 luciferase reporter assays, soft agar colony assays, and cisplatin sensitivity assays.
