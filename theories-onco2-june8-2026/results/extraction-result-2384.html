candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The paper does not propose a hidden non-fusion driver for the negative tumors; instead, it frames negative kinase-fusion status itself as a poor-prognosis category and implies that many driver-negative cases may still harbor undiscovered kinase fusions or other molecular drivers not captured here. The dominant mechanistic idea is therefore unresolved hidden oncogenic alteration rather than a defined pathway state.

driver_negative_subtype An implicit clinical-pathologic subtype defined by lack of kinase fusions, and by contrast to the fusion-positive subtype it is enriched for poorer prognosis. The paper does not assign a formal molecular subtype name to these cases.
clinical_or_exposure_context Negative kinase-fusion status was one of four independent adverse prognostic factors in adenocarcinoma, alongside age >=50 years, male sex, and high pathological stage. The fusion-positive counterpart was associated with younger age, low smoking index, small tumor size, TTF1 positivity, and mucinous cribriform histology.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper compares RET fusions in lung cancer with RET fusions in papillary thyroid carcinoma, especially radiation-associated thyroid cancers, suggesting a possible exposure-linked parallel that warrants further study. It also notes secretory breast carcinoma as another fusion-driven cancer with a cribriform pattern, but that comparison is to ETV6-NTRK3 rather than to driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma specifically.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication For driver-negative adenocarcinoma, the paper does not identify a direct targeted therapy. The main implication is that proper molecular target diagnosis can identify patients who may benefit from kinase inhibitors, and that negative cases may need broader molecular screening to discover actionable fusions or other targets.
validation_evidence_and_model_system The negative classification is supported by integrated screening across 1,528 lung cancers using IHC, multiplex RT-PCR, FISH split/fusion assays, and RACE-based fusion partner identification; control series of previously known positive and negative tumors showed complete IHC concordance. However, no functional validation model is provided for the negative tumors themselves.
