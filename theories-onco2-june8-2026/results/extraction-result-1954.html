Paper Abstract: Introduction: Despite apparently complete surgical resection, approximately half of resected early‐stage lung cancer patients relapse and die of their disease. Adjuvant chemotherapy reduces this risk by only 5% to 8%. Thus, there is a need for better identifying who benefits from adjuvant therapy, the drivers of relapse, and novel targets in this setting. Methods: RNA sequencing and liquid chromatography/liquid chromatography–mass spectrometry proteomics data were generated from 51 surgically resected non–small cell lung tumors with known recurrence status. Results: We present a rationale and framework for the incorporation of high‐content RNA and protein measurements into integrative biomarkers and show the potential of this approach for predicting risk of recurrence in a group of lung adenocarcinomas. In addition, we characterize the relationship between mRNA and protein measurements in lung adenocarcinoma and show that it is outcome specific. Conclusions: Our results suggest that mRNA and protein data possess independent biological and clinical importance, which can be leveraged to create higher‐powered expression biomarkers.

driver_negative_definition This paper does not define oncogene-negative or driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma using canonical LUAD driver criteria. It explicitly states as a limitation that the patients do not have matched DNA sequencing data, so 'a comprehensive catalogue of driver mutations is lacking'; thus, driver-negative status is not established by assays in this study. The available assays are RNA sequencing from fresh frozen tissue and LC-MS/LC-MS/MS proteomics from FFPE tumor tissue, with recurrence status as the main clinical grouping.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main mechanistic theme is not a hidden oncogenic driver but rather recurrence-associated dysregulation captured by altered RNA-protein coupling and differential expression. The authors propose that poor or altered mRNA-protein correlation may reflect post-transcriptional and post-translational regulation (for example splicing, microRNA, RNA localization, phosphorylation, ubiquitination, and altered degradation) and can serve as a state marker of tumor biology.
alteration_or_activation_mode Altered pathway or state is inferred from differential RNA-protein correlation and differential abundance rather than from a specific recurrent mutation or fusion. The operational mode is mostly expression-level dysregulation, including weak differential RNA expression without protein change for some genes and cohort-specific changes in correlation structure.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway No canonical hidden-driver axis is established. The paper highlights mRNA splicing as a pathway whose correlation behavior differs from other cancers and discusses TIMM50, SUMO1, PCBD1, PSMC5, ARCN1, PPA2, and SRI as recurrence-associated integrative biomarker genes, but these are not validated as oncogenic drivers. The only explicit pathway-level example tied to a gene is TIMM50 in the mitochondrial apoptosis pathway.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is indirect and statistical rather than biochemical: genome-wide RNA-protein Spearman correlations differed significantly between recurrent and nonrecurrent tumors (Wilcoxon p < 10^-16), and pathway enrichments were examined among low- and high-correlated genes. The paper reports that mRNA splicing is enriched for high mRNA-protein correlation in lung adenocarcinoma, but it does not present pERK/pAKT/pS6, kinase activity, IHC, phosphoproteomics, or other direct pathway activation readouts.
driver_negative_subtype An implicit subtype is recurrence-associated versus nonrecurrent lung adenocarcinoma, defined by clinical outcome rather than genomics. The study further stratifies tumors by RNA-protein correlation state and by integrative biomarker signatures, but it does not propose a named molecular subtype of driver-negative LUAD.
clinical_or_exposure_context The cohorts are surgically resected early-stage lung adenocarcinomas, with half selected for rapid recurrence and half for long-term survival. Clinical context includes adjuvant chemotherapy status, stage distribution, sex imbalance between recurrent and nonrecurrent groups, and recurrence outcome; the authors note that some misclassifications in the integrative model involved patients who received chemotherapy.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares RNA-protein correlation patterns with colorectal, breast, ovarian, and other cancers, noting that mRNA splicing is enriched for poor RNA-protein correlation in colorectal, breast, and ovarian cancers but for high correlation in lung adenocarcinoma. It also cites a prior lung cancer proteogenomic study and a general statement that RNA-protein correlation can be phenotype-dependent across diseases and species.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The therapeutic implication is prognostic and stratification-oriented rather than a direct targeted therapy recommendation. The authors suggest that combined RNA-protein biomarkers could help identify who benefits from adjuvant therapy and potentially spare toxicity, but they also state that the 71% accuracy is not yet clinically useful. No specific drug vulnerability is validated, although gene annotations mention processes related to chemotherapy resistance, apoptosis, radiosensitivity, proteasome function, and multidrug resistance.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is limited to the study cohort and internal leave-one-out cross-validation. The integrative biomarker reached 71% overall accuracy in recurrence prediction (36/51) and outperformed RNA-only or protein-only approaches, but there is no independent validation cohort, no cell-line or animal validation, and the authors explicitly call for independent validation to avoid overfitting.
