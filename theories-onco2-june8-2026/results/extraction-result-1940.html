Paper Abstract: SUMMARY RTK/RAS/RAF pathway alterations (RPAs) are a hallmark of lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD). In this study, we use whole-genome sequencing (WGS) of 85 cases found to be RPA(–) by previous studies from The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) to characterize the minority of LUADs lacking apparent alterations in this pathway. We show that WGS analysis uncovers RPA(+) in 28 (33%) of the 85 samples. Among the remaining 57 cases, we observe focal deletions targeting the promoter or transcription start site of STK11 (n = 7) or KEAP1 (n = 3), and promoter mutations associated with the increased expression of ILF2 (n = 6). We also identify complex structural variations associated with high-level copy number amplifications. Moreover, an enrichment of focal deletions is found in TP53 mutant cases. Our results indicate that RPA(–) cases demonstrate tumor suppressor deletions and genome instability, but lack unique or recurrent genetic lesions compensating for the lack of RPAs. Larger WGS studies of RPA(–) cases are required to understand this important LUAD subset.

driver_negative_definition The paper defines driver-negative LUAD as cases lacking an RTK/RAS/RAF pathway alteration after multi-platform genomic profiling. Initial negative status (RPA(–)E) was assigned by WES and RNA-seq for absence of activating mutations in KRAS, EGFR, BRAF, ERBB2, MET, RIT1, NRAS, RAF1, HRAS, ARAF, MAP2K1, SOS1; loss-of-function mutations in NF1 or RASA1; fusions in ALK, ROS1, RET, MET, NTRK2; and amplification of EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, MET, FGFR1, MAPK1. WGS then reclassified 28/85 as hidden RPA(+) lesions, leaving 57/85 WGS-negative cases (RPA(–)G) that lacked an RTK/RAS/RAF alteration by WGS as well as WES and RNA-seq.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The paper argues that the remaining WGS-negative tumors do not show a single recurrent hidden oncogenic driver in the RTK/RAS/RAF pathway; instead, they appear to be a heterogeneous subset marked by tumor suppressor loss, elevated genomic instability, recurrent noncoding regulatory events, and complex structural variation. The main candidate driver in the noncoding space is ILF2 promoter mutation with overexpression, while a broader mechanistic theme is loss of tumor suppressors such as TP53, STK11, KEAP1, SMARCA4 and amplification of downstream growth-promoting genes such as MYC/NKX2-1/MCL1.
alteration_or_activation_mode Mixed mechanisms rather than one canonical lesion: focal deletions, promoter/transcription start site deletions, promoter mutations, copy-number amplifications, complex SV-driven amplifications (double minutes, BFBC, tyfonas, pyrgo), and LOH coupled to deletion. For the proposed ILF2 axis, the mode is recurrent promoter mutation in open chromatin associated with increased ILF2 expression; for STK11/KEAP1/NF1/RASA1, the mode is focal loss/deletion with reduced expression.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway RTK/RAS/RAF-MAPK signaling as the reference pathway; within driver-negative tumors the implicated axes are tumor suppressor genes TP53, STK11, KEAP1, SMARCA4, and the noncoding ILF2 promoter/expression axis. Additional altered genes include SETD2, NKX2-1, KAT6A, CCNE1, MDM2, MYC, MCL1, MYCL, ESR1, BLM, FOXO3, UBL3, SOX21, and LIG4, with particular emphasis on loss of negative regulators and amplification of downstream growth-associated programs.
pathway_activation_evidence For the WGS-negative subset, the paper does not present direct biochemical pathway activation readouts such as pERK/pAKT/pS6. Instead, it infers biology from genomic patterns: high TP53 mutation frequency, elevated tumor mutation burden, enrichment of focal deletions/SVs, reduced expression of deleted tumor suppressors, and ILF2 promoter mutations associated with increased ILF2 expression and higher SV burden. The authors explicitly state that they could not identify unique or recurrent genetic lesions compensating for the lack of RTK/RAS/RAF pathway alterations.
driver_negative_subtype An explicitly proposed subtype is RPA(–)G LUAD, defined genomically as LUAD lacking any RTK/RAS/RAF alteration by WGS, WES, and RNA-seq. Its defining features are heterogeneity, frequent TP53 mutation, high TMB, focal deletions of STK11/KEAP1/SMARCA4/TP53, recurrent ILF2 promoter mutations in a small subset, and abundant complex SVs; the subtype is primarily genomics-based with some transcriptomic corroboration.
clinical_or_exposure_context Compared with RPA(+) LUAD, the WGS-negative RPA(–)G group had more recent smokers (last smoking year < 15). The study reports no significant differences in leukocyte fraction, genome doubling, degree of aneuploidy, age at diagnosis, or genetic ancestry, and it notes that STK11 loss-of-function events were anti-correlated with leukocyte fraction, consistent with an immune-excluded context in some cases.
cross_cancer_parallel The discussion compares this biology to other cancers where RTK/RAS/RAF alterations are rare and tumors show early TP53 loss and high TMB, implying a different evolutionary path less dependent on canonical proliferative signaling. The paper also explicitly references multiple myeloma for ILF2 amplification-driven genomic instability and DNA damage response dysregulation, and it cites general cancer biology work on extrachromosomal DNA, oncogene addiction, and chromosome instability.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications are cautious and mostly indirect. The paper emphasizes that false-negative calls matter because rescued KRAS p.G12C cases could be eligible for targeted KRAS inhibitors, and that better genomic capture/deeper sequencing may alter treatment selection. It also notes that STK11 alterations may be prognostic/predictive biomarkers and have been associated with poor response to immunotherapy; more generally, the authors speculate that RPA(–) tumors may harbor distinct therapeutic vulnerabilities, but they do not establish a validated treatment strategy for the WGS-negative subset.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is genomic and transcriptomic rather than functional. Evidence includes independent reanalysis of WES confirming missed KRAS calls in some cases, WGS detection of cryptic RTK/RAS/RAF lesions, concordant RNA-seq exon-skipping signals for focal deletions in STK11/SMARCA4/TP53, expression changes for deleted genes, FunSeq2 motif annotation for ILF2 promoter variants, and statistical association of ILF2 promoter mutation with increased expression and SV burden. No cell line, organoid, mouse, rescue, or drug sensitivity experiments were performed in this paper.
