brief_description A recurrent somatic chimeric transcript joining the first six exons of CD74 to exons encoding the EGF-like domain of the NRG1 III-β3 isoform. In oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma, it defines a hidden structural driver that is enriched in invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma of never-smokers.
citation_title CD74-NRG1 Fusions in Lung Adenocarcinoma

oncogene_negative_definition The validation cohort consisted of 102 lung adenocarcinomas of never-smokers that were wild-type for EGFR, KRAS, BRAF, ERBB2, ALK, ROS, and RET; the discovery cohort was 25 lung adenocarcinomas of never-smokers lacking KRAS or EGFR mutations. Negative status was established by chromosomal gene copy-number analysis and transcriptome sequencing in the discovery set, and then RT-PCR/FISH confirmation was used to identify the fusion among pan-negative tumors.
assay_strategy_and_detection_limits Discovery used chromosomal copy-number analysis plus RNA-seq/transcriptome sequencing with split-read, read-pair, and de novo assembly-based fusion detection. The paper explicitly notes that the index case was initially among tumors lacking known canonical alterations by routine mutation/fusion screening, and that the novel driver was only detected when RNA-based fusion discovery and breakpoint-level genomic validation were applied; the broader validation then used RT-PCR and break-apart/fusion FISH. Likely blind spots included failure to detect a noncanonical fusion if only hotspot DNA mutation testing had been used, lack of prior knowledge of NRG1 as a target, and the need for transcriptome-level fusion calling to capture the chimeric transcript.
missed_driver_reclassified_after_broader_testing Yes. A tumor initially classified as driver-negative was reclassified as CD74-NRG1-positive after transcriptome sequencing in the discovery cohort, and additional pan-negative tumors were rescued by RT-PCR and confirmed by break-apart FISH. The alteration was missed initially because it was a novel fusion not captured by routine testing for known oncogenic alterations; it required RNA-based fusion discovery and genomic breakpoint validation.
driver_event_class gene fusion / structural rearrangement
candidate_driver_gene_or_locus CD74-NRG1, specifically a fusion of CD74 exons 1-6 with the exons encoding the EGF-like domain of NRG1 III-β3
structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism The CD74 moiety replaces the transmembrane domain of wild-type NRG1 III-β3, yielding a membrane-tethered fusion protein with the NRG1 EGF-like domain displayed extracellularly. This creates a juxtacrine/ligand-presentation mechanism that can engage ERBB2-ERBB3 receptor complexes.
functional_consequence Fusion-positive tumors showed high expression of the NRG1 III-β3 isoform, extracellular presentation of NRG1, phosphorylation of ERBB3, and activation of downstream PI3K-AKT signaling. Ectopic expression increased colony formation in soft agar, indicating oncogenic transformation potential.
co_alteration_or_combinatorial_requirement The paper notes ERBB2 and ERBB3 expression in the index case, and functional activation depended on ERBB2/ERBB3-expressing recipient cells. It also reports that H1568/NIH-3T3 cells expressing ERBB2 and ERBB3 can be oncogenic and that CD74-NRG1 acts through ERBB2-ERBB3 heterodimers; no obligatory additional genomic co-driver is identified.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment Detected in 1/25 discovery tumors and 4/102 additional pan-negative validation tumors, for 5 total cases in the study; all 5 were invasive mucinous adenocarcinomas of never-smokers and women. In a separate small IMA cohort of 15 tumors, 4/15 (27%) carried CD74-NRG1 and 6/15 (40%) carried KRAS mutations. The authors estimate an overall lung adenocarcinoma frequency of approximately 1.7% in their validation context.
functional_or_clinical_validation Validated by transcriptome sequencing, RT-PCR, break-apart FISH, and hybrid-capture genomic sequencing spanning the breakpoint. Functional evidence included intracellular/extracellular flow cytometry localization, induction of p-ERBB2/p-ERBB3/p-AKT/p-S6K in lung cancer cells, loss of signaling with an EGF-like-domain-deleted fusion, coculture-induced AKT activation, and enhanced anchorage-independent growth in soft agar.
