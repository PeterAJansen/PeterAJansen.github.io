Paper Abstract: Lung cancer in never smokers (LCINS) is a common cause of cancer mortality but its genomic landscape is poorly characterized. Here high-coverage whole-genome sequencing of 232 LCINS showed 3 subtypes defined by copy number aberrations. The dominant subtype (piano), which is rare in lung cancer in smokers, features somatic UBA1 mutations, germline AR variants and stem cell-like properties, including low mutational burden, high intratumor heterogeneity, long telomeres, frequent KRAS mutations and slow growth, as suggested by the occurrence of cancer drivers’ progenitor cells many years before tumor diagnosis. The other subtypes are characterized by specific amplifications and EGFR mutations (mezzo-forte) and whole-genome doubling (forte). No strong tobacco smoking signatures were detected, even in cases with exposure to secondhand tobacco smoke. Genes within the receptor tyrosine kinase–Ras pathway had distinct impacts on survival; five genomic alterations independently doubled mortality. These findings create avenues for personalized treatment in LCINS. driver_negative_definition The paper does not define a formal 'oncogene-negative' LUAD panel by a single exclusion list; instead, its closest driver-negative category is the 'piano' subtype, in which LUAD rarely harbored known recurrent drivers and showed a scarcity of TP53 deficiency and RTK-RAS pathway aberrations, with low burden of SNVs/indels, SCNAs, SVs, kataegis, and WGD. The assay basis was high-coverage whole-genome sequencing with SCNA clustering plus driver-gene/SV calling; negative here means absence or rarity of canonical recurrent drivers/fusions and lack of major copy-number instability rather than complete absence of all alterations.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver hypothesis is a stem cell-like, quiescent-state lineage program rather than a single dominant oncogenic pathway lesion. The authors propose that mutations in KRAS, UBA1, RET, ARID1A, NOTCH1, SETD2, or NKX2-1, and perhaps hormone-related germline variation (AR, CYP21A2), may promote exit from quiescence and generate high intra-tumor heterogeneity, but they emphasize that some piano tumors may be diagnosed before acquisition of a dominant clone.
alteration_or_activation_mode Predominantly rare somatic driver mutations, occasional fusions (notably RET fusions), and germline susceptibility variants, occurring in a background of low copy-number disruption. The proposed mechanism is not a single recurrent amplification or canonical pathway activation, but a combination of rare driver events and lineage-state / stemness-associated regulation.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Stem cell quiescence/activation and developmental lineage programs, with specific genes highlighted including KRAS, UBA1, RET, ARID1A, NOTCH1, NKX2-1, SETD2, SOX2, SOX9, and HMGA2. The paper also notes mutual exclusivity among KRAS, UBA1, RET, and ARID1A in piano tumors.
pathway_activation_evidence No phosphoproteomic or direct signaling readout is presented for piano. Evidence is indirect: the tumors have long telomeres, low mutational burden, high subclonal mutation ratio, low ROS-associated SBS18, few known drivers, and higher RNA-seq-derived 'development score' and a basal-cell marker associated with lineage infidelity. These observations are interpreted as consistent with a stem cell-like state rather than a genetically obvious activated oncogenic pathway.
driver_negative_subtype The explicitly proposed driver-negative-like subtype is 'piano' (genomically defined by low arm-level SCNAs). It is a genomic/copy-number subtype with an inferred lineage-state/stemness phenotype from RNA-seq, especially in LUAD within the subtype.
clinical_or_exposure_context Piano tumors occurred in never-smokers; compared with forte and mezzo-forte, they had longer latency from most recent common ancestor to diagnosis, overall better survival, and were enriched for carcinoids. The authors note that some piano LUADs rarely harbored known recurrent drivers, and they speculate that these cases may be diagnosed prior to dominant clonal sweep.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper compares piano-like stemness/quiescence biology to adult stem cells and to normal or near-normal bronchial epithelial cells from former smokers that also show long telomeres and polyclonal origins. It also links ARID1A, NOTCH1, KRAS, and RET to broader stem-cell biology across hematopoietic, pluripotent, and regenerating tissues.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic options are limited because of the scarcity of canonical driver targets and low TMB/HLA-LOH, which may reduce immunotherapy benefit. The authors propose exploring KRAS targeting, stem cell-associated signaling pathways, and stem-cell microenvironment regulation; they also suggest that early detection may be particularly valuable because the founder cell appears about a decade before diagnosis.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated primarily by WGS in 232 never-smoker lung cancers, RNA sequencing in 35 adenocarcinomas, SCNA clustering, evolutionary timing analyses, and comparison with TCGA smoker LUAD and PCAWG timing frameworks. The stemness hypothesis is supported only indirectly and the authors explicitly state that larger studies and single-cell RNA-seq/methylome analyses are needed for confirmation.
