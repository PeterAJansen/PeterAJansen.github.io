oncogene_negative_definition The discovery cohort comprised 25 lung adenocarcinomas from never-smokers lacking mutations in KRAS or EGFR, analyzed by chromosomal gene copy-number analysis and transcriptome sequencing. For validation, the authors screened 102 additional pan-negative adenocarcinomas of never-smokers that were wild-type for EGFR, KRAS, BRAF, ERBB2, ALK, ROS, and RET, using RT-PCR for the chimeric transcript and confirmatory break-apart FISH.
assay_strategy_and_detection_limits Driver search used SNP 6.0 copy-number profiling, RNA-seq with a split-read/read-pair/de novo assembly fusion-calling pipeline, targeted capture genomic sequencing of 333 cancer genes, RT-PCR validation, and FISH. Likely blind spots include alterations not covered by the 333-gene capture panel, fusions missed without RNA evidence or if computational breakpoint detection fails, and events outside the tested canonical genes; the paper also notes that NRG1 was generally not expressed in lung adenocarcinoma, emphasizing that the fusion was detected via chimeric transcript sequencing rather than expression alone.
missed_driver_reclassified_after_broader_testing Yes. One tumor initially considered driver-negative in the validation cohort was reclassified as CD74-NRG1 positive after broader RNA-based screening (RT-PCR for the fusion transcript) and confirmation by break-apart FISH. The rescue alteration was a CD74-NRG1 fusion, which likely would have been missed by initial canonical driver testing because it is a rare noncanonical fusion outside the standard set of EGFR/KRAS/BRAF/ERBB2/ALK/ROS/RET alterations.

candidate_driver_gene_or_locus CD74-NRG1 (CD74 fused to NRG1 III-β3, specifically the EGF-like domain-encoding exons of NRG1)
structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism The fusion replaces the normal NRG1 III-β3 transmembrane context with CD74-derived sequence while preserving and presenting the EGF-like ligand domain at the cell surface; this creates a membrane-bound ligand that can engage ERBB2-ERBB3 receptor complexes.
functional_consequence Activation of ERBB3 phosphorylation and the PI3K-AKT pathway, increased p-ERBB2/p-ERBB3/p-AKT/p-S6K in engineered cells, and enhanced anchorage-independent colony formation.
co_alteration_or_combinatorial_requirement The fusion acts in the context of cells/tumors expressing ERBB2 and ERBB3; the paper specifically shows signaling in cells with normal ERBB2/ERBB3 levels and notes that NIH-3T3 cells ectopically expressing ERBB2 and ERBB3 are oncogenic. No additional cooperating genomic alteration is reported as required for the fusion’s activity.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment Detected in 1 of 25 never-smoker lung adenocarcinomas in the discovery cohort and 4 of 102 pan-negative validation tumors, for a total of 5 cases. All 5 fusion-positive tumors were invasive mucinous adenocarcinomas of women who had never smoked. In a small East Asian IMA cohort of 15 tumors, 4 (27%) had CD74-NRG1 and 6 (40%) had KRAS mutations; the authors estimate overall frequency in lung adenocarcinoma at about 1.7%.
functional_or_clinical_validation Validated by RT-PCR, break-apart and fusion FISH, and targeted genomic sequencing showing breakpoint-spanning and encompassing reads. Functional validation included ectopic expression in NIH-3T3, H322, H1568, and Ba/F3-based systems, demonstrating membrane localization, ERBB3/AKT activation, dependence on the EGF-like domain, and increased colony formation in soft agar.
