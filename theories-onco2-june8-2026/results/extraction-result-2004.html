Paper Title: Oncogenic and drug sensitive NTRK1 rearrangements in lung cancer

Paper Venue: Nature Medicine

Paper TL;DR: Tumor samples from 3 of 91 patients with lung cancer without known oncogenic alterations assayed by next-generation sequencing or fluorescence in situ hybridization demonstrated evidence of NTRK1 gene fusions.

Paper Abstract: We identified new gene fusions in patients with lung cancer harboring the kinase domain of the NTRK1 gene that encodes the high-affinity nerve growth factor receptor (TRKA protein). Both the MPRIP-NTRK1 and CD74-NTRK1 fusions lead to constitutive TRKA kinase activity and are oncogenic. Treatment of cells expressing NTRK1 fusions with inhibitors of TRKA kinase activity inhibited autophosphorylation of TRKA and cell growth. Tumor samples from 3 of 91 patients with lung cancer (3.3%) without known oncogenic alterations assayed by next-generation sequencing or fluorescence in situ hybridization demonstrated evidence of NTRK1 gene fusions.

driver_negative_definition The paper defines the driver-negative set as lung adenocarcinomas that did not contain known genetic alterations on standard clinical assays, specifically using FoundationOne targeted next-generation sequencing and/or fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) to exclude common actionable oncogenic alterations. In the cohort, 36 lung adenocarcinoma samples were selected because their tumors 'did not contain known genetic alterations using standard clinical assays,' and additional cases were screened by NTRK1 break-apart FISH after no detectable oncogenic alterations were found.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver hypothesis is that a rare receptor tyrosine kinase fusion, NTRK1 rearrangement, acts as the oncogenic driver in otherwise driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma. The mechanism is constitutive TRKA activation through fusion partner-mediated dimerization/localization, leading to downstream MAPK and PI3K/AKT signaling and drug sensitivity to TRKA inhibitors.
alteration_or_activation_mode In-frame gene fusion/rearrangement involving the kinase domain of NTRK1. The fusion partners (MPRIP or CD74) provide domains predicted to mediate dimerization or membrane localization, enabling constitutive kinase activation and autophosphorylation.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway NTRK1/TRKA signaling with downstream MAPK/ERK and PI3K/AKT pathways; the fusions also support IL-3-independent proliferation in Ba/F3 cells and anchorage-independent growth in NIH3T3 cells.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence for pathway activation includes TRKA autophosphorylation at critical tyrosine residues in patient material and engineered cells, increased pERK and pAKT in fusion-expressing cells, and suppression of autophosphorylation plus downstream MAPK/AKT activation by TRKA inhibitors. Quantitative PCR showed high NTRK1 kinase-domain expression only in rearranged tumors or in the KM12 control fusion line; FISH confirmed NTRK1 rearrangements.
driver_negative_subtype An implicit genomic subtype of lung adenocarcinoma defined by absence of known canonical oncogenic alterations but presence of an actionable kinase fusion. The subtype is not given a formal label beyond 'NTRK1 rearranged' or 'without known oncogenic alterations,' and it is based on integrated DNA-based NGS, FISH, RT-PCR, and expression validation.
clinical_or_exposure_context The index cases were lung adenocarcinoma patients lacking standard actionable alterations; one index patient had no standard therapies or clinical trials available and received crizotinib off-trial. The paper does not define a consistent smoking or histologic exposure pattern for the NTRK1-rearranged cases in the quoted text, but they are explicitly within lung adenocarcinoma and NSCLC.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares NTRK1-fusion biology to ALK, EGFR, ROS1, RET, and FGFR fusion-driven cancers, including parallels to colorectal and thyroid cancers where NTRK1 fusions had previously been identified. It also notes that CD74 is an activating fusion partner of ROS1 and that TPM3-NTRK1 was already known in colorectal cancer.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Selective TRKA inhibition is the key therapeutic implication. ARRY-470 and CEP-701 were the most potent in vitro inhibitors, with crizotinib showing weaker but measurable activity; all suppressed fusion autophosphorylation and proliferation, and the authors conclude that clinical studies of selective TRKA inhibitors in NTRK1-rearranged NSCLC are warranted. The index patient had a minor radiographic response to crizotinib followed by progression after about 3 months, supporting only modest clinical activity for crizotinib.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Strong multi-level validation was performed: targeted NGS discovery in lung adenocarcinoma samples; orthogonal confirmation by RT-PCR, cDNA cloning, and FISH; expression of fusion constructs in 293T, NIH3T3, and Ba/F3 cells; demonstration of autophosphorylation and downstream signaling; IL-3-independent Ba/F3 proliferation; anchorage-independent growth in soft agar; tumor formation in nude mice; knockdown of TPM3-NTRK1 in KM12 cells reducing proliferation; and partial clinical evidence from crizotinib treatment of one patient.
