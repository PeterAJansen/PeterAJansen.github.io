Paper Abstract: The molecular underpinnings that drive the heterogeneity of KRAS-mutant lung adenocarcinoma (LUAC) are poorly characterized. We performed an integrative analysis of genomic, transcriptomic and proteomic data from early-stage and chemo-refractory LUAC and identified three robust subsets of KRAS-mutant LUAC dominated, respectively, by co-occurring genetic events in STK11/LKB1 (the KL subgroup), TP53 (KP) and CDKN2A/B inactivation coupled with low expression of the NKX2-1 (TTF1) transcription factor (KC). We further reveal biologically and therapeutically relevant differences between the subgroups. KC tumors frequently exhibited mucinous histology and suppressed mTORC1 signaling. KL tumors had high rates of KEAP1 mutational inactivation and expressed lower levels of immune markers, including PD-L1. KP tumors demonstrated higher levels of somatic mutations, inflammatory markers, immune checkpoint effector molecules and improved relapse-free survival. Differences in drug sensitivity patterns were also observed; notably, KL cells showed increased vulnerability to HSP90-inhibitor therapy. This work provides evidence that co-occurring genomic alterations identify subgroups of KRAS-mutant LUAC with distinct biology and therapeutic vulnerabilities.

driver_negative_definition The paper does not define oncogene-negative/driver-negative LUAD as a primary cohort. Instead, it defines KRAS-mutant LUAC subgroups and, for one key point, notes that many KC tumors lack an obvious recurrent genomic co-alteration yet still show a convergent lineage-state phenotype (low NKX2-1/TTF1, mucinous/GI-like differentiation) and reduced mTORC1 signaling. candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver logic is that co-occurring tumor suppressor alterations and lineage-state changes, rather than KRAS allele identity, explain biological divergence. Specifically, STK11/LKB1 loss defines a KL program with functional LKB1-AMPK inactivation and stress-adaptation pathways; TP53 defines KP with inflammatory/immune-engaged biology; and CDKN2A/B loss coupled to NKX2-1/TTF1 low expression defines a KC lineage-reprogrammed, mucinous/GI-like state.
alteration_or_activation_mode Combinatorial co-loss or co-mutation of tumor suppressors (STK11/LKB1, TP53, CDKN2A/B), plus lineage-factor downregulation (NKX2-1/TTF1 low), copy-number loss, and pathway-state changes (protein/phosphoprotein suppression or activation signatures). The paper explicitly argues against KRAS codon/substitution subtype as the primary driver of the clusters.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway STK11/LKB1-AMPK, KEAP1-NRF2 oxidative stress response, CDKN2A/B cell-cycle control, NKX2-1/TTF1 lineage differentiation, mTORC1 signaling, and immune checkpoint/inflammatory pathways (PD-L1/PD-1/CTLA-4, JAK/STAT, interferon signaling).
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is multi-omic: RPPA showed low LKB1 and phospho-AMPK Thr172 in KL; low phospho-S6 and phospho-4EBP1 in KC indicating suppressed mTORC1; NRF2 gene-expression signatures were enriched in KL and KC; KL also had elevated chaperone/UPR signatures (XBP1, ATF4); KP had higher inflammatory/immune checkpoint signatures and elevated PD-L1, PD-1, and CTLA-4. The paper emphasizes that KL pathway state can be functionally inactivated even in STK11-wild-type tumors via suppressed expression and copy-number loss, and that KC mTORC1 suppression is not always fully explained by STK11.
driver_negative_subtype KC is the closest thing to an implicit driver-negative/occult-driver subtype: it is defined by CDKN2A/B inactivation coupled with low NKX2-1/TTF1 expression, frequent mucinous histology, GI-like differentiation (HNF4A, PDX1, CK20, MUC5B, AGR2), and suppressed mTORC1 signaling. However, the paper does not call it driver-negative; rather, it is an integrated multi-omic subtype with a lineage-state component that may substitute for a classical kinase driver explanation in many tumors lacking a single recurrent co-mutation.
clinical_or_exposure_context The cohorts span chemotherapy-naïve mostly early-stage LUAC and metastatic platinum-refractory disease. KP tumors had higher somatic mutation burden and improved relapse-free survival after surgery; KL tumors showed low immune-marker expression and increased KEAP1 co-mutation; KC tumors frequently showed mucinous histology and low/absent TTF1 IHC staining. KRAS alleles were generally not subtype-defining in early-stage disease, but G12D was somewhat enriched in KC in BATTLE-2.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper draws explicit parallels to KRAS-driven murine LUAC models with Nkx2-1 loss causing mucinous/GI-like differentiation, and to immune checkpoint biology seen in other cancers. It also cites colorectal cancer where lymphocytic infiltration correlates with better prognosis, as an analog for the immune-engaged KP subgroup.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication KL/LKB1-inactivated tumors show increased sensitivity to HSP90 inhibitors (17-AAG, ganetespib, AUY922) in cell lines and are proposed to be vulnerable to NRF2-linked oxidative-stress/proteostasis targeting. KP tumors, with inflammatory and checkpoint-high profiles, are suggested to be more amenable to immune checkpoint blockade (PD-L1/PD-1/CTLA-4 axis). The paper also notes that pathway-state biomarkers such as LKB1 or phospho-AMPK IHC, serum biomarkers, expression signatures, or surrogate KEAP1/ATM mutations could improve patient stratification for therapy.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is strong and multi-layered: independent human cohorts (TCGA, PROSPECT, Chitale, BATTLE-2) replicated cluster structure; IHC confirmed low TTF1 in KC; clonality analysis supported early clonal co-mutation events; isogenic KRAS-mutant NSCLC cell lines with LKB1 gain/loss showed altered HSP90 inhibitor sensitivity; a broad drug-sensitivity panel and GDSC reanalysis supported the HSP90 vulnerability; and literature/GEMM data were used to contextualize lineage-state and immune findings.
