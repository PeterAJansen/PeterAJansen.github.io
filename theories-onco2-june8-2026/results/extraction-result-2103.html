assay_strategy_and_detection_limits The discovery pipeline used matched tumor-normal whole-genome sequencing (37.9x tumor, 37.7x normal) plus RNA sequencing of the primary tumor. It detected 25,150 somatic mutations, 101 nonsynonymous coding changes, and 2 in-frame fusions by WGS, but only 15 variants were expressed at >2 supporting RNA reads. The paper explicitly notes that broad low-level copy gains were present without high-level focal amplifications, and that initial profiling did not reveal known recurrent oncogenic alterations or other sorafenib-target alterations.
missed_driver_reclassified_after_broader_testing Yes. A tumor initially lacking known actionable drivers was reclassified as harboring a likely driver after broader unbiased WGS/RNA-seq revealed the expressed ARAF S214C mutation. It was missed by standard driver-gene expectations because it is a noncanonical RAF-family alteration outside classic hotspots such as BRAF V600/ G469 and because the tumor otherwise lacked common lung adenocarcinoma oncogenes.

structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism Non-kinase-domain Raf-family hotspot mutation in the CR2 regulatory region; the paper suggests it likely disrupts 14-3-3 binding and thereby relieves negative regulation of RAF signaling.
functional_consequence Increased MEK phosphorylation and anchorage-independent growth/soft agar colony formation in immortalized tracheobronchial epithelial cells, consistent with MAPK pathway activation and oncogenic transformation.
co_alteration_or_combinatorial_requirement The tumor also showed low-level broad gains including 1-2 copy gain of 4q12 (PDGFRA, KIT, KDR), but the paper argues these were not the primary driver because there were no high-level focal amplifications and no other known oncogenic alterations. The main explanatory event was the ARAF codon 214 mutation.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment Observed as 3 ARAF codon 214 mutant cases among 564 lung adenocarcinomas profiled across TCGA and the authors' prior cohort, corresponding to roughly 1% frequency; the paper also reports one sorafenib-responsive index case. Across 4,608 TCGA pan-cancer cases, additional codon 214/nearby RAF1 hotspot mutations were found in 21 tumor types, with notable counts in colorectal adenocarcinoma, gastric adenocarcinoma, and cutaneous melanoma.
functional_or_clinical_validation Expressing ARAF S214C (and two other codon 214 variants) in AALE cells increased soft agar colony formation and MEK phosphorylation, and these phenotypes were inhibited by sorafenib and trametinib. The index patient had a near-complete response to sorafenib lasting about 5 years, providing matched clinical support.
