Paper Title: Combinatorial inactivation of tumor suppressors efficiently initiates lung adenocarcinoma with therapeutic vulnerabilities

Paper Venue: Cancer Research

Paper TL;DR: Generation of hundreds of diverse combinations of tumor suppressor alterations demonstrates that inactivation of suppressors of the RAS and PI3K pathways drives the development of oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma.

Paper Abstract: Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer death worldwide, with lung adenocarcinoma being the most common subtype. Many oncogenes and tumor suppressor genes are altered in this cancer type, and the discovery of oncogene mutations has led to the development of targeted therapies that have improved clinical outcomes. However, a large fraction of lung adenocarcinomas lacks mutations in known oncogenes, and the genesis and treatment of these oncogene-negative tumors remain enigmatic. Here, we perform iterative in vivo functional screens using quantitative autochthonous mouse model systems to uncover the genetic and biochemical changes that enable efficient lung tumor initiation in the absence of oncogene alterations. Generation of hundreds of diverse combinations of tumor suppressor alterations demonstrates that inactivation of suppressors of the RAS and PI3K pathways drives the development of oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma. Human genomic data and histology identified RAS/MAPK and PI3K pathway activation as a common event in oncogene-negative human lung adenocarcinomas. These Onc-negativeRAS/PI3K tumors and related cell lines are vulnerable to pharmacological inhibition of these signaling axes. These results transform our understanding of this prevalent yet understudied subtype of lung adenocarcinoma.

driver_negative_definition The paper operationalizes oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma as tumors with no alterations in known proto-oncogenes/high-confidence oncogenic alterations, while oncogene-indeterminate tumors carry alterations of unknown significance in known proto-oncogenes. In the TCGA/GENIE analyses, negativity was assessed from somatic mutation data, copy-number alteration data, fusion data, and MET exon skipping data; a tumor was negative only if it lacked known oncogenic driver alterations in the curated proto-oncogene set. The authors report that ~17–18% of LUAD are oncogene-negative and ~32–45% lack known oncogene mutations when indeterminate cases are included.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main proposed hidden driver is combinatorial tumor suppressor inactivation leading to activation of the RAS and PI3K pathways. The paper specifically identifies a subtype in which loss of negative regulators of these pathways produces an oncogene-negative but pathway-activated state.
alteration_or_activation_mode Combinatorial co-loss/inactivation of tumor suppressors, including genetic deletion or CRISPR-mediated inactivation of Nf1, Rasa1, and Pten, with additional mention that epigenetic silencing or other non-genomic mechanisms can also reduce RAS GAPs/PTEN in human tumors. The mechanism is not a single rare oncogene but multi-gene pathway de-repression.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway NF1/RASA1/PTEN axis, with downstream RAS/MAPK and PI3K/AKT signaling. The therapeutic node tested was SHP2 upstream of RAS and AKT1/2 downstream of PI3K.
pathway_activation_evidence In mouse tumors, immunohistochemistry showed positive pERK and pAKT staining in Nf1/Rasa1/Pten-deficient tumors, indicating RAS and PI3K pathway activation. RNA-seq/ssGSVA confirmed reduced RAS pathway scores relative to KRAS-driven tumors and similar PI3K-AKT scores to KRAS tumors. In human oncogene-negative LUAD, pERK/pAKT IHC showed ~45% with moderate-to-strong activation of both pathways; TCGA analysis found >60% with alterations in either RAS or PI3K pathway components and 22% with alterations in both.
driver_negative_subtype Onc-negative^RAS/PI3K^ subtype. Defined by oncogene-negative status plus biochemical activation of both RAS/MAPK and PI3K/AKT pathways, with frequent NF1/RASA1/PTEN alterations and an expression signature derived from Nf1/Rasa1/Pten-deficient mouse tumors.
clinical_or_exposure_context The paper reports broadly similar mutational burden and clinical characteristics across oncogene-negative, oncogene-indeterminate, and oncogene-positive LUAD in TCGA, but does not identify a strong exposure or demographic enrichment for this subtype. It does note that GENIE frequencies were lower than TCGA, likely due to limited panel coverage. Some cited prior work associates RASA1/NF1-mutant lung cancer with smoking, but this paper itself does not establish a specific smoking or histologic enrichment for the subtype.
cross_cancer_parallel The authors explicitly compare the logic to other cancers in which pathway convergence and co-mutation define biologically distinct subsets, and they reference RASA1/NF1-mutant lung cancer as a distinct smoking-associated subset sensitive to MEK inhibition. The general concept is analogous to cancers where loss of tumor suppressors activates canonical driver pathways rather than a single oncogene.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Onc-negative^RAS/PI3K^ tumors are vulnerable preclinically to SHP2 inhibition with RMC-4550 and AKT1/2 inhibition with capivasertib, with the combination being more effective than either agent alone. In mice, RMC-4550 reduced tumor burden, capivasertib monotherapy was ineffective in vivo, and the combination reduced burden further; in cell lines, the combination synergized to inhibit growth, induce apoptosis, and reduce proliferation, and it also regressed allografts. The authors suggest additional targeting of SOS, MEK, ERK, PI3K, and mTOR as rational future strategies.

The mechanistic claim is supported by both mouse and human data plus pharmacologic perturbation.
