Paper Abstract: Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer death worldwide, with lung adenocarcinoma being the most common subtype. Many oncogenes and tumor suppressor genes are altered in this cancer type, and the discovery of oncogene mutations has led to the development of targeted therapies that have improved clinical outcomes. However, a large fraction of lung adenocarcinomas lacks mutations in known oncogenes, and the genesis and treatment of these oncogene-negative tumors remain enigmatic. Here, we perform iterative in vivo functional screens using quantitative autochthonous mouse model systems to uncover the genetic and biochemical changes that enable efficient lung tumor initiation in the absence of oncogene alterations. Generation of hundreds of diverse combinations of tumor suppressor alterations demonstrates that inactivation of suppressors of the RAS and PI3K pathways drives the development of oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma. Human genomic data and histology identified RAS/MAPK and PI3K pathway activation as a common event in oncogene-negative human lung adenocarcinomas. These Onc-negativeRAS/PI3K tumors and related cell lines are vulnerable to pharmacological inhibition of these signaling axes. These results transform our understanding of this prevalent yet understudied subtype of lung adenocarcinoma.

oncogene_negative_definition Human LUADs were classified as oncogene-negative if they had no alterations in known proto-oncogenes; oncogene-positive if they had high-confidence oncogenic alterations in previously described proto-oncogenes; oncogene-indeterminate if they had alterations of unknown significance in known proto-oncogenes. The data sources were TCGA LUAD and AACR GENIE v8, with somatic mutation data (SNPs/indels, including silent mutations), GISTIC2 thresholded copy-number variation, fusion data, and MET exon-skipping data; the paper also sequenced 10 genomic regions in KRAS, BRAF, NRAS, and EGFR in mouse tumors to exclude hotspot oncogene mutations.
assay_strategy_and_detection_limits The paper used targeted human genomic analyses (TCGA/GENIE somatic mutations, CNV, fusions, MET exon skipping) and mouse tumor hotspot sequencing of KRAS/BRAF/NRAS/EGFR regions, plus whole-exome sequencing on selected mouse tumors, RNA-seq/ssGSVA for pathway-state inference, and IHC for pERK, pAKT, and PTEN protein. The authors explicitly note blind spots: a noncomprehensive actionable mutation panel (STAMP) likely missed some pathway alterations; epigenetic silencing and other non-genomic mechanisms can inactivate tumor suppressors without detectable DNA alterations; and GENIE frequencies were lower possibly because only a fraction of known pathway genes were analyzed.
missed_driver_reclassified_after_broader_testing Yes, in the sense that many oncogene-negative tumors initially lacked explanatory DNA-level alterations on limited panels but were later reinterpreted as pathway-driven after broader testing. Specifically, RAS/PI3K pathway activation was found by pERK/pAKT IHC and supported by RNA-seq/ssGSVA even when NF1/PTEN/other panel genes were not mutated; 13/20 oncogene-negative tumors without genomic PTEN mutations had low PTEN protein by IHC, implying epigenetic/non-genomic PTEN loss. The paper does not report a single rescued hidden oncogenic SNV/fusion/exon-skipping event, but rather a broader reclassification from 'driver-negative' to pathway-activated due to assay limitations.
driver_event_class Combinatorial tumor suppressor loss, with non-genomic/epigenetic tumor suppressor inactivation and pathway activation rather than a hidden canonical oncogene SNV/fusion.
candidate_driver_gene_or_locus NF1, RASA1, PTEN, and related negative regulators of the RAS and PI3K pathways; in mouse models the key combinatorial genotype was Nf1/Rasa1/Pten loss.
structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism Loss of negative regulation of RAS and PI3K signaling through inactivation of RAS GAPs (NF1, RASA1) and PTEN, with some tumors likely driven by epigenetic silencing or other non-genomic mechanisms of suppressor downregulation. The mechanism is not structural rearrangement but functional pathway derepression.
functional_consequence Constitutive activation of RAS/MAPK and PI3K/AKT signaling, evidenced by pERK and pAKT staining and pathway-gene-signature activation; this biochemical state supports lung adenocarcinoma initiation and growth and creates vulnerability to SHP2 and AKT inhibition.
co_alteration_or_combinatorial_requirement Single and pairwise tumor suppressor inactivation was rarely sufficient; combinatorial inactivation of three or more suppressors was generally required for efficient tumor initiation. The most potent combination was simultaneous Nf1, Rasa1, and Pten inactivation, and additional Trp53 loss did not materially increase early initiation in the model.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment Approximately 30% of LUADs lack a driving oncogene; in this study 17–18% of LUADs were oncogene-negative and 32–45% lacked known oncogene mutations when oncogene-indeterminate tumors were included. Among oncogene-negative human tumors analyzed, ~45% had moderate to strong activation of both RAS and PI3K pathways by IHC; in TCGA, over 60% of oncogene-negative LUADs had alterations in either the RAS or PI3K pathways, and 22% had alterations in components of both pathways.
functional_or_clinical_validation Mouse autochthonous models showed that combinatorial Nf1/Rasa1/Pten loss initiates tumors nearly as potently as KRASG12D. Human validation came from pERK/pAKT IHC, PTEN IHC, and TCGA/GENIE pathway-alteration analyses. Therapeutically, these tumors were vulnerable to SHP2 inhibitor RMC-4550 and AKT inhibitor capivasertib, with synergy in mouse tumors, murine cell lines, and human oncogene-negative LUAD cell lines (NCI-H1838, NCI-H1623).
