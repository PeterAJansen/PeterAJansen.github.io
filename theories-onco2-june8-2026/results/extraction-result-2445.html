brief_description A novel oncogenic fusion discovered in a lung adenocarcinoma from a never-smoker patient lacking canonical EGFR, KRAS, and EML4-ALK alterations. The fusion joins the KIF5B coiled-coil dimerization region to the RET tyrosine kinase domain, producing an overexpressed chimeric RET kinase that can spontaneously transform cells and is proposed as a driver event in a subset of NSCLCs.

driver_negative_definition The paper operationalized driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma primarily as tumors negative for the canonical 'triple-markers' EGFR, KRAS, and EML4-ALK. In the index case, pathologic testing found no known driver mutation in EGFR or KRAS and no EML4-ALK fusion; in replication cohorts, five additional 'triple-negative' primary lung adenocarcinomas were negative for EGFR, KRAS, and EML4-ALK, and 15 additional 'double-negative' tumors were negative for EGFR and EML4-ALK while KRAS status was unknown. The negative call was made by clinical PCR/DNA sequencing for EGFR/KRAS exon hotspots and FISH/pathology for EML4-ALK, then further interrogated by whole-genome and transcriptome sequencing.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden driver hypothesis is a kinase-fusion oncogene mechanism: a chromosomal inversion creates KIF5B-RET, which activates RET receptor tyrosine kinase signaling through KIF5B-mediated dimerization. The authors frame this as a previously unrecognized driver mutation explaining a subset of oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinomas rather than a broader pathway-state phenotype.
alteration_or_activation_mode Chromosomal inversion generating an in-frame fusion transcript/protein; the fusion preserves the KIF5B coiled-coil dimerization domain and RET kinase domain, leading to constitutive dimerization and autophosphorylation/activation of RET. The fusion also causes RET exon 12 onward to be highly expressed from the fusion transcript.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway KIF5B-RET / RET receptor tyrosine kinase axis; mechanistically tied to kinase activation by coiled-coil-mediated dimerization. The paper does not present downstream pathway readouts such as MAPK or PI3K activation, but the biologically implicated axis is RET oncogenic signaling.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is transcript-level and structural rather than phosphoproteomic: 34 discordant paired-end reads and 60 spanning reads support the fusion; PCR and Sanger sequencing validate cDNA and genomic breakpoints; RET exons 12-20 are exclusively expressed in fusion-positive tumor tissue and are ~10-fold higher than in RET-rearrangement-negative lung cancers; the fusion protein architecture predicts an active kinase with a dimerization domain. No pRET/pERK/pAKT IHC or phosphoproteomic data are reported.
driver_negative_subtype An implicit genomically defined subtype of lung adenocarcinoma: young, never-smoker, triple-negative or double-negative tumors enriched for a hidden kinase-fusion driver. The subtype is based on integrated DNA/RNA sequencing rather than histology alone, and is characterized by absence of common actionable EGFR/KRAS/EML4-ALK events but presence of KIF5B-RET in a subset.
clinical_or_exposure_context The index patient was a 33-year-old never-smoker with no family history of cancer and poorly differentiated lung adenocarcinoma with liver and bone metastases. The recurrent cases were also primary lung adenocarcinomas from patients selected as EGFR/KRAS/EML4-ALK negative (or EGFR/EML4-ALK negative) in the clinical pathology workflow.
cross_cancer_parallel RET fusion biology is explicitly compared with papillary thyroid carcinoma, where RET translocations/inversions are established drivers; the paper also notes activated RET in prostate cancer, pancreatic cancer, melanoma, and RET transgenic mice. The KIF5B dimerization strategy is also contextualized alongside other fusion oncogenes such as EML4-ALK and KIF5B-ALK.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The authors propose KIF5B-RET as a promising molecular target for personalized diagnosis and treatment of lung adenocarcinoma, and state that development of specific agents targeting KIF5B-RET could provide more advanced therapeutic strategies. This is inferential and preclinical; no drug sensitivity experiments or patient treatment responses are reported.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated by orthogonal sequencing and PCR/Sanger methods in the index case (liver metastasis, primary lung cancer, and bone metastasis) and replicated in an independent set of 20 primary lung adenocarcinomas, where KIF5B-RET was found in 2/20 cases. The paper also cites prior literature on RET transforming activity and notes that the fusion can spontaneously induce cellular transformation, but it does not present a cell-line or animal model experiment in this study.
