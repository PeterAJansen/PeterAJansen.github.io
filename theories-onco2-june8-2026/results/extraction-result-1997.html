Paper Title: Somatic mutations affect key pathways in lung adenocarcinoma

Paper Venue: Nature

Paper TL;DR: Somatic mutations in primary lung adenocarcinoma for several tumour suppressor genes involved in other cancers and for sequence changes in PTPRD as well as the frequently deleted gene LRP1B are found.

Paper Abstract: Determining the genetic basis of cancer requires comprehensive analyses of large collections of histopathologically well-classified primary tumours. Here we report the results of a collaborative study to discover somatic mutations in 188 human lung adenocarcinomas. DNA sequencing of 623 genes with known or potential relationships to cancer revealed more than 1,000 somatic mutations across the samples. Our analysis identified 26 genes that are mutated at significantly high frequencies and thus are probably involved in carcinogenesis. The frequently mutated genes include tyrosine kinases, among them the EGFR homologue ERBB4; multiple ephrin receptor genes, notably EPHA3; vascular endothelial growth factor receptor KDR; and NTRK genes. These data provide evidence of somatic mutations in primary lung adenocarcinoma for several tumour suppressor genes involved in other cancers—including NF1, APC, RB1 and ATM—and for sequence changes in PTPRD as well as the frequently deleted gene LRP1B. The observed mutational profiles correlate with clinical features, smoking status and DNA repair defects. These results are reinforced by data integration including single nucleotide polymorphism array and gene expression array. Our findings shed further light on several important signalling pathways involved in lung adenocarcinoma, and suggest new molecular targets for treatment.

driver_negative_definition No explicit operational definition of oncogene-negative/driver-negative lung adenocarcinoma is provided. The closest definition in the paper is the set of 25 tumors in which no mutations were found in the 623 sequenced genes; however, these cases are not separately analyzed as a driver-negative subgroup, and the authors note that stromal contamination may have reduced mutation detection sensitivity. The paper also discusses tumors without mutations in canonical genes such as EGFR, KRAS, STK11, TP53, and CDKN2A only indirectly, mainly through mutual exclusivity and pathway analyses.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hypothesis is pathway-level driver dependence rather than a single hidden driver gene: recurrent disruption of MAPK signaling, p53 signaling, Wnt signaling, cell-cycle control, and mTOR signaling. The paper also proposes rare tyrosine kinase drivers/proto-oncogenes (for example ERBB4, EPHA3, KDR, FGFR4, NTRK family members) and loss of tumor suppressors such as NF1, ATM, RB1, APC, PTEN, LRP1B, and PTPRD as mechanistic explanations for tumorigenesis.

For pathway-level interpretation, the paper emphasizes combinatorial loss of negative regulators and recurrent pathway mutation burdens rather than a single recurrent fusion or hotspot event.

pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is genomic/inferential rather than direct phospho-signaling readout. The authors report that 132/188 tumors have at least one MAPK-pathway mutation; 85 tumors have at least one p53-pathway mutation; 29 tumors have Wnt-pathway mutations; and mTOR-pathway components are mutated in 17 genes in more than 30% of sequenced tumors. They also integrate copy number and expression data, showing that some oncogenes (EGFR, KRAS) are associated with copy-number gain and higher mRNA expression, whereas tumor suppressors (TP53, APC, STK11, ATM) correlate with lower copy number and lower expression, but they do not measure pERK, pAKT, pS6, or other direct pathway activity markers.
driver_negative_subtype No explicit molecular subtype of driver-negative LUAD is proposed. The paper does, however, identify a histologic subtype association: mutations in LRP1B, TP53, and INHBA are positively correlated with solid subtype and negatively correlated with acinar, papillary, and BAC subtypes, while EGFR mutations are positively correlated with papillary subtype and negatively correlated with solid subtype. This is histology-based, not a dedicated driver-negative molecular class.
clinical_or_exposure_context The paper links mutation patterns to smoking status, tumor grade, tumor stage, and histologic subtype. EGFR mutations correlate with never-smoker status, KRAS and STK11 mutations correlate with smoker status, and the tumors without detected mutations are enriched for higher stromal contamination. The never-smoker tumors also had a much lower mutation burden overall, with none exceeding five mutations in the resequenced genes.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares several events to other cancers: NF1 mutations are known in neurofibromatosis type I, ATM is a checkpoint kinase regulating p53, RB1 and TP53 are classic targets of DNA tumor viruses, APC is discussed in relation to other lung cancer histologies, and KRAS/EGFR patterns are contextualized with prior studies. It also notes prior findings of LRP1B inactivation in lung and esophageal cancers and PTEN mutation in lung cancer.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The authors suggest that newly identified genes and pathways expand therapeutic options. They specifically propose testing MEK inhibitors in tumors with NF1 mutations and testing KDR inhibitors such as sorafenib and sunitinib in tumors with KDR mutations. They also state that dysregulation of mTOR is a potential therapeutic target and that the effectiveness of rapamycin and its analogues in lung adenocarcinoma should be further tested.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is primarily genomic and integrative: non-synonymous mutations were confirmed by orthogonal methods or independent PCR amplification and sequencing; SNP array and gene expression array data were integrated with mutation calls; and copy-number/mRNA correlations were analyzed in a subset of 41 tumors with all three data types. The pathway and subtype conclusions are not validated with functional cell-line, organoid, xenograft, or drug-response experiments in this paper.
