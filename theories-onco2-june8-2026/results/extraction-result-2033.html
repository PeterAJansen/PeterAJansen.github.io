citation_title Kras G12D and Nkx2-1 haploinsufficiency induce mucinous adenocarcinoma of the lung
mention_or_use use
driver_negative_definition The paper does not define a broad oncogene-negative/driver-negative LUAD cohort; instead, it focuses on mucinous adenocarcinoma, which is characterized pathologically and genomically by decreased/absent NKX2-1 and frequent KRAS mutation but rare EGFR mutation. The experimental negative state is operationalized as reduced Nkx2-1 dosage (Nkx2-1+/-) in mouse respiratory epithelium, combined with oncogenic KrasG12D; the paper also contrasts this with EGFRL858R-driven tumors and with NKX2-1-high settings.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The main hidden-driver concept is lineage-state and transcriptional-program control rather than a new canonical oncogene: loss of NKX2-1 removes a differentiation/goblet-cell suppressor and permits a Kras-driven mucinous tumor program. The paper also identifies AP-1-linked transcriptional activation as a downstream mechanistic axis, with NKX2-1 normally inhibiting AP-1 and mucin/tumor-promoting genes.
alteration_or_activation_mode Combinatorial co-loss of a tumor suppressive lineage regulator (Nkx2-1 haploinsufficiency) with activating KrasG12D; in the broader transcriptional mechanism, NKX2-1 binds chromatin and represses AP-1-associated transcription and mucin/tumorigenic genes. In vitro, enforced NKX2-1 expression suppresses AP-1 activity and colony formation.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Kras/MAPK-driven mucinous adenocarcinoma with a NKX2-1–AP-1 transcriptional axis; downstream suppressed/induced targets include MUC5AC, MUC5B, AGR2, FGFR1, CDK6, FAS, TIMP3, SFTPA1, SFTPB, HNF4A, and SOX2. The paper also discusses EGFRL858R as a contrasting driver context.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence includes increased tumor number/volume and invasive mucinous histology in KrasG12D;Nkx2-1+/- mice, reduced NKX2-1 staining in tumor goblet cells, expression of mucins and goblet-cell markers (Alcian blue, MUC5AC, MUC5B, AGR2, CK7/CK20), microarray-defined NKX2-1-suppressed oncogenic/mucin genes, and ChIP-seq showing direct NKX2-1 binding at target promoters (e.g., MUC5AC, FGFR1, SFTPA1). AP-1 activity was functionally inhibited by NKX2-1 in reporter assays; PMA rescued NKX2-1-mediated suppression of FGFR1 mRNA. The activation state of the mucinous program is therefore experimentally demonstrated and not merely inferred.
driver_negative_subtype A mucinous adenocarcinoma subtype defined by goblet-cell morphology, abundant intracytoplasmic mucin, CK7/CK20 positivity, and low/absent NKX2-1. The subtype is transcriptomically distinct from airway or intestinal goblet cells and is separated from EGFRL858R-driven tumors, which do not form mucinous adenocarcinomas in this model.
clinical_or_exposure_context Human mucinous adenocarcinoma is linked here to KRAS mutations (~76%) and infrequent EGFR mutations, with decreased/absent NKX2-1 expression and abundant mucins such as MUC5AC. The paper notes higher malignant potential than acinar/papillary adenocarcinoma and references association with poor prognosis when the 14q13.3 locus is amplified, whereas NKX2-1 protein levels correlate with favorable prognosis. In the mouse model, tumors are induced in respiratory epithelium and regress upon doxycycline withdrawal after 2 months, indicating continued Kras dependence.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares this lung-lineage mechanism with mucous metaplasia in asthma, COPD, cystic fibrosis, and house dust mite-induced airway disease, where SPDEF/FOXA3 programs operate. It also discusses a thyroid adenoma model in Nkx2-1 reduction and notes that SOX2 is an oncogene in squamous cell lung carcinoma, illustrating context-dependent lineage oncogenicity.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications are preclinical and mechanistic: NKX2-1 suppresses mutant-Kras mucinous adenocarcinoma, suggesting that restoring or mimicking NKX2-1 function, inhibiting AP-1 signaling, or targeting downstream mucin/tumor-promoting genes could be useful. The paper also shows NKX2-1 sensitizes A549 cells to cisplatin-induced apoptosis, linking NKX2-1 status to chemotherapy response.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated across multiple systems: genetically engineered mice (Scgb1a1-rtTA;[tetO]-Kras4bG12D crossed to Nkx2-1+/-), doxycycline-inducible transgene expression, urethane carcinogenesis assays, EGFRL858R comparative mouse models, human lung cancer cell lines (A549, H441, H2122, H3255), lentiviral NKX2-1 overexpression, ChIP-seq, AP-1 luciferase reporter assays, soft agar colony formation, and cisplatin sensitivity/apoptosis assays. The strongest evidence is in vivo GEMM-based and is supported by molecular and functional assays.
