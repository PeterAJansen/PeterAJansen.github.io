Paper Title: Genomic profiling of lung adenocarcinoma patients reveals therapeutic targets and confers clinical benefit when standard molecular testing is negative

Paper Venue: OncoTarget

Paper TL;DR: Fifty eight percent of patients wild type by standard testing for EGFR/KRAS/ALK have GAs identifiable by CGP that suggest benefit from target therapy, and CGP used when standard molecular testing for NSCLC is negative can reveal additional avenues of benefit from targeted therapy.

Paper Abstract: Background: Identification of clinically relevant oncogenic drivers in advanced cancer is critical in selecting appropriate targeted therapy. Using next-generation sequencing (NGS)-based clinical cancer gene assay, we performed comprehensive genomic profiling (CGP) of advanced cases of lung adenocarcinoma. Methods: Formalin-fixed paraffin-embedded tumors from 51 lung adenocarcinoma patients whose tumors previously tested negative for EGFR/KRAS/ALK by conventional methods were collected, and CGP was performed via hybridization capture of 4,557 exons from 287 cancer-related genes and 47 introns from 19 genes frequently rearranged in cancer. Results: Genomic profiles of all 51 cases were obtained, with a median coverage of 564x and a total of 190 individual genomic alterations (GAs). GAs per specimen was a mean of 3.7 (range 0-10).Cancer genomes are characterized by 50% (80/190) non-synonymous base substitutions, 15% (29/190) insertions or deletion, and 3% (5/190) splice site mutation. TP53 mutation was the most common GAs (15%, n=29/190), followed by CDKN2A homozygous loss (5%, n=10/190), KRAS mutation (4%, n=8/190), EGFR mutation (4%, n=8/190) and MDM2 amplification (2%, n=5/190). As per NCCN guidelines, targetable GAs were identified in 16 patients (31%) (BRAF mutation [n=1], EGFR mutation [n=8], ERBB2 mutation [n=4], MET amplification [n=1], KIF5B-RET rearrangement [n=2], CCDC6-RET rearrangement [n=1], CD74-ROS1 rearrangement [n=1], EZR-ROS1 rearrangement [n=5], and SLC34A2-ROS1 rearrangement [n=1]). Conclusion: Fifty eight percent of patients wild type by standard testing for EGFR/KRAS/ALK have GAs identifiable by CGP that suggest benefit from target therapy. CGP used when standard molecular testing for NSCLC is negative can reveal additional avenues of benefit from targeted therapy.

Evidence is genomic/inferential: recurrent alterations affecting PI3K/mTOR components (PIK3CA, AKT1, PIK3R2, STK11, MTOR, RICTOR), plus one PIK3CA E542K mutation noted as previously reported to activate PI3K-AKT signaling. The authors also cite that MET exon 14 skipping is a distinct subtype in NSCLC, but in this cohort they observed MET S163Y with MET amplification and only questioned potential sensitivity to c-MET inhibition.

Implicitly, the paper describes a 'triple-negative' or 'EGFR/KRAS/ALK-negative' advanced lung adenocarcinoma subset, enriched for never-smokers and females, in which broader sequencing reveals hidden actionable drivers and recurrent cell-cycle/PI3K pathway alterations.
clinical_or_exposure_context All cases were lung adenocarcinoma; 76% were never-smokers, 65% female, median age 58 years, and 49% were stage IV at diagnosis. The cohort consisted of patients previously negative by routine EGFR/KRAS/ALK testing, and many were being evaluated for an ROS1 inhibitor trial.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares CDK4/MDM2 co-amplification to patterns seen in sarcomas such as liposarcoma and rhabdomyosarcoma, and notes that co-amplification is known in other cancers. It also cites broader matched-therapy evidence across lung cancer.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Matched targeted therapy is the major implication: EGFR inhibitors, BRAF inhibitor vemurafenib, ALK/ROS1/MET-directed agents such as crizotinib or ceritinib, afatinib for HER2 mutations, cabozantinib for RET rearrangements, and trial-based strategies including MEK inhibitors for NF1/KRAS alterations, CDK4/6 inhibitors for CDKN2A loss/CDK4 amplification, MDM2 inhibitors for MDM2 amplification, and PI3K inhibitors for PIK3CA mutation. Evidence is strongest for ROS1 rearrangements, where treated patients had objective radiologic responses.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is clinical rather than experimental: broader CGP reclassified many 'negative' cases and identified actionable alterations; seven ROS1-rearranged patients were enrolled on an ongoing ceritinib trial and six achieved objective radiologic responses.
