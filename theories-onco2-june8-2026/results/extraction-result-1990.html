driver_negative_definition The paper does not explicitly define 'oncogene-negative' or 'driver-negative' LUAD. Instead, it identifies driver alterations broadly across 16 adenocarcinomas and one large-cell carcinoma using whole-genome sequencing (WGS), RNA-seq, custom capture validation, and recurrence screening in 96 additional primary lung adenocarcinomas. Tumors lacking well-known canonical alterations are not formally categorized as negative; rather, the study emphasizes that some tumors harbor EGFR, KRAS, ROS1, ALK, RET, JAK2, HGF, MET, and other alterations, while others show chromatin/DNA repair pathway lesions, structural variation, and heterogeneous subclonal architectures.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism For tumors without obvious canonical kinase drivers, the paper points to several possible hidden driver classes: chromatin modification genes, DNA repair pathway lesions, cell-cycle pathway alterations, JAK-STAT activation, and novel fusions involving metabolic/other partners. The authors also propose that some alterations may be late-acquired passenger-like events, but that dominant-clone and subclone drivers both need consideration for therapy.
alteration_or_activation_mode The candidate mechanisms include recurrent point mutations, frameshifts, nonsense mutations, copy-number loss, structural rearrangements/fusions, and pathway-level alteration inferred from combined mutation/CNV/RNA data. The paper also discusses mutant-biased expression and high RNA expression as potential functional evidence, but not a specific driver-negative mechanism in the modern sense.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Chromatin modification genes (including DACH1, SETD2, ARID1A, ARID2, MLL3, MLL4, WHSC1L1, ASH1L), DNA repair genes, JAK-STAT signaling, cell-cycle genes, ECM/focal adhesion programs, and receptor tyrosine kinase alterations involving EGFR, KRAS, MET, JAK2, EPHA3, ROS1, and ALK are implicated as functionally important in this cohort.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence comes from PathScan KEGG enrichment, combined analysis of point mutations, indels, copy-number variation, and mRNA expression, and RNA-seq-based expression classification. The JAK-STAT pathway was significantly enriched (p = 0.04), with recurrent JAK2 kinase-domain missense mutations plus JAK3 and STAT1 alterations supporting inferred activation; however, the paper does not provide phosphoproteomic or IHC markers such as pERK or pAKT.
driver_negative_subtype No explicit driver-negative LUAD subtype is named. The closest implicit substructure is the never-smoker adenocarcinoma subset, which is genomically distinct from smoker tumors, enriched for EGFR and fusion oncogenes, and shows a higher proportion of expressed variants. A second implicit structure is clonal architecture (monoclonal versus biclonal tumors), but this is not specific to driver-negative disease.
clinical_or_exposure_context The most emphasized context is smoking status: never-smokers show lower mutation burden and a different substitution spectrum than smokers. The cohort includes 16 adenocarcinomas and one large-cell carcinoma, with 5 never-smokers and 1 former light smoker; the paper notes that EGFR mutations and ALK fusions occur more often in lifelong never-smokers than smokers.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares DNA repair lesions to BRCA-deficient cancers, suggesting potential PARP inhibitor sensitivity, and compares chromatin-modifier alterations to ovarian clear cell carcinoma, hepatocellular carcinoma, glioma, prostate cancer, and endometriosis-associated ovarian cancer. It also references cystic fibrosis as a parallel for CFTR mutation-targeted therapy and myeloproliferative neoplasms for JAK2 gain-of-function biology.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication Therapeutic implications are broad and mostly hypothesis-generating. The authors suggest PARP inhibitors for DNA repair-defective tumors, HDAC inhibitors/epigenetic therapy for chromatin-modifier lesions, and drugs targeting EGFR, ALK, ROS1, RET, JAK2, MET, HGF, EPHA3, BRAF, and other potentially druggable genes. They also stress that therapies may need to target both founder and subclonal alterations to avoid outgrowth of resistant clones.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validation is via targeted sequence capture, recurrence screening in an independent set of 96 primary lung adenocarcinomas, orthogonal validation of fusion calls with WGS, and deep digital sequencing to estimate variant allele frequency and clonality. The study is purely patient-sample based; it does not include cell-line, organoid, xenograft, or perturbation validation of hidden-driver hypotheses.
