citation_title Nkx2-1 Represses a Latent Gastric Differentiation Program in Lung Adenocarcinoma
mention_or_use use
driver_negative_definition The paper does not define driver-negative LUAD by absence of canonical oncogenic mutations in a clinical sequencing sense; instead, it studies tumors that are NKX2-1-negative or engineered to lose Nkx2-1 in Kras-driven mouse models. In human cohorts, NKX2-1-negative tumors are contrasted with NKX2-1-positive tumors by IHC, and the mucinous subset is described as typically lacking NKX2-1 expression; in mice, negative status is created by conditional Nkx2-1 deletion in KrasLSL-G12D or KrasFSF-G12D models, with evaluation by IHC, qRT-PCR, microarray, and histology.
candidate_driver_pathway_or_mechanism The central mechanism is lineage-state reprogramming rather than a new rare oncogene: loss of the pulmonary master regulator NKX2-1 unleashes a latent gastric differentiation program in lung epithelium and tumors. The paper further implicates Foxa1/2 relocalization and Hnf4α-dependent gastric lineage regulation, with Hmga2 de-repression emerging when both Nkx2-1 and Hnf4a programs are lost.
alteration_or_activation_mode Combinatorial loss of a lineage-maintenance transcription factor (Nkx2-1) with downstream epigenetic redistribution of Foxa1/2 binding; in a later step, combined loss of Nkx2-1 and Hnf4a de-represses embryonal Hmga2. The operative mode is transcription factor loss with indirect activation of an alternative differentiation program, not mutation-based kinase activation.
implicated_gene_axis_or_pathway Nkx2-1/Foxa1/2/Hnf4α axis controlling pulmonary versus gastric differentiation, with Hmga2 as an embryonal de-repressed target; gastric markers include Gkn1, CTSE, Muc5AC, PDX1, and HNF4α, and pulmonary markers include proSPC, Sftpa1, Sftpb, and Sftpc. The pathway logic is lineage-state control and epigenetic enhancer/promoter rewiring rather than a canonical RTK/MAPK or PI3K pathway lesion.
pathway_activation_evidence Evidence is transcriptional, histologic, and chromatin-based: NKX2-1 loss causes induction of gastric genes by microarray and qRT-PCR, expression of stomach proteins by IHC, and Foxa1/2 redistribution on ChIP-seq with increased H3K4me1/H3K27ac at de novo gastric sites. No pERK/pAKT-type signaling biomarker is presented as the primary readout; Kras is the initiating oncogene, but the altered state is not genetically explained as a known kinase-pathway mutation.
driver_negative_subtype An explicit or implicit mucinous/gastric-differentiated LUAD subtype is proposed, corresponding to NKX2-1-negative human mucinous adenocarcinoma and Nkx2-1-deleted mouse tumors. Defining features include mucin production, glandular architecture, gastric marker expression (GKN1, CTSE, HNF4α), absence of pulmonary markers, and close morphologic resemblance to human mucinous lung adenocarcinoma.
clinical_or_exposure_context The human counterpart is the mucinous lung adenocarcinoma subtype, comprising about 5-10% of human LUAD and typically lacking NKX2-1 expression; the paper notes KRAS mutations in 60-80% of such cases. It also states that NKX2-1-negative tumors have worse prognosis than NKX2-1-positive tumors and are associated with altered differentiation state; smoking status or treatment history are not central to this paper.
cross_cancer_parallel The paper explicitly compares latent differentiation program de-repression to pancreatic intraepithelial neoplasia and to gastric/esophageal precursor lesions adopting intestinal identity. It also analogizes master-regulator logic to Foxa1/2 interactions in liver, pancreas, and breast, and notes HNF4α roles in gut and liver neoplasia.
therapeutic_vulnerability_or_implication The main implication is biomarker- and state-based rather than a specific approved therapy: lineage state and gastric differentiation may influence tumor burden and differentiation-linked behavior, and HNF4α loss reduces tumor initiation in the Nkx2-1-negative setting. The paper suggests that differentiation programs constrain tumor growth, but it does not test a targeted drug; the evidence for therapeutic relevance is preclinical and mechanistic.
validation_evidence_and_model_system Validated in conditional GEMMs and derived tumor analyses: KrasLSL-G12D;Nkx2-1F/F and KrasFSF-G12D;RosaCreERT2;Nkx2-1F/F mice, KrasLA2 tumors, and human LUAD IHC cohorts. Supports include histology, IHC, Alcian blue/PAS, qRT-PCR, microarray, ChIP-seq, Foxa1/2 and Nkx2-1 rescue in cell lines, and Hnf4a double-deletion experiments that alter tumor burden and Hmga2 expression.
