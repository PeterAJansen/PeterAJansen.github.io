Extraction Schema Details for extraction-schema-1845

Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-1845

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of driver-negative or oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma cases, cohorts, models, or subgroups; the evidence for why they are considered driver-negative; candidate alternative drivers or mechanisms; proposed subtypes; validation strategies; comparisons to other cancers; and therapeutic implications.

Extraction Schema (Details)

Field Name Type Description
cohort_or_model str The patient cohort, tumor subset, cell line, organoid, mouse model, PDX, or dataset being studied. Include cohort name or model identifier if given.
cancer_context str The cancer type and context studied, such as lung adenocarcinoma, NSCLC adenocarcinoma, early-stage LUAD, metastatic LUAD, never-smoker LUAD, or pan-cancer comparison.
driver_negative_definition str How the paper defines oncogene-negative or driver-negative status. Include which canonical drivers were excluded and by what assays if stated, for example EGFR/KRAS/ALK/ROS1/BRAF/MET/RET/ERBB2 negative by DNA/RNA sequencing.
driver_negative_frequency str Reported prevalence or proportion of driver-negative cases in the studied cohort, including numerical value and units or percentages.
profiling_modalities str The molecular or functional profiling methods used, such as whole-exome sequencing, whole-genome sequencing, RNA-seq, fusion calling, methylation, ATAC-seq, proteomics, phosphoproteomics, metabolomics, single-cell, spatial transcriptomics, microbiome profiling, CRISPR screens, or drug screens.
candidate_mechanism_category str The broad category of the proposed driver or mechanism. Examples: rare coding mutation, fusion/rearrangement, copy-number amplification, enhancer hijacking, epigenetic dysregulation, lineage plasticity, autocrine/paracrine ligand signaling, alternative pathway activation, phosphoproteomic activation without mutation, microbiome-associated effect, immune-mediated mechanism, metabolic dependency, tumor suppressor loss, noncoding alteration.
candidate_driver_or_mechanism str The specific candidate driver gene, pathway, program, ligand-receptor axis, microbe, cellular state, or biological mechanism proposed to explain driver-negative disease.
mechanistic_description str A concise mechanistic summary of how the candidate is proposed to drive tumorigenesis or maintenance, such as MAPK activation via ligand autocrine loop, YAP/TEAD dependence after STK11 loss, or phospho-ERBB signaling despite no ERBB mutation.
evidence_type str The main type of evidence supporting the candidate mechanism. Examples: recurrent alteration in tumors, pathway enrichment, phosphosite activation, functional dependency screen, perturbation experiment, drug sensitivity, single-cell state association, survival association, microbiome association, or cross-cancer analogy.
key_evidence str A brief, information-dense summary of the main evidence or result supporting the mechanism. Include important quantitative findings when available.
negative_or_counter_evidence str Any evidence in the paper that limits, contradicts, or weakens the candidate mechanism, such as non-reproducibility, low prevalence, lack of functional validation, confounding, or absence in independent cohorts. Null if not discussed.
subtype_name str If the paper proposes a molecular or biological subtype within driver-negative tumors, extract the subtype name or label.
subtype_features str Key defining features of the subtype, such as expression signature, lineage markers, smoking status, immune contexture, phosphoproteomic state, co-mutations, histology, microbiome features, or clinical behavior.
parallel_in_other_cancers str Any analogous driver-negative or noncanonical-driver phenomenon described in other cancers, including the cancer type and the analogous mechanism.
validation_approach str Specific validation strategies used or proposed to test the mechanism, such as orthogonal sequencing, RNA/protein confirmation, phosphoproteomics, ligand blockade, co-culture, microbiome manipulation, CRISPR knockout, rescue experiments, organoid testing, or prospective biomarker study.
validation_result str Outcome of the validation experiment or proposed rationale for why the validation would be informative. Include quantitative results if reported.
therapeutic_implication str Any therapy, target, biomarker strategy, or treatment hypothesis linked to the candidate mechanism or subtype, such as kinase inhibitor sensitivity, ligand blockade, immunotherapy relevance, metabolic targeting, synthetic lethality, or combination therapy.
therapeutic_evidence str Evidence for the therapeutic implication, including preclinical drug response, clinical response, resistance pattern, or biomarker-treatment association.
clinical_or_demographic_association str Any association with smoking status, age, sex, ethnicity, stage, metastatic site, histologic pattern, prognosis, or treatment history that may help categorize driver-negative tumors.
study_limitations str Important limitations explicitly noted or strongly evident from the paper, such as small sample size, incomplete driver testing, retrospective design, assay sensitivity limits, batch effects, or lack of functional follow-up.
driver_negativity_confidence str Assign a standardized confidence label for true oncogene-negative status: strict, moderate, low, or not_applicable. Base this only on assay breadth, orthogonal validation, tumor purity caveats, and whether whole-genome/RNA/fusion/CNV/noncoding layers were assessed.
negative_status_basis str State the exact operational basis of negativity in normalized form, such as egfr_wt_only, egfr_kras_alk_triple_negative, eight_gene_panel_negative, non_caga, rtk_ras_raf_wes_negative, or rtk_ras_raf_wgs_negative.
tested_driver_classes str List the alteration classes explicitly assayed in the paper as a standardized list, choosing from hotspot_snv_indel, exome_mutation, genome_mutation, fusion_rearrangement, exon_skipping_splicing, focal_amplification, broad_copy_number, noncoding_regulatory, protein_abundance, phosphoproteomics, epigenomics, and spatial_microenvironment.
untested_or_underpowered_driver_classes str List major driver classes not assessed or likely underpowered because of panel limitations, low purity, stromal contamination, low allele fraction, RNA-only inference, missing orthogonal validation, or incomplete intronic/noncoding coverage.
false_negative_rescue_evidence str Record whether deeper or orthogonal profiling later identified an occult driver in an initially negative cohort, and summarize the rescued mechanism concisely, such as hidden_egfr, ret_fusion, met_ex14, ntrk_fusion, or cnv_driver.
sample_purity_detection_limit_issue str Extract explicit evidence that technical limitations may have hidden drivers, including low tumor purity, stromal contamination, low VAF, assay coverage gaps, or failed FISH/RNA quality issues; represent as a short standardized list plus brief note.
mapk_output_state str Extract MAPK pathway output as high, low, mixed, or unresolved, based on direct evidence such as phospho-ERK, phospho-MEK, phosphoproteomics, RPPA, or stated pathway-activity signatures.
pi3k_akt_mtor_output_state str Extract PI3K/AKT/mTOR output as high, low, mixed, or unresolved, based on phospho-AKT, phospho-S6, phospho-4EBP1, phosphoproteomics, RPPA, or explicit pathway-activity statements.
rtk_or_her_family_activation_state str Record direct upstream receptor activation evidence, such as activated EGFR, HER2/HER3, ALK, MET, RET, TRKA, or other RTKs, using phosphosite, IHC, or ligand-response data.
signaling_readout_assay str List the assay and marker(s) used to infer signaling state, such as pERK_IHC, phosphoproteomics, RPPA, conditioned_media_phosphorylation_assay, or kinase_enrichment.
genome_protein_discordance_flag str Mark yes, no, or unclear for whether the paper explicitly reports activated signaling without a matching canonical genomic driver lesion.
driver_mechanism_route str Assign the primary route by which the candidate mechanism acts, using a standardized category such as activating_point_mutation, truncating_tumor_suppressor_loss, in_frame_fusion, exon_skipping_splice_alteration, focal_amplification, broad_gain, enhancer_hijacking, promoter_noncoding_mutation, phosphoprotein_activation_without_genomic_lesion, ligand_driven_signaling, lineage_factor_dependency, or microenvironmental_state.
altered_molecular_layer str Record the main molecular layer where the abnormality is detected: dna_sequence, structural_dna, rna_splice_fusion, rna_outlier_expression, protein_abundance, phosphoprotein, chromatin_epigenome, cell_state_composition, or spatial_microenvironment.
functional_consequence_type str Extract the standardized functional effect, such as pathway_activation, loss_of_negative_regulation, transcriptional_reprogramming, ligand_receptor_signaling, immune_exclusion, ferroptosis_resistance, dna_repair_defect, genome_instability, or lineage_survival_dependency.
canonical_pathway_reinstatement_flag str Mark yes, no, or unclear for whether the paper interprets the mechanism as restoring RTK/RAS/RAF-MAPK or PI3K signaling despite absence of canonical genomic drivers.
alternative_driver_axis str If not primarily canonical-pathway reinstatement, record the alternative axis emphasized by the paper, such as hippo_yap, wnt, ferroptosis, immune, angiogenesis, stemness_quiescence, hrd, apobec, chromatin_splicing, or metabolic_stress.
