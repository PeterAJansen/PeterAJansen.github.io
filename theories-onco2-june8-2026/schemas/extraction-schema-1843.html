Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-1843

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of hidden genomic, structural, regulatory, or assay-missed drivers in oncogene-negative lung adenocarcinoma.

Extraction Schema (Details)

Field Name Type Description
oncogene_negative_definition str Operational definition of the negative cohort, including the exact tested genes, events, and platforms used to call the tumor negative for canonical drivers.
assay_strategy_and_detection_limits str The discovery pipeline used to search for drivers, including targeted DNA panel, WES, WGS, RNA-seq, fusion assay, methylation, cDNA methods, FISH, IHC, or other tests, plus stated blind spots or failure modes.
missed_driver_reclassified_after_broader_testing str Whether a tumor initially labeled driver-negative was later reclassified after broader or deeper testing. Include the rescued alteration, rescue modality, and why it was missed initially, such as poor coverage, no RNA assay, structural complexity, low purity, or noncanonical domain location.
driver_event_class str The class of hidden genomic or regulatory event discovered. Examples: rare SNV, indel, fusion, exon skipping, amplification, focal deletion, complex rearrangement, enhancer hijacking, promoter mutation, noncoding regulatory event, or transcriptional outlier.
candidate_driver_gene_or_locus str The specific gene, fusion, locus, or regulatory element implicated as the hidden driver. Include partner genes for fusions and nearby target genes for regulatory events.
structural_or_regulatory_activation_mechanism str Mechanistic interpretation of the event, such as kinase-domain-preserving fusion, promoter swapping, enhancer hijacking, super-enhancer formation, coiled-coil mediated activation, dosage increase, biallelic suppressor loss, or truncating inactivation.
functional_consequence str The reported biological consequence of the event, such as pathway activation, overexpression, exon skipping, loss of negative regulation, receptor activation, or transcriptional outlier behavior.
co_alteration_or_combinatorial_requirement str Any reported cooperating alterations or minimal combinations required for tumorigenesis. Include co-occurring suppressor losses, mutual exclusivity, or multi-hit combinations that together explain driver-negative disease.
human_prevalence_or_enrichment str Frequency or enrichment of the hidden event in the driver-negative subset or human cohorts, including counts, percentages, and comparisons against driver-positive tumors when reported.
functional_or_clinical_validation str Evidence that the hidden event is a real driver, such as transformation assays, knockdown, overexpression, xenografts, pathway readouts, matched targeted therapy response, or independent cohort replication.
